\section{Introduction}

The ADW (Alexandrov-Diakonov-Vergeles) emergent-gravity programme
\cite{Sakharov1968,Adler1982,Diakonov2011,VladimirovDiakonov2012,Vergeles2025}
posits that the Einstein-Hilbert effective action arises from
integrating out a microscopic 8-fermion sector at a hard ultraviolet
cutoff $\LambdaUV$. Working with tetrads $e^{a}_{\mu}$ rather than the
metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ is a load-bearing convention of the programme:
the fermion sector demands tetrad indices to host the Dirac
$\gamma^{a}$ matrices, and the resulting heat-kernel expansion
\cite{Vassilevich2003,Roehm2026Wave1} naturally lives on the
tetrad bundle.

A direct structural consequence of working with tetrads is that the
underlying gravitational theory is Einstein-Cartan rather than
strict Riemannian: torsion $T^{a}_{\mu\nu} = e^{a}_{\mu;\nu}
- e^{a}_{\nu;\mu}$ is generically non-zero, sourced by the
\emph{fermion spin current} $S^{a}_{\mu\nu}$ via the Hehl-style
algebraic Cartan equation \cite{Hehl1976,Trautman1973}
\begin{equation}
  T^{a}_{\mu\nu} \;\propto\; \GN \cdot S^{a}_{\mu\nu}.
  \label{eq:cartan-algebraic}
\end{equation}
The natural question is whether the predicted torsion amplitude at
natural microscopic parameters respects the tightest published
torsion observational bounds: Kostelecký-Russell-Tasson
\cite{KosteleckyRussellTasson2008}
($\TKost = 10^{-31}\,\mathrm{GeV}$) and Hughes-Drever
\cite{Lammerzahl2001} ($\THD = 10^{-29}\,\mathrm{GeV}$).

We formalize the answer in the Lean~4 proof assistant: the prediction
sits ~46 orders of magnitude below Kostelecký at all natural
parameter points. The Wave~6 result is therefore not a structural
surprise but a quantitative consistency check --- a non-trivial
falsifiability anchor for the ADW emergent-gravity programme.

\section{Discussion: torsion as a soft observational signature}

A common motivation for the absence of torsion-related observational
constraints in the ADW emergent-gravity literature is the tacit
assumption that torsion is ``small''. Wave~6 quantifies this: at
the natural microscopic point, the predicted amplitude is
$\simeq 2\times 10^{-77}\,\mathrm{GeV}$, which is~46 orders of
magnitude below the Kostelecký bound. Three structural observations
follow:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Non-zero}: the prediction is structurally non-zero
(positivity theorem
\texttt{torsionAmplitude\_pos}), so the Wave~6 model is
\emph{not} the trivial-torsion limit; the bound passes via small
amplitude rather than via cancellation.

\item \emph{Linear in spin density}: $|\TEC|$ scales linearly with
$\nspin$, so any astrophysical environment with much higher spin
density (e.g.\ inside neutron stars, $\nspin \sim m_{n}^{3} \sim
10^{-3}\,\mathrm{GeV}^{3}$) would still produce
$|\TEC| \sim 10^{-39}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ at $\MPl$ cutoff --- still
8 orders of magnitude below Kostelecký. Local environments do not
alter the verdict.

\item \emph{Inverse in $\Nf\,\LambdaUV^{2}$}: lowering the cutoff
to TeV scales $\LambdaUV \sim 10^{3}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ amplifies
$|\TEC|$ by $\sim 10^{32}$, but the prediction still sits at
$\sim 10^{-45}\,\mathrm{GeV}$ --- safely below Kostelecký by 14
decades. The scenario does not produce observable torsion in any
natural parameter regime.
\end{enumerate}

The Wave~6 result is therefore observationally \emph{conservative}:
torsion is structurally present but quantitatively too small to be
observed by current Lorentz-violation precision tests. This is
consistent with the broader Phase~6e finding that the ADW emergent-
gravity programme is observationally consistent at the macroscopic
level (Wave~4 PPN observables, Wave~5 cosmological-constant
reproduction in emergent form).
