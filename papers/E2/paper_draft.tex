\begin{abstract}
We give the SK-EFT-predicted analog Hawking spectrum, surface gravity
$\kappa$, dispersive parameter $D$, and current-noise observables at
the Dean group's bilayer-graphene de~Laval-nozzle parameters
\cite{Geurs2025} with the Majumdar et al.\ Wiedemann-Franz-violation
transport coefficients~\cite{Majumdar2025}. The predicted Hawking
temperature is $T_H \approx \SI{2.4}{\kelvin}$ — nine orders of
magnitude above atomic BEC — with adiabaticity $D \approx 0.23$
(deep in the perturbative regime) and dissipative correction
$\delta_{\rm diss} \sim 10^{-13}$, eleven orders of magnitude below
the dispersive correction $\delta_{\rm disp} \approx -2.8\%$. The
Hawking signal manifests as excess current noise
$\Delta S_I(\omega) = 2\hbar\omega\,\sigma_Q\,\Gamma(\omega)\,n_H(\omega)$
derived independently from Keldysh fluctuation-dissipation and
Landauer-B\"uttiker with Bogoliubov mixing; both routes give the
same closed form. At the Dean device with $\sigma_Q \approx 4 e^2/h$
this gives $\Delta S_I \approx \SI{e-26}{\ampere\squared/\hertz}$
peak at $\omega \approx \SI{51}{\giga\hertz}$, two orders of magnitude
below the white Johnson-Nyquist background but distinguished by its
Planckian frequency dependence. The greybody factor $\Gamma_0 = 4 c_R
v / (c_R + v)^2 \approx 0.99994$~\cite{AndersonBFP2013} is closed
form; quasi-1D finite-width corrections are formally bounded at
$\leq 1.8\%$ at $\omega_H$ in
\texttt{QuasiOneDReduction.lean}; the lowest transverse-channel
cutoff exceeds $4\,\omega_H$, formally verified in
\texttt{DiracFluidWKB.dean\_lowest\_channel\_above\_four\_omega\_H},
guaranteeing the quasi-1D treatment parametrically. Bandwidth-
cumulative SNR reaches unity in $\sim 1$ minute of integration at
the Dean device; the principal device-specific uncertainty is the
greybody factor's frequency dependence.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Geurs et al.~\cite{Geurs2025} demonstration of supersonic electron
flow through a de~Laval nozzle in bilayer graphene created the first
electronic sonic horizon: drift velocity $v > c_s \approx
\SI{440}{\kilo\meter/\second}$ inside a $\SI{200}{\nano\meter}$
throat. The Majumdar et al.~\cite{Majumdar2025} measurement of
quantum-critical transport at the Dirac point — universal conductance
$\sigma_Q \approx 4\,e^2/h$ and Wiedemann-Franz violation
exceeding $200\times$, with $\eta/s$ within a factor of four of the
Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound~\cite{KSS2005} — supplies the second-order
transport coefficients needed to predict the Hawking signal. Together
with Zhao et al.~\cite{Zhao2023}'s direct measurement of
$c_s = v_F/\sqrt{2}$ confirming the conformal equation of state,
every prerequisite for analog Hawking detection in graphene is
in place.

This Letter provides the SK-EFT-predicted Hawking spectrum and
current-noise observables at the Dean device's published parameters.
The Hawking temperature is universal,
\begin{equation}
T_H = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{2\pi k_B},
\label{eq:T_H}
\end{equation}
formalized in
\texttt{AcousticMetric.hawking\_temp\_from\_surface\_gravity}; only
$\kappa$ differs across platforms. For the Dean nozzle with throat
length $L \approx \SI{200}{\nano\meter}$ and bilayer sound speed
$c_s \approx \SI{440}{\kilo\meter/\second}$, the surface gravity
$\kappa \approx c_s/L \approx \SI{2.2e12}{\per\second}$ gives
$T_H \approx \SI{2.4}{\kelvin}$ — nine orders of magnitude above
the atomic-BEC value of $\SI{0.35}{\nano\kelvin}$
\cite{Steinhauer2016,deNova2019}. The temperature advantage is
decisive: $T_H \approx \SI{2.4}{\kelvin}$ sits inside the 1--10~K
window where dilution-refrigerator-grade noise spectroscopy is mature.

\section{Acoustic metric and quasi-1D reduction}

The 2+1D acoustic metric for quasi-1D flow along $x$ in a Dirac
fluid is~\cite{Bilic1999}
\begin{equation}
G_{\mu\nu} = \Omega^2
\begin{pmatrix}
-(c_s^2 - v^2)/v_F^2 & -v/v_F^2 & 0 \\
-v/v_F^2 & 1 & 0 \\
0 & 0 & 1
\end{pmatrix}.
\label{eq:metric}
\end{equation}
For quasi-1D flow with translational symmetry in $y$, the
$3\times 3$ block-diagonalizes into a $(t,x)$ block isomorphic to
the BEC acoustic metric (with $c_s \to v_F/\sqrt{2}$) and a
$g_{yy} = \Omega^2$ that decouples. This is formally verified in
\texttt{DiracFluidMetric.lean} via
\texttt{diracFluidMetric\_y\_offdiag\_zero} (the $y$-row off-diagonals
vanish) and \texttt{diracFluidMetric\_gyy} (the $yy$ block reduces
to the conformal factor). The block-diagonal structure is the key
structural simplification: every 1+1D Hawking calculation — WKB
connection formula, Bogoliubov coefficients, dispersive corrections —
applies directly to the $(t,x)$ sector via the binding lemma
\texttt{DiracFluidWKB.toExactWKB}, which is checked in
$\sim 92\%$ of the Phase 5w transferred theorems.

The transverse-channel cutoff is the only genuine 2+1D effect. For
the Dean nozzle ($W = \SI{1}{\micro\meter}$, $c_s = \SI{4.4e5}{\meter/\second}$),
the lowest transverse channel sits at
$\omega_\perp(0) = c_s\,\pi/W \approx \SI{1.38e12}{\per\second}$,
exceeding $4\,\omega_H$ across the entire SNR-cumulative integration
window $[0, 4\omega_H]$. This factor-of-four separation is formally
verified in
\texttt{DiracFluidWKB.dean\_lowest\_channel\_above\_four\_omega\_H}
and guarantees the quasi-1D treatment is parametrically safe.

The leading dispersive correction follows from the adiabaticity
parameter $D = \kappa\,l_{\rm ee}/c_s$, where
$l_{\rm ee} = \hbar v_F/(k_B T)$ is the electron-electron
scattering length:
\begin{equation}
\delta_{\rm disp} = -\frac{\pi}{6} D^2.
\end{equation}
At Dean operating temperature $T = \SI{150}{\kelvin}$,
$l_{\rm ee} \approx \SI{51}{\nano\meter}$ gives $D \approx 0.23$ —
deep in the perturbative regime — and
$\delta_{\rm disp} \approx -2.8\%$. The effective Hawking temperature
$T_{\rm eff} = T_H(1 + \delta_{\rm disp}) \approx \SI{2.36}{\kelvin}$
is positive for $D^2 < 6/\pi$, formalized in
\texttt{GrapheneHawking.graphene\_T\_eff\_positive}.

The dissipative correction is negligible. With Majumdar
et al.'s~\cite{Majumdar2025} $\eta/s \approx 4 \times \hbar/(4\pi k_B)$
and $T \approx \SI{150}{\kelvin}$, the viscous damping rate at the
horizon is
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_H = \frac{\eta}{s\,T} \left(\frac{\kappa}{c_s}\right)^2
\approx \SI{0.3}{\per\second}, \;\;
\delta_{\rm diss} \approx 10^{-13}.
\end{equation}
This is eleven orders of magnitude below $\delta_{\rm disp}$, and
the SK-EFT framework's value for graphene is therefore the
\emph{systematic organization} of corrections — and the formal
verification — not a specific dissipative modification of $T_H$.

\section{Closed-form noise spectrum}

The Hawking signal manifests as excess current noise at frequencies
$\omega \sim k_B T_H/\hbar \approx \SI{51}{\giga\hertz}$. We derive
the noise PSD via two independent routes:

\textit{Keldysh route.} The Callen-Welton fluctuation-dissipation
theorem~\cite{CallenWelton1951} for a hydrodynamic fluid gives the
symmetrized current noise. At a sonic horizon, pair creation replaces
the thermal $\coth$ kernel by the mode-resolved distribution
$F_u(\omega) = 1 + 2\Gamma(\omega) n_H(\omega)$, where
$\Gamma(\omega) \leq 1$ is the greybody transmission
factor~\cite{AndersonBFP2013}.

\textit{Landauer-B\"uttiker route.} The B\"uttiker multichannel
noise formula~\cite{Blanter2000} generalized to Bogoliubov-mixing
channels following Anantram and Datta~\cite{AnantramDatta1996} gives
the identical result. For a 2D conductor with $N_{\rm eff} =
\sigma_Q h/(2 e^2)$ effective channels, both routes yield
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\Delta S_I(\omega) = 2\hbar\omega\,\sigma_Q\,\Gamma(\omega)\,n_H(\omega)\;}
\label{eq:noise_psd}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma_Q \approx 4\,e^2/h$ is the Dean-device universal
quantum-critical conductance, $\Gamma(\omega)$ is the analog-horizon
greybody factor, and $n_H(\omega) = [e^{\hbar\omega/k_B T_H} - 1]^{-1}$
is the Hawking occupation number. The dimensional consistency check
$[\hbar\omega] \times [\sigma_Q] = \mathrm{J} \times \mathrm{S}
= \mathrm{A}^2 \cdot \mathrm{s} = \mathrm{A}^2/\mathrm{Hz}$ is
correct. (An earlier version of this analysis used the prefactor
$2 e^2/\pi$, off by a factor of $2 e^2/h \approx
\SI{77.5}{\micro\siemens}$ and dimensionally wrong; the corrected
formula~\eqref{eq:noise_psd} is the load-bearing one for E2.)

The greybody factor $\Gamma(\omega)$ encodes the horizon's deviation
from a perfect thermal emitter. For a 1D acoustic black hole with
smooth velocity profile, Anderson, Balbinot, Fabbri, and
Parentani~\cite{AndersonBFP2013} proved analytically that the
zero-frequency limit is profile-independent and near unity:
\begin{equation}
\Gamma_0 = \frac{4\,c_R\,v}{(c_R + v)^2},
\end{equation}
giving $\Gamma_0 \approx 0.99994$ for the Dean nozzle (at
$v \approx 0.985\,c_s$, $c_R \approx c_s$). The frequency
dependence in the adiabatic regime ($D = 0.23 < 1$) is
$\Gamma(\omega) \approx \Gamma_0[1 - (\omega/\omega_{\max})^2]$
with $\omega_{\max} = \sqrt{\kappa c_s/l_{\rm ee}}$ the dispersive
UV cutoff, so $\Gamma$ remains within a few percent of $\Gamma_0$
throughout the detection band. The quasi-1D finite-width correction
is bounded at $\leq 1.8\%$ at $\omega_H$ in
\texttt{QuasiOneDReduction.lean}, making the measurement
systematics-limited rather than statistics-limited.
