\title{Kernel-Verified Quantum-Network and Device-Characterization
Certification Substrate}

\author{John Roehm}

\begin{abstract}
We report a machine-checked Lean~4 / Mathlib4 substrate for certifying
quantum networks and quantum devices from universally stated parameters:
five interlocking layers, comprising (at the time of writing) 103 Lean
modules and over 900 kernel-checked theorems, with no project-local axioms
and no compiled-evaluation trust escapes. The layers are (i) a
\emph{diamond-norm program} --- trace distance, Uhlmann fidelity, both
Fuchs--van de Graaf inequalities, the two-sided Choi-matrix sandwich on the
diamond distance, the full Watrous semidefinite-programming duality with
attainment of both the primal supremum and the dual infimum, and exact
diamond distances for the named channel families (Pauli and general
unitary-error-basis channels, dephasing, depolarizing, qutrit
Weyl--Heisenberg, readout bit-flip), with two-sided brackets where the
channel is not covariant (amplitude damping and its thermal
generalization); (ii) an \emph{entropy and majorization corpus} --- the
Klein inequality, subadditivity, concavity, the Gibbs variational bound,
the Fannes--Audenaert continuity bound including the sharp unconditional
Audenaert form with constant $\log(d-1)$, Lidskii--Wielandt-type
majorization, and a negativity/PPT/partial-transpose ladder up to a
regularized distillation-rate bound; (iii) \emph{network envelopes} ---
a model-independent fidelity envelope for Werner entanglement-swap chains
and its memory-decay-inclusive generalization, DEJMPS/BBPSSW distillation,
the unconditional Horodecki teleportation fidelity, BB84 secret-key
positivity with a proven (not hardcoded) crossover, repeater and W-state
rates, repeaterless rate ceilings in the PLOB and erasure forms, and
network capacity with max-flow/min-cut weak duality, witnessed optimality,
and full strong duality, built in-project; (iv) a
\emph{device-characterization envelope family} --- the coherence-limited
average-gate-fidelity ceiling from $T_1/T_2$ data, composed-gate and SPAM
process fidelities, a randomized-benchmarking-style two-sided certificate,
classical Johnson--Nyquist and derived quantum Callen--Welton
($\tfrac{\hbar\omega}{2}\coth$) fluctuation--dissipation noise floors, and
readout-window envelopes (relaxation decay probability and a thermal
two-level-population assignment floor derived from a community-library
canonical ensemble); and (v) a \emph{kernel-only rational-enclosure
technique} that brackets every decaying-exponential operating point
between rational bounds with no floating-point transcendentals. The
methodological through-line, which we propose as a contribution in its own
right, is a strict \emph{two-layer posture}: the formula layer is
machine-verified; the identification of a formula with a physical device
or channel quantity is literature-cited and carried as an explicit,
named model hypothesis in every floor and ceiling theorem --- never
conflated with the verified mathematics. Known limits of the verified
layer (notably the Caves amplifier added-noise bound, whose bosonic
commutation-relation substrate is absent from current formal libraries)
are documented as cited hypotheses rather than silently absorbed.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Quantum networks and quantum processors are characterized through a small
set of recurring mathematical quantities: fidelities and trace distances
between states, diamond distances between channels, entropies and
entanglement measures, protocol rates, and noise floors. The literature
that defines these quantities is mature, but the chains of inequalities
that connect a measured device parameter (a $T_1$ time, a link
transmissivity, a readout window) to a certified statement (``the
end-to-end fidelity lies in this interval''; ``the average assignment
error cannot be below this floor'') are assembled per-paper, per-simulator,
and per-vendor, with conventions and side conditions that drift.
Quantum-network simulators are a concrete instance: cross-validation
studies have found quantitative disagreement between mature simulators on
timing predictions, while fidelity predictions agree under matched error
models~\cite{Chung2025} --- precisely the situation in which a
machine-checked, model-independent envelope provides a deductive
reference. Any computed end-to-end fidelity falling outside the proven
interval is provably inconsistent with the stated model, independently of
implementation details.

This paper reports such a reference, developed in the Lean~4 proof
assistant on Mathlib: a certification substrate for entanglement-based
quantum networks and for the characterization of quantum devices, in five
interlocking layers. Every theorem in the corpus is verified to the Lean
kernel with axiom set
$\{\texttt{propext},\,\texttt{Classical.choice},\,\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$
(the standard Lean kernel), with no project-local axioms, no \texttt{sorry}
placeholders, and no compiled-evaluation (\texttt{native\_decide}) trust
escapes. At the time of writing the corpus comprises 103 Lean modules and
over 900 kernel-checked theorems in the \lean{QuantumNetwork} module
family.

\paragraph*{The two-layer posture.} The contribution we wish to emphasize
ahead of any individual theorem is a scoping discipline that the corpus
enforces uniformly and that we believe certification-grade formalization
requires. Every result lives on one of two layers, and the layers are
never conflated:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{The formula layer (machine-verified).} The mathematical
content --- a closed form, an inequality, a monotonicity, an enclosure ---
is a kernel-checked theorem about explicitly defined mathematical objects.

\item \textbf{The identification layer (literature-cited).} The reading of
that mathematical object as a \emph{physical} quantity --- ``this Kraus
family is the device's readout channel,'' ``realized noise is at least the
coherence-limited model,'' ``the excited-branch misassignment is at least
the window decay probability'' --- is a modeling step. It enters every
floor and ceiling theorem as an explicit, named hypothesis, with the
literature source cited in the module header, and is never silently
absorbed into the verified statement.
\end{enumerate}

A representative example: the repeaterless secret-key bound of
Pirandola--Laurenza--Ottaviani--Banchi~\cite{PLOB2017} rests on
continuous-variable channel-capacity theory (the relative entropy of
entanglement of bosonic Gaussian channels) that no current formal library
provides. The corpus therefore formalizes the \emph{rate-bound function}
$\mathrm{plob}(\eta) = -\log_2(1-\eta)$ with its rigorous loss-penalty
properties, and states plainly that the channel-converse semantics is the
cited theorem of Ref.~\cite{PLOB2017}, not a formalized one. The same
posture governs the Caves amplifier bound (Sec.~\ref{sec:walls}), the
coherence-limited fidelity ceiling, and the readout floors. We regard this
honest two-layer bookkeeping --- which keeps the verified artifact
maximally strong without overclaiming physics --- as the paper's
methodological headline.

\paragraph*{Contributions.} Concretely, the five layers are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{The diamond-norm program} (Sec.~\ref{sec:diamond}): the
finite-dimensional distinguishability calculus --- trace norm and trace
distance as a genuine metric, Uhlmann fidelity, both Fuchs--van de Graaf
bounds~\cite{FuchsvandeGraaf1999}, CPTP data processing in both the
trace-distance and fidelity domains, the diamond
distance~\cite{Kitaev1997} with attainment, the two-sided Choi sandwich
and the full Watrous SDP duality~\cite{Watrous2018} with both attainments,
and a library of exact diamond distances for named channels.

\item \emph{The entropy and majorization corpus} (Sec.~\ref{sec:entropy}):
Klein, subadditivity, concavity, the Gibbs variational bound, the
Fannes~\cite{Fannes1973}--Audenaert~\cite{Audenaert2007} continuity chain
through an unconditional Mirsky eigenvalue step~\cite{Mirsky1960}, the
sharp Audenaert constant $\log(d-1)$ in unconditional form, spectral
majorization in the Lidskii--Wielandt style~\cite{Bhatia1997}, and the
negativity/PPT ladder~\cite{Peres1996,HorodeckiPPT1996,VidalWerner2002}
up to a regularized distillation-rate bound.

\item \emph{Network envelopes} (Sec.~\ref{sec:network}): the
model-independent end-to-end fidelity envelope for Werner swap
chains~\cite{BDCZ1998,WernerState1989} and its decay-inclusive
generalization; BBPSSW~\cite{BBPSSW1996} and DEJMPS~\cite{DEJMPS1996}
distillation including a verified non-monotonicity witness; the
unconditional Horodecki teleportation fidelity~\cite{Horodecki1999} with
its Haar integral proven rather than assumed; BB84 secret-key
positivity~\cite{BB84,ShorPreskill2000} with the crossover produced by the
intermediate-value theorem; W-state random-pair
rates~\cite{FortescueLo2007}; linear-optics Bell-measurement and
elementary-link rate anchors~\cite{CalsamigliaLutkenhaus2001,Sangouard2011};
the PLOB~\cite{PLOB2017} and erasure~\cite{BennettDiVincenzoSmolin1997}
rate ceilings in two-layer form; and network capacity with weak duality,
witnessed max-flow/min-cut equality, and full strong
duality~\cite{FordFulkerson1956} --- the latter built in-project, as
Mathlib at our pin has no max-flow/min-cut theory.

\item \emph{The device-characterization envelope family}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:device}): the average-gate/entanglement-fidelity identity
$F_{\mathrm{avg}} = (dF_e+1)/(d+1)$~\cite{Horodecki1999,Nielsen2002}
proven constructively; the coherence-limited fidelity ceiling from
$T_1/T_2$ data; composed-gate and SPAM process fidelities; a
randomized-benchmarking-style certificate
bracket~\cite{Magesan2011}; the classical
Johnson--Nyquist~\cite{Nyquist1928} and the \emph{derived} quantum
Callen--Welton~\cite{CallenWelton1951} fluctuation--dissipation floors,
the latter obtained by instantiating a community-library canonical
ensemble~\cite{Meiburg2025PhysLib} on the oscillator spectrum; and the
readout-window envelopes (relaxation decay
probability~\cite{Gambetta2007,Walter2017,Geerlings2013} and the thermal
assignment floor~\cite{Jin2015}), each with rational-enclosure
corollaries.

\item \emph{The kernel-only rational-enclosure technique}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:enclosure}): a uniform method --- Bernoulli brackets plus
degree-$n$ Taylor squeezes --- that converts any decaying-exponential
operating point into a machine-checked rational interval with no
floating-point transcendentals, making every envelope in the corpus
\emph{evaluable} at device parameters inside the proof assistant.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The diamond-norm program}
\label{sec:diamond}

The first layer is the finite-dimensional distinguishability calculus,
built concretely on $\mathrm{Matrix}\;(\mathrm{Fin}\,n)\;(\mathrm{Fin}\,n)\;
\mathbb{C}$ with no Stinespring dilation and no abstract
$C^*$-algebra detour. Throughout, $\mathrm{diamondDist}$ denotes the
\emph{halved} diamond norm, $\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_1,\Phi_2) =
\tfrac12\dnorm{\Phi_1-\Phi_2}$, so that it is $[0,1]$-valued on channel
pairs.

\subsection{Trace distance, fidelity, and the Fuchs--van de Graaf
inequalities}

The base objects are density operators (\lean{IsDensityOperator}:
positive-semidefinite, unit trace), the Schatten-1 norm
(\lean{traceNorm}), and the trace distance
$D(\rho,\sigma)=\tfrac12\trnorm{\rho-\sigma}$. The linchpin
\lean{traceNorm\_posSemidef} ($\trnorm{A} = \operatorname{tr} A$ for
positive semidefinite $A$) is proven without a continuous-functional-calculus
instance, via a characteristic-polynomial root-multiset argument; the
general (non-Hermitian) triangle inequality is obtained through the
Hermitian dilation, making the trace distance a genuine metric
(\lean{traceDist\_triangle}) with range $[0,1]$ on density operators
(\lean{traceDist\_mem\_Icc}). The Uhlmann fidelity~\cite{Uhlmann1976}
enters as the root fidelity $\sqrt F(\rho,\sigma) =
\trnorm{\sqrt\sigma\sqrt\rho}$ (\lean{sqrtFidelity}), proven equal to the
literal Uhlmann trace expression, with $F \le 1$
(\lean{sqrtFidelity\_le\_one}) by a double Cauchy--Schwarz assembly. Both
Fuchs--van de Graaf bounds~\cite{FuchsvandeGraaf1999} are kernel-checked:
\[
1 - \sqrt F(\rho,\sigma) \;\le\; D(\rho,\sigma) \;\le\;
\sqrt{1 - F(\rho,\sigma)},
\]
the lower bound (\lean{one\_sub\_sqrtFidelity\_le\_traceDist}) by a
Powers--St\o rmer-type argument with the non-commuting Loewner step
\emph{removed} rather than assumed, and the upper bound
(\lean{traceDist\_le\_sqrt\_one\_sub\_sqrtFidelity\_sq})
purification-free, via Holevo--Helstrom~\cite{Helstrom1976} plus the
classical inequality, powered by a sharp Schatten-2 Cauchy--Schwarz
$\trnorm{AB} \le \lVert A\rVert_F\,\lVert B\rVert_F$
(\lean{traceNorm\_mul\_le}) built with no singular-value decomposition.

Channels enter as Kraus families: for trace-preserving
$\Phi(\rho)=\sum_k K_k\rho K_k^{\dagger}$, the corpus proves trace-norm
contractivity, trace-distance data processing
(\lean{traceDist\_krausMap\_le}), density-operator preservation, and
positivity of the Choi matrix~\cite{Choi1975}
(\lean{choiMatrix\_krausMap\_posSemidef}). Data processing is then lifted
to the \emph{fidelity} domain in full generality via the Alberti
form~\cite{Alberti1983} of the fidelity, with both the forward bound and
its attainment proven for all positive-semidefinite inputs:
\lean{sqrtFidelity\_krausMap\_ge\_psd\_inputs} gives Uhlmann monotonicity
$F(\Phi\rho,\Phi\sigma) \ge F(\rho,\sigma)$ for \emph{all} density
operators and any trace-preserving Kraus channel --- no full-rank
assumption on inputs or outputs --- together with joint concavity
(\lean{sqrtFidelity\_jointly\_concave}) and Loewner monotonicity
(\lean{sqrtFidelity\_mono\_left}).

\subsection{The diamond distance: metric, attainment, Choi sandwich, and
SDP duality}

The diamond distance~\cite{Kitaev1997,Watrous2018} is defined as the
supremum of output trace distances of the stabilized channels
$\Phi\otimes\mathrm{id}$ over input density operators on the doubled
space; the stabilized channel is proven CPTP
(\lean{isKrausChannel\_tensorKraus}), the supremum is well-defined from
boundedness, and the result is a genuine $[0,1]$-valued metric on
channels (\lean{diamondDist\_triangle}). Three structural results turn
the definition into a certification tool:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Attainment.} The supremum is attained by an optimal input
density operator (\lean{exists\_diamondDist\_eq}), via trace-norm
continuity (\lean{continuous\_traceNorm}, a Lipschitz bound that
sidesteps the discontinuity of individual eigenvalues) and compactness of
the density-operator set.

\item \emph{The two-sided Choi sandwich.} With $J(\Phi)$ the Choi matrix
on an $n$-dimensional system,
\[
\tfrac{1}{2n}\,\trnorm{J(\Phi_1)-J(\Phi_2)} \;\le\;
\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_1,\Phi_2) \;\le\;
n\,\lVert J(\Phi_1)-J(\Phi_2)\rVert_{\infty},
\]
the lower bound from the maximally entangled input
(\lean{diamondDist\_ge\_maxEntangled},
\lean{diamondDist\_ge\_choi\_traceNorm}) and the upper bound
(\lean{diamondDist\_le\_choi\_opNorm}) via a vectorization identity that
moves the Helstrom pairing onto the Choi matrix --- the formalizable
content of the Watrous Choi characterization with no conic duality
required.

\item \emph{Full SDP duality with attainment.} The weak-duality direction
(\lean{diamondDist\_le\_dual\_witness}: any Hermitian $W \succeq 0$ with
$W \succeq J(\Phi_1)-J(\Phi_2)$ certifies
$\mathrm{diamondDist} \le \lVert\mathrm{Tr}_2 W\rVert_\infty$) is
elementary; the \emph{equality}
\[
\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_1,\Phi_2) \;=\;
\inf\{\lVert\mathrm{Tr}_2 W\rVert_\infty :
W \succeq 0,\; W \succeq J(\Phi_1)-J(\Phi_2)\}
\]
(\lean{diamondDist\_eq\_choiSDP}) --- the Watrous semidefinite
program~\cite{Watrous2018} --- is proven unconditionally, via the
geometric Hahn--Banach separation over a self-adjoint-matrix
inner-product carrier whose Riesz vector is the optimal dual witness. The
dual infimum is itself attained (\lean{exists\_choiDualValue\_eq}), so
both sides of the program are genuine optima.
\end{itemize}

Composition turns per-stage distances into end-to-end budgets:
$\mathrm{diamondDist}$ is subadditive under channel composition
(\lean{diamondDist\_composeKraus\_le}), with a worked two-stage instance
(\lean{diamondDist\_dephasing\_after\_depolarizing\_le}: a
depolarizing-then-dephasing pipeline is within $\gamma+p$ of the
identity, discharged from the exact per-stage distances below).

\subsection{Exact diamond distances for named channels}
\label{sec:diamond-named}

For the channel families that device benchmarks actually target, the dual
witness closes the sandwich to exact values:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Dephasing and depolarizing} (covariant):
$\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_\gamma,\mathrm{id}) = \gamma$
(\lean{diamondDist\_dephasing\_eq}) and
$\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Delta_p,\mathrm{id}) = p$
(\lean{diamondDist\_depolarizing\_eq}), each from an explicit optimal
witness, with no twirling machinery.

\item \emph{The Pauli channel}, exactly its total error weight:
$\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_{\mathbf p},\mathrm{id}) = 1-p_0$
(\lean{diamondDist\_pauliKraus\_eq}). This generalizes to any
\emph{unitary error basis} --- a family $\{U_i\}$ with $U_0 = 1$ and
Hilbert--Schmidt orthogonality on $\mathbb{C}^n$ ---
(\lean{diamondDist\_errorBasisKraus\_eq}), with the two-qubit Pauli
channel ($1-p_{00}$, \lean{diamondDist\_twoQubitPauliKraus\_eq}) and the
qutrit Weyl--Heisenberg channel (\lean{diamondDist\_weylKraus\_eq}) as
instances; the pure cyclic-shift leakage channel has diamond distance
exactly its leakage probability (\lean{diamondDist\_shiftLeakage\_eq}).

\item \emph{SPAM readout bit-flip}: the classical readout-error channel
$\Phi_q(\rho)=(1-q)\rho + qX\rho X$ has
$\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_q,\mathrm{id}) = q$
(\lean{diamondDist\_spamBitFlip\_eq}) --- the readout error probability
itself.

\item \emph{Amplitude damping and its thermal generalization}
(non-covariant): honest two-sided brackets in place of unsupported exact
claims, $\gamma/2 \le \mathrm{diamondDist}(\mathcal A_\gamma,\mathrm{id})
\le \gamma + 1 - \sqrt{1-\gamma}$ (\lean{diamondDist\_ampDamp\_ge},
\lean{diamondDist\_ampDamp\_le}) and, for generalized amplitude damping
toward a bath of excited population $N$, the bracket
$(1-N)\gamma \le \mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_{\gamma,N},\mathrm{id}) \le 1$
(\lean{diamondDist\_genAmpDamp\_bracket}), the lower endpoint achievable
and recovering $\gamma$ at $N=0$.
\end{itemize}

The unitary-channel conversion
$\mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_U,\Phi_V) \le \lVert U - V\rVert_{\mathrm{op}}$
and its composition with a verified compiler into the end-to-end
compiled-gate certificate are reported in the companion compilation
bundle (D8), which consumes the present layer; we do not restate that
content here.

\section{The entropy and majorization corpus}
\label{sec:entropy}

The second layer is the entropy calculus on the same concrete matrix
carrier, together with the eigenvalue-majorization toolbox that continuity
bounds require.

\subsection{Entropy inequalities}

The von Neumann entropy (\lean{vonNeumannEntropy}) carries its range
theorems ($0 \le S(\rho) \le \log d$), and the quantum relative entropy
its foundational chain~\cite{NielsenChuang2010}:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Klein inequality}: $S(\rho\,\Vert\,\sigma) \ge 0$
(\lean{relativeEntropy\_nonneg}), by a unitary-overlap Jensen argument on
the eigenbases.

\item \emph{Subadditivity and mutual information}:
$S(\rho_{AB}) \le S(\rho_A) + S(\rho_B)$
(\lean{vonNeumannEntropy\_subadditive}) and $I(A{:}B)\ge 0$
(\lean{mutualInformation\_nonneg}).

\item \emph{Concavity}: $S(\sum_k w_k\rho_k) \ge \sum_k w_k S(\rho_k)$
(\lean{vonNeumannEntropy\_concave}).

\item \emph{Gibbs variational bound}: the free-energy gap identity
$-\mathrm{Re}\operatorname{tr}(\rho\log\sigma) - S(\rho) =
S(\rho\,\Vert\,\sigma)$
(\lean{cross\_sub\_entropy\_eq\_relativeEntropy}) and the bound
$S(\rho) \le -\mathrm{Re}\operatorname{tr}(\rho\log\sigma)$
(\lean{vonNeumannEntropy\_le\_cross}), saturated at $\rho=\sigma$
(\lean{cross\_self\_eq\_entropy}) --- the thermal-state variational
principle in matrix form.
\end{itemize}

For the deeper convexity results the corpus \emph{adopts} rather than
re-proves: a faithful bridge (\lean{toMState}, \lean{toCPTPMap},
\lean{toCPTPMap\_apply\_m}) identifies the corpus's Kraus-family channels
with the bundled channel representation of the community
quantum-information library PhysLib~\cite{Meiburg2025PhysLib}, pinned to
the same Mathlib commit, so that PhysLib's data-processing inequality for
the sandwiched R\'enyi relative entropy transfers to the corpus's channels
(\lean{krausMap\_sandwichedRenyi\_DPI}) and strong subadditivity and
ordinary subadditivity read back as statements about the corpus's density
operators (\lean{repo\_strong\_subadditivity},
\lean{repo\_subadditivity}). A relative-entropy-of-entanglement layer
(\lean{relEntEntanglement}) with separable-witness upper bounds completes
the interoperation. The bridge is an identification, not a Choi
reconstruction, and its kernel-purity is verified by axiom audit.

\subsection{Eigenvalue majorization and the Fannes--Audenaert chain}

Entropy continuity rests on eigenvalue perturbation. The corpus proves
the Schur--Horn-type step that diagonal entries of a Hermitian matrix in
any orthonormal frame are a doubly stochastic mixture of eigenvalues
(\lean{eigenvalue\_eq\_doublyStochastic\_combination}), the Ky Fan-type
partial-sum subadditivity (\lean{sum\_top\_subadditive}), the
Weyl/minimax perturbation bounds with their Lipschitz consequence
(\lean{weyl\_lipschitz})~\cite{Bhatia1997}, and the Mirsky
inequality~\cite{Mirsky1960} in unconditional form
(\lean{mirsky\_unconditional}): the $\ell^1$ distance between sorted
eigenvalue sequences of Hermitian $A, B$ is at most $\trnorm{A-B}$.

On this base the Fannes--Audenaert continuity chain is closed in two
stages. The quantum continuity bound
\lean{quantum\_fannes\_audenaert}~\cite{Fannes1973,Audenaert2007}
delivers $|S(\rho)-S(\sigma)|$ control from the trace distance through
the Mirsky step; and the \emph{sharp} Audenaert
form~\cite{Audenaert2007} is proven \emph{unconditionally} for density
operators (\lean{quantum\_fannes\_audenaert\_sharp}): for
$d = \dim \ge 2$ and trace distance
$T = \tfrac12\trnorm{\rho-\sigma}$ in the monotone regime
$T \le 1 - 1/d$,
\[
|S(\rho)-S(\sigma)| \;\le\; T\log(d-1) + H_2(T),
\]
with the sharp constant $\log(d-1)$ and \emph{no residual hypothesis}:
the classical sharp bound is established on the eigenvalue distributions
by a maximal-coupling/spreading argument and transferred through the
unconditional Mirsky step. To our knowledge this is the first
machine-checked discharge of the sharp Audenaert constant; the statement
matches Ref.~\cite{Audenaert2007} exactly on its stated domain.

\subsection{The negativity/PPT/partial-transpose ladder}

The corpus builds the entanglement-measure ladder concretely, from
two-qubit Bell-diagonal states to arbitrary finite bipartitions:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Exact negativity and the PPT criterion} on Bell-diagonal
states (\lean{negativityBellDiag\_eq}, \lean{ppt\_bellDiagState\_iff})
~\cite{Peres1996,HorodeckiPPT1996,VidalWerner2002}; for the Werner state
of singlet fraction $F$, $N = (2F-1)/2$ for $F\ge\tfrac12$
(\lean{negativityBellDiag\_werner}) and PPT exactly at $F \le \tfrac12$
(\lean{ppt\_werner\_iff}) --- the same threshold as the BBPSSW/DEJMPS
distillability cutoff of Sec.~\ref{sec:network}.

\item \emph{Monotonicity under local operations} and a one-shot
distillation no-go: a state with $\trnorm{\rho^{\Gamma}} < 2$ cannot be
mapped to a Bell pair by any local operation on one party
(\lean{no\_local\_distillation\_to\_bellPair}) --- the axiom-free
operational content of ``local operations cannot create entanglement,''
with no LOCC abstraction.

\item \emph{Log-negativity additivity}
$E_N(\rho\otimes\sigma)=E_N(\rho)+E_N(\sigma)$
(\lean{logNegativity\_add})~\cite{VidalWerner2002,Plenio2005}, whose
substantive content is the dimension-general trace-norm
multiplicativity $\trnorm{A\otimes B} = \trnorm{A}\trnorm{B}$
(\lean{traceNorm\_kronecker}); and Lipschitz continuity of the
partial-transpose trace norm in the trace distance
(\lean{abs\_traceNorm\_pt2\_sub\_le\_traceDist}).

\item \emph{The general-bipartition capstone}: the partial transpose,
monotone, and additive log-negativity at arbitrary finite party types;
the maximal value $E_N(\Phi_d) = \log_2 d$ on the maximally entangled
state (\lean{logNegB\_maxEntState}); the single-copy distillation bound
$\log_2 k \le E_N(\rho)$ (\lean{distillation\_single\_copy\_bound}); and,
on the genuine grouped $n$-copy state, $E_N(\rho^{\otimes n}) = n E_N(\rho)$
(\lean{logNegB\_ncopy}) with the regularized rate reading
$E_N(\Lambda(\rho^{\otimes n})) \le n E_N(\rho)$
(\lean{logNegB\_ncopy\_localKraus\_le}) --- the per-$n$ form of
$E_D \le E_N$~\cite{VidalWerner2002}.
\end{itemize}

\section{Network envelopes}
\label{sec:network}

The third layer certifies protocol-level quantities for
entanglement-based networks. Its design principle is that the
Bell-diagonal/Werner protocol class admits explicit real-parameter
representations --- no density matrices are needed for the envelope
theorems, which makes them maximally robust as deductive references for
simulators~\cite{Chung2025}; the general-state layer of
Secs.~\ref{sec:diamond}--\ref{sec:entropy} then removes the
protocol-class restriction where needed.

\subsection{The swap-chain fidelity envelope}

For a chain of $k$ entanglement swaps of Werner links~\cite{WernerState1989}
with per-link fidelity $F$, the Werner-parameter representation
$w = (4F-1)/3$ makes the swap multiplicative
(\lean{wernerSwapFidelity}, the
Briegel--D\"ur--Cirac--Zoller composition law~\cite{BDCZ1998}, with the
end-to-end closed form $F_{\mathrm{e2e}} = (1+3w^k)/4$,
\lean{endToEndFidelity}). The envelope theorem,
\[
\lean{swapChain\_fidelity\_envelope} :\;
\tfrac14 \le F \le 1 \;\Longrightarrow\;
\tfrac14 \le F_{\mathrm{e2e}}(F,k) \le 1,
\]
together with monotonicity in $F$ and antitonicity in $k$, is --- to our
knowledge --- the first formally verified protocol-level fidelity bound
for quantum networks. Three generalizations keep the envelope realistic:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Memory decay}: the depolarizing memory model
$F(t) = F e^{-2t/\tau} + (1-e^{-2t/\tau})/4$
(\lean{memoryDegradedFidelity}) multiplies the Werner parameter by
$e^{-2t/\tau}$, and the decay-inclusive envelope
(\lean{decayInclusive\_fidelity\_envelope}) bounds the $k$-swap chain of
memory-degraded links in $[\tfrac14, 1]$ for all $t \ge 0$, $\tau > 0$.

\item \emph{General Bell-diagonal swaps}: the Klein-four convolution map
with normalization and the bridge to the Werner case
(\lean{bellDiagSwapA\_werner}).

\item \emph{Repeater recursion}: the BDCZ nesting-doubling identity
(\lean{endToEndFidelity\_nest\_double}) and the
teleportation-usefulness threshold over the chain
($F_{\mathrm{e2e}} > \tfrac12 \iff w^k > \tfrac13$,
\lean{endToEnd\_teleportation\_useful}).
\end{itemize}

For \emph{arbitrary} states, the data-processing chain
\lean{traceDist\_applyChain\_le} bounds the trace distance to a target
reference as non-increasing under any chain of CPTP steps (swaps,
distillation, memory channels), and its fidelity-domain mirror
\lean{sqrtFidelity\_applyChain\_ge\_psd} gives the same chain in the
opposite monotone direction --- the network-level data-processing
inequalities with the uniform-Werner restriction removed.

\subsection{Distillation, teleportation, and key rates}

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{BBPSSW and DEJMPS}~\cite{BBPSSW1996,DEJMPS1996}: the
distillability flagship \lean{bbpsswRecurrence\_gt} (purification
strictly increases fidelity on $F\in(\tfrac12,1)$, the whole statement
reducing to one cubic inequality), the corrected-pairing DEJMPS map with
a monotone single-step increase on the phase-flip-only sub-basin
(\lean{dejmps\_increase\_phaseFlipOnly}), and --- the honesty witness ---
a verified \emph{non-monotonicity} counterexample
(\lean{dejmps\_single\_step\_can\_decrease}): $\lambda_{00} > \tfrac12$
does \emph{not} guarantee a single-step increase. The full asymptotic
convergence basin rests on Macchiavello's non-monotone
argument~\cite{Macchiavello1998} and is cited, not formalized.

\item \emph{Teleportation}~\cite{Horodecki1999,MassarPopescu1995}: the
average teleportation fidelity $(2F+1)/3$ is \emph{unconditional}
(\lean{teleportAvgFidelity\_horodecki\_unconditional}): its single
analytic input, the Haar--Pauli quadratic integral
$\int_{S^2}\langle\psi|\sigma_z|\psi\rangle^2\,d\mu = \tfrac13$, is
itself proven in the Bloch picture (\lean{haarPauliZSqAverage\_eq})
rather than assumed; the classical threshold
$f_{\mathrm{avg}} > \tfrac23 \iff F > \tfrac12$ holds with no hypothesis
(\lean{teleport\_beats\_classical\_iff\_unconditional}) and composes over
chains (\lean{teleport\_useful\_over\_chain}).

\item \emph{BB84}~\cite{BB84,ShorPreskill2000}: the asymptotic key rate
$r(e) = 1 - 2h_2(e)$ (\lean{bb84KeyRate}, with the bits-normalized
binary entropy), strict decrease (\lean{bb84KeyRate\_strictAntiOn}),
positivity as $h_2(e) < \tfrac12$
(\lean{bb84KeyRate\_pos\_iff\_binEntropy\_lt}), and a genuine crossover
$e^* \in (0,\tfrac12)$ with $r(e^*) = 0$ produced by the
intermediate-value theorem (\lean{bb84\_crossover\_exists}) --- the
conventional $\approx 11\%$ threshold is recovered as the implicit root
of $h_2(e) = \tfrac12$; no decimal is asserted in the formal layer. The
composition with the end-to-end QBER gives the fidelity threshold for
positive key over a chain
(\lean{bb84\_positiveKey\_fidelity\_threshold}).

\item \emph{Multipartite rates}~\cite{FortescueLo2007}: the
Fortescue--Lo random-pair yield $D/(D+1)$ surpasses the single-copy
specified-pair bound $\tfrac23$ for $D \ge 3$
(\lean{fortescueLoYield\_gt\_two\_thirds}), the $W_3$-vs-GHZ$_3$
randomization advantage is strictly positive
(\lean{w3\_beats\_ghz\_randomization\_advantage}), and the yield tends to
$1$ (\lean{fortescueLoYield\_tendsto\_one}); the asymptotic optimality
conjecture of Ref.~\cite{FortescueLo2007} is \emph{not} claimed.

\item \emph{Physical-layer anchors}: the dB$\leftrightarrow$Np fiber
attenuation identity (\lean{fiberTransmission\_eq\_exp\_neg\_attenuationNp});
the linear-optics Bell-measurement bound, success $\le \tfrac12$
(\lean{bsmSuccessProb\_le\_half\_of\_linearOptics})
~\cite{CalsamigliaLutkenhaus2001}; and the elementary-link rate
$\tau = L/(c\,p_{\mathrm{link}})$ (\lean{linkRate}) with the
expected-attempts factor \emph{derived} as a geometric-distribution mean
(\lean{geometric\_expected\_attempts})~\cite{Sangouard2011}.
Handshake-inclusive link timing is model-dependent and deliberately not
bounded --- this is exactly the layer on which simulators
disagree~\cite{Chung2025}.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Rate ceilings and network capacity}
\label{sec:network-capacity}

Two repeaterless ceilings ship in the two-layer posture of
Sec.~\ref{sec:intro}. The PLOB bound~\cite{PLOB2017} enters as the
rate-bound function $\mathrm{plob}(\eta) = -\log_2(1-\eta)$
(\lean{plobBound}) with its rigorous loss-penalty properties --- zero at
total loss, strictly increasing on transmissivity
(\lean{plobBound\_strictMonoOn}), bounded below by the linear penalty
$\eta/\ln 2$ (\lean{linear\_le\_plobBound}), and decaying to zero along
an exponentially lossy fiber
(\lean{plobBound\_tendsto\_zero\_of\_exp\_decay}); the channel-converse
semantics (that no two-way-assisted protocol exceeds it) is the cited
theorem of Ref.~\cite{PLOB2017}, resting on continuous-variable
machinery absent from current formal libraries. The erasure-channel
two-way capacity formula $1-p$~\cite{BennettDiVincenzoSmolin1997} enters
the same way (\lean{erasureBound}), with the cross-link
$1 - p \le \mathrm{plob}(1-p)$ (\lean{erasureBound\_le\_plobBound})
proven --- the two ceilings are mutually consistent, with the erasure
form tighter on its domain.

Multipath distribution requires flow theory. Mathlib at our pin has no
max-flow/min-cut development, so the corpus builds one
(\lean{NetworkCapacity}, \lean{NetworkCapacityStrongDuality}): weak
duality (\lean{flowValue\_le\_cutCap}), witnessed optimality (a flow
saturating a cut is maximal, \lean{maxFlow\_minCut\_of\_saturating}),
existence of a maximal flow by compactness (\lean{maxFlow\_exists}), the
augmenting-path theorem, and full strong duality
\[
\lean{maxFlow\_eq\_minCut} :\;
\max_{f}\,\mathrm{val}(f) \;=\; \min_{S}\,\mathrm{cap}(S)
\]
in the Ford--Fulkerson sense~\cite{FordFulkerson1956}, on real-capacity
finite networks. Worked instances connect the flow picture to repeater
chains: the two-edge path network has capacity exactly the bottleneck
(\lean{path3\_capacity\_eq\_bottleneck}), and instantiating the
capacities with PLOB ceilings of two fiber segments certifies the
repeaterless series bottleneck (\lean{path3\_repeaterless\_bottleneck}).

\section{The device-characterization envelope family}
\label{sec:device}

The fourth layer turns the channel calculus into device-facing envelopes:
closed-form ceilings and floors, parametrized by universally stated
device data ($T_1$, $T_2$, temperature, frequency, window length), that
an achievable quantity provably cannot cross \emph{within the stated
model}. Every theorem in this section carries the two-layer posture
explicitly: the formula and its monotonicity are kernel-checked; the
identification of the model channel with the physical device is a cited
hypothesis.

\subsection{Gate fidelity: identity, ceiling, and composition}

The bench-data bridge is the average-gate/entanglement-fidelity identity
$F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi) = (d\,F_e(\Phi)+1)/(d+1)$
(\lean{avgGateFidelity\_eq})~\cite{Horodecki1999,Nielsen2002}, proven
\emph{constructively} --- the degree-$(2,2)$ complex-sphere moment is
computed along a Gaussian-to-sphere route (one-dimensional Gaussian
moments, a Wick/Isserlis tensor, polar reduction), so the
unitary-2-design second moment is obtained as a theorem rather than
imported. On it sits the \emph{coherence-limited fidelity ceiling}: for
the standard $T_1/T_2$ model channel of a duration-$t$ gate (amplitude
damping with $\gamma = 1-e^{-t/T_1}$ composed after dephasing with
$p = (1-e^{-t/T_2})/2$),
\[
F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi_t) \;=\;
\tfrac12 + \tfrac16 e^{-t/T_1} + \tfrac13 e^{-t/(2T_1)-t/T_2}
\]
(\lean{avgGateFidelity\_coherenceChannel}, with
\lean{entanglementFidelity\_coherenceChannel}), monotone decreasing in
the gate duration (\lean{avgGateFidelity\_coherenceChannel\_antitone})
with exact endpoints $1$ at $t=0$ and floor $\tfrac12$
(\lean{avgGateFidelity\_coherenceChannel\_ge\_half},
\lean{avgGateFidelity\_coherenceChannel\_le\_one}). These are exact
statements about the model channel; reading the ceiling as a bound on a
physical gate is the separate modeling assumption that realized noise is
at least coherence-limited, stated as such. Composition with a control
error budget gives the composed-gate bound
(\lean{diamondDist\_coherence\_after\_control\_le}) with its fidelity
reading (\lean{sqrtFidelity\_coherence\_after\_control\_ge}) and
both binding regimes identified
(\lean{diamondDist\_coherence\_after\_control\_le\_coherence\_binds},
\lean{diamondDist\_coherence\_after\_control\_le\_control\_binds}).

SPAM characterization exposes the process fidelity
(\lean{processFidelity}) with the identity restated for it
(\lean{avgGateFidelity\_eq\_processFidelity}) and the readout bit-flip
diamond distance of Sec.~\ref{sec:diamond-named}. The
randomized-benchmarking-style certificate~\cite{Magesan2011} is the
two-sided bracket connecting an averaged benchmark number to worst-case
channel error: $F_{\mathrm{avg}}$ controls and is controlled by the
diamond distance to the identity
(\lean{avgGateFidelity\_diamondDist\_bound},
\lean{avgGateFidelity\_diamondDist\_two\_sided}, via the Fuchs--van de
Graaf bracket on the Choi states,
\lean{entanglementDistinguishability\_fvdg\_bracket}). The two-layer
note applies with force here: these theorems certify the
\emph{fidelity-to-diamond conversion}; the identification of an
experimental RB decay constant with $F_{\mathrm{avg}}$ of a fixed channel
(gate-independence of noise, Markovianity) is the cited RB model
hypothesis~\cite{Magesan2011}, not a theorem of this corpus.

\subsection{Fluctuation--dissipation noise floors}
\label{sec:device-fdt}

The classical Johnson--Nyquist floor~\cite{Nyquist1928} ships with
operating-point monotonicity in temperature and conductance and worked
detector/amplifier operating points
(\lean{johnsonNyquistPSD\_mono\_temp},
\lean{fdt\_noise\_floor\_bound}, \lean{fdt\_noise\_floor\_detector}),
plus the linear-response large-deviation rare-event tail and its
Gallavotti--Cohen-type symmetry (\lean{fdt\_rare\_event\_tail},
\lean{fdt\_gallavotti\_cohen}), consuming the large-deviation
foundations of the I3 bundle.

The quantum (Callen--Welton~\cite{CallenWelton1951}) floor is
\emph{derived, not posited}: instantiating the PhysLib canonical-ensemble
framework~\cite{Meiburg2025PhysLib} on the harmonic-oscillator spectrum
$E_n = \hbar\omega(n+\tfrac12)$ (\lean{qhoEnsemble}) and computing its
mean energy yields
\[
\langle E\rangle \;=\; \frac{\hbar\omega}{2}\,
\coth\!\Big(\frac{\beta\hbar\omega}{2}\Big)
\]
(\lean{qho\_meanEnergy}), bounded below by the zero-point energy
(\lean{qho\_meanEnergy\_ge\_zeroPoint}), with the resulting
zero-point-corrected noise floor (\lean{fdt\_quantum\_noise\_floor})
replacing the classical floor in the cryogenic regime
$\hbar\omega \gtrsim k_BT$ where the classical formula vanishes and
misses the dominant half-quantum. By contrast, the Caves
phase-insensitive-amplifier added-noise bound
$A \ge \hbar\omega/2$~\cite{Caves1982} remains a \emph{cited hypothesis}
(\lean{fdt\_noise\_floor\_amplifier} takes it as the explicit assumption
\lean{hcaves}): its proof requires the bosonic ladder algebra
$[a,a^{\dagger}]=1$, which no current formal library provides
(Sec.~\ref{sec:walls}).

\subsection{Readout-window envelopes}
\label{sec:device-readout}

The family is completed by measurement-side envelopes from the same
universally stated parameters. The \emph{relaxation envelope}: for a
readout window $t$ against relaxation time $T_1$, the excited-state decay
probability $p_{\mathrm{decay}}(t,T_1) = 1 - e^{-t/T_1}$
(\lean{readoutDecayProb}) carries range
($0 \le p < 1$, \lean{readoutDecayProb\_nonneg},
\lean{readoutDecayProb\_lt\_one}), strict monotonicity in the window
(\lean{readoutDecayProb\_strictMono\_time}), strict antitonicity in
$T_1$ (\lean{readoutDecayProb\_strictAnti\_relax}), endpoints
(\lean{readoutDecayProb\_zero\_time},
\lean{readoutDecayProb\_tendsto\_one}), and the rational enclosure
\[
\frac{t/T_1}{1+t/T_1} \;\le\; p_{\mathrm{decay}} \;\le\; \frac{t}{T_1}
\]
(\lean{readoutDecayProb\_enclosure}) --- the short-window rule
$p \lesssim t/T_1$ proven as an exact inequality on the full domain.
Under the literature-cited readout model (QND-ideal, no re-excitation,
decay flips the outcome~\cite{Gambetta2007,Walter2017,Geerlings2013}),
the uniform-prior averaged assignment error is floored at
$p_{\mathrm{decay}}/2$ (\lean{avgAssignmentError\_relaxation\_floor};
the model prefactor is exactly $\tfrac12$), with the rational
operating-point form
$\tfrac{t/T_1}{2(1+t/T_1)} \le \varepsilon_{\mathrm{avg}}$
(\lean{avgAssignmentError\_rational\_floor}).

The \emph{thermal envelope}: the excited-state thermal occupancy
$p_{\mathrm{th}} = 1/(1+e^{x})$, $x = \beta\hbar\omega$
(\lean{thermalExcitedPop}), is \emph{derived} from the PhysLib two-state
canonical ensemble --- the library's $\tanh$-form closed-form
probability is converted by the bridge identity
$\tfrac12(1-\tanh u) = 1/(1+e^{2u})$ (\lean{half\_one\_sub\_tanh}) into
the Boltzmann-occupancy theorem \lean{twoState\_excited\_probability},
so the occupancy is a theorem of statistical mechanics here, not a
definition. It carries strict antitonicity in $x$
(\lean{thermalExcitedPop\_strictAnti}), strict monotonicity in
temperature and strict antitonicity in frequency through a
temperature-$\beta$ bridge
(\lean{thermalExcitedPop\_strictMono\_temperature},
\lean{thermalExcitedPop\_strictAnti\_frequency}), endpoints ($\tfrac12$
at $x=0$, $\to 0$ as $x\to\infty$), and two-sided rational enclosures
(\lean{thermalExcitedPop\_rational\_upper},
\lean{thermalExcitedPop\_rational\_lower}). Under the cited
thermal-population readout model~\cite{Jin2015,Geerlings2013}, the
assignment error is floored at $p_{\mathrm{th}}/2$
(\lean{avgAssignmentError\_thermal\_floor}) with its rational
operating-point companion
(\lean{avgAssignmentError\_thermal\_rational\_floor}). The family
capstone combines both mechanisms:
\[
\frac{\max\big(p_{\mathrm{decay}}(t,T_1),\, p_{\mathrm{th}}(x)\big)}{2}
\;\le\; \varepsilon_{\mathrm{avg}}
\]
(\lean{avgAssignmentError\_combined\_floor}) --- a readout subject to
both relaxation during the window and thermal population is floored by
the worse of the two, from $(t, T_1, T, \omega)$ alone.

\subsection{Interface to the logical layer}
\label{sec:device-qec}

The abstract code-distance suppression relation
$A\,(p/p_{\mathrm{th}})^{\lfloor(d+1)/2\rfloor}$ carries its two
threshold properties --- antitone in distance below threshold
(\lean{logicalErrorBound\_antitone\_distance}) and vanishing in the
distance limit strictly below threshold
(\lean{logicalErrorBound\_tendsto\_zero}) --- as neutral QEC
mathematics: the interface across which the physical-layer envelopes of
this section feed the logical layer owned by the companion
fault-tolerance bundle (D6).

\section{The rational-enclosure technique, library contributions, and
documented walls}
\label{sec:enclosure}

\subsection{Kernel-only rational enclosures}

Every envelope above is \emph{evaluable}: the cross-cutting method is the
Bernoulli bracket
\[
\lean{expNeg\_enclosure} :\; 0 \le r \;\Longrightarrow\;
1 - r \,\le\, e^{-r} \,\le\, \frac{1}{1+r},
\]
whose endpoints are rational at rational $r$, so every decay factor
$e^{-t/\tau}$ (fiber loss, memory decoherence, readout decay, thermal
occupancy via \lean{thermalExcitedPop\_expNeg\_form}) admits a
machine-checked rational bracket with no floating-point transcendentals
and no external interval-arithmetic oracle. When more precision is
needed, the degree-$n$ Taylor squeeze on Mathlib's exponential remainder
bound tightens the bracket at kernel-checked cost: the representative
fiber-loss operating point $e^{-0.46}$ (0.2\,dB/km at 10\,km, through
the dB$\leftrightarrow$Np identity) is bracketed loosely by the
Bernoulli form (\lean{expNeg046\_enclosure}) and to three decimals,
$0.631 \le e^{-0.46} \le 0.632$, by the degree-5 squeeze
(\lean{expNeg046\_tight}). The consumers inside the corpus ---
\lean{readoutDecayProb\_enclosure},
\lean{avgAssignmentError\_rational\_floor},
\lean{avgAssignmentError\_thermal\_rational\_floor} --- demonstrate the
intended pattern: a certification claim at a device operating point
reduces to rational arithmetic inside the kernel. We propose this
pattern as the right interface between formal envelopes and
device-parameter tables: no step of the evaluation leaves the proof
assistant, and no step trusts a float.

\subsection{Library-contribution portfolio}

Several components are general-purpose and absent from the underlying
libraries at our pin, and are candidates for upstream contribution: the
max-flow/min-cut development of Sec.~\ref{sec:network-capacity}
(Mathlib has no network-flow theory); the Kronecker-with-identity
operator-norm bound
$\lVert A\otimes\mathbf 1\rVert \le \lVert A\rVert$
(\lean{l2\_opNorm\_kronecker\_one\_le}) and the trace-norm
multiplicativity \lean{traceNorm\_kronecker}; the complex-sphere moment
toolchain beneath \lean{avgGateFidelity\_eq} (Gaussian Wick tensors,
polar reduction, realification); the unconditional Mirsky inequality and
the Weyl/minimax eigenvalue toolbox; and the Haar--Pauli spherical
integral. Conversely, the corpus deliberately \emph{adopts} the
community PhysLib library~\cite{Meiburg2025PhysLib} where it is
authoritative (sandwiched-R\'enyi data processing, strong
subadditivity, canonical ensembles), through the faithful bridge of
Sec.~\ref{sec:entropy} --- the derived Callen--Welton floor and the
derived thermal occupancy are worked demonstrations that bridging beats
re-deriving.

\subsection{Documented walls}
\label{sec:walls}

We record plainly what the verified layer does \emph{not} contain, since
the two-layer posture is only honest if its boundary is explicit:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{The Caves added-noise bound}~\cite{Caves1982}. The
phase-insensitive-amplifier half-quantum bound $A \ge \hbar\omega/2$
requires the bosonic commutation relation $[a,a^{\dagger}]=1$ and its
ladder-operator calculus, absent from PhysLib and Mathlib at our pin.
It is carried as the explicit hypothesis \lean{hcaves} of
\lean{fdt\_noise\_floor\_amplifier} --- never as an axiom --- and is the
canonical example of an identification-layer input.

\item \emph{Continuous-variable channel capacity}. The PLOB and erasure
ceilings (Sec.~\ref{sec:network-capacity}) are formula-layer objects;
their converse semantics (relative entropy of entanglement of Gaussian
channels, teleportation stretching) is cited to
Refs.~\cite{PLOB2017,BennettDiVincenzoSmolin1997}.

\item \emph{DEJMPS asymptotics}. The full convergence basin is
Macchiavello's non-monotone argument~\cite{Macchiavello1998}; the corpus
proves the single-step results and a counterexample delimiting them, and
cites the rest.

\item \emph{Non-covariant exact diamond distances}. Amplitude damping
and its thermal generalization carry two-sided brackets, not exact
values; the exact optimum requires an eigenvector witness the corpus
documents but does not claim.

\item \emph{Asymptotic multipartite optimality}. The Fortescue--Lo
$D/(D+1)\to 1$ limit is proven; the optimality of $1$ is the cited open
conjecture~\cite{FortescueLo2007} and is not claimed.
\end{itemize}

\section{Scope and discussion}
\label{sec:scope}

Almost every theorem in this corpus formalizes known mathematics, and we
state that plainly: the contribution is not new quantum information
theory but a \emph{certification substrate} --- the known results,
machine-checked at full strength, organized so that a device or network
claim traces through named theorems from stated parameters to a
certified interval, with every modeling step surfaced as an explicit
hypothesis. The named exceptions where the formal development itself
contributes are circumscribed: the in-project max-flow/min-cut
development (a library gap, not new mathematics), the unconditional
machine-checked form of the sharp Audenaert bound, the swap-chain
envelope as (to our knowledge) the first formally verified
protocol-level network fidelity bound, and the constructive
sphere-moment derivation of the average-gate-fidelity identity. The
methodological claims --- the two-layer posture and the kernel-only
rational-enclosure pattern --- are, we believe, the transferable
content: they make the difference between a formalization that
\emph{mentions} devices and one that can be cited line-by-line in an
error budget without overclaiming.

What this paper does not contain: gate-synthesis and compilation theory
(the D8 bundle, which consumes Sec.~\ref{sec:diamond} for its
channel-level certificate); the logical layer of fault tolerance (D6);
runtime tooling, vendor-specific parameter fits, or experimental data
analysis. The corpus is a mathematical substrate; its numerical
companion pipeline (figure generation and a cross-validation gate
against the Python layer) is part of the public repository's automated
validation suite.

The formal verification layer of this work was developed with
