\title{Center-symmetry confinement and Polyakov-loop order parameter
in Lean~4: a formal Svetitsky--Yaffe spine with a Walker--Wang
transport bracket}

\author{John G. Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher; \texttt{NetRxn137@gmail.com}}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We formalize confinement as $\mathbb{Z}_N$ 1-form center-symmetry
unbreaking in Lean~4, treating the Polyakov loop~\cite{Polyakov1978}
$\Polyakov$ as the
complex order parameter. The $\mathbb{Z}_N$ center acts on $\Polyakov$
by multiplication by $\zeta_N = \exp(2\pi i / N)$; we prove this action
is a primitive $N$-th root of unity, fixes only $\Polyakov = 0$
(confining) when $\zeta_N \neq 1$, and reduces $\zeta_2 = -1$ exactly
on the SU(2) center. The Svetitsky--Yaffe universality
correspondence~\cite{SvetitskyYaffe1982} between $D$-dimensional
deconfinement and $(D{-}1)$-dimensional $\mathbb{Z}_N$ spin models is
encoded as a structural map: $\mathrm{SU}(2) \to$ 3D Ising universality
class ($\nuIsing = 0.6299$~\cite{PelissettoVicari2002,
KosPolandSimmonsDuffin2016}), $\mathrm{SU}(3) \to$ 3D 3-state Potts
($\nuPotts = 0.5$). The Kovtun--Son--Starinets viscosity bound
$\KSS \ge 1/(4\pi)$~\cite{KovtunSonStarinets2005} is pinned to the
literature-anchored bracket $[0.07, 0.08]$ via Mathlib $\pi$-bound
lemmas. A Walker--Wang anyon-mediated transport prediction is encoded
as a structural assumption $H_{\mathrm{WW}} : \KSS \in [\mathrm{KSS},
2\,\mathrm{KSS}]$~\cite{HofmanIqbal2018} with two concrete numerical
falsifiers ($\KSS = 0$ and $\KSS = 1$). The module ships \centerSymmThms{}
substantive Lean theorems with zero \texttt{sorry} statements and zero
new axioms beyond the kernel's three.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The standard QCD-confinement framing as a Wilson-loop area law admits
an alternative, structurally cleaner formulation: confinement as
unbreaking of a $\mathbb{Z}_N$ 1-form center symmetry, with the
Polyakov loop as the local order parameter and the deconfinement
transition matched to a $(D{-}1)$-dimensional $\mathbb{Z}_N$ spin
model via Svetitsky--Yaffe universality~\cite{SvetitskyYaffe1982}.
This formulation is natural in the modern higher-form-symmetry
language~\cite{KitaevAnyons2003}, makes precise contact with critical
phenomena via the order-parameter framework, and gives the universal
Kovtun--Son--Starinets viscosity lower
bound~\cite{KovtunSonStarinets2005} a clean physical interpretation
in terms of anyon-mediated transport near the bound.

This paper presents a Lean~4 formalization of the structural backbone:
the $\mathbb{Z}_N$ center as an algebraic structure on $\mathbb{C}$,
the Polyakov loop as the complex order parameter and confining
predicate, the Svetitsky--Yaffe universality map, the KSS bound with
explicit literature-anchored numerical bracket, and a Walker--Wang
transport prediction~\cite{HofmanIqbal2018} as a tracked structural
assumption with two falsifiers.

\section{Center symmetry, Polyakov loop, and confinement}
\label{sec:center}

\subsection{The cyclic center $\mathbb{Z}_N$}

The cyclic center $\mathbb{Z}_N$ for $\mathrm{SU}(N)$ is encoded as a
Lean structure with a load-bearing constraint $N \ge 2$. The
fundamental phase $\zeta_N = \exp(2\pi i / N)$ generates
$\mathbb{Z}_N \subset \mathbb{C}^{\ast}$. Two algebraic properties
ship as theorems:
\begin{align}
\zeta_N^N &= 1, \label{eq:zeta-pow} \\
|\zeta_N| &= 1. \label{eq:zeta-norm}
\end{align}
Equation~\eqref{eq:zeta-pow} (theorem
\texttt{centerPhase\_pow\_N}) follows from
\texttt{Complex.exp\_nat\_mul} together with
\texttt{Complex.exp\_eq\_one\_iff}; equation~\eqref{eq:zeta-norm}
(theorem \texttt{centerPhase\_norm\_one}) follows from
\texttt{Complex.norm\_exp} and the vanishing real part of
$2\pi i / N$. A specialization to $N = 2$ ships as
\texttt{centerPhase\_Z2\_eq\_neg\_one}: $\zeta_2 = -1$ via
\texttt{Complex.exp\_pi\_mul\_I}, the SU(2) center as the
multiplicative group $\{+1, -1\}$ acting on the Polyakov loop.

\subsection{Polyakov loop and the confining predicate}

The Polyakov loop is encoded as a complex-valued type
\texttt{PolyakovLoop} with confining predicate
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Confining}\,\Polyakov \iff \Polyakov = 0
\label{eq:confining}
\end{equation}
and center action
$\mathrm{centerAction}\,Z\,\Polyakov := \zeta_N \cdot \Polyakov$. Two
substantive theorems link the order parameter to the symmetry-breaking
structure:

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{confining\_invariant\_under\_center\_action}:
$\Polyakov = 0$ implies $\zeta_N \cdot \Polyakov = \Polyakov$
(confining configurations are center-invariant).
\item \texttt{nonzero\_breaks\_center\_invariance}:
$\Polyakov \neq 0$ together with $\zeta_N \neq 1$ implies
$\zeta_N \cdot \Polyakov \neq \Polyakov$ (non-zero Polyakov loops are
not center-fixed).
\end{itemize}

These together formalize the order-parameter / symmetry-breaking
correspondence: $\Polyakov = 0$ is the unique fixed point of the
non-trivial $\mathbb{Z}_N$ center action on $\mathbb{C}$.

\subsection{Modulus as a real-valued order parameter}

The conventional lattice order parameter is the modulus $|\Polyakov|$.
We prove
\texttt{confining\_iff\_magnitude\_zero},
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Confining}\,\Polyakov \iff |\Polyakov| = 0,
\end{equation}
together with the strict-positivity statement
\texttt{deconfining\_implies\_magnitude\_positive}:
$\neg\,\mathrm{Confining}\,\Polyakov$ implies $0 < |\Polyakov|$.

\section{Svetitsky--Yaffe universality}
\label{sec:svetitsky-yaffe}

The Svetitsky--Yaffe map is encoded as a function
$\texttt{svetitskyYaffeClass}: \texttt{CenterZN} \to
\texttt{UniversalityClass}$, with concrete cases:
$\mathrm{SU}(2) \to$ 3D Ising and $\mathrm{SU}(3) \to$ 3D 3-state
Potts. The 3D correlation-length critical exponents are anchored to
the conformal-bootstrap and renormalization-group literature:
\begin{equation}
\nuIsing = 0.6299, \qquad \nuPotts = 0.5,
\label{eq:nu}
\end{equation}
where the literal $\nuIsing = 0.6299$ matches the Kos--Poland--
Simmons-Duffin--Vichi 4-decimal conformal-bootstrap
value~\cite{KosPolandSimmonsDuffin2016}; the earlier
Pelissetto--Vicari renormalization-group calculation gives
$\nuIsing = 0.6301(4)$~\cite{PelissettoVicari2002}, consistent at
that precision. $\nuPotts \approx 1/2$ is a
mean-field placeholder: the 3D 3-state Potts transition is
weakly first order, so $\nuPotts$ is not a measured exponent and the
literal value is illustrative only. What is load-bearing is the
\emph{ordering} $\nuPotts < \nuIsing$, which separates the two
universality classes regardless of the precise Potts value.

The substantive separation between Z$_2$ and Z$_3$ deconfinement is
formalized as the direct comparison
\begin{equation}
\nuPotts < \nuIsing,
\label{eq:ising-gt-potts}
\end{equation}
shipping as theorem \texttt{ising\_nu\_gt\_potts\_nu}. We use the
direct ordering rather than threshold-pair theorems against an
arbitrary value (e.g.\ $0.6$): the load-bearing physics content is
the comparison itself, not the threshold.

Figure~\ref{fig:polyakov} (left) visualizes the schematic $|\Polyakov|$
versus $T/\Tc$ behavior across the two universality classes via
$|\Polyakov| \propto (T/\Tc - 1)^\beta$ scaling
($\beta_{\mathrm{Ising}} = 0.326$,
$\beta_{\mathrm{Potts}} = 0.111$).

\section{KSS viscosity bound and Walker--Wang transport}
\label{sec:walker-wang}

The Kovtun--Son--Starinets bound on shear viscosity to entropy
density is~\cite{KovtunSonStarinets2005}
\begin{equation}
\KSS \;\ge\; \mathrm{KSS} := \frac{1}{4\pi}.
\label{eq:kss}
\end{equation}
We prove a triple of statements pinning $\mathrm{KSS}$ to a tight
literature-anchored bracket:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \texttt{KSS\_bound\_positive}: $0 < \mathrm{KSS}$.
\item \texttt{KSS\_bound\_below\_0\_08}: $\mathrm{KSS} < 0.08$.
\item \texttt{KSS\_bound\_above\_0\_07}: $0.07 < \mathrm{KSS}$.
\end{enumerate}
Statements~(2) and~(3) use Mathlib $\pi$-bound lemmas
\texttt{Real.pi\_gt\_d4} and \texttt{Real.pi\_lt\_d4}, giving
$0.07 < 1/(4\pi) < 0.08$ as a true numerical bracket; the bracket
width is $\sim 12\%$ of the value, well outside the trivial
within-own-tolerance regime.

The Walker--Wang anyon-mediated transport prediction is encoded as a
\emph{project-originated} structural assumption (tracked Prop, not a
derived theorem) with two independent constraints:
\begin{equation}
H_{\mathrm{WW}}(\KSS) :=
(\mathrm{KSS} \le \KSS) \,\wedge\, (\KSS \le 2\,\mathrm{KSS}).
\label{eq:H-ww}
\end{equation}
The two conjuncts are independent (each can fail). The lower bound is
the universal KSS constraint; the upper bound is a \emph{modelling
threshold} of this paper, motivated by viewing the deconfined-side
transport as carried by Walker--Wang anyon-line
excitations~\cite{WalkerWang2012} of a (3+1)-dimensional topological
phase, with higher-form-symmetry hydrodynamics
machinery~\cite{HofmanIqbal2018} as the natural EFT scaffold. We do
\emph{not} derive the factor-of-two upper bound from
ref.~\cite{WalkerWang2012} or~\cite{HofmanIqbal2018}; the bound is a
falsifiable hypothesis whose truth is to be tested by HPC validation
in Phase~6B.

A boundary witness theorem
\texttt{walker\_wang\_witness\_at\_kss\_lower} confirms that the
predicate is non-vacuous: $\eta/s = \KSS$ saturates the lower
bound. Two concrete numerical falsifiers complete the bracket:
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{walker\_wang\_zero\_eta\_violator}: $\KSS = 0$ violates
the lower bound of equation~\eqref{eq:H-ww}, via the load-bearing
\texttt{KSS\_bound\_positive}.
\item \texttt{walker\_wang\_unit\_eta\_violator}: $\KSS = 1$ violates
the upper bound, via the load-bearing
\texttt{KSS\_bound\_below\_0\_08} ($2\,\mathrm{KSS} < 0.16 < 1$).
\end{itemize}

\section{Cross-bridges}
\label{sec:bridges}

\subsection{Higher-form symmetry biconditional}

The most substantive cross-bridge to the project's existing
gauge-erasure infrastructure is the biconditional
\texttt{higher\_form\_discrete\_iff\_non\_abelian}: among continuous
gauge groups, the 1-form symmetry is exactly discrete if and only if
the gauge group is non-abelian. This identifies center-symmetry
confinement as the gauge-erasure dichotomy specialized to non-abelian
groups.

\subsection{Modular-tensor-category center bridge}

The cross-bridge \texttt{su3k1\_fusion\_card\_matches\_z3\_order}
connects the formalization to the SU(3)$_1$ fusion category in a
companion module: the three simple objects \{vac, fund, conj\} form
the $\mathbb{Z}_3$ group ring under fusion, matching the center order
$|\mathbb{Z}_3| = 3$. This realizes the categorified center symmetry.

\section{Outlook}
\label{sec:outlook}

The center-symmetry framing of confinement aligns naturally with the
project's gauge-erasure infrastructure (continuous non-abelian gauge
group $\to$ discrete 1-form symmetry $\to$ Polyakov-loop order
parameter), specializes to a precise dichotomy on the Lean side, and
provides a clean bracket for the Walker--Wang transport prediction
near the KSS bound. Numerical validation of $\KSS$ for non-abelian
anyon-mediated transport in concrete substrates is the natural
follow-on; the analytical bracket reported here is a structural
input.
