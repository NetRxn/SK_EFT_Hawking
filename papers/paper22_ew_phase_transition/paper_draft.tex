The order of the electroweak phase transition (first-order vs.\ crossover)
is a microscopic prediction of the scalar-rung condensate hierarchy on
the WetterichNJL substrate. Building on the Wave 1b BHL gauge-embedding
formalization (\texttt{BHLGaugeEmbedding.lean}, $23$ theorems) — itself

onto the Clifford-16 Fierz basis~\cite{BardeenHillLindner,Hill2025Redux}
— we ship \texttt{EWPhaseTransition.lean}, $21$ theorems formalizing
the leading-order high-T effective potential, the first-order /
crossover predicate, the latent-heat order parameter, and the EW
baryogenesis viability marker. The transition order is determined
microscopically by the cubic coefficient $E$: first-order if $E > 0$
(barrier survives), crossover if $E = 0$.

\textit{At the strict-leading-order high-T expansion}, the SM benchmark
($m_H = 125.20$ GeV, $\lambda_{\rm SM} \approx 0.13$, $E_{\rm SM} \approx
0.01$) yields $E > 0$ and is therefore predicted first-order; this is
the load-bearing Lean theorem \texttt{sm\_benchmark\_is\_first\_order}.
The physical SM, however, is a \textit{crossover}: lattice analyses
demonstrate that thermal-loop corrections to $E$ at the physical
$m_H = 125.20$ GeV~\cite{PDG2024} push the effective cubic coefficient
below the threshold needed for a first-order transition. The

GeV, refined by the lattice study of Csikor, Fodor, and
Heitger~\cite{CsikorFodorHeitger1999} (extending the earlier $70$--$95$
GeV range from~\cite{KLRS1996}); the physical $m_H$ is well above
this value. The strict-LO Lean prediction (first-order) and the
physical lattice verdict (crossover) thus diverge at a known and
literature-documented boundary. The Lean theorem
\texttt{crossover\_excludes\_baryogenesis} formalizes the consequence:
a crossover transition is not viable for sphaleron-decoupling
baryogenesis, forcing baryogenesis to leptogenesis or BSM mechanisms ---
the load-bearing input to the future Phase 6c.2 EW-baryogenesis bridge
theorem. Two tracked hypotheses
(\texttt{H\_VacuumStableUnderRG}, \texttt{H\_HierarchyEWLambdaUV})
encode the SM meta-stability and EW-Λ_UV hierarchy as falsifiable
parametric conditions; both have explicit non-trivial witnesses and
falsifiers. The full formalization is zero-sorry, introduces zero new

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The order of the electroweak phase transition is a quantitative
prediction of the SM Higgs sector at finite temperature. The
Kajantie-Laine-Rummukainen-Shaposhnikov 1996 lattice
analysis~\cite{KLRS1996} established that, \emph{after thermal-loop
resummation}, the SM is a crossover for $m_H \gtrsim 70$--$95$ GeV,
with the endpoint refined by Csikor, Fodor, and
Heitger~\cite{CsikorFodorHeitger1999} to $m_H = 72.4 \pm 1.7$
GeV; with the PDG observed $m_H = 125.20$
GeV~\cite{PDG2024}, the resummed SM transition is a crossover, and EW
baryogenesis in the strict-SM (without BSM extensions of the
electroweak Higgs sector) is excluded. The strict-LO Lean prediction
in this paper instead encodes the un-resummed cubic coefficient and
is reframed below as a structural reference point against which the
resummed lattice result improves.

The Phase 5z Wave 3 program formalizes this prediction at the level
of the WetterichNJL scalar-rung embedding (Wave 1, with BHL gauge
extension Wave 1b). The leading-order high-T expansion of the Higgs
effective potential takes the form
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:vt-expansion}
V_T(\HiggsField, T) = \tfrac{1}{2} (c_T T^2 - \mu^2) \HiggsField^2
                       - \tfrac{1}{3} \CubicCoeff T \HiggsField^3
                       + \tfrac{1}{4} \lambda \HiggsField^4,
\end{equation}
where $c_T$ is the thermal-mass coefficient ($\sim (g^2 + g'^2 + 4 y_t^2)/16$
in the SM), $\CubicCoeff$ is the cubic coefficient from
hard-thermal-loop W/Z resummation ($\sim g^3$), and the cubic term is
what distinguishes a first-order transition (with a barrier between
false and true vacua) from a crossover (smooth interpolation).

This paper formalizes \eqref{eq:vt-expansion} as
\texttt{EWPhaseTransition.finiteTPotential}, the cubic-coefficient
order parameter as \texttt{IsFirstOrderEW} / \texttt{IsCrossoverEW},
the latent-heat consequence as \texttt{latentHeat\_nonneg} +
\texttt{crossover\_has\_zero\_latent\_heat} +
\texttt{first\_order\_has\_positive\_latent\_heat}, and the
baryogenesis viability marker as
\texttt{IsBaryogenesisViable} +
\texttt{crossover\_excludes\_baryogenesis} --- in a single Lean module
of $\substantivetheorems{}$-substantive-theorem project context.

\section{Finite-T Effective Potential and Order Parameter}
\label{sec:finite-T-potential}

\subsection{Structure invariants}

The Lean structure \texttt{EWFiniteTParams} carries the four LO-expansion
parameters $(\mu^2, \lambda, c_T, \CubicCoeff)$ together with positivity
or non-negativity invariants on each:
$\mu^2 > 0$, $\lambda > 0$, $c_T > 0$, $\CubicCoeff \geq 0$. The
non-negativity (rather than strict positivity) of $\CubicCoeff$ is
load-bearing for the order classifier: it allows the partition into
\textit{first-order} ($\CubicCoeff > 0$) and \textit{crossover}
($\CubicCoeff = 0$) without permitting the unphysical $\CubicCoeff < 0$
branch (which the high-T expansion does not produce at the SM gauge
content). All downstream substantive theorems (latent-heat positivity,
crossover ↔ zero latent heat) consume this invariant explicitly.

\subsection{High-T expansion}

The thermal mass-squared $\mu^2(T) = c_T T^2 - \mu^2$ defines the
critical temperature
\begin{equation}
T_c = \sqrt{\mu^2 / c_T}
\end{equation}
where $\mu^2(T_c) = 0$. The Lean theorem
\texttt{thermalMassSq\_at\_T\_c} certifies this exactly; below $T_c$,
$\mu^2(T) < 0$ is the broken phase
(\texttt{thermalMassSq\_neg\_below\_T\_c}). At the SM benchmark
($\mu^2 = (88\,{\rm GeV})^2$, $c_T = 0.4$), $T_c \approx 139.13$ GeV,
consistent with literature.

\subsection{Transition-order classifier}

We formalize the LO classifier as the cubic-coefficient sign:
\begin{align}
\texttt{IsFirstOrderEW}(p) &\iff p.\CubicCoeff > 0,\\
\texttt{IsCrossoverEW}(p) &\iff p.\CubicCoeff = 0.
\end{align}
The Lean disjointness theorem
\texttt{first\_order\_and\_crossover\_disjoint} certifies the partition.
The definitional reduction
\texttt{transition\_order\_unfolds\_to\_cubic\_sign} unfolds the order
predicates back to the cubic-coefficient inequality (a purely
syntactic step). The substantive correctness-push content is carried
by two non-trivial biconditionals connecting the microscopic order
parameter to the macroscopic latent-heat observable:
\texttt{first\_order\_iff\_positive\_latent\_heat} (proved via
positivity + non-negativity \texttt{linarith}/\texttt{positivity}
reasoning) and \texttt{latentHeat\_zero\_iff\_crossover}, both of which
require the structural non-negativity invariant
\texttt{cubic\_coeff\_nonneg} of \texttt{EWFiniteTParams}.

\subsection{Latent-heat consequence}

The leading-order latent heat is
\begin{equation}
L = \frac{\CubicCoeff^2 T_c^2}{2 \lambda},
\end{equation}
formalized as \texttt{latentHeat}, which is non-negative
(\texttt{latentHeat\_nonneg}); zero exactly for crossover
(\texttt{crossover\_has\_zero\_latent\_heat}); strictly positive for
first-order with positive $T_c$
(\texttt{first\_order\_has\_positive\_latent\_heat}). The latent heat
is therefore an *order parameter* for the phase transition order: its
vanishing flags the crossover branch.

\label{sec:baryogenesis}

\textit{strong-enough} first-order transition that
sphaleron processes are decoupled in the broken phase as bubble walls
pass; the canonical threshold is $v(T_c) / T_c > 1$, equivalently
$\CubicCoeff / (\lambda T_c) > 1$ at LO. The predicate
\texttt{IsBaryogenesisViable} formalizes this.

The load-bearing exclusion theorem
\texttt{crossover\_excludes\_baryogenesis} certifies that any
crossover transition fails the baryogenesis viability check, regardless
of the threshold value. In Lean:
\begin{verbatim}

  (p : EWFiniteTParams) (threshold : ℝ)
  (h_co : IsCrossoverEW p) :

The proof discharges by contradiction: a baryogenesis-viable transition
implies first-order; first-order and crossover are disjoint; therefore
no crossover transition can be baryogenesis-viable.
