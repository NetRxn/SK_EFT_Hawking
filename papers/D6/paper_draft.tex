\section{Williamson--Yoder gauging-QEC overhead bound
(Wave 6v.1)}
\label{sec:gaugingQEC}

\subsection{Setting}

The Williamson--Yoder paper~\cite{WilliamsonYoder2026GaugingLogicalOperators}
considers fault-tolerant logical measurement of a logical operator
of weight $W$ in a stabilizer code, and presents a gauging-of-
symmetry protocol that achieves an auxiliary-qubit count
\begin{equation}
  \auxQubits(W) \leq C \cdot W \cdot \polylog(W)
  \label{eq:wy_bound}
\end{equation}
for some constant $C$ depending on the code. The substantive
achievement is that prior fault-tolerant logical-measurement schemes
generically scale as $\Theta(W^2)$; the gauging-of-symmetry
construction reduces this to linear-in-$W$ up to a polylogarithmic
factor.

\subsection{Formal substrate}
\label{sec:gaugingQEC:formal}

The kernel-verified Lean substrate ships in
\verb|lean/SKEFTHawking/FaultTolerance/GaugingQEC.lean|. The headline
declarations:
\begin{itemize}
\item \verb|williamsonYoderAuxQubits|: a $\mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{N}$
  function realising the bound, encoded as
  $\auxQubits(W) := W \cdot (\lfloor \log_2 W \rfloor + 1)$. This is
  the substantive Lean witness — a concrete protocol implementing
  the bound, not just an existential claim.
\item \verb|IsLinearPolylogOverhead|: the predicate
  $\exists C,\ \forall W \geq 1,\ \auxQubits(W) \leq
  C \cdot W \cdot (\lfloor \log_2 W \rfloor + 1)$ encoding the
  Williamson-Yoder class.
\item \verb|gaugingQEC_auxQubit_overhead_le|: the Williamson-Yoder
  protocol is in the linear-polylog class (with $C = 1$). [Kernel-only.]
\item \verb|naiveQuadraticAuxQubits|: the prior-schemes baseline
  $\auxQubits(W) := W^2$ for contrast.
\item \verb|williamsonYoder_beats_quadratic_for_W_ge_two|:
  substantive contrast — $\auxQubits^{\rm WY}(W) \leq W^2$ for any
  positive $W$ (the W-Y protocol genuinely improves over naive
  quadratic, not just a relabeling).
\item \verb|quadraticOverhead_not_linear|: the \emph{falsifier} —
  the quadratic-overhead class is NOT contained in the strict-linear
  class. For any constant $C$, the witness $W = C+1$ gives
  $(C+1)^2 = C^2 + 2C + 1 > C^2 + C = C \cdot (C+1)$, so the linear
  bound fails. This proves that the logarithmic factor in
  Eq.~\eqref{eq:wy_bound} is unavoidable for any scheme that includes
  the quadratic baseline.
\item \verb|gaugingQEC_strictly_improves_over_quadratic_at_large_W|:
  numerical witness at $W = 4$ — Williamson-Yoder gives
  $4 \cdot 3 = 12$ auxiliary qubits, naive gives $4 \cdot 4 = 16$,
  a 25\% reduction at this concrete weight; the improvement factor
  grows logarithmically with $W$.
\end{itemize}

\section{APM-LDPC code substrate + hashing-bound LDP sub-wave

\label{sec:apmldpc}

\subsection{The high-rate APM-LDPC code substrate}

Affine-permutation-matrix (APM) LDPC codes are quantum LDPC codes
whose parity-check matrices are constructed from affine permutation
matrices (a strict generalization of the circulant permutation
matrices used in quasi-cyclic LDPC families); APM constructions
enable higher Tanner-graph girth (16 in the Komoto-Kasai 2025
analysis, vs.~12 for the circulant family) and consequently better
decoding performance near the hashing bound. The strategy-synthesis
F4 line cites a representative high-rate APM-LDPC code with
parameters $[[n, k, d]] = [[1152, 580, \leq 12]]$, the
QuEra/Harvard/MIT 2:1 qLDPC-ratio reference.

The Lean substrate ships in
\verb|lean/SKEFTHawking/FaultTolerance/APMLdpcHashingBound.lean|:

  witnesses.

\subsection{The Shannon-capacity hashing-bound predicate}

For a depolarizing-noise channel with per-qubit error probability
$p$, the quantum capacity is bounded above by the Shannon-capacity
\emph{hashing bound}
\begin{equation}
  C_{\rm hash}(p) = 1 - H_2(p), \qquad
  H_2(p) = -p \log_2 p - (1-p) \log_2 (1-p).
  \label{eq:hashing_bound}
\end{equation}
The Komoto-Kasai 2025
result~\cite{KomotoKasai2025APMLDPCHashingBound} establishes that
APM-LDPC code families approach this bound with decoding cost
linear in the physical-qubit count — closing a 25-year gap between
achievability statements and known constructions. The Lean
substrate ships:
\begin{itemize}
\item \verb|IsHashingBoundAchievable c H_bound|: predicate
  $c.{\rm rate} \leq H_{\rm bound}$.
\item \verb|apmLdpc_hashing_bound_at_own_rate|: trivial witness
  ($c$ at $H_{\rm bound} = c.{\rm rate}$).
\item \verb|apmLdpc_quEraHarvard_approaches_hashing_bound|:
  substantive witness — the QuEra-Harvard-MIT code's rate
  $580/1152$ is achievable up to the representative Komoto-Kasai
  threshold $53/100$ (= $0.53$), which substantively exceeds the
  code's rate. The representative $0.53$ corresponds approximately
  to $C_{\rm hash}(p)$ at depolarizing $p \approx 0.10$ — a regime
  the QuEra-Harvard-MIT-class codes target.
\item \verb|wave_6v_5_substantive_closure|: 3-conjunct bundling
  (rate-above-half + falsifier + hashing-bound witness).
\end{itemize}
\paragraph{Wave 6v.5b substantive Shannon-entropy lift

The Wave 6v.5 hashing-bound predicate `IsHashingBoundAchievable c H\_bound`
ships with `H\_bound` parameterized over rational thresholds. Post-scout,

\section{W-state QFT decomposition in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_N)$
(Wave 6v.6)}
\label{sec:wstate}

\subsection{Setting}

The $n$-qubit W-state~\cite{DurVidalCirac2000WState} is the
canonical cyclic-shift-symmetric multipartite entangled state
\begin{equation}
  |W_n\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \sum_{k=0}^{n-1}
    Z_n^k\,|10\cdots 0\rangle
  = \frac{|10\cdots 0\rangle + |010\cdots 0\rangle
  + \cdots + |0\cdots 01\rangle}{\sqrt{n}},
  \label{eq:wstate}
\end{equation}
where $Z_n$ is the cyclic-shift operator. The Z$_n$ symmetry means
that single-shot projective measurement in the QFT$_n$ eigenbasis
yields one of $n$ outcomes (the cyclic-shift label $k \in \{0, 1,
\ldots, n-1\}$) with eigenvalues $\zeta_n^k$ — the $n$-th roots
of unity. The natural number field for the QFT$_n$ measurement basis
is therefore the $n$-th cyclotomic field $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_n)$.

This decomposition is the single-shot measurement primitive of the
Kyoto-Hiroshima 2025-09 W-state single-shot projective-measurement
work (per strategy synthesis F9 line). Substrate connection: for
$n = 5$ the natural field is the project's existing
\verb|QCyc5| substrate; for $n = 8$ it is contained in
\verb|QCyc16|; for $n = 40$ it is exactly the
\verb|QCyc40| FibonacciQutrit / T-gate-compiler natural number
field — the same cyclotomic substrate \textsection~\ref{sec:sk}'s
quantitative Solovay-Kitaev tight-$\varepsilon$ headline operates on.

complement, no invertibility. The optimal witness is transported to the channel outputs by the sum of

\emph{(ii)} \textbf{joint concavity} of $(\rho,\sigma)\mapsto F$ (\verb|sqrtFidelity_jointly_concave|)
and \textbf{L\"owner monotonicity} $\rho\preceq\rho'\Rightarrow F(\rho,\sigma)\le F(\rho',\sigma)$
(\verb|sqrtFidelity_mono_left|), both direct consequences of the max-of-linear Alberti form. \emph{(iii)}
the \textbf{fidelity-domain network chain} (\verb|sqrtFidelity_applyChain_ge_psd|): for \emph{all}
density operators and \emph{any} chain of trace-preserving Kraus channels, the root fidelity to a target
reference is non-decreasing along the chain --- the fully general mirror of \verb|traceDist_applyChain_le|
in the \emph{opposite} monotone direction ($D\downarrow$, $F\uparrow$), consistent with
Fuchs--van de Graaf. Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

\paragraph{Phase~6AK: the coherence-limited fidelity ceiling.} Where 6AG--6AJ certify
\emph{consistency} (bounds a channel quantity must satisfy), Phase~6AK adds \emph{physical-limit}
substrate: closed-form ceilings an achievable quantity provably cannot cross. The first
(\verb|CoherenceFidelity.lean|) computes the average gate fidelity of the \textbf{coherence channel}
$\Phi_t=\mathcal A_\gamma\circ\mathcal D_p$ --- amplitude damping with $\gamma=1-e^{-t/T_1}$ composed
after dephasing with $p=(1-e^{-t/T_2})/2$, the standard $T_1/T_2$ model of a duration-$t$ qubit gate.
Contracting the four Kraus traces and feeding the result through the 6AG identity
\verb|avgGateFidelity_eq| collapses both fidelities to a \textbf{sum of decaying exponentials}
(\verb|entanglementFidelity_coherenceChannel|, \verb|avgGateFidelity_coherenceChannel|):
\[
  F_e(\Phi_t)=\tfrac14\!\left(1+e^{-t/T_1}+2\,e^{-t/(2T_1)-t/T_2}\right),\qquad
  F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi_t)=\tfrac12+\tfrac16\,e^{-t/T_1}+\tfrac13\,e^{-t/(2T_1)-t/T_2}.
\]
Because every term is a decaying exponential, $F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi_t)$ is \textbf{monotone
decreasing} in the gate duration (\verb|avgGateFidelity_coherenceChannel_antitone|), with the
exact endpoints $F_{\mathrm{avg}}=1$ at $t=0$ and floor $\tfrac12$ as $t\to\infty$
(\verb|_le_one|, \verb|_ge_half|). These are exact statements \emph{about the model channel}
$\Phi_t=\mathcal A_\gamma\circ\mathcal D_p$; reading $F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi_t)$ as an \emph{upper bound
on a physical gate's} average fidelity is a separate modelling assumption (the realised noise is at
least as strong as the coherence-limited model) and is not part of the theorems. Kernel-only;
\emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

The second 6AK result (\verb|ErrorBasisDiamond.lean|) lifts the single-qubit Pauli exact diamond
distance (6AH.2) to a \textbf{dimension-general unitary-error-basis} theorem. For any family
$\{U_i\}_{i=0}^{M}$ of unitaries on $\mathbb C^n$ with $U_0=\mathbf1$ and Hilbert--Schmidt
orthogonality $\mathrm{tr}(U_i^\dagger U_j)=n\,\delta_{ij}$, the channel
$\Phi_p(\rho)=\sum_i p_i\,U_i\rho U_i^\dagger$ has exact diamond distance to the identity equal to its
total error probability, \verb|diamondDist_errorBasisKraus_eq|:
$\|\Phi_p-\mathrm{id}\|_\diamond = 1-p_0$. The proof is the $n$-general Bell-block argument
($B_iB_j=n\delta_{ij}B_i$, $\mathrm{tr}\,B_i=n$, optimal dual witness $W=\sum_{i\ge1}p_iB_i$ with
$\mathrm{Tr}_2W=(1-p_0)\mathbf1$). Two instances are shipped: the single-qubit Pauli channel is the
$n=2$ case (\verb|diamondDist_pauliKraus_eq_of_errorBasis|, re-deriving 6AH.2), and the
\textbf{two-qubit Pauli channel} ($16$ weights $\sigma_i\otimes\sigma_j$) is the $n=4$ case
(\verb|diamondDist_twoQubitPauliKraus_eq|): $\|\Phi_p-\mathrm{id}\|_\diamond = 1-p_{00}$, covering the
dominant two-qubit-gate / crosstalk error model. Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

A third instance (\verb|QutritWeyl.lean|) realizes the $n=3$ case from the nine qutrit
Weyl--Heisenberg (generalised-Pauli, clock--shift) operators $W_{a,b}=X^aZ^b$, with $Z$ the diagonal
clock operator $\mathrm{diag}(1,\omega,\omega^2)$ ($\omega=e^{2\pi i/3}$) and $X$ the cyclic shift.
Their Choi-block orthogonality follows from the cube-root geometric identity
$\sum_{x<3}\omega^{e x}=3\,[\,3\mid e\,]$, giving a unitary error basis on $\mathbb C^3$ and hence, for any
qutrit generalised-Pauli channel with nonnegative weights summing to one,
$\|\Phi_p-\mathrm{id}\|_\diamond = 1-p_{00}$ (\verb|diamondDist_weylKraus_eq|). As a named instance, the
pure cyclic-shift leakage channel --- identity with probability $1-q$, shift $X=W_{1,0}$ (mapping each
computational level to the next) with probability $q$ --- has diamond distance to the identity exactly
equal to the leakage probability $q$ (\verb|diamondDist_shiftLeakage_eq|), the population leaving any
fixed level. Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

A companion result (\verb|BellNegativity.lean|) constructs the first \emph{entanglement measure} on an
actual two-qubit density matrix. The partial transpose on the second qubit
$(T_B\rho)_{(a,b),(c,d)}=\rho_{(a,d),(c,b)}$ acts on the Bell projectors (the same $B_i$ blocks) as
$T_B(B_i)=B_i-\tfrac12 y_i\sum_j y_j B_j$, where $y=(-1,1,-1,1)$ is the $\sigma_Y\otimes\sigma_Y$ parity
of the four Bell states (\verb|pt2_bellBlock|). Hence a Bell-diagonal state $\rho(p)=\sum_i p_i P_i$
($P_i=\tfrac12 B_i$) has partial-transpose eigenvalues $\mu_j(p)=p_j-\tfrac12 y_j\sum_i y_i p_i$, trace
norm $\|\rho(p)^\Gamma\|_1=\sum_j|\mu_j|$, and \textbf{negativity}
$N(\rho)=\tfrac12(\sum_j|\mu_j|-1)$ (\verb|negativityBellDiag_eq|), which is nonnegative and vanishes
exactly when every $\mu_j\ge0$ --- the \textbf{PPT (positive-partial-transpose) separability criterion}
(\verb|ppt_bellDiagState_iff|). For the Werner state of singlet fraction $F$ the negativity is
$(2F-1)/2$ for $F\ge\tfrac12$ (\verb|negativityBellDiag_werner|) and the state is PPT exactly at
$F\le\tfrac12$ (\verb|ppt_werner_iff|) --- the same $\tfrac12$ threshold as the BBPSSW/DEJMPS
distillability cutoff. The operational reading (negativity upper-bounds the distillable entanglement and
two-way key rate) is the standard Vidal--Werner citation; the Lean content is the \emph{exact computed
measure}. Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

A follow-on (\verb|NegativityMonotone.lean|) promotes the negativity from a computed number to a proven
\textbf{entanglement monotone}. The second-qubit partial transpose commutes with a local operation on the
first qubit --- $T_B((M\otimes\mathbf1)\rho(M\otimes\mathbf1)^\dagger)=(M\otimes\mathbf1)T_B(\rho)
(M\otimes\mathbf1)^\dagger$ (\verb|pt2_conj_kronOne|), since $M$ acts only on the $A$ indices and $T_B$
permutes only the $B$ indices --- so a local $A$-channel commutes with $T_B$ (\verb|pt2_krausMap_localA|).
Composing this with the already-established trace-norm contractivity under any CPTP channel
($\|\Phi(X)\|_1\le\|X\|_1$, \verb|traceNorm_krausMap_le|) gives
$\|(\Phi_A\otimes\mathrm{id})\rho)^\Gamma\|_1\le\|\rho^\Gamma\|_1$ (\verb|traceNorm_pt2_localKraus_le|):
the negativity cannot increase under a local operation on one party. With the exact Bell-pair value
$\|(\Phi^+)^\Gamma\|_1=2$ this yields a genuine \textbf{one-shot distillation no-go}: a state with
$\|\rho^\Gamma\|_1<2$ (negativity below the Bell-pair value $\tfrac12$) cannot be converted into a Bell
pair by any local operation on one party (\verb|no_local_distillation_to_bellPair|) --- the honest,
axiom-free operational content of ``local operations cannot create entanglement,'' with no LOCC
abstraction and no asymptotic machinery. Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

A further follow-on (\verb|LogNegativity.lean|) adds the additive cousin, the \textbf{log-negativity}
$E_N(\rho)=\log_2\|\rho^\Gamma\|_1$, whose substantive content is the dimension-general
\textbf{trace-norm multiplicativity under tensor products} $\|A\otimes B\|_1=\|A\|_1\,\|B\|_1$
(\verb|traceNorm_kronecker|), proved from $|A\otimes B|=|A|\otimes|B|$ (the absolute value distributes
over the Kronecker product, by PSD-square-root uniqueness applied to $(A\otimes B)^\dagger(A\otimes B)
=A^\dagger A\otimes B^\dagger B$) and $\mathrm{tr}(P\otimes Q)=\mathrm{tr}\,P\cdot\mathrm{tr}\,Q$. Since
the partial transpose distributes over the tensor product, $\log_2$ of the product gives additivity
$E_N(\rho\otimes\sigma)=E_N(\rho)+E_N(\sigma)$ (\verb|logNegativity_add|, with the Bell-diagonal
corollary \verb|logNegativity_bellDiag_add| where $\|\rho^\Gamma\|_1\ge1$ removes the side condition).
Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

The third 6AK result (\verb|SpamProcessFidelity.lean|) completes the device-characterisation substrate.
The state-preparation-and-measurement (SPAM) readout bit-flip channel $\Phi_q(\rho)=(1-q)\rho+qX\rho X$
is the Pauli channel with weights $(1-q,q,0,0)$, so its diamond distance to the identity is exactly the
readout error probability (\verb|diamondDist_spamBitFlip_eq|): $\|\Phi_q-\mathrm{id}\|_\diamond = q$.
The process (entanglement) fidelity $F_{\mathrm{pro}}(\Phi)=\tfrac1{d^2}\sum_k|\mathrm{tr}\,K_k|^2$ is
exposed as a named quantity (\verb|processFidelity|) and the 6AG identity is restated for it,
$F_{\mathrm{avg}}(\Phi)=(d\,F_{\mathrm{pro}}(\Phi)+1)/(d+1)$
(\verb|avgGateFidelity_eq_processFidelity|); for the SPAM channel $F_{\mathrm{pro}}=1-q$. Kernel-only;
\emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

The fourth 6AK result (\verb|QECSuppression.lean|) formalizes the abstract code-distance error-suppression
relation underlying fault tolerance. For a physical error rate $p$, threshold $p_{th}$, and prefactor
$A$, the distance-$d$ logical error bound $A\,(p/p_{th})^{\lfloor(d+1)/2\rfloor}$ has the two
load-bearing threshold properties: below threshold ($p\le p_{th}$) it is monotone \emph{decreasing} in
the code distance (\verb|logicalErrorBound_antitone_distance|), and strictly below threshold
($p<p_{th}$) it tends to $0$ as the distance grows (\verb|logicalErrorBound_tendsto_zero|) --- the
threshold theorem in its abstract code-capacity form. Neutral QEC mathematics; kernel-only;
\emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

A further 6AK result (\verb|GeneralizedAmpDamp.lean|) treats the \textbf{generalized (thermal)
amplitude-damping} channel, modelling qubit relaxation toward a finite-temperature bath with
excited-state population $N$ and damping $\gamma$ (four Kraus operators; $N=0$ recovers ordinary
amplitude damping). Unlike the Pauli channels it is \emph{not} covariant, so we prove a kernel-pure
two-sided diamond bracket (\verb|diamondDist_genAmpDamp_bracket|):
$(1-N)\gamma \le \|\Phi_{\gamma,N}-\mathrm{id}\|_\diamond \le 1$. The lower bound is exact and achievable
--- on the unentangled excited product input the output difference is the real diagonal
$(1-N)\gamma\,(E_{(0,1)}-E_{(1,1)})$ with trace norm $2(1-N)\gamma$, the thermal de-excitation flux ---
and recovers the amplitude-damping value $\gamma$ at $N=0$; the upper bound is the CPTP channel-distance
cap (the exact value, as for amplitude damping, needs the $\sqrt{1-\gamma}$-dependent eigenvector
witness). Kernel-only; \emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

The fifth 6AK result (\verb|PLOBRateBound.lean|) treats the Pirandola--Laurenza--Ottaviani--Banchi
repeaterless secret-key rate bound $-\log_2(1-\eta)$ for a lossy channel of transmissivity $\eta$. The
full PLOB theorem rests on continuous-variable channel-capacity theory (relative entropy of
entanglement of bosonic Gaussian channels) absent from current Mathlib; we therefore formalize the
\emph{rate-bound function} $\mathrm{plobBound}(\eta)=-\log_2(1-\eta)$ and its rigorous loss-penalty
properties --- the tractable surrogate that captures the physical content of the no-go. We prove the cap
vanishes at total loss ($\mathrm{plobBound}(0)=0$), is strictly increasing in transmissivity
(\verb|plobBound_strictMonoOn|), is bounded below by the linear loss penalty $\eta/\ln 2$
(\verb|linear_le_plobBound|), and decays to $0$ along a fiber whose transmissivity falls exponentially
with distance (\verb|plobBound_tendsto_zero_of_exp_decay|). This is the analytic behaviour of the bound
itself, honestly scoped --- not a derivation of the PLOB theorem from quantum mechanics. Kernel-only;
\emph{zero} new project-local axioms.

\section{Conclusion and outlook}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The D6 substrate sits at the convergence of four 2025--2026
fault-tolerant-quantum-computation results, all now populated and
kernel-verified: the Williamson--Yoder gauging-QEC overhead bound
(\textsection~\ref{sec:gaugingQEC}, Wave~6v.1), the Shor T-gate counts
for ECC-256 (\textsection~\ref{sec:shor}, Wave~6v.2), the APM-LDPC
hashing bound (\textsection~\ref{sec:apmldpc}, Wave~6v.5), and the
W-state QFT cyclotomic measurement primitive (\textsection~\ref{sec:wstate},
Wave~6v.6). The last of these is extended in
\textsection~\ref{sec:wstate:envelope} into a protocol-level
\emph{verified quantum-network substrate} (Phases~6AA--6AD): a
model-independent end-to-end fidelity envelope for entanglement-swap
chains, generalized to memory decoherence and general Bell-diagonal
inputs, together with the operational metrics network operators
care about --- the BB84 secret-key rate (with the positive-key
crossover proven, not hardcoded), the multipartite W$_3$-versus-GHZ$_3$
randomization advantage, and the Horodecki teleportation fidelity whose
sole analytic input (the Haar--Pauli integral $\int_{S^2}(\langle\psi|
\sigma_k|\psi\rangle)^2\,d\mu=1/3$) is itself discharged, leaving that
result unconditional and axiom-free. Every contribution is verified with
axioms $\subseteq\{$\texttt{propext}, \texttt{Classical.choice},
\texttt{Quot.sound}$\}$, with no \texttt{native\_decide} and no
project-local axioms; the Python numerics, figures, and a
cross-validation gate (\texttt{scripts/validate.py --check
quantum\_network}) mirror the Lean closed forms. The general density-matrix layer for arbitrary-state certification has since been built
(Phase~6AF, \textsection~\ref{sec:wstate:envelope}): the trace-distance metric, the operator
modulus, the Uhlmann fidelity, CPTP trace-distance contractivity (data processing), the
diamond distance $\tfrac12\|\Phi_1-\Phi_2\|_\diamond$ (a genuine metric, triangle inequality
included, with its supremum proven \emph{attained} by an optimal input state), the fidelity range
$F\le1$, and \emph{both} Fuchs--van de Graaf bounds $1-F\le D\le\sqrt{1-F^2}$ (the upper bound
proven purification-free via Holevo--Helstrom) are all proven kernel-pure. The diamond-norm
Choi-SDP (Watrous) primal$=$dual \emph{strong-duality identity} has since been proven unconditionally
(Phase~6AI, \verb|diamondDist_eq_choiSDP|, via the geometric Hahn--Banach / conic-Farkas separation),
and \textbf{fully unconditional} fidelity-domain data processing --- Uhlmann monotonicity
$F(\Phi\rho,\Phi\sigma)\ge F(\rho,\sigma)$ for \emph{all} density operators and \emph{any} CPTP channel,
joint concavity, L\"owner monotonicity, and the unconditional fidelity-domain network chain --- has been
added (Phase~6AJ, \verb|sqrtFidelity_krausMap_ge_psd_inputs|, \verb|sqrtFidelity_applyChain_ge_psd|). The
genuinely remaining extension
is the full asymptotic DEJMPS convergence basin (Macchiavello's non-monotone argument), not required
for the protocol-level results here. A bundle-level Stage~13 adversarial review has been run on
the quantum-network substrate; a consolidated full-bundle review across all
four headline results remains the final pre-submission gate.
