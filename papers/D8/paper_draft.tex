\title{Kernel-Verified Universal Quantum Gate Compilation:\\
Alphabet-Agnostic Solovay--Kitaev across Dimensions}

\author{John Roehm}

\begin{abstract}
We report what is, to our knowledge, the first machine-checked development of
universal quantum gate compilation as a \emph{theory} rather than a single
algorithm: an
alphabet-agnostic, dimension-agnostic quantitative Solovay--Kitaev substrate,
formally verified in the Lean~4 proof assistant, together with its
instantiation across five gate alphabets and a verified resource lower bound.
The substrate packages the Dawson--Nielsen super-quadratic recursion,
Cartan-type density-from-witness, Baker--Campbell--Hausdorff bracket closure,
and the $\varepsilon_0$-net behind a generic generating-set abstraction, so that
a new universal alphabet ships its verified quantitative compiler by
\emph{instantiation} rather than re-derivation. We lift the substrate to the
special unitary group $\su{d}$ of arbitrary dimension---to our knowledge the
first kernel-verified quantitative Solovay--Kitaev theorem at general
dimension in any proof assistant---by constructing a Banach-algebra matrix
logarithm with a named convergence radius, which removes an existential
obstruction that otherwise blocks the generalization. We instantiate the
machinery on Fibonacci anyons, Clifford$+T$, Read--Rezayi anyons at levels
$5$ and $7$, the trapped-ion M\o lmer--S\o rensen gate set at $\su{4}$, and
Clifford$+$CCZ at $\su{8}$. For the three-qubit target $\su{8}$ we give two
fundamentally distinct, formally verified density mechanisms: one using the
$T$ gate, and one using \emph{no} $T$ gate at all, in which the doubly
controlled-$Z$ (CCZ) gate is essential---accompanied by a converse proving
that the Clifford group alone generates only a finite subgroup of $\su{8}$ and
hence is not dense. We machine-check, unconditionally, a lower bound on
the number of Toffoli gates required to implement a unitary, expressed through
a signed-parity-invariant measure on the gate's channel representation.
Beyond existence, we verify the compilation \emph{algorithm} itself: a named
correct-by-construction Clifford$+T$ compiler whose error and length contract
is a theorem about the algorithm; an $O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$-length
(exponent-$1$) synthesis theory along two further mechanisms---the
Ross--Selinger grid route, formalized down to its Appendix-C Diophantine
solvability theory with its one remaining number-theoretic existence carried
as an explicit hypothesis, and the Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca ancilla route,
fully unconditional via Lagrange's four-square theorem with an explicit
verified word bound---and an end-to-end diamond-distance certificate
converting compiler accuracy $\varepsilon$ into worst-case channel error
$\le \sqrt 2\,\varepsilon$. Every
headline theorem is verified to the Lean kernel with no project-local axioms,
and the corpus contains no compiled-evaluation
(\texttt{native\_decide}) trust escapes.
We delimit the scope explicitly: this is the verified \emph{existence},
universality, algorithm-correctness, synthesis, and lower-bound theory;
runtime-compiler implementation, hardware-specific tuning, and
constant-factor length optimization are a separate deliverable.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

A quantum processor exposes a fixed, finite set of physical gates---an
\emph{alphabet}---yet must approximate an essentially continuous family of
target unitaries. The Solovay--Kitaev theorem guarantees that any
sufficiently rich alphabet can approximate any target to precision
$\varepsilon$ using a circuit whose length grows only poly-logarithmically in
$1/\varepsilon$~\cite{KitaevShenVyalyi2002,DawsonNielsen2006,AharonovArad2011}. It is the
foundational existence result behind every fault-tolerant compilation
pipeline. Its proof, however, is a delicate interplay of Lie theory (the
generating set must densely fill the group), differential geometry (the
balanced-commutator recursion lives on the group manifold near the identity),
and number theory (density of a discrete alphabet typically rests on an
irrationality or algebraic-integer obstruction). This combination has made the
theorem a recurring source of subtle hypotheses in the literature, and an
attractive but difficult target for formal verification.

Prior formal-verification efforts in quantum computation have largely targeted
\emph{exact} circuit semantics and optimization: VOQC/SQIR in
Coq~\cite{Hietala2020VOQC} verifies equivalence-preserving circuit
optimizations, and the broader verified-quantum-programs landscape (Why3/Qbricks,
Isabelle, and others) is surveyed in~\cite{Lewis2021VerifQC}. These establish
that a circuit computes what it should; none develops the \emph{quantitative
approximation} theory---density of a discrete alphabet, error-vs-length
trade-off, resource lower bounds---that is the subject of this work. The
distinction is approximation versus exact optimization: D8 verifies that a
target unitary can be \emph{approached} to arbitrary precision with bounded
circuit length, which is a different question from verifying that a given exact
circuit is correctly optimized.

This paper reports a machine-checked development, in the Lean~4 proof
assistant, that treats universal compilation not as one algorithm for one
alphabet but as a reusable \emph{theory}. Our contributions are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item An \emph{alphabet-agnostic} quantitative Solovay--Kitaev substrate
(Sec.~\ref{sec:substrate}): the Lie-algebraic core is parametrized over an
abstract generating set, so a new universal gate set inherits the full
verified quantitative compiler by discharging a single density witness.

\item The \emph{lift to arbitrary dimension} $\su{d}$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:sud}): the first kernel-verified quantitative
Solovay--Kitaev at general $d$, enabled by a concrete-radius matrix logarithm
that is a contribution to the underlying mathematical library in its own right.

\item A \emph{five-alphabet instantiation survey} (Sec.~\ref{sec:alphabets}):
Fibonacci, Clifford$+T$, Read--Rezayi $\su 2_5$ and $\su 2_7$, trapped-ion
M\o lmer--S\o rensen at $\su 4$, and Clifford$+$CCZ at $\su 8$.

\item \emph{Two complementary density mechanisms for $\su 8$}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:su8}): a $T$-based route and a $T$-free, CCZ-essential route,
with a converse establishing that the Clifford group alone is finite in
$\su 8$.

\item A \emph{verified resource lower bound} (Sec.~\ref{sec:resource}): an
unconditional machine-checked lower bound on Toffoli count via a
signed-parity-invariant measure on the channel representation, following
Mukhopadhyay~\cite{Mukhopadhyay2024}.

\item A \emph{correct-by-construction compiler} (Sec.~\ref{sec:compiler}):
a named Clifford$+T$ compilation function whose termination, loop invariant,
error, and length bounds are theorems about the algorithm itself.

\item A \emph{verified optimal-length synthesis theory}
(Secs.~\ref{sec:rs}--\ref{sec:rsnt}): the Ross--Selinger grid
route~\cite{RossSelinger2016} formalized down to its Appendix-C Diophantine
solvability chain (over a newly verified Euclidean $\mathbb Z[\zeta_8]$),
with its single remaining number-theoretic existence isolated as an explicit
hypothesis and honestly documented as open; and the
Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca ancilla route~\cite{KMM2013ancilla}, verified
\emph{unconditionally} at $O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$ with exponent $1$ and an
explicit word bound --- a third compilation mechanism alongside the
Solovay--Kitaev recursion and the grid route.

\item An \emph{end-to-end channel certificate} (Sec.~\ref{sec:diamond}):
an Aharonov--Kitaev--Nisan-style operator-norm-to-diamond
conversion~\cite{AKN1998} composed with the verified compiler, certifying
that compiling to accuracy $\varepsilon$ bounds the worst-case channel error
by $\sqrt 2\,\varepsilon$, every link kernel-checked.

\item A \emph{Mathlib-upstream contribution portfolio}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:mathlib}).
\end{enumerate}

All headline theorems are verified to the Lean kernel with axiom set
$\{\texttt{propext},\,\texttt{Classical.choice},\,\texttt{Quot.sound}\}$ (the
standard Lean kernel) and no project-local axioms. We state the boundary of
the work plainly in Sec.~\ref{sec:scope}: we verify that a compilation
\emph{exists} with provable error and length bounds; the engineering of a
runnable, hardware-tuned, length-optimal compiler is deliberately out of
scope.

\section{The alphabet-agnostic substrate}
\label{sec:substrate}

The quantitative Solovay--Kitaev argument has four reusable ingredients that
do not depend on the specific alphabet: (i) a density statement, that the group
generated by the alphabet is dense in the target group; (ii) an
$\varepsilon_0$-net, a finite set of words approximating every target to a base
precision $\varepsilon_0$; (iii) the Dawson--Nielsen recursion, which uses a
balanced group commutator to convert a precision-$\varepsilon$ approximation
into a precision-$O(\varepsilon^{3/2})$ one at controlled length cost; and
(iv) a Baker--Campbell--Hausdorff (BCH) estimate controlling the commutator's
deviation from the corresponding Lie-bracket flow.

We factor these into a substrate parametrized over an abstract generating set,
realized in the \lean{GenericSU2GeneratingSet} and
\lean{GenericSolovayKitaevQuantitative} module families. The headline of the
substrate is a bundled-strict theorem asserting, for any generating set
satisfying the density and net hypotheses, the simultaneous error bound
$\lVert \mathrm{compile}(U,\varepsilon) - U\rVert \le \varepsilon$ and a
poly-logarithmic word-length bound, at the \emph{same} algorithmic compile
level. The design claim is that adding a new universal alphabet to the verified
chain is an \emph{instantiation} problem, not a re-derivation problem.

We validate the abstraction on the canonical fault-tolerant alphabet
Clifford$+T$. The density witness---that $\langle H, T\rangle$ is dense in
$\su 2$---is discharged through a Niven-style algebraic-integer obstruction on
the trace $\sqrt 2\,\sin(\pi/8)$ of $H_{\mathrm{SU}}\,T_{\mathrm{SU}}$
(\lean{CliffordTInfiniteOrder.lean}, theorem
\lean{cliffordT\_accPt\_one\_unconditional}); this is the 1999
Boykin--Mor--Pulver--Roychowdhury--Vatan folklore
result~\cite{BMPRV1999}, now machine-verified. The result is the
unconditional bundled-strict headline
\lean{solovayKitaev\_dawson\_nielsen\_quantitative\_cliffordT\_strict\_constructive\_tight\_unconditional}.
The algebraic-integer obstruction, rather than a cyclotomic computation, is the
load-bearing novelty: it is the generic technique the remaining alphabets
reuse.

\section{The lift to arbitrary dimension}
\label{sec:sud}

The substrate of Sec.~\ref{sec:substrate} targets single-qubit unitaries
($\su 2$). Multi-qubit compilation requires the same machinery at $\su d$ for
$d = 2^n$. We lift all four ingredients to general $d$, yielding the headline
\lean{solovayKitaev\_dawson\_nielsen\_quantitative\_generic\_sud\_strict\_constructive\_tight}
(\lean{GenericSUdQuantitative.lean}): for every $d \ge 2$, every
$U \in \su d$, and every $\varepsilon > 0$, a compilation exists with error
$\le \varepsilon$ and poly-logarithmic length. To our knowledge this is the
first kernel-verified quantitative Solovay--Kitaev at arbitrary dimension in
any proof assistant. The generic statement is parametrized over the alphabet's
density witness and a word-length hypothesis; the per-alphabet instantiations of
Sec.~\ref{sec:alphabets} discharge these, and their headlines are
unconditional.

\subsection{The concrete-radius matrix logarithm}

The single obstruction to the lift was the \emph{regime} step: the balanced
commutator construction requires writing a near-identity unitary $\Delta$ as
$\exp(H)$ for a Hermitian-traceless $H$ of controlled norm, and reasoning about
the round trip. The natural tool, an inverse-function-theorem matrix logarithm,
provides only an \emph{existential} convergence radius, which propagates a
circular dependency through the recursion. We instead construct an explicit
Banach-algebra logarithm with a \emph{named} convergence radius,
\[
  \lean{matrixMercatorLog}\,X \;=\; \sum_{n\ge 0}
  \frac{(-1)^n}{n+1}\,X^{n+1},\qquad \lVert X\rVert < 1,
\]
and prove the round trip $\exp(\lean{matrixMercatorLog}\,X) = \mathbf 1 + X$
(\lean{GenericSUdMatrixMercatorLog.lean}, theorem
\lean{exp\_matrixMercatorLog}). With a concrete radius in hand, the regime
conjuncts---Hermiticity, tracelessness, and the exp round trip---are all
discharged unconditionally on a named ball, and the headline cascade
(\lean{GenericSUdSkHeadlineCascadeConcrete.lean}) reduces to the density
witness and the length bound alone. The matrix logarithm fills a genuine gap
in the underlying library, which lacks a Banach-algebra logarithm with a named
radius; we return to it in Sec.~\ref{sec:mathlib}.

\subsection{The length exponent}

The Dawson--Nielsen recursion contracts error as $\varepsilon \mapsto
O(\varepsilon^{3/2})$ with a fivefold word-expansion per level, giving a
word-length that grows as $O\!\big((\log 1/\varepsilon)^{\gamma}\big)$ with
exponent $\gamma = \log 5/\log(3/2) \approx 3.97$ (Fig.~\ref{fig:recursion}).
We single-source this value
as \lean{skLengthExponent\_sud} (\lean{GenericSUdSkLengthExponent.lean}) and
discharge the poly-logarithmic length bound unconditionally. We emphasize that
the often-quoted exponent $\log 5/\log 2 \approx 2.32$ would require a
quadratic ($\varepsilon \mapsto O(\varepsilon^2)$) contraction, which our
construction does not establish; we report the honest $3.97$.

Figure~\ref{fig:coverage} summarizes the coverage across alphabets and

\item \textbf{Fibonacci anyons} ($\su 2$): density via the

\item \textbf{Read--Rezayi $\su 2_5$ and $\su 2_7$}: the next universal-anyon

\item \textbf{Clifford$+$CCZ} ($\su 8$): two distinct routes, treated in

\section{Two density mechanisms for \texorpdfstring{$\su 8$}{SU(8)}}
\label{sec:su8}

The three-qubit target $\su 8$ admits two formally verified density
mechanisms that are fundamentally different, and we present both because their
contrast is itself the result (Fig.~\ref{fig:su8duality}).

\paragraph{Route A: \texorpdfstring{$T$}{T}-based, CCZ over-complete.} Using
the universal alphabet $\{H, T, \mathrm{CNOT}, \mathrm{CCZ}\}$, density follows
entirely from the $\{H, T, \mathrm{CNOT}\}$ sub-alphabet, in the manner of
Aaronson--Gottesman~\cite{AaronsonGottesman2004}; CCZ is present but
over-complete (unused in the witness). This yields the headline
\lean{cliffordCCZSU8\_solovayKitaev\_headline\_unconditional}. We flag the
scope honestly: in Route~A, CCZ is \emph{not} load-bearing.

\paragraph{Route B: \texorpdfstring{$T$}{T}-free, CCZ-essential.} Using the
literal alphabet $\langle H, S, \mathrm{CNOT}, \mathrm{CCZ}\rangle$ with
\emph{no} $T$ gate, we prove density directly:
\lean{cliffordCCZLiteral\_dense} (\lean{CliffordCCZSU8Density.lean}; the
bundled-strict headline \lean{cliffordCCZLiteral\_solovayKitaev\_headline\_unconditional}
is in \lean{CliffordCCZSU8LiteralHeadline.lean}).
The mechanism is distinct from Route~A. Density does not come from a per-qubit
infinite-order element; it comes from the seed $\mathrm{CCZ}\cdot(H\otimes
H\otimes H)$, whose trace $1/\sqrt 2 \notin \mathcal O$ (an algebraic-integer
obstruction) forces infinite order (\lean{CliffordCCZSU8LiteralSeed.lean}). A
one-parameter flow accumulated from the seed is then spread across the full Lie
algebra $\mathfrak{su}(8)$ by Clifford-conjugation irreducibility, the Clifford
group acting transitively on the $63$ non-identity Pauli lines. The
bundled-strict headline is
\lean{cliffordCCZLiteral\_solovayKitaev\_headline\_unconditional}.

\paragraph{CCZ is essential.} Route~B is complemented by a converse
(Sec.~\ref{sec:resource}): the Clifford group alone, $\langle H, S,
\mathrm{CNOT}\rangle$, generates a \emph{finite} subgroup of $\su 8$ and is
therefore not dense. CCZ is the unique resource whose addition makes the
difference. The pairing of Routes~A and~B settles the question of why two
$\su 8$ density proofs coexist: they rest on genuinely distinct mechanisms (a
per-qubit $T$ route and a global CCZ-seed route), not on redundancy or
over-generality.

\section{A verified resource lower bound}
\label{sec:resource}

Compilation existence has a dual: how \emph{cheaply} can a target be built? We
formalize the channel-representation resource theory of
Mukhopadhyay~\cite{Mukhopadhyay2024} for Clifford$+$CCZ in $\su 8$ and
machine-check, \emph{unconditionally}, a Toffoli-count lower bound.

The central object is a signed-parity-invariant measure $\mathrm{sde}_2$ on
the channel representation $\widehat U$ of a unitary $U$. We prove that this
measure is non-increasing under Clifford multiplication and increases by at most
one under a CCZ (equivalently Toffoli) gate; the channel-representation entries
of any Clifford$+$CCZ word are rational (Lemma~3.10,
\lean{channelRep\_interp\_isRat}, which kernel-verifies rationality of the
entries; the stronger dyadic $\mathbb Z[1/2]$ form of Mukhopadhyay's Lemma~3.10
follows from the half-integer CCZ row structure of Theorem~3.8). Telescoping
these facts gives the lower bound
\[
  T^{\mathrm{of}}(U) \;\ge\; \mathrm{sde}_2(\widehat U),
\]
formalized as \lean{channelSde2\_le\_toffoliCost}
(\lean{MukhopadhyayToffoliUnconditional.lean}). The earlier form of this bound
was \emph{parametric} (conditional on the Clifford-preservation and
CCZ-increment hypotheses); we discharge those hypotheses and obtain the
unconditional statement. A fully machine-checked unconditional resource lower
bound for a fault-tolerant gate is, to our knowledge, the first of its kind and
is directly usable in magic-state-distillation resource accounting.

The converse of Sec.~\ref{sec:su8} is the same machinery read in the
density direction: the channel representation maps the Clifford group into
signed-permutation matrices, a finite group, giving
\lean{cliffordOnly\_not\_dense} (\lean{MukhopadhyayCliffordNotDense.lean}). We
note the scope: the matching Toffoli-count \emph{upper} bound (minimality;
Mukhopadhyay's Conjecture~4.8) is not addressed here; the lower bound stands
unconditionally on its own.

\section{From existence to a verified algorithm: a correct-by-construction
Clifford$+T$ compiler}
\label{sec:compiler}

The theory developed so far certifies that good compilations \emph{exist}. A
strictly stronger guarantee is a verified \emph{algorithm}: a single named
compile function whose error and length contract is a theorem about the
algorithm itself, universally quantified over targets and precisions, rather
than a certificate checked per output --- the gate-compilation analogue of a
verified compiler in the CompCert tradition. The substrate of
Sec.~\ref{sec:substrate} already contains the two load-bearing ingredients: the
Dawson--Nielsen composition is realized as a \emph{structural} recursion on the
depth (so termination is by construction), and the per-level super-quadratic
error contraction is proved unconditionally. Packaging these yields the
compiler function
\[
  \lean{cliffordTCompile} : \su{2} \times \mathbb{R} \longrightarrow
  \mathrm{FreeGroup}(\mathrm{Fin}\;2),
\]
which maps a target $U$ and a precision $\varepsilon$ to a word in the free
group on the two generators $H, T$ (\lean{FKLW/CliffordTCompiler.lean}). Its
verification contract has
the shape of a classical loop-correctness proof, with each piece a named
kernel-checked theorem:

\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{termination / finite depth}
(\lean{cliffordTCompile\_eq\_recursion}): the compiler equals the structural
Dawson--Nielsen recursion run to the explicit finite depth
$\lean{skLevel\_polylog}\,\varepsilon$;
\item \emph{loop initialization}
(\lean{cliffordTCompile\_recursion\_base}): at depth $0$ the compiler returns
the constructive base-finder word;
\item \emph{loop invariant} (\lean{cliffordTCompile\_loop\_invariant}): at
every recursion depth $n$ and for every target $U$, the depth-$n$ output
approximates $U$ within the super-quadratically shrinking budget --- each
refinement step preserves and tightens the contract, unconditionally;
\item \emph{correctness} (\lean{cliffordTCompile\_correct}): for every
$U \in \su 2$ and every $\varepsilon \in (0, \varepsilon_0]$, the output
simultaneously (i) approximates $U$ within $\varepsilon$ in operator norm,
(ii) has recursion-level length within the poly-logarithmic
Sec.~\ref{sec:substrate} bound, and (iii) has actual output-word length bounded
by an explicit closed-form expression in the recursion depth and the
constructive $\varepsilon_0$-cover's maximum word length.
\end{itemize}

A worked instantiation (\lean{cliffordTCompile\_correct\_example}) witnesses
that the contract is non-vacuous at a concrete operating point. We flag the
honest residual in (iii): the output-word length constant is the constructive
$\varepsilon_0$-cover's, not the information-theoretically optimal
Ross--Selinger constant --- precisely the gap the next two sections address.
We also reiterate the scope boundary: \lean{cliffordTCompile} is a
mathematical function inside the proof assistant, verified once for all
inputs; engineering it into a runnable, performance-tuned software compiler
remains outside this paper's scope (Sec.~\ref{sec:scope}).

\section{Optimal-length synthesis: the Ross--Selinger grid route and an honest
number-theoretic wall}
\label{sec:rs}

The Solovay--Kitaev recursion delivers word length
$O\!\big((\log 1/\varepsilon)^{3.97}\big)$; the information-theoretic optimum
is $O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$ with exponent $1$, achieved for Clifford$+T$
$z$-rotations by the Ross--Selinger grid-synthesis
algorithm~\cite{RossSelinger2016} building on exact synthesis over
$\mathbb Z[\omega][1/\sqrt 2]$~\cite{KMM2013}. We formalize the
\emph{efficiency layer} of this route
(\lean{FKLW/RossSelinger/LogLengthHeadline.lean}): conditional on the grid
finder succeeding, the synthesized Clifford$+T$ word has length
$\le N_3 + 8k$ at precision parameter $k$ --- equivalently
(\lean{rossSelinger\_log\_length\_explicit}) length
$\le N_3 + 16\log_2(1/\delta) + 16\log_2(1+\sqrt2)$ at grid precision
$\delta = (1+\sqrt 2)/\sqrt 2^{\,k}$ --- i.e.\ $O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$ with
exponent $1$.

The conditionality is not left opaque. The theorem
\lean{gridFindT\_isSome\_of\_residual} proves that the grid finder's success
is implied by a single, precise number-theoretic existence: that the
residual $2^k - u^\dagger u$ of a grid candidate $u$ is realized as a bounded
relative norm $t^\dagger t$ from $\mathbb Z[\omega]$,
$\omega = e^{i\pi/4}$ (Ross's Problem~3.2.4), and
\lean{rossSelinger\_synth\_of\_residual} then delivers the full
$O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$ synthesis from that hypothesis alone. The remaining
gap is therefore isolated, and we state its status plainly: the source
literature itself discharges this existence only as a \emph{randomized}
algorithm under a prime-distribution hypothesis (Hypothesis~8.3 of
Ref.~\cite{RossSelinger2016}, which we verified against the primary sources
to be the same statement as Hypothesis~29 of Ref.~\cite{Selinger2015}); an
unconditional discharge would need primes (or relative norms) of prescribed
form in growing grid windows, a short-interval analytic-number-theory
statement that is open in the formalized-mathematics ecosystem and, in its
primality form, in the literature. The needed existence is strictly weaker
than the prime-distribution hypothesis --- relative-norm density rather than
primality --- and is plausibly unconditional, but the known analytic routes
(Landau--Ramanujan-type lower bounds via half-dimensional sieves) are far
beyond current formal libraries. Per this project's standing posture, the gap
is carried as an \emph{explicit hypothesis in the theorem statement} --- never
as an axiom --- so the zero-project-local-axiom claim of this paper is
preserved; the conditional theorems are kernel-checked exactly as stated. The
next section shows that a different, ancilla-assisted mechanism removes the
wall entirely.

\section{Completing the Ross--Selinger substrate, and a fully constructive

\subsection{The Diophantine correctness theory and a Euclidean
\texorpdfstring{$\mathbb Z[\zeta_8]$}{Z[zeta8]}}
\label{sec:rsnt-appc}

The grid route of Sec.~\ref{sec:rs} rests on the Diophantine equation
$t^\dagger t = \xi$ over $\mathbb Z[\omega]$, whose solvability theory is
Appendix~C of Ref.~\cite{RossSelinger2016}. We machine-check the load-bearing
chain end-to-end (\lean{FKLW/RossSelinger/} module family): Lemma~C.2, that
doubly-positive units of $\mathbb Z[\sqrt 2]$ are squares
(\lean{zsqrt2\_isSquare\_of\_unit\_pos\_pos}); Lemma~C.16, the solvability
criterion --- $t^\dagger t = \xi$ is solvable iff $\xi$ is doubly positive and
$\dagger$-decomposable (\lean{relNorm\_iff\_doublyPositive\_decomposable});
Lemma~C.19, multiplicativity of $\dagger$-decomposability across coprime
factors (\lean{daggerDecomposable\_mul\_iff}); the mod-$8$ obstruction at
primes over $p \equiv 7 \pmod 8$
(\lean{not\_daggerDecomposable\_of\_norm\_pow\_seven}); the Lemma~C.20 engine
(\lean{daggerDecomposable\_of\_prime\_dvd\_normSq}) with its constructive
prime-case instances; and Lemma~C.21, the even-power criterion as a
biconditional (\lean{daggerDecomposable\_pow\_iff\_seven}): for $\xi$ of norm
$\pm p$ with $p \equiv 7 \pmod 8$, the power $\xi^m$ is
$\dagger$-decomposable iff $m$ is even.

The gcd machinery underneath the chain required proving that
$\mathbb Z[\omega] = \mathbb Z[\zeta_8]$ is a Euclidean domain
(\lean{ZOmegaEuclideanDomain.lean}), an instance absent from Mathlib and a
standalone upstream candidate (Sec.~\ref{sec:mathlib}). The formalization
also caught a planning error worth recording: an earlier internal roadmap
targeted a Euclidean-domain structure on the subring
$\mathbb Z[\sqrt 2][i]$, which is mathematically impossible --- it is an
index-$2$, non-integrally-closed subring of $\mathbb Z[\omega]$ and hence
never a UFD. The primary source was re-read end-to-end during this
formalization pass and our internal cross-reference scaffolding corrected
against it; all section and lemma numbers cited here follow
arXiv:1403.2975v3.

On the geometric side, we sharpen the one-dimensional grid-existence input:
\lean{oneDim\_grid\_exists\_product}
(\lean{FKLW/RossSelinger/GridExistenceSharp.lean}) establishes Lemma~4.4 of
Ref.~\cite{RossSelinger2016} in its sharp product form --- the product
condition $\delta\Delta \ge (1+\sqrt 2)^2$ alone guarantees a grid point at
every position, where the naive center-rounding argument needs \emph{both}
interval lengths to be at least $1+\sqrt 2$. The product form is exactly what
the asymmetric two-disk scaled-grid analysis (a tiny $\varepsilon$-region
against a large disk) consumes.

\subsection{A fully constructive third mechanism: the
Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca ancilla route}
\label{sec:kmm}

The corpus now contains \emph{three} formally verified compilation
mechanisms, resting on genuinely different mathematics: the Dawson--Nielsen
recursion (unconditional, alphabet-agnostic, length exponent $\approx 3.97$;
Secs.~\ref{sec:substrate}--\ref{sec:sud}), the Ross--Selinger grid route
(optimal exponent $1$, conditional on the relative-norm existence;
Sec.~\ref{sec:rs}), and --- the headline of this section --- the
Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca ancilla-assisted route~\cite{KMM2013ancilla},
which achieves the optimal exponent \emph{unconditionally}, with no
number-theoretic hypothesis of any kind. The mechanism is the one identified
in Ref.~\cite{KMM2013ancilla}: adjoining ancilla qubits turns the synthesis
Diophantine equation into a four-square problem, which Lagrange's
four-square theorem solves \emph{always}. The formalization combines that
keystone with a constructive dimension-$4$ column lemma in the style of
Giles--Selinger exact synthesis~\cite{GilesSelinger2013} and a verified Euler
$Z$--$X$--$Z$ decomposition of $\su 2$.

The result is the unconditional headline
(\lean{kmm\_universal\_headline},
\lean{FKLW/RossSelinger/KMMUniversal.lean}): \emph{for every}
$U \in \su 2$ and every $k \in \mathbb N$ there exist a three-qubit
Clifford$+T$ word $W$ of length
\[
  |W| \;\le\; 50400\,k + 812
\]
and a global phase $c$ with $\lvert c\rvert = 1$, such that for every unit
input pair $(\alpha, \beta)$, applying $W$ to the ancilla-initialized state
$(\alpha\lvert 0\rangle + \beta\lvert 1\rangle)\otimes\lvert 00\rangle$
produces a state within squared $\ell^2$-distance
\[
  9\left(\frac{2}{4^k} + \frac{2\sqrt 2}{2^k}\right)
\]
of the ancilla-restored ideal output for $c^{-1}U$. The error scale is
$\varepsilon \sim 2^{-k}$ up to constants, so the word length is
$O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$ with exponent exactly $1$ --- and the proof consumes
\emph{no} prime-density input: only Lagrange four squares, the constructive
column lemma, and the Euler decomposition. We state the contract's shape
honestly: it is a state-level guarantee on ancilla-initialized inputs
(matching the published construction's own contract), with the ancillas
restored in the comparison state up to the stated error and a global phase;
and the explicit constants $50400\,k + 812$ are verified bounds, not
optimized ones --- tightening them is engineering, outside this paper's
scope.

\subsection{Kernel purity made uniform: eliminating
\texorpdfstring{\lean{native\_decide}}{native-decide}}
\label{sec:purity}

Lean offers two ways to discharge a large finite check: kernel-checked
evaluation (\lean{decide}), and compiled evaluation (\lean{native\_decide}),
which extends the trusted base from the kernel to the Lean compiler and host
toolchain. The exact-synthesis layer beneath the
Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca development originally relied on four
\lean{native\_decide} finite sweeps (the largest over
$\sim$$16.7$~million tuples). All four have been eliminated by structural
reproofs: a parity/ramification calculus replaces the bridge-step box sweep
(\lean{kSO3\_reconstruct\_le\_three}); a Giles--Selinger-style parity
argument replaces the orthogonal-triple sweep
(\lean{someKills\_of\_orthogonal}), and in doing so \emph{strengthens} the
statement by dropping a hypothesis; a torsion classification of
$\mathbb Z[\omega]$ elements of small norm reduces the Clifford base-case
coverage to chunked kernel-checked \lean{decide}s; and a pairing-form
calculus reduces the mod-$8$ valuation algebra to two kernel-checked sweeps.
As a result, the entire compilation corpus this paper reports --- substrate,
instantiations, resource bounds, compiler, grid route, ancilla route, and
channel certificates --- is verified to the kernel with the standard axiom
set and \emph{zero} compiler-trust escape hatches; every remaining finite
check is kernel-checked.

\section{From compiled gates to physical channels: an end-to-end diamond
certificate}
\label{sec:diamond}

A synthesis bound controls the \emph{operator-norm} distance between
unitaries; what a hardware error budget consumes is the \emph{worst-case
channel} error --- the diamond distance between the induced channels. We
close this gap with a kernel-checked chain
(\lean{QuantumNetwork/UnitaryDiamond.lean}). The central inequality is the
Aharonov--Kitaev--Nisan-style conversion~\cite{AKN1998}
(\lean{diamondDist\_unitaryKraus\_le}): for unitary channels
$\Phi_U(\rho) = U\rho U^\dagger$ and $\Phi_V(\rho) = V\rho V^\dagger$,
\[
  \mathrm{diamondDist}(\Phi_U, \Phi_V) \;\le\; \lVert U - V\rVert_{\mathrm{op}}
\]
in this development's $\tfrac12\lVert\cdot\rVert_\diamond$ convention
(equivalently $\lVert \Phi_U - \Phi_V\rVert_\diamond \le
2\lVert U - V\rVert_{\mathrm{op}}$), with the ancilla register handled by a
Kronecker-factor operator-norm bound
(\lean{l2\_opNorm\_kronecker\_one\_le}) and the trace-norm H\"older toolbox
transferred from the community quantum-information library of
Ref.~\cite{Meiburg2025PhysLib} through a single norm identification
(\lean{traceNorm\_eq\_physlib}). The add-subtract telescope underlying the
proof is established for arbitrary contractions, strictly more general than
the unitary case. A known caveat is itself shipped \emph{as a theorem} rather
than as prose: the bound is not sharp under global phase ---
\lean{diamondDist\_unitary\_smul\_phase} proves that $U$ and $e^{i\theta}U$
generate the same channel (diamond distance zero) while their operator-norm
distance is positive. The phase-optimized tightening is documented and
deliberately not claimed.

The capstone composes this with the verified compiler of
Sec.~\ref{sec:compiler}
(\lean{diamondDist\_cliffordTCompile\_le},
\lean{FKLW/CompiledGateDiamond.lean}): for every $U \in \su 2$ and
$\varepsilon \in (0, \varepsilon_0]$, compiling $U$ to accuracy
$\varepsilon$ implies the compiled word's \emph{physical channel} is within
diamond distance $\sqrt 2\,\varepsilon$ of the target's. Every link is
kernel-checked: the compiler contract (an $L^\infty$ operator-norm bound)
yields per-row $\ell^1$ sums $\le \varepsilon$; a row-sum-to-$L^2$
operator-norm bridge (\lean{l2\_opNorm\_le\_sqrt\_card\_mul\_of\_rowSum\_le})
gives $\lVert W - U\rVert_{\mathrm{op}} \le \sqrt 2\,\varepsilon$; the
conversion above finishes. The $\sqrt 2$ is the dimension constant of the
norm bridge ($\sqrt{\mathrm{card}}$ at one qubit), not the conversion
constant --- a sharper bridge would tighten it. The result is the
certificate-grade statement an error budget can cite by name: gate-synthesis
accuracy $\varepsilon$ implies worst-case channel error at most
$\sqrt 2\,\varepsilon$, with no unverified step between the algorithm and
the channel.

\section{Scope and discussion}
\label{sec:scope}

We state the boundary of this work explicitly, both to forestall over-reading
and because the boundary is itself a deliberate design choice. This paper
establishes the verified \emph{existence}, \emph{universality},
\emph{algorithm-correctness}, \emph{optimal-length synthesis}, and
\emph{lower-bound} theory of universal quantum gate compilation: that a
compilation exists, with provable error and word-length bounds, at arbitrary
dimension, for the alphabets treated here; that a named compilation algorithm
meets its contract as a theorem (Sec.~\ref{sec:compiler}); that
$O(\log 1/\varepsilon)$-length synthesis is verified along three distinct
mechanisms (Secs.~\ref{sec:rs}--\ref{sec:rsnt}); that compiled gates carry a
kernel-checked channel-level certificate (Sec.~\ref{sec:diamond}); and that
the associated resource costs obey machine-checked bounds. An earlier version
of this paper recorded the algorithmic-synthesis refinement following
Kliuchnikov--Maslov--Mosca~\cite{KMM2013,KMM2013ancilla} and
Ross--Selinger~\cite{RossSelinger2016} over the ring
$\mathbb Z[\omega][1/\sqrt 2]$ as future work; that refinement has since been
carried out at the theory level and is reported in
Secs.~\ref{sec:compiler}--\ref{sec:diamond}. What remains \emph{not} in scope
is the engineering of a runnable compiler: the runtime gate-word-emitting
implementation, hardware-specific tuning for particular devices, certificate
formats, and constant-factor length optimization (e.g., tightening the
verified $50400\,k + 812$ word bound of Sec.~\ref{sec:kmm} toward practical
$T$-counts) are a separate engineering deliverable. One mathematical gap is
also stated plainly: the \emph{ancilla-free} unconditional form of
Ross--Selinger synthesis rests on a number-theoretic existence that is open
(Sec.~\ref{sec:rs}); we carry it as an explicit hypothesis, and the
ancilla-assisted route of Sec.~\ref{sec:kmm} is unconditional.

The development demonstrates a methodology as much as a result: by factoring
the alphabet-specific from the alphabet-agnostic and lifting the latter to
arbitrary dimension, the cost of verifying a new universal gate set is reduced
to discharging a single density witness, and the verified theory now spans
dimensions $2$, $4$, and $8$ and five physically distinct alphabets. The
two-mechanism $\su 8$ result and the unconditional Toffoli bound suggest that
formal verification is reaching the point where it can serve not only as a
correctness check on known compilation results but as a tool for settling
structural questions---such as the essentiality of a gate---that are easy to
state and surprisingly delicate to prove. The synthesis layer reinforces the
point from two directions: the formalization isolated the \emph{exact}
number-theoretic boundary of unconditional ancilla-free synthesis
(Sec.~\ref{sec:rs}), and it caught a mathematically impossible plan
($\mathbb Z[\sqrt 2][i]$ as a Euclidean domain, Sec.~\ref{sec:rsnt-appc})
before any effort was wasted on it.

\paragraph{Relationship to companion work.} The Fibonacci-universality and
topological/categorical foundations are developed in a companion bundle, which
this paper cites for the Fibonacci anchor and generalizes; the
fault-tolerant-computation substrate (codes, measurement, gauging) is developed
in a sibling bundle that consumes the quantitative Solovay--Kitaev developed
here as its universal-compilation primitive.
