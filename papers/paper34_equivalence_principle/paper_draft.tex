mechanisms: a vestigial-only violation surface in an emergent-gravity

\author{John G. Roehm}

\begin{abstract}
We classify six Phase 5x dark-matter--related mechanisms of an
emergent-gravity Anderson-Higgs--type Wilsonian (ADW) substrate by
which level of the equivalence principle (\WEP, \EEP, \SEP) they
violate. Two mechanisms violate \WEP\ and hence all three levels:
\emph{vestigial differential coupling} (vestigial phase: metric VEV
$\neq 0$, tetrad VEV $= 0$, so bosons and fermions couple to
different gravitational fields, with $\Delta_{\rm EP} = 1$ maximal
violation~\cite{VestigialGravityLean}) and \emph{vestigial relics}
(residual differential coupling in the full-tetrad phase from defect
remnants, $\eta \sim 10^{-18}$, satellite-EP-test detectable). The
remaining four mechanisms --- Fang-Gu torsion DM~\cite{FangGu2024},
fracton subdiffusion~\cite{Pretko2017}, SFDM Thomas-Fermi
condensate~\cite{Berezhiani2015}, and hidden-sector
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ singlets~\cite{HiddenSectorClassificationLean} ---
satisfy all three EP levels. The structural punchline: the project's
EP-violation surface is \emph{vestigial-only}. Vestigial-phase
in-phase coupling is already ruled out by current MICROSCOPE bounds
$|\eta| < 10^{-15}$~\cite{Touboul2017}; vestigial-relic-induced
violations at $\eta \sim 10^{-18}$ become testable in next-generation
satellite-EP missions (STEP-class). The classification ships in Lean
4.29 with zero new axioms, $24$ substantive theorems
(12 initial + 12 from a post-wave 6-pattern-audit strengthening
pass), and a Python mirror with $39$ pytest cross-checks pinning
the Lean theorems to numerical agreement. The Lean module
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/EquivalencePrinciple.lean} forces explicit
commitment per mechanism, surfacing structural tensions between
mechanisms that were previously prose-level.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction and motivation}
\label{sec:intro}

The equivalence principle (EP) is the foundational symmetry of
classical general relativity~\cite{Will2018}. It admits three
nested formulations:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Weak EP (\WEP)}: test masses follow the same trajectory
in a gravitational field, regardless of internal composition.
Violation parameter
$\eta = (a_1 - a_2) / \frac{1}{2}(a_1 + a_2)$.
The MICROSCOPE satellite mission~\cite{Touboul2017} sets
$|\eta| < 1.0 \times 10^{-15}$, the strongest current bound.
\item \textbf{Einstein EP (\EEP)}: \WEP\ + Local Lorentz
Invariance + Local Position Invariance.
\item \textbf{Strong EP (\SEP)}: \EEP\ + universal coupling of
gravitational self-energy (Nordtvedt parameter $\eta_N$),
tested by lunar laser ranging.
\end{itemize}
A mechanism that violates \WEP\ thereby violates \EEP\ and \SEP.
Conversely, a mechanism may violate \EEP\ while satisfying \WEP\
(via Lorentz-invariance violation in subsystems with degenerate
gravitational coupling), and similarly for \SEP\ alone. The
classification is therefore a partial commitment: each mechanism is
assigned a weakest-violated level (\texttt{None} if all three are
satisfied) and propagates upward.

In the Phase 5x dark-sector landscape of the emergent-gravity ADW
program, we have six mechanisms whose EP-violation status was
previously prose-level. This wave forces an explicit commitment per
mechanism, classifies the resulting 6$\times$3 matrix in Lean 4.29
(zero new axioms, all theorems decidable), and surfaces the
structural tension that EP violation is \emph{vestigial-only}.

\section{Mechanism enumeration and classification}
\label{sec:enum}

The six mechanisms classified are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item \texttt{vestigialDifferentialCoupling}. In the vestigial phase
(metric correlator $\neq 0$, tetrad VEV $= 0$~\cite{VestigialGravityLean}),
bosons couple minimally to the metric $g_{\mu\nu}$ while fermions
require a nonzero tetrad VEV $\langle e^a_\mu \rangle$ to couple to
gravity. The differential coupling parameter
$\Delta_{\rm EP} = 1 - \langle e^a_\mu \rangle / \sqrt{\langle e^a_\mu
e^b_\nu\rangle \eta_{ab}}$ takes the maximal value $\Delta_{\rm EP} = 1$
in the vestigial phase. Already ruled out at any current EP precision.
\item \texttt{vestigialReliscSTEPClass}. In the full-tetrad phase,
residual differential coupling from defect remnants gives
$\eta \sim 10^{-18}$~\cite{DarkSectorSynthesisW8}. Sub-MICROSCOPE,
testable in STEP-class satellite missions.
\item \texttt{fangGuTorsionTrace}. Boos-Hehl Einstein-Cartan
torsion-trace loop coupling. Per the W4 Phase 5x assessment, the
failure mode of FG-torsion as a DM candidate is kinematic
($w_{\rm FG} = 1/3 \neq 0$, hence not dust per
\texttt{fg\_cdm\_obstruction})~\cite{FangGu2024,FangGuLean}, not EP. The
torsion-trace loop coupling is universal across charged species, so
no species-dependent acceleration arises at tree level.
\item \texttt{fractonSubdiffusion}. Pretko dipole-conservation
enforced universal mobility decay~\cite{Pretko2017}. The
subdiffusion rate is identical for all matter species (uniform
charge-conservation constraint), so no \WEP\ violation.
\item \texttt{sfdmThomasFermi}. Berezhiani-Khoury single-field
condensate~\cite{Berezhiani2015} with uniform coupling to
gravity. The Thomas-Fermi profile is composition-independent;
phenomenology lives in galactic merger sonic-boom signatures, not
EP.
\item \texttt{hiddenSectorZ16Singlet}. SM-singlet DM candidates
(T-0, S-0, C-1) carrying the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ topological
charge but no SM gauge charge~\cite{HiddenSectorClassificationLean}.
Decoupled from visible-sector EP tests by construction.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Lean formalization}
\label{sec:lean}

The classification is shipped in
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/EquivalencePrinciple.lean}
($24$ substantive theorems + 1 module-summary marker,
$\sorrycount{}$ sorry, 0 new axioms; verified
\texttt{propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound} only on key
theorems via \texttt{lean\_verify}; several theorems are even
axiom-free since they discharge by \texttt{decide}/\texttt{rfl}
on the typed enumeration). Three EP levels
\texttt{WEP}, \texttt{EEP}, \texttt{SEP} are encoded as an inductive
type with a numerical-order projection $\texttt{epLevelOrder} : \texttt{EPLevel} \to \mathbb{N}$,
$\texttt{WEP} \mapsto 0 < \texttt{EEP} \mapsto 1 < \texttt{SEP} \mapsto 2$.
Six mechanisms are an inductive enumeration; the
$\texttt{violationLevel} : \texttt{EPMechanism} \to \texttt{Option EPLevel}$
function assigns each mechanism its weakest-violated level (or
\texttt{none}). The decidable predicates
$\texttt{violatesAt} : \texttt{EPMechanism} \to \texttt{EPLevel} \to \texttt{Bool}$
and $\texttt{satisfiesAt}$ implement the level propagation: a
\WEP-violator violates \WEP, \EEP, and \SEP\ via
$\texttt{epLevelOrder L}_0 \le \texttt{epLevelOrder L}$.

Substantive theorems include:
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{vestigialDifferentialCoupling\_violates\_WEP},
\texttt{vestigialReliscSTEPClass\_violates\_WEP} (the two violators).
\item \texttt{fangGuTorsionTrace\_satisfies\_all\_EP},
\texttt{fractonSubdiffusion\_satisfies\_all\_EP},
\texttt{sfdmThomasFermi\_satisfies\_all\_EP},
\texttt{hiddenSectorZ16Singlet\_satisfies\_all\_EP} (the four
non-violators).
\item \texttt{ep\_violation\_is\_vestigial\_only}: among the six
mechanisms, exactly the two vestigial-phase phenomena violate \WEP.
\item \texttt{vestigial\_microscope\_violation\_consistent}: the
6c.3 classification commits to the same conclusion as
\texttt{ClassificationTableDark.MicroscopeStatus.violated} for
vestigial gravity.
\item \texttt{vestigial\_vs\_fangGu\_distinct\_EP\_profile}: the
two mechanisms have distinct \texttt{violationLevel} values, so
they are distinct mechanisms by EP-status alone.
\end{itemize}

The post-wave strengthening pass (a 6-pattern-audit per the project's
post-wave-audit protocol) added 12 substantive theorems closing
three audit findings on the initial ship: (i) bundle redundancy
in the four \texttt{*\_satisfies\_all\_EP} 3-conjunct theorems
(replaced by single-claim \texttt{*\_has\_no\_violation} theorems
plus a shared \texttt{noViolation\_implies\_satisfiesAt} extraction
lemma); (ii) absence of quantitative numerical content tying the
Lean classification to the published $\eta$-bound constants
(closed by \texttt{vestigial\_phase\_eta\_violates\_microscope\_bound}

(closed by \texttt{fangGu\_failure\_mode\_is\_kinematic\_not\_ep},
which actually imports \texttt{FangGuTorsionDM} and calls

A Python mirror at \texttt{src/equivalence\_principle/} provides the

\section{Cross-mechanism tensions}
\label{sec:tensions}

The matrix surfaces three structural tensions:
\begin{itemize}
\item \emph{Vestigial-only violation surface.} Among six mechanisms
spanning four distinct DM physics families (vestigial gravity,
torsion, fracton, scalar-field, hidden-sector), only the two
vestigial-phase phenomena violate EP. This is the load-bearing
substantive content of the matrix.
\item \emph{Two distinct vestigial $\eta$ scales.} Within the
vestigial family, the in-phase mechanism ($\eta_{\max} = 1$, ruled
out) and the relic mechanism ($\eta \sim 10^{-18}$, STEP-detectable)
are at different scales. The MICROSCOPE bound ($\eta < 10^{-15}$,
horizontal reference line in Fig.~\ref{fig:matrix} right panel)
separates them: vestigial-phase coupling is already ruled out at
current precision, while vestigial relics require next-generation
satellite EP tests.
\item \emph{Cross-mechanism distinguishability.} The four
non-violating mechanisms are degenerate by EP-status alone (all
satisfy all three levels), so EP tests do not distinguish them
from one another --- distinguishability requires kinematic-EoS
($w_{\rm FG} = 1/3$ vs $w_{\rm fracton} = 0$ vs $w_\Lambda = -1$,
the W8 Phase 5x synthesis~\cite{DarkSectorSynthesisW8}), cosmological
core-profile signatures (W5/W7), or direct-detection floor (W2 +
W7 collective-invisibility theorem). EP tests \emph{do} distinguish
the vestigial family from the rest.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion and outlook}

The Wave 6c.3 classification consolidates four prior Phase 5x waves
(W4 FangGu, W5 SFDM, W7 fracton, W2 hidden-sector) plus
VestigialGravity (Phase 4 W2) under a single typed enumeration.
The output is a publishable formalization note (this paper) and
$24$ Lean theorems with zero new axioms. The matrix
forces explicit commitment per mechanism, ruling out the previously
ambiguous "EP-status unspecified" prose-level designation, and
surfaces the vestigial-only character of the project's EP-violation
surface.

The next-generation satellite EP test (STEP-class, $\eta \sim 10^{-18}$
target) is the empirical-detection hook for vestigial-relic-induced
violations~\cite{DarkSectorSynthesisW8}. A positive STEP-class
result at $\eta \sim 10^{-18}$ corroborates the vestigial-relics
mechanism; a null result at $\eta \lesssim 10^{-19}$ rules it out and
forces a Phase 6f-side reassessment. The four non-vestigial
mechanisms make no testable EP-prediction at any precision (their
EP-status is "satisfies"), so EP tests are agnostic to them.

Future Phase 6c work integrates this classification with Phase 6b
cosmological-perturbation constraints (different EP profiles can
produce distinct CMB-polarization signatures via differential
coupling of photons vs neutrinos to the substrate) and Phase 6f
Wave 3 energy-conditions infrastructure (mechanisms that violate
\SEP\ may also violate the dominant energy condition on
$T^{\mu\nu}_{\rm emerg}$, opening cosmological-singularity-theorem
loopholes).
