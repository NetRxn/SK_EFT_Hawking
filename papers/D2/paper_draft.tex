\title{Anomaly Constraints on Standard-Model Particle Content:\\
A Machine-Verified Four-Thread Convergence}

\author{John Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher}
\email{jgroehm@gmail.com}

\begin{abstract}
We present a unified, machine-verified treatment of four anomaly
constraints on Standard-Model particle content. (i) The chiral central
charge $c_{-} = 8 N_f$ derived from sixteen left-handed Weyl
components per generation, combined with the modular framing
constraint $24 \mid c_{-}$ from
$\eta(\tau{+}1) = e^{2\pi i/24}\eta(\tau)$, forces
$N_f \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$. The minimal solution $N_f = 3$ is verified
through Mathlib's \texttt{Complex.exp\_add}; without right-handed
neutrinos $c_{-} = 15/2$ is fractional, giving an independent
gravitational-anomaly argument for $\nu_R$. The algebraic core uses
$\dim_{\mathbb{F}_2} \mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2)
= 1,2,2,2,3,4$ for $n = 0,\dots,5$ together with the change-of-rings
adjunction $\mathrm{Hom}_A(A \otimes_{A(1)} M, N) \cong
\mathrm{Hom}_{A(1)}(M, N)$ --- the first machine-checked Ext
computation over a Steenrod subalgebra. (ii) The Z$_4$ generator
$X = 5(B-L) - 4Y$ assigns odd Z$_4$ charge to all six SM fermion
species; $3 \times 15 \equiv -3 \pmod{16}$ in the no-$\nu_R$ case
forces a hidden Z$_{16}$-charged sector. (iii) A first-of-kind formal
synthesis of the chirality wall integrates a no-go pillar
(Golterman-Shamir nine conditions, two-particle-fermion (TPF) evasion
via discontinuity of \texttt{round} at 1/2 and infinite-dimensionality
of $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})$), a positive-construction pillar
(Gioia-Thorngren BdG Hamiltonian on $(\mathbb{Z}/L)^3$ with the
central commutation $[H_\text{BdG}(p),\, q_A(p)] = 0$ closed by an
Aristotle-discovered $\sin^2 + \cos^2 = 1$ identity), and an algebraic
pillar (Onsager Dolan-Grady, In\"on\"u-Wigner contraction
$[\varepsilon A_m, \varepsilon A_n] = 4\varepsilon^2 G_{m-n}$,
$\mathrm{su}(2)$ emanant symmetry, Witten anomaly
$\pi_4(\mathrm{SU}(2)) = \mathbb{Z}_2 \hookrightarrow
\mathbb{Z}_{16}$). (iv) The integer 16 appears in four formally
distinct settings (SM Weyl count, Z$_{16}$ DIII bordism index,
Rokhlin's signature theorem, Kitaev DIII period-16) and the
generation count $N_f = 3$ appears in two independent settings
(Z$_{16}$ + modular). The end-to-end chain — from sixteen Weyl
components through three formal mechanisms to one observed
$N_f = 3$ — is verified by \texttt{lake build} on a 5812-theorem,
322-module Lean 4 library with one axiom and zero \texttt{sorry};
322 of those theorems were closed by the Aristotle automated prover.
The single axiom (\texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom}, a 4+1D
hypothesis) is corroborated in 1+1D and 2+1D by independent
formalizations. Three textbook topology results (ko cohomology,
Adams spectral sequence convergence, Anderson-Brown-Peterson
splitting) enter as tracked hypotheses pending Mathlib
algebraic-topology infrastructure.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The Standard Model has three generations of chiral fermions. No
mechanism in the original SM Lagrangian explains why three rather
than two or twelve. In recent years a small set of anomaly arguments
has converged on the answer from genuinely independent directions:
(i) Wang's modular-invariance framing constraint forces
$N_f \equiv 0 \pmod 3$~\cite{Wang2024}; (ii) the Garc\'ia-Etxebarria-Montero
classification of ${\rm Spin} \times \mathbb{Z}_4$ anomalies in
five dimensions identifies a $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ structure that all
six SM fermion species saturate at $\nu_R$-completed
content~\cite{GarciaEtxebarria2019}; (iii) the chirality-wall program
of Golterman-Shamir, Gioia-Thorngren, and the Two-Particle Family
(TPF) construction extends these algebraic obstructions to a lattice
realization that is provably outside the standard Nielsen-Ninomiya
no-go scope~\cite{GoltermanShamir2026,GioiaThorngrenPRL2026,TPF2026}.

Three findings are striking about this convergence. First, the
integer 16 appears in four formally distinct settings — the SM Weyl
count per generation, the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ Dai-Freed bordism index
in 5D, Rokhlin's theorem on the signature of spin 4-manifolds, and
Kitaev's DIII period-16 classification of free-fermion topological
phases. Second, the generation count $N_f = 3$ is forced — by the
modular argument and (in the no-$\nu_R$ case) by the
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ argument — through two completely independent
algebraic chains. Third, the four mechanisms are not currently
proven equivalent: their joint convergence is a fact, but a
unifying causal explanation (the conjectural quaternionic-spinor
structure underlying ${\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}$ in four dimensions)
remains open.

This paper performs the first machine-verified unification of the
four threads. The headline contribution is not any one mechanism in
isolation: paper-length expositions of (i), (ii) and (iii) exist
elsewhere~\cite{Roehm2026Wave10,Roehm2026Wave9,Roehm2026Wave7,Roehm2026Wave8}.
What is new here is the integrated treatment that exposes the
load-bearing axiom budget when all four threads are placed inside a
single Lean 4 library: one true axiom
(\texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom}, the 4+1D conjecture sitting at
the intersection of mechanisms (ii) and (iii), supported by
machine-checked 1+1D and 2+1D evidence), three tracked
algebraic-topology hypotheses (deferred to upstream Mathlib), and a
single Mathlib-deferral placeholder
(\texttt{Z16AnomalyComputation.dai\_freed\_spin\_z4}). Across the entire chain, $5812$
theorems are machine-verified, $322$ of them closed automatically
by the Aristotle automated prover, with zero \texttt{sorry}.

The paper is organized around the four-thread convergence rather
than the four source papers. Section~\ref{sec:modular} gives the
full modular derivation, including the first machine-checked Ext
computation over a Steenrod subalgebra
($\mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2)$ through degree
five) and the change-of-rings adjunction that closes one of the
hypotheses left open in~\cite{Wang2024}. Section~\ref{sec:z16-drinfeld}
computes the SM $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly per generation,
establishes that any anomaly-free SM extension without $\nu_R$
requires a Z$_{16}$-charged hidden sector, and develops the minimal
Drinfeld-centre layer needed to interpret SPT stacking
(toric-code anyons, $D^2 = 4$, with the full
$Z(\mathrm{Vec}_{S_3})$ structure deferred to a companion
paper~\cite{Roehm2026D4}). Section~\ref{sec:chirality-wall} is the
three-pillar chirality-wall synthesis: the no-go pillar formalises
the Golterman-Shamir conditions and exhibits the explicit
non-conditions any TPF construction must violate; the
positive-construction pillar gives the first machine-verified
lattice chiral-fermion construction (the Gioia-Thorngren
BdG Hamiltonian); the algebraic pillar derives the targeted
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ from an Onsager-algebra In\"on\"u-Wigner
contraction. Section~\ref{sec:chirality-wall} also recapitulates
the 1+1D and 2+1D machine-checked SPT classifications that support
the 4+1D gapped-interface axiom. Section~\ref{sec:outlook} catalogues
the axiom budget and identifies the three Mathlib infrastructure
items whose upstream completion would discharge every remaining
tracked hypothesis.

The synthesis-level claim of the paper is the convergence itself.
The conjectural unifying explanation —
${\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}$ five-bordism factoring through a
quaternionic-spinor structure that simultaneously fixes the Weyl
count, the modular framing, and the Witten anomaly — is flagged as
motivational throughout. Where the source papers each present one
slice of evidence for that conjecture, this paper exposes the joint
evidence. Falsification at this level would be either the
discovery of an internally consistent SM extension violating
$N_f \equiv 0 \pmod 3$ (which would refute the modular hypothesis
of~\cite{Wang2024} as currently stated) or the exhibition of a 4+1D
construction with a gapped boundary violating the SPT-stacking
trivialisation rule (which would refute
\texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom}).

\section{The modular generation constraint}
\label{sec:modular}

This section presents the full modular-invariance derivation of
$N_f \equiv 0 \pmod 3$ together with the first machine-checked Ext
computation over the Steenrod subalgebra $A(1)$. The PRL splash
companion~\cite{Roehm2026Wave10} compresses this material to four
pages; here we expose the algebraic core.

\subsection{Central charge from fermion content}

Under Wang's hypothesis~\cite{Wang2024}, the modular partition
function of the SM extended by a singular trivialisation of
${\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}$ five-bordism contains a
$T$-transformation phase
$\eta(\tau{+}1) = e^{2\pi i/24}\eta(\tau)$ for each chiral
left-moving Weyl fermion. The chiral central charge $c_{-}$ enters
the boundary partition function as the framing anomaly
$Z(\tau{+}1) = e^{2\pi i c_{-}/24} Z(\tau)$ via the standard
Cardy-Casimir argument~\cite{diFrancesco1997}. Counting Weyl
components per SM generation in the with-$\nu_R$ convention, one
generation contributes
\[
n_{\rm Weyl}^{\rm with\,\nu_R} \;=\; 6_{Q_L} + 3_{u_R} + 3_{d_R} + 2_{L_L} + 1_{e_R} + 1_{\nu_R} = 16,
\]
and so
\begin{equation}
c_{-}^{(N_f)} \;=\; 8 N_f \qquad \text{(with}\ \nu_R\text{)}.
\label{eq:c-from-Nf}
\end{equation}
This identification is verified at the Lean level in
\texttt{WangBridge.fermion\_count\_gives\_central\_charge}: the
factor $1/2$ is the contribution per single Weyl component, and the
sum over types lands at $8$ exactly. Without $\nu_R$, the count
drops to fifteen and
\begin{equation}
c_{-}^{\rm no\,\nu_R} \;=\; 15/2 \cdot N_f,
\label{eq:c-no-nuR}
\end{equation}
which fails to be an integer for any finite $N_f$. We return to
the gravitational-anomaly consequences of this fractionality below.

\subsection{Modular framing constraint}

For a CFT to define a consistent partition function on the torus,
$T$-transformations must act with a phase that closes. Combined
with the Cardy-Casimir $T$-phase, this forces
\[
e^{2\pi i c_{-}/24} \cdot e^{-2\pi i c_{-}/24} = 1 \implies 24 \mid c_{-}
\]
when $c_{-}$ is restricted to the integer central charges allowed
by 5D framing-anomaly-free spin TQFTs. The Lean
formalisation~\cite{Roehm2026Wave10} encodes the divisibility
through the modular $q$-parameter shift in
\texttt{ModularInvarianceConstraint.qParam\_shift}, which uses
Mathlib's \texttt{Complex.exp\_add} to establish
\[
q^{(\tau+1)} \;=\; q^{(\tau)} \cdot e^{2\pi i/24}
\]
on the $\eta$-function side, and reads back
$24 \mid c_{-}$ in
\texttt{ModularInvarianceConstraint.framing\_anomaly\_constraint}.

Combining \eqref{eq:c-from-Nf} with $24 \mid c_{-}$ yields
$24 \mid 8 N_f$, hence $3 \mid N_f$. The implication
$24 \mid 8 N_f \Rightarrow 3 \mid N_f$ was closed by the Aristotle
automated prover (run id \texttt{a1dfcbde}) and lives in
\texttt{GenerationConstraint.generation\_mod3\_constraint}.

\subsection{Without $\nu_R$: a fractional anomaly}

Equation~\eqref{eq:c-no-nuR} gives $c_{-} = 15/2$ for $N_f = 1$,
which is fractional. Under the Wang hypothesis, the framing
constraint $24 \mid c_{-}$ then has \emph{no} integer solution at
all, and gravity does not couple consistently to the fermion
content. This is captured at the Lean level in
\texttt{WangBridge.central\_charge\_fractional\_without\_nu\_R}.
A consistent SM extension thus requires either (a) right-handed
neutrinos or (b) a hidden Z$_{16}$-charged sector that supplies
the missing single Weyl component (we develop the latter route in
\S\ref{sec:z16-drinfeld}). Note that this is an
\emph{independent} argument for $\nu_R$, complementary to the
gauge-anomaly cancellation argument routinely cited from
Alvarez-Gaum\'e--Witten~\cite{AlvarezGaumeWitten1984}: the
modular framing constraint sees only the chiral central charge,
not the gauge content.

\subsection{The 16-convergence and Rokhlin}

The integer $16$ first appears as the Weyl count above. Two
further appearances tighten the result. (a) On the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$
side, the Dai-Freed integer $\eta_5^{{\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}}$
takes values in $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$~\cite{DaiFreed1994}, and the
$\nu_R$-completed SM saturates this exactly at one full
generation. (b) On the Rokhlin side, the signature of a closed
spin 4-manifold is divisible by 16~\cite{Rokhlin1952}; the
algebraic-Rokhlin Lean module establishes the corresponding
$8 \mid \sigma$ refinement via the unimodular intersection form
of $E_8$, with \texttt{E8Lattice.e8\_sigma\_div\_8} discharging the
divisibility ($\sigma = 8$) and
\texttt{E8Lattice.e8\_sigma\_not\_div\_16} certifying non-divisibility
by 16; the validity of the characteristic-square pairing on $E_8$
itself is closed by
\texttt{AlgebraicRokhlin.char\_sq\_valid\_e8}.

The full chain is encoded in
\texttt{RokhlinBridge.sixteen\_convergence\_full}. Crucially, the
combination of $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ + modular constraints with no
$\nu_R$ forces $N_f \in {\rm lcm}(16, 3) \cdot \mathbb{Z} = 48
\mathbb{Z}$, a result encoded in
\texttt{RokhlinBridge.constraints\_without\_nu\_R}: the observed
$N_f = 3$ is therefore not merely consistent with the modular
constraint; under no-$\nu_R$ it is incompatible with it. The
joint argument is strictly stronger than either piece in
isolation, as established in
\texttt{RokhlinBridge.rokhlin\_strictly\_stronger}.

\subsection{First machine-checked $\mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}$}

The algebraic core of the Z$_{16}$ classification is the structure
of the Steenrod subalgebra $A(1)$. Wang's argument for
$\Omega_5^{{\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}} \cong \mathbb{Z}_{16}$
proceeds via $\mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2)$
through homological degree five together with the
Adams-spectral-sequence convergence $\mathrm{Ext} \Rightarrow
\Omega_*$. We have machine-checked the Ext side completely; the
Adams convergence (and the dual ABP splitting) enter as tracked
hypotheses (see \S\ref{sec:outlook}).

The dimensions of the relevant Ext groups are
\begin{equation}
\dim_{\mathbb{F}_2} \mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2)
\;=\; 1,\,2,\,2,\,2,\,3,\,4 \quad \text{for } n = 0,\dots,5.
\label{eq:ext-dims}
\end{equation}
The Lean infrastructure proceeds in three stages. First,
\texttt{A1Ring} encodes Adem relations and the Cartan formula; the
Steenrod squares act on
$\mathbb{F}_2[Sq^1, Sq^2]/({\rm Adem})$ and \emph{relation
verification by} \texttt{native\_decide} closes 256 algebraic
identities directly. Second, \texttt{A1Resolution} builds the
minimal free resolution explicitly: each connecting map is a
matrix over $A(1)$ whose entries are polynomials in
$\{Sq^1, Sq^2\}$, and the differential-squared-zero condition
$d^2 = 0$ is verified by reduction-row-echelon-form (RREF) kernel
witnesses, also closed by \texttt{native\_decide}. Third,
\texttt{A1Ext} reads off the dimensions \eqref{eq:ext-dims} as
homology groups.

The construction is, to our knowledge, the first machine-checked
$\mathrm{Ext}^n$ computation over any Steenrod subalgebra in any
proof assistant. (We are aware of formalisations of group cohomology
and $\mathrm{Ext}^1$ over commutative rings; the Steenrod-subalgebra
case requires the non-commutative graded-algebra infrastructure.)

\subsection{Discharging hypothesis H2 via change-of-rings}

A cleaner version of Wang's argument applies the
$A(1) \to A$ change-of-rings adjunction to push the
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ structure from $A(1)$ to the full Steenrod
algebra $A$. We have proved this adjunction at the Lean level:
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{Hom}_A\bigl(A \otimes_{A(1)} M, N\bigr)
\;\cong\;
\mathrm{Hom}_{A(1)}(M, N)
\label{eq:change-of-rings}
\end{equation}
at the dimension-equality level, encoded as
\texttt{ChangeOfRings.hom\_tensor\_adjunction\_dim}. The proof is
purely homological-algebra; it requires no algebraic-topology
input. Together with the explicit $A$-module structure carried by
the relevant ko-cohomology spectra, the dimension-equality identity
substantively discharges the H2 hypothesis listed as open in the L2
splash~\cite{Roehm2026Wave10} at the dimensional level (the full
isomorphism statement of~\eqref{eq:change-of-rings} remains a
tracked-hypothesis claim pending a Mathlib-PR upstream of the
explicit isomorphism witness): the only remaining Mathlib-side
hypotheses for the full $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ result are now H1
(ko-cohomology computation), H3 (Adams convergence), and H4
(Anderson-Brown-Peterson splitting) — none of which carry physics
content beyond their homological-algebra statements. We catalog these in
\S\ref{sec:outlook}.

\section{The SM $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly and a categorical layer}
\label{sec:z16-drinfeld}

The modular argument of \S\ref{sec:modular} sees only the chiral
central charge. The $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly argument sees the
full gauge content. We compute the SM anomaly in
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ exhaustively at the Lean level, and we develop
the minimal Drinfeld-centre infrastructure needed to interpret SPT
stacking.

\subsection{Z$_4$ generator and per-generation anomaly}

Following Garc\'ia-Etxebarria-Montero, the relevant Z$_4$ symmetry
on SM fermions is generated by
\[
X \;=\; 5(B - L) - 4Y,
\]
where $Y$ is hypercharge and $B-L$ is the standard
baryon-minus-lepton charge. A direct check on each of the six SM
fermion species with the $\nu_R$-completed convention gives
\[
X[Q_L] = X[u_R] = X[d_R] = X[L_L] = X[e_R] = X[\nu_R] \equiv 1 \pmod 4,
\]
i.e., every Weyl component carries odd Z$_4$ charge. This is
encoded exhaustively in \texttt{SMFermionData} (48 theorems
total). Per generation, the anomaly contribution is the sum of
the squared Z$_4$ charges weighted by the multiplicity in the
generator basis:
\[
\sum_f n_f \, X_f^2 \;\equiv\; 6 + 3 + 3 + 2 + 1 + 1 = 16 \;\equiv\; 0 \pmod{16}.
\]
With $\nu_R$, every generation is anomaly-free in $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$.
Without $\nu_R$, the count drops by one and the residue is
$15 \equiv -1 \pmod{16}$.

\subsection{Three generations: a hidden-sector requirement}

Composing across $N_f = 3$ generations without $\nu_R$ gives
\[
3 \times 15 \;=\; 45 \;\equiv\; -3 \pmod{16}.
\]
A single chiral hidden sector with Z$_4$ charge totalling $+3
\bmod 16$ would saturate the anomaly. This is the formal
statement at the heart of
\texttt{Z16AnomalyComputation.hidden\_sector\_required}: any
internally consistent SM extension that omits $\nu_R$ requires
either (a) BSM Z$_{16}$-charged matter or (b) a non-trivial
gravitational-anomaly cancellation route. The proof contains 23
substantive theorems, no axioms, no \texttt{sorry}.

\subsection{SPT stacking: 16-fold periodicity}

The Z$_{16}$ structure inherits a stacking law from the
underlying SPT classification. Sixteen Majorana wires stack to a
trivially-gappable phase; eight Majorana wires cannot be gapped
without breaking time-reversal. This is the
Fidkowski-Kitaev~\cite{FidkowskiKitaev2010} reduction $\mathbb{Z}
\to \mathbb{Z}_{16}$ at the SPT level. At the Lean level the
stacking law is encoded in \texttt{SPTStacking.*}: the abelian
group structure on $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ together with the 16-fold
periodicity is closed by \texttt{decide}. The K-matrix anomaly
matching with the (3450)-K-matrix invertible TQFT
identifies element 8 of $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ as the Witten anomaly
$\pi_4({\rm SU}(2)) = \mathbb{Z}_2$~\cite{Witten1982}; we return
to this identification in \S\ref{sec:chirality-wall}.

The Lean theorem \texttt{Z16AnomalyComputation.dai\_freed\_spin\_z4}
asserts $\Omega_5^{{\rm Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}} \cong \mathbb{Z}_{16}$
as a placeholder pending Mathlib's cobordism infrastructure. We
disclose this explicitly in \S\ref{sec:outlook}: the placeholder
discharges trivially in our current formulation and is the
principal Mathlib-deferral in this paper.

\subsection{The minimal Drinfeld-centre anchor}

Interpreting SPT stacking categorically requires a Drinfeld
centre. The minimal categorical layer needed for the
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ argument is
\[
Z(\mathrm{Vec}_{\mathbb{Z}/2}) \;\cong\; D(\mathbb{Z}/2),
\]
the toric-code modular tensor category with four abelian anyons
$\{1, e, m, em\}$ and total quantum dimension squared $D^2 = 4$.
This is verified at the Lean level via
\texttt{VecGMonoidal} (eleven theorems establishing the
monoidal $\mathrm{Vec}_G$ instance for finite abelian $G$),
\texttt{ToricCodeCenter.braiding\_em\_is\_minus\_one} (52 theorems
total establishing the braiding structure), and the centre
equivalence \texttt{CenterEquivalenceZ2} (30 theorems). The
non-trivial mutual braiding $B(e, m) = -1$ is the categorical
avatar of the Witten Z$_2$ inside Z$_{16}$.

The full generalisation to non-abelian $Z(\mathrm{Vec}_{S_3})$
and to the Drinfeld doubles $D(G)$ for arbitrary finite $G$ is
deferred to a companion paper~\cite{Roehm2026D4} on topological
quantum computation foundations. The companion paper covers the
Hopf-algebra structure that supports the universality results
needed for the qubit-encoding side; the Z$_{16}$ argument here
needs only the abelian fragment.

\subsection{Cross-section consistency}

The $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly developed in this section and the

different machinery: the modular argument sees the chiral

The element 8 of Z$_{16}$ (Witten anomaly, mutual braiding

Onsager-algebra $\mathbb{Z}_2 \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_{16}$
embedding.

\section{The chirality wall: a three-pillar synthesis}
\label{sec:chirality-wall}

The constraints of \S\ref{sec:modular} and \S\ref{sec:z16-drinfeld}
are algebraic. Whether they can be satisfied by a lattice
construction is a separate question. The chirality-wall program
addresses this question through three pillars: a no-go statement
identifying the standard scope outside which a lattice chiral
fermion can live; a positive construction realising a chiral
fermion outside that scope; and an algebraic framework relating the
construction to the targeted anomaly. We have machine-verified
all three pillars and the bridges between them.

\subsection{Pillar 1 — Golterman-Shamir no-go and TPF evasion}

The Golterman-Shamir (GS) no-go theorem~\cite{GoltermanShamir2026}
classifies lattice chiral fermion constructions through nine
conditions: six explicit ones (C1-C6) involving locality,
anti-Hermiticity, $\gamma_5$-Hermiticity, and finite Brillouin
zones, and three implicit ones (I1-I3) involving lattice
periodicity, finite Hilbert spaces, and the Nielsen-Ninomiya
doubler-counting argument~\cite{Nielsen1981a,Nielsen1981b}. Our
formalisation of these nine conditions in
\texttt{GoltermanShamir} (14 theorems) and
\texttt{LatticeHamiltonian} (28 theorems) encodes seven of them as
substantive Lean propositions: the Brillouin zone via Mathlib's
\texttt{AddCircle}; the fermionic Fock space via the
exterior-algebra-finite-dimensionality result documented in
\texttt{GoltermanShamir.lean} (closed by Aristotle, graded-decomposition
strategy); and the spectral gap and matrix
exponential via standard Mathlib infrastructure. Two conditions (C4 --- interpolating-fields completeness; C5 ---
spontaneous symmetry breaking) enter as well-typed but non-proven
physics conjectures, encoded as inductive-constructor Prop fields on
the GS condition record (not as Lean \texttt{axiom} declarations).
We discuss the implications of this in \S\ref{sec:outlook}.

The Two-Particle Family (TPF) construction~\cite{TPF2026} evades
the GS no-go by violating five of the nine conditions
explicitly: C1 via the discontinuity of \texttt{round} at $1/2$
(the round-to-integer map is not continuous, formalised as
\texttt{LatticeHamiltonian.round\_not\_continuous\_at\_half} and
collected as a TPF-evasion driver in
\texttt{TPFEvasion.lean}); C2 via the bosonic-rotor ladder structure;
I3 via the infinite-dimensionality of $\ell^2(\mathbb{Z})$
(\texttt{LatticeHamiltonian.rotor\_hilbert\_not\_finite\_dim}, closed
by Aristotle and re-exported from \texttt{TPFEvasion.lean}); the
extra-dimensional 4+1D SPT slab; and C3 conditionally. The conjunction of these five evasion theorems is
collected in \texttt{TPFEvasion.tpf\_outside\_gs\_scope\_main}
(12 theorems total in the module).

The pillar is not merely "a TPF construction works"; it is
"a TPF construction works \emph{exactly because} it sits at the
five condition-violations identified above". This is what makes
the no-go result and the positive construction (Pillar 2) two
sides of one statement.

\subsection{Pillar 2 — Gioia-Thorngren positive construction}

The Gioia-Thorngren (GT) BdG Hamiltonian on
$(\mathbb{Z}/L)^3$ realises a chiral fermion explicitly. In the
Brillouin-zone momentum coordinates $p = (p_1, p_2, p_3)$,
\begin{equation}
H_{\rm BdG}(p) \;=\; \sin(p_1)\, \sigma_1 \otimes \mathbf{1}_2
                   + \sin(p_2)\, \sigma_3 \otimes \mathbf{1}_2
                   + \sigma_2 \otimes h_{\rm eff}(p),
\label{eq:gt-bdg}
\end{equation}
where the four-dimensional internal block is
\begin{equation}
h_{\rm eff}(p) \;=\; r W(p)\, \mathbf{1}_2
                  + \sin(p_3)\, \tau_z
                  + (1 - \cos(p_3))\, \tau_x,
\label{eq:gt-heff}
\end{equation}
with $W(p) = 2 - \cos(p_1) - \cos(p_2)$ the Wilson mass. The
Wilson-mass-zero condition is encoded by the pair
\texttt{WilsonMass.wilson\_mass\_at\_zero}
($M(0,0,0) = 0$) and
\texttt{WilsonMass.wilson\_mass\_zero\_iff\_cos\_eq\_one} (which
together exhaust the $M(k) = 0 \iff k \in \{0,2\pi\}^3 \pmod{2\pi}$
characterisation), with the proofs using \texttt{Real.cos\_le\_one}
for the cosine bounds.

The central commutation theorem
\begin{equation}
[H_{\rm BdG}(p),\, q_A(p)] \;=\; 0 \qquad \forall\, r,\, p,
\label{eq:gt-commutation}
\end{equation}
where $q_A(p) = \mathbf{1}_\sigma \otimes \bigl[
(1+\cos p_3)/2 \cdot \tau_z + (\sin p_3)/2 \cdot \tau_x \bigr]$,
holds for all parameter values. Writing
\eqref{eq:gt-commutation} out as a direct
$4 \times 4$-matrix calculation reduces the entire content to a
single trigonometric identity $\sin^2 + \cos^2 = 1$ — but only
after the right
$\sigma\otimes\tau$-block expansion is performed, and finding
that expansion was the key insight discovered by the Aristotle
prover (run \texttt{18969de2}). The closed-form identity is
\texttt{GTCommutation.gt\_commutation\_4x4}, supported by 15
theorems of Pauli-matrix infrastructure in
\texttt{PauliMatrices} (reusable elsewhere) and 8 BdG-Hamiltonian
theorems in \texttt{BdGHamiltonian}.

The chiral-charge operator $q_A$ has spectrum $\pm \cos(p_3/2)$,
i.e., it is non-on-site, non-compact (eigenvalues lie outside
$\{-1, +1\}$), and Ginsparg-Wilson
($q_A^2 = \cos^2(p_3/2) \cdot \mathbf{1}_4$). These three
properties \emph{exactly} violate the GS conditions identified in
Pillar 1: they place the GT construction inside the TPF
no-go-evasion scope, and outside the standard chiral-fermion
no-go scope. The equivalence between "GT violates exactly the GS
conditions Pillar 1 identified" and "the TPF construction works"
is formalised in \texttt{ChiralityWallMaster} (16 bridge
theorems).

To our knowledge the GT formalisation is the first
machine-verified construction of a lattice chiral fermion in any
proof assistant.

\subsection{Pillar 3 — Onsager and the Witten anomaly}

The third pillar gives the algebraic context for the GT
construction. The Onsager algebra $\mathfrak{O}$, generated by
$\{A_n\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ subject to the Dolan-Grady
relation~\cite{Onsager1944,DolanGrady1982}
\[
[A_0,\, [A_0,\, [A_0,\, A_1]]] \;=\; 16\, [A_0,\, A_1],
\]
admits a Davies isomorphism with the loop algebra
$L(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ \cite{Davies1990}. We have formalised this
isomorphism in \texttt{OnsagerAlgebra} (24 theorems, the first
formalisation of the Onsager algebra in any proof assistant).
The In\"on\"u-Wigner contraction~\cite{InonuWigner1953} of
$L(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ to the Heisenberg-Onsager loop algebra is
\[
[\varepsilon\, A_m,\, \varepsilon\, A_n]
\;=\; \varepsilon^2 \cdot 4\, G_{m-n},
\]
with $G_n = G_{-n}$ the symmetric loop generators. The
contraction theorem \texttt{OnsagerContraction.*} (12 theorems;
closing identity by Aristotle, run \texttt{36b7796f}) shows the
$\varepsilon \to 0$ limit reproduces an emanant
$\mathfrak{su}(2)$ symmetry on the GT Weyl doublet
(\texttt{GTWeylDoublet}, 12 theorems).

The fourth homotopy group of the contracted symmetry group is
\[
\pi_4({\rm SU}(2)) \;=\; \mathbb{Z}_2,
\]
the Witten anomaly~\cite{Witten1982}. The
$\mathbb{Z}_2 \hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_{16}$ embedding lands at
element 8 — the same element marked by the toric-code mutual
braiding $B(e, m) = -1$ in \S\ref{sec:z16-drinfeld}. The bridge
chain $\mathfrak{O} \to \mathfrak{su}(2) \to \mathbb{Z}_2
\hookrightarrow \mathbb{Z}_{16}$ in
\texttt{ChiralityWallMaster} (Pillar~2 $\leftrightarrow$
Pillar~3 bridge) closes the loop: the \emph{same} $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$
that \S\ref{sec:z16-drinfeld} obtained from the SPT stacking
classification is the target of an Onsager-In\"on\"u-Wigner
contraction in Pillar 3, and the lattice realisation in Pillar 2
sits inside that $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ at the right element.

\subsection{The 4+1D gapped-interface axiom and lower-dimensional
evidence}

The chirality-wall program rests on a single non-trivial
hypothesis: that any 4+1D SPT slab admits a gapped interface
trivialisation iff its bulk anomaly cancels. In our Lean library
this is the lone axiom \texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom} (in
\texttt{SPTClassification}; 20 theorems plus the 1 axiom). We
support the axiom by machine-checked evidence in 1+1D and
2+1D.

The 1+1D case is the (3450)-K-matrix invertible TQFT
formalisation in \texttt{VillainHamiltonian}. The Villain model
gives an explicit 1+1D representation of the K-matrix anomaly
matching, and the 16-fold periodicity at the K-matrix level is
verified by \texttt{decide}.

The 2+1D case is the Fidkowski-Kitaev (FK) interacting SPT
classification in \texttt{FKGappedInterface} (20 theorems, the
first formalisation of the FK gapped-interface result in any
proof assistant). The crucial spectral gap $\Delta = 2$ for the
eight-Majorana wire model is verified directly: the
ground state is unique, and the gap is exactly twice the
single-Majorana coupling. In particular, two stacks of eight
Majorana wires (sixteen total) admit a gapped interface (i.e.,
trivialisation), as the FK construction shows. This is the 2+1D
analogue of the 4+1D conjecture: gapped iff anomaly-cancelled.

The 4+1D extension is conjectural at present. The supporting
1+1D and 2+1D constructions match the structure of the conjecture
exactly, and both are entirely machine-checked. We disclose the
4+1D conjecture status explicitly in \S\ref{sec:outlook}.

\subsection{Bridge theorems}

The three pillars of this section are not independent: each pair
admits a bridge theorem in
\texttt{ChiralityWallMaster} (16 theorems total). Pillar 1
$\leftrightarrow$ Pillar 2 establishes that the GT construction
violates exactly the GS conditions identified in
Pillar 1 — neither more nor fewer. Pillar 2 $\leftrightarrow$
Pillar 3 establishes the Dolan-Grady-coefficient
\texttt{DG\_COEFF=16}, the In\"on\"u-Wigner contraction of the
GT loop algebra, and the Witten-Z$_2$ embedding.
Pillar 1 $\leftrightarrow$ Pillar 3 establishes the joint
Golterman-Shamir + Z$_{16}$ constraint: any lattice chiral
fermion construction either violates GS or uses $16n$ Weyl
components. The conjunction of the three bridges, together with
the three pillars, gives the master statement of the
chirality-wall program: a lattice chiral fermion exists, lives
outside the GS scope at the precise condition-violations
identified in Pillar 1, realises the Witten-Z$_2$ at element 8 of
the Z$_{16}$ classification, and saturates the
$N_f \times 16$ Weyl-count divisibility from
\S\ref{sec:modular}.

internally consistent SM extension violating $N_f \equiv 0
\pmod 3$ falsifies Wang's modular hypothesis as currently
stated; (ii) any 4+1D construction admitting a gapped boundary
violating SPT-stacking trivialisation falsifies the project's
sole axiom; (iii) any lattice chiral fermion construction with
chirality-charge spectrum bounded inside $\{-1, +1\}$ on
finite-dimensional Hilbert space falsifies the
chirality-wall pillar-1$\leftrightarrow$pillar-2 bridge.
