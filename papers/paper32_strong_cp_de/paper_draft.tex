QCD-topological dark-energy mechanism in Lean 4.29 as a bridge between
the strong-CP problem and the cosmological-constant scale. The
prediction $\rhoDE \sim \Lqcd^6/\MP^2 \approx (1.4\,\text{meV})^4$
contains \emph{no free parameters}: at the PDG QCD scale
$\Lqcd = 0.1$~GeV and $\MP = 1.22 \times 10^{19}$~GeV, the prediction
sits within $\sim 240\times$ of the observed $\rhoDE = (2.3\,\text{meV})^4
\approx 2.8 \times 10^{-11}$~eV$^4$ — well within the 3-orders-of-magnitude
window characterizing the Zhitnitsky claim and $\sim 120$ orders below
the Planck-natural $\MP^4$ estimate. The QCD $\theta$-angle is encoded
as a structure with the load-bearing neutron-EDM invariant
$|\theta| \le 10^{-9}$ (Pendlebury et al.\ PRD 92, 2015); a falsifier
theorem rules out Planck-natural $\theta = 1$. The anomaly-matching
chain Z$_{16}$ $\leftrightarrow$ strong-CP $\leftrightarrow$
cosmological-$\Lambda$ ships as a 3-conjunct tracked-hypothesis Prop
\verb|IsAnomalyMatchingCompatible| with a witness (CP-symmetric vacuum
at PDG $\Lqcd$) and a falsifier (Planck-natural $\theta$). The Phase
6c.1 \emph{correctness-push}
(\verb|H_BothActiveGivesInconsistency|) shows that BOTH the Zhitnitsky
mechanism AND a residual q-theory DE contribution being simultaneously
active produces $\rho > 10^{-10}$~eV$^4$ ($> 3\times$ observed),
identifying the project's required \emph{yielding}: commit to one DE
mechanism, not both. The module ships $11$ substantive Lean theorems
with $0$ sorry and $0$ new axioms, a Python mirror with $14$ pytest
cross-checks, and substantive cross-bridges to
\verb|Z16AnomalyComputation| (calls
\verb|sm_anomaly_with_nu_R|) and
\verb|ModularInvarianceConstraint|.

\section{Motivation: anomaly-chain DE bridge}
\label{sec:motivation}

Phase 5x identified two parallel mechanisms for sourcing the observed
dark-energy density: (a) Klinkhamer--Volovik q-theory equilibrium
relaxation~\cite{KlinkhamerVolovik2010}, formalized in Phase 5y closure
modules; and (b) Van Waerbeke--Zhitnitsky 2025 topological QCD-vacuum
contribution~\cite{VanWaerbekeZhitnitsky2025}. Both predict
$\rhoDE$ at scales close to the observed value $(2.3\,\text{meV})^4$
without fine-tuning. The phase-6c bridge makes this concrete: under
which conditions does the strong-CP $\theta$-vacuum source the
observed $\rhoDE$? The answer is: \emph{only if $\theta$ is dynamically
small} (consistent with the neutron-EDM bound $\theta \le 10^{-9}$).

This wave ships the formal bridge: $\theta$-vacuum, Zhitnitsky DE
prediction, anomaly-matching chain, and the correctness-push
inconsistency for both-mechanism activation.

\section{Zhitnitsky DE structural form}
\label{sec:zhitnitsky}

The Zhitnitsky mechanism predicts
\begin{equation}
  \rhoDE \sim \frac{\Lqcd^6}{\MP^2}
  \label{eq:zhitnitsky}
\end{equation}
in eV$^4$ units. With $\Lqcd^6$ in GeV$^6$ converted via
$10^{54}\,(\text{eV/GeV})^6$ and $\MP^2 = (1.22 \times 10^{19}\,\text{GeV})^2
= 1.49 \times 10^{56}\,\text{eV}^2$, the conversion factor is
\begin{equation}
  \rhoDE \approx 6.71 \times 10^{-3}\,\Lqcd^6\quad
  \text{(eV}^4 \text{ per GeV}^6\text{)}.
\end{equation}
At $\Lqcd = 0.1$~GeV (PDG QCD scale), this gives
$\rhoDE \approx 6.71 \times 10^{-9}\,\text{eV}^4$ — within $\sim 240$ of
the observed $\rhoDE = 2.8 \times 10^{-11}\,\text{eV}^4$, well under the
3-orders-of-magnitude window characterizing the claim of
``no-free-parameters match''. The Lean theorem
\verb|zhitnitsky_DE_at_lambda_qcd_within_3_orders| pins this with a
\verb|norm_num|-backed comparison, and
\verb|zhitnitsky_DE_far_below_planck| confirms the prediction lies
$\sim 120$ orders below the Planck-natural $\MP^4 \approx 10^{112}\,\text{eV}^4$
estimate.

the Zhitnitsky mechanism (this wave) AND a residual q-theory DE
contribution (Phase 5y) cannot simultaneously source the observed
$\rhoDE$. The Lean tracked hypothesis is
\begin{verbatim}

  rho_combined > 1e-10
\end{verbatim}
A concrete falsifier theorem
\verb|combined_zhitnitsky_qtheory_exceeds_observation| shows that ANY
positive q-theory contribution combined with Zhitnitsky at PDG $\Lqcd$
exceeds the threshold $10^{-10}\,\text{eV}^4$ — Zhitnitsky alone gives
$\sim 6.7 \times 10^{-9}$, $\sim 240\times$ observed, and any positive
addition makes the combined exceed observation by even more.

The proof body uses the load-bearing
\verb|zhitnitsky_DE_at_lambda_qcd_within_3_orders| numerical bound. The
identification: the project must commit to ONE DE mechanism. The data
favor Zhitnitsky (no free parameters; matches order of magnitude); the
q-theory contribution must be sub-leading or absent.
