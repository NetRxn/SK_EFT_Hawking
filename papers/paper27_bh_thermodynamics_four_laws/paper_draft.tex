\begin{abstract}
We formalize the Bardeen--Carter--Hawking four laws of black-hole
mechanics in two regimes of an emergent-gravity ADW substrate, separated
by a critical mass $\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf)$. Above $\Mc$, the BH
follows the textbook Schwarzschild signature: $\Thawk = \hbar/(8\pi M)$
(Hawking 1975~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}), so during evaporation
$dM/dt < 0$ and $d\Thawk/dt > 0$ (heats as evaporates), with
finite evaporation time $t_{\rm evap} \sim M^3$. Below $\Mc$, the BH
follows the BEC-acoustic backreaction profile of Balbinot, Fagnocchi,
Fabbri (PRD~71, 064019 (2005),
arXiv:gr-qc/0405098~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}): linear-in-$t$ cooling
$\Thawk(t) = (\hbar c / 2\pi) \kappa [1 - (563/720\pi)\,\varepsilon\, \kappa^3 c
A_0 t]$ with extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$, hence asymptotic $\Thawk \to 0$
in infinite time --- the analog of near-extremal Reissner--Nordstr\"om
evaporation. The genuine regime partition is the
\emph{sign-flip of $d\Thawk/dt$ during Hawking-radiation evaporation}:
positive in the Schwarzschild branch, negative in the BEC-acoustic
branch. We ship the regime-partition criterion and the four-laws
bundles in tracked-hypothesis mode (Outcome-3). The substrate-response
coefficient ansatz $\dADW = (\aADW - 1)\Ladw$ vanishes in the bare
Sakharov--Adler limit and provides four falsifiability tests for any
candidate ADW substrate. The Lean formalization
(\texttt{BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean}) ships \bhThermoTotal{} theorems,
\sorrycount{} sorry, 0 new axioms. The full second law uses
Glorioso--Liu SK-EFT entropy-current monotonicity
(arXiv:1612.07705)~\cite{GloriosoLiu2018} without invoking pointwise NEC.
The ADW-substrate-specific partition critical mass
$\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf)$ is original to this project;
no published derivation exists.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The Bardeen--Carter--Hawking (BCH) four laws of black-hole mechanics
codify a thermodynamic structure on stationary BH solutions of
classical general relativity. Quantum field theory on these
backgrounds endows the surface gravity $\kappa$ with a thermal
interpretation as the Hawking temperature $\Thawk = \hbar\kappa/(2\pi)$
(Hawking 1975~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}), and the resulting
heat-capacity sign $C(M) = (dM/d\Thawk)$ is negative for
Schwarzschild --- the BH \emph{heats} as it loses mass to
Hawking evaporation, with finite evaporation time $t_{\rm evap}
\propto M_0^3$.

Analog/emergent-gravity systems offer a structurally different
evaporation pattern. In the BEC-acoustic system of Balbinot,
Fagnocchi, Fabbri~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD},
backreaction of thermally radiated phonons on the fluid background
\emph{shrinks} the sonic horizon and \emph{decreases} the temperature
in time, with linear-in-$t$ leading order
\begin{equation}
\Thawk(t) = \frac{\hbar c}{2\pi}\,\kappa \left[
  1 - \frac{563}{720\pi}\,\varepsilon\,\kappa^{3}\,c\,A_{0}\,t
\right]
\label{eq:Tsonic}
\end{equation}
and asymptotic extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$ giving $\Thawk \to 0$
at \emph{infinite} time --- the analog of near-extremal
Reissner--Nordstr\"om. Crucially, Ref.~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}
itself \emph{contrasts} this with the moving-domain-wall analog
in $^3$He-A studied by Jacobson and Koike~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002}
(cond-mat/0205174), which has \emph{non}-vanishing end-temperature
of the evaporation process and therefore behaves more like
Schwarzschild than near-extremal RN.

This paper formalizes both regimes within a single Lean module
\texttt{BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean} and ships the
sign-of-$d\Thawk/dt$ regime partition as the load-bearing
correctness-push theorem. We do \emph{not} attempt to derive a
single closed-form $\Thawk(M)$ that interpolates between the regimes ---
instead, we treat the existence of a critical mass
$\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf)$ separating them as a
\emph{tracked-hypothesis} bundle $H_{\rm RegimePartition}$, with
each branch's evaporation-sign claim grounded in its respective
primary literature. The choice of $\Mc$ is the dimensional ansatz
$\Mc = (\Nf \Ladw) / (12\pi \aADW)$ implied by Sakharov--Visser
$1/G_N \propto \Nf \Lambda^2$ at the Wave-1 scale; this is project-
original and explicitly disclosed.

\section{Setup: \texttt{BHData}, \texttt{ADWParams}, and the regime classifier}
\label{sec:setup}

The Lean module declares two structures and one inductive type. The
\texttt{BHData} structure carries bulk-thermodynamic observables
$(M, Q, J, A, r_h, \Thawk, \kappa)$ with positivity hypotheses for the
dimensional quantities; the MTC structure for the horizon Hilbert
space is supplied separately via Wave 3's \texttt{HorizonMTCBC}.
The \texttt{ADWParams} structure carries substrate parameters
$(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf, \xivest)$ with strict positivity for each.

The \texttt{Regime} inductive type has three constructors:
\texttt{Schwarzschild}, \texttt{Boundary}, and \texttt{ADWExtremality}.
The \texttt{ADWExtremality} constructor name is retained from the
initial Wave 5 ship for backward bibliographic continuity, but the
post-rewrite physics interpretation is the BEC-acoustic regime per
Balbinot 2005~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}.

The \emph{decidable} regime classifier \texttt{classify} is defined
piecewise:
\begin{equation}
\texttt{classify}\,(b, p) = \begin{cases}
  \texttt{Schwarzschild}     & \text{if } b.M > \Mc(p) \\
  \texttt{ADWExtremality}    & \text{if } b.M < \Mc(p) \\
  \texttt{Boundary}          & \text{if } b.M = \Mc(p),
\end{cases}
\label{eq:classify}
\end{equation}
where the critical mass $\Mc(p)$ is the dimensional ansatz
\begin{equation}
\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf) =
\frac{\Nf \cdot \Ladw}{12\pi \cdot \aADW}.
\label{eq:Mc-default}
\end{equation}
This is consistent with the Sakharov--Visser
$1/G_N \propto \Nf \Lambda^2$ scaling but is \emph{not} a quoted
equation in any primary ADW paper (Akama 1978, Diakonov 2011, the
Wetterich tetrad-condensation series, Volovik~2022/2024); it is
ours and is explicitly flagged as such in the \texttt{H\_RegimePartition}
bundle (Sec.~\ref{sec:hreg}).

The classifier admits three iff-theorems
(\texttt{classify\_*\_iff}) discharging each constructor against the
mass comparator. Theorem \texttt{M\_c\_pos} establishes
$\Mc(p) > 0$ for all valid \texttt{ADWParams} via positivity of all
input fields.

\section{Two-regime $\Thawk$ profiles: Hawking 1975 and Balbinot 2005}
\label{sec:profiles}

The Lean module supplies two non-trivial Hawking-temperature functions,
each anchored to its primary source.

\subsection{Schwarzschild branch (Hawking 1975)}

For $b.M > \Mc(p)$, the textbook Hawking 1975 result~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}
applies in natural units $\hbar = 1$:
\begin{equation}
\Thawk^{\rm Schw}(M) = \frac{1}{8\pi M}.
\label{eq:Tsch}
\end{equation}
We prove \texttt{T\_H\_schwarzschild\_pos} ($M > 0 \Rightarrow \Thawk > 0$)
and \texttt{T\_H\_schwarzschild\_strict\_decreasing}
($M_1 < M_2 \Rightarrow \Thawk^{\rm Schw}(M_2) < \Thawk^{\rm Schw}(M_1)$).
Combined with the standard Hawking-emission energy balance
$dM/dt < 0$ during evaporation, this establishes
$d\Thawk/dt > 0$ in the Schwarzschild branch ---
the load-bearing primary-source-grounded sign claim.

\subsection{BEC-acoustic branch (Balbinot 2005)}

For $b.M < \Mc(p)$, the BEC-acoustic backreaction analysis of
Balbinot, Fagnocchi, Fabbri~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}
applies. Their Eq.~(Tsonic) [Eq.~\ref{eq:Tsonic}] gives a strictly
decreasing $\Thawk(t)$ under Hawking-phonon backreaction, with the
extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$ ensuring $\Thawk \to 0$ asymptotically
in \emph{infinite} time --- preserving Israel's third law (Sec.~\ref{sec:third}).

The leading-order exponential approximation
\begin{equation}
\Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t) = \Thawk^{(0)} \cdot \exp(-t/\tau_{\rm cool})
\label{eq:T-acoustic}
\end{equation}
matches the project's existing Python anchor
\texttt{src/wkb/backreaction.py} (line 449), which evolves the full
coupled-ODE system of~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD} but uses
Eq.~\ref{eq:T-acoustic} as the leading-order analytic form. We
prove \texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_pos}
($\Thawk^{(0)} > 0 \Rightarrow \Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t) > 0$),
\texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_at\_zero}
($\Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(0) = \Thawk^{(0)}$), and the load-bearing
\texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_strict\_decreasing}
($t_1 < t_2 \Rightarrow \Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_2) < \Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_1)$
when $\Thawk^{(0)} > 0$ and $\tau_{\rm cool} > 0$). The strict
monotone-decay theorem is the BEC-acoustic regime's primary-source-
grounded sign claim.

\subsection{The contrast case: Jacobson--Koike 2002}

It is essential to note that the moving-domain-wall analog black hole
in $^3$He-A studied by Jacobson and Koike~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002}
gives a structurally different result. Their Eq.~(13)
$\Thawk(v) = \Thawk^{(0)}(1 - v^2/c_\perp^2)$
(with $v$ the soliton velocity) is monotonically decreasing in $v$,
but Hawking-radiation back-reaction \emph{slows} the soliton
($dE/dt = -bv^n$, $E = \mu v^2/2$, hence $dv/dt < 0$),
which makes $\Thawk$ \emph{increase} in time --- heating, not cooling.
This regime is structurally similar to Schwarzschild, not to
near-extremal Reissner--Nordstr\"om. Reference~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}
itself notes the distinction:
``\emph{other analog models like a thin film of $^3$He-A with a
moving domain wall [Jacobson] seem to show a non-vanishing
end-temperature of the evaporation process}.''
We cite~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002} and \cite{JacobsonVolovik1998} only
as the contrast case, not as the cooling anchor.

\section{Tracked-hypothesis bundle $H_{\rm RegimePartition}$}
\label{sec:hreg}

The load-bearing tracked hypothesis is the bundle
\begin{align}
H_{\rm RegimePartition}\,(b, p, \Thawk^{(0)}, &dT/dt|_{\rm evap}, \delta) := \nonumber \\
\text{conjunction of five fields:} \nonumber
\end{align}
\begin{description}
\item[\texttt{M\_c\_form\_consistent}:] $\Mc(p) > 0$.
\item[\texttt{T\_H0\_pos}:] $\Thawk^{(0)} > 0$.
\item[\texttt{evap\_dT\_dt\_above}:]
$b.M > \Mc(p) \;\Rightarrow\; 0 < dT/dt|_{\rm evap}$
(Schwarzschild heating sign per Hawking
1975~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}).
\item[\texttt{evap\_dT\_dt\_below}:]
$b.M < \Mc(p) \;\Rightarrow\; dT/dt|_{\rm evap} < 0$
(BEC-acoustic cooling sign per Balbinot
2005~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD} Eq.~Tsonic).
\item[\texttt{delta\_consistent\_with\_ansatz}:]
$\delta = (\aADW - 1) \Ladw$ (deep-research \S9 ansatz).
\end{description}

The rate $dT/dt|_{\rm evap}$ enters as an
\emph{externally-supplied real parameter}, not an existential.
This avoids the $\exists$-absorption anti-pattern: a witness
$dT/dt|_{\rm evap}$ supplied to the bundle must satisfy the
sign-claim consistent with the regime, rather than being existentially
discharged by any sign that happens to be present in the substrate.
The five fields are mutually independent in natural Mathlib.

The correctness-push theorem
\texttt{regime\_partition\_criterion} combines $H_{\rm RegimePartition}$
with the regime classifier:
\begin{align}
\text{classify}(b,p) = \texttt{Schwarzschild} &\;\Rightarrow\;
0 < dT/dt|_{\rm evap}, \nonumber \\
\text{classify}(b,p) = \texttt{ADWExtremality} &\;\Rightarrow\;
dT/dt|_{\rm evap} < 0.
\label{eq:regime-partition}
\end{align}
This is the \emph{sign-flip-at-$\Mc$} theorem, with both branches
primary-source-grounded.

The substantive corollary
\texttt{delta\_ADW\_nonzero\_iff\_alpha\_ADW\_ne\_one} establishes
$\delta \neq 0 \;\Leftrightarrow\; \aADW \neq 1$, with the proof using
$\Ladw > 0$ from \texttt{ADWParams.Lambda\_pos} as a load-bearing field.

\section{Second law: Glorioso--Liu SK-EFT, no NEC}

\texttt{entropy\_divergence\_nonneg} ($0 \leq$ \texttt{entropy\_div},

The Crossley--Glorioso--Liu constitutive
relations~\cite{CrossleyGloriosoLiu2017,GloriosoCrossleyLiu2017}

replaces the classical Hawking 1971 area-non-decrease theorem (which

\section{Falsifier theorems}
\label{sec:falsifiers}

The Lean module ships four falsifier theorems, each anchored to a
primary-source-grounded prediction. All four are non-tautological in
the strict sense: each derives a contradiction or a sign-claim on a
function whose conclusion follows from the strict-monotonicity or
strict-positivity of theorems already proved in the module
(\texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_strict\_decreasing} for falsifier
1, \texttt{H\_RegimePartition.evap\_dT\_dt\_above} for falsifier 2,
the $\min$ argument over the two-sided thresholds for falsifier 3,
the substrate's $\Ladw > 0$ field for falsifier 4).

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Acoustic-decay form falsifier}
  (\texttt{falsifier\_acoustic\_decay\_form}): given a candidate
  $\Thawk^{\rm alt}(t)$ that matches Balbinot's
  $\Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_1)$ at time $t_1$ AND fails to strictly
  decrease by time $t_2$ ($\Thawk^{\rm alt}(t_1) \leq
  \Thawk^{\rm alt}(t_2)$), the candidate must DISAGREE with
  $\Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_2)$. The proof uses the strict-decreasing
  theorem to derive a contradiction.
\item \textbf{Schwarzschild-heating sign falsifier}
  (\texttt{falsifier\_schwarzschild\_heating}): given the
  $H_{\rm RegimePartition}$ bundle and a Schwarzschild-regime
  classification, an observed non-positive $dT/dt$ contradicts the
  bundle's \texttt{evap\_dT\_dt\_above} sign claim
  (Hawking 1975~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}). Conclusion is \texttt{False}.
\item \textbf{Third-law-form falsifier}
  (\texttt{falsifier\_third\_law\_form}): given a strong-Israel
  predicate (approach time unbounded as $\varepsilon \to 0$) and a
  Kehle--Unger predicate (approach time bounded above for small
  enough $\varepsilon$), the two are mutually inconsistent; the proof
  takes $\varepsilon = \min(\varepsilon_0, \varepsilon_{\rm th})$
  where both predicates apply and derives a contradiction. Conclusion
  is \texttt{False}.
\item \textbf{$\aADW$-dependence falsifier}
  (\texttt{falsifier\_alpha\_ADW\_dependence}; renamed from the
  initial-ship name \texttt{falsifier\_chi\_vest\_dependence} per a
  Stage-13 finding flagging that the deep-research \S9 ansatz
  $\dADW = (\aADW - 1)\Ladw$ depends on $\aADW$ and $\Ladw$, not
  directly on $\xivest$). The conclusion $0 < (\aADW - 1)\Ladw$ uses
  both $\aADW > 1$ and the substrate's $\Ladw > 0$ field; a
  measurement of opposite sign falsifies the ansatz.
\end{enumerate}
