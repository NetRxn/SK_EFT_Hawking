\title{Hayden--Preskill structural quantum error correction on a
modular-tensor-category horizon substrate: a Lean~4 formalization}

\author{John G. Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher; \texttt{NetRxn137@gmail.com}}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We formalize, in the Lean~4 proof assistant, the structural content of
the Hayden--Preskill (2007) information-recovery
protocol~\cite{HaydenPreskill2007} on the modular-tensor-category
(MTC) horizon-boundary-condition substrate previously developed for
black-hole microstate counting in this project. The formalization
defines two information-theoretic observables on top of the existing
horizon data: a code-distance proxy
$\dC := \log\dmax$ identifying the topological-shielding scale of an
encoded logical qubit, and a Hayden--Preskill scrambling-time bound
$\Tscr := \log\Dsq = \log\sum_a d_a^2$ giving the substrate-only part
of $(\beta/2\pi)\,S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ after the area law is pulled out.
The central structural theorem proves that positive code distance is
equivalent to non-abelian fusion ($\dmax > 1$) and that admissibility
of the substrate forces a positive scrambling-time bound, via the
sum-bound $\Dsq \ge \dmax^2$. The Fibonacci substrate is the concrete
non-abelian witness ($\dC = \log\varphi \approx 0.481 < \log 2$), and
the singleton (vacuum-only) substrate is the falsifier. Hayden--Preskill
recovery at the scrambling-time bound is established universally over
encoding choices, by case-splitting on the encoding anyon's quantum
dimension. The module ships ten substantive theorems with zero
\texttt{sorry} statements and no new axioms beyond the kernel's three.
The full holographic-dictionary side --- AdS/CFT spectrum identification
and explicit Yoshida--Kitaev decoder construction~\cite{YoshidaKitaev2017}
--- is intentionally deferred.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The Hayden--Preskill protocol~\cite{HaydenPreskill2007} establishes
that information thrown into an old (more-than-half-evaporated) black
hole can be recovered by an observer with access to the early Hawking
radiation, on a scrambling timescale
$t_{\mathrm{scr}} \simeq (\beta/2\pi)\log S_{\mathrm{BH}}$. This
result, together with subsequent work on holographic quantum error
correction~\cite{AlmheiriDongHarlow2015,PYHP2015}, established a
quantum-information-theoretic interpretation of black-hole horizons
that has become foundational to modern approaches to the information
puzzle.

Anyonic substrates --- modular tensor categories arising in fault-
tolerant topological quantum computation~\cite{KitaevAnyons2003,
NayakAnyons2008} --- provide a natural setting for the bulk side of
this story. Topological entanglement entropy~\cite{KitaevPreskill2006}
identifies the universal sub-leading area-law contribution as
$\gamma = \log D$, where $D = \sqrt{\sum_a d_a^2}$ is the total
quantum dimension. Recent project work uses the abstract MTC
horizon-boundary-condition data --- per-object positive quantum
dimensions, an attainable maximum $\dmax$, and the Immirzi tuning
parameter --- as the substrate for emergent black-hole microstate
counting.

This paper takes that horizon-boundary-condition data and asks: what
information-theoretic content can be derived from it directly, without
invoking the full holographic dictionary? Two natural observables
emerge:

\begin{itemize}
\item A \emph{code-distance proxy} $\dC := \log \dmax$ measuring the
fusion-channel entropy that shields an encoded logical qubit from
boundary errors.
\item A \emph{scrambling-time bound} $\Tscr := \log \Dsq$ measuring
the substrate-only part of the Hayden--Preskill scrambling time.
\end{itemize}

The structural connection between them --- positive code distance
forces positive scrambling time --- is the load-bearing content of the
formalization. We do not derive the AdS/CFT dictionary or construct
explicit decoders; the Yoshida--Kitaev protocol~\cite{YoshidaKitaev2017}
remains outside the scope. What we do is encode the structural
correspondence between admissibility (in the sense of supporting
fault-tolerant logical operation) and substrate non-trivialty in a
form that is mechanically checkable.

\section{Hayden--Preskill code structure}
\label{sec:hpcode}

The Hayden--Preskill code on an MTC horizon boundary condition is
encoded as
\begin{verbatim}
structure HPCode where
  horizon : HorizonMTCBC
  encoding_obj : Fin horizon.num_objects
\end{verbatim}
augmenting the substrate with a single \emph{encoding anyon} --- the
simple-object choice carrying the encoded logical qubit. The MTC
categorical data ($S$, $T$ modular matrices; $F$, $R$ braided data) is
not required at this abstract level; concrete witnesses live in the
Fibonacci instance \texttt{fibonacciHorizonBC} (used for the
witness theorems below) and the singleton instance
\texttt{trivialAbelianHorizonBC} (used for the falsifier).

\section{Code distance and scrambling time}
\label{sec:dC_tscr}

The two structural observables are
\begin{align}
\dC &\;:=\; \log\dmax, \\
\Tscr &\;:=\; \log\Dsq \;=\; \log\sum_a d_a^2.
\end{align}
The first reuses the area-law leading coefficient as the
topological-shielding scale of the encoded qubit: a localized
boundary error must traverse $\dC$ units of fusion-channel entropy
before disturbing the logical operator. The second is the
substrate-only Hayden--Preskill scrambling-time lower bound (in
dimensionless units, absorbing $\beta/2\pi$); after the area law
$S_{\mathrm{BH}} = (A/4G_N) + \log\Dsq$ is pulled out, the
$\log\Dsq$ term is the part of the scrambling time that lives on
the MTC alone.

The minimal substantive content needed to upgrade $0 < \Dsq$ to
$1 \le \Dsq$ ships as the lemma \texttt{one\_le\_globalDimSq}: the
unit object's contribution $d_{\mathrm{unit}}^2 = 1$ to the sum
gives the lower bound, via a one-line application of single-element
Finset bounds. This unlocks $0 \le \Tscr$ via monotonicity of the
logarithm.

\section{Admissibility theorem}
\label{sec:admissibility}

The wave's named structural anchor is
\begin{verbatim}
theorem
  code_distance_scaling_matches_
   anyonic_fusion_iff_fusion_in_
   admissible_class :
  (0 < H.codeDistance <-> 1 < H.horizon.d_max)
  AND
  (0 < H.codeDistance ->
   0 < H.scramblingTimeBound)
\end{verbatim}
Both halves are load-bearing. The biconditional connects the
information-theoretic observable $\dC > 0$ to the structural
fusion-class condition $\dmax > 1$ via $\log_{>0}$ monotonicity. The
forward implication shows that admissible substrates ($\dmax > 1$)
force a positive scrambling-time bound, via $\Dsq \ge \dmax^2$ (the
sum bound from the attainment of $\dmax$ as a quantum dimension) and
the strict inequality $1 < \dmax \Rightarrow 1 < \dmax^2 \le \Dsq
\Rightarrow 0 < \log\Dsq$.

\section{Hayden--Preskill recovery}
\label{sec:recovery}

Information encoded on \texttt{encoding\_obj} is recoverable from
boundary radiation of accumulated entropy $B$ when
\begin{equation}
\log d_{\mathrm{encode}} \;\le\; B,
\end{equation}
i.e.\ when the boundary radiation has accumulated at least the
log-quantum-dimension of the encoding alphabet. The substantive
result is universal recoverability at the scrambling-time bound:

\paragraph*{Theorem
(\texttt{recovery\_at\_scrambling\_bound}).}
For every \texttt{HPCode}, recovery is information-theoretically
possible when boundary entropy reaches the substrate scrambling-time
bound: $\log d_{\mathrm{encode}} \le \log \Dsq$.

The proof case-splits on $d_{\mathrm{encode}} \ge 1$ versus
$d_{\mathrm{encode}} < 1$. In the first branch,
$d_{\mathrm{encode}} \le d_{\mathrm{encode}}^2 \le \Dsq$ (using
$1 \le d_{\mathrm{encode}}$ and the sum bound), so logarithm
monotonicity gives the result. In the second branch,
$\log d_{\mathrm{encode}} \le 0 \le \log \Dsq$ directly.

The Fibonacci substrate is the concrete non-abelian witness
($d_\tau = \varphi$, $\dC = \log\varphi \approx 0.481 < \log 2$).
The quantitative bound \texttt{fibonacci\_HPCode\_codeDistance\_lt\_log\_two}
proves $\dC < \log 2$ via $\varphi < 2 \Leftrightarrow \sqrt{5} < 3
\Leftrightarrow 5 < 9$, locating Fibonacci as the minimal
non-abelian admissible MTC by code-distance.

The scrambling-time witness
\texttt{fibonacci\_HPCode\_scramblingTimeBound\_pos} forwards the
substrate's positive code distance through the admissibility
theorem to obtain $\Tscr > 0$, making the admissibility theorem
load-bearing.

The singleton-substrate falsifier
\texttt{trivialAbelian\_violates\_admissibility} disposes of the
$\dmax = 1$ MTC: the proof invokes the admissibility biconditional
to extract $1 < 1$, contradicting irreflexivity.

\section{Cross-bridge to the substrate hypothesis}
\label{sec:bridge}

The substantive cross-bridge
\texttt{horizon\_BC\_implies\_HP\_admissible} takes a hypothesis
instance of the project's
\texttt{H\_HorizonBoundaryCondition} structural assumption (which
captures the area-law / second-law / non-abelian envelope on a
horizon-MTC boundary condition) and returns $1 < \dmax$. The proof
body invokes the structural assumption's area-law field
($0 < \log\dmax$) and rewrites it via logarithm-positivity. This
ensures the named cross-reference is not phantom: the body literally
calls the assumption's method and uses its content as the substantive
step.

\section{Formalization summary}
\label{sec:formalization}

The Lean module
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/QECHolographyBridge.lean}
ships ten substantive theorems, zero \texttt{sorry} statements, and
zero new axioms beyond the kernel's three. The Python mirror in
\texttt{src/qec\_holography/} provides numerical evaluation across
four concrete MTC substrates (Fibonacci, Ising, $SU(3)_{k=2}$
sub-sector, singleton) and a 30-case \texttt{pytest} suite pinning
the Lean theorems to numerical agreement.

\section{What is and is not done here}
\label{sec:scope}

The formalization deliberately stays on the structural side. It
does not:

\begin{itemize}
\item Identify which conformal field theory on the boundary
corresponds to a given MTC; the AdS/CFT spectrum dictionary is not
constructed.
\item Construct an explicit decoder for the Hayden--Preskill recovery
in the sense of Yoshida--Kitaev~\cite{YoshidaKitaev2017}; the
existence of recovery at the scrambling-time threshold is the
information-theoretic statement we prove, not the algorithm that
realizes it.
\item Reproduce the Page curve quantitatively; the formalization
records the threshold but not the dynamics.
\end{itemize}

These are natural extensions but require additional infrastructure
(higher-form-symmetry machinery, replica-trick formalization, and
holographic-bulk constructions) that we leave for future work.
