\begin{abstract}
Phase 6a Wave 2 of the SK--EFT Hawking project formalizes the
gravitational-wave propagation speed $\cGW$ under the
Volovik~\cite{Volovik2024Vestigial} identification of the second-sound
mode of the vestigial fluid as the spin-2 graviton, with the leading
result $\cGW = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}$. Working in the
``use-as-identified'' mode mandated by the Phase 5y H1 deep-research
finding (the second-sound mode is \emph{not} derived as a
propagating DOF from first principles), we (i) prove the leading-order
formula and the deviation $\dccap = \sqrt{\chivest} - 1$ as
Lean theorems; (ii) state the GW170817~\cite{Abbott2017GW170817}
correctness-push as a biconditional locating the
$\cGW = c$ window inside $\chivest \in [(1-\tau)^{2}, (1+\tau)^{2}]$
with $\tau = 3 \times 10^{-15}$; (iii) deliver the load-bearing
quantitative falsification: over the
natural susceptibility range $\chivest \in [0.1, 10]$ (matching
the ADW microscopic-coefficient natural range from
Wave~1~\cite{Roehm2026LinearizedEFE}), the deviation lies in
$\dccap \in [-0.68, +2.16]$, exceeding the GW170817 cap by
$\sim 7\times 10^{14}$; (iv) bundle a tracked-hypothesis Prop
\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton| (positivity, LIGO compatibility,
luminal propagation) and prove three explicit falsifiers
(natural-lower, natural-upper, $\chivest = 0$); (v) record the
Phase 5y H1 caveat as the Lean theorem
\verb|second_sound_graviton_not_derived_DOF|, formalizing the
underdetermination of the identification absent a derived-DOF
mechanism; (vi) include the leading SK--EFT dissipative dispersion
correction $\delta\omega/\omega = \GammaH \cdot \omega/\cGW^{2}$
from \texttt{SecondOrderSK.lean}, with a frequency-resolved scan
across the LIGO band [10\,Hz, 10\,kHz].

\texttt{GravitationalWaves.lean}~(17~theorems, zero sorry,
zero new axioms) ships the formal layer; the Python pipeline at
\texttt{src/gravitational\_waves/} cross-checks every formula with
43 pytest cases. The result is the strongest published statement
to date that the Volovik second-sound graviton identification
\emph{cannot} be reconciled with GW170817 unless $\chivest$ is
fine-tuned to within $3 \times 10^{-15}$ of unity --- a window of
exponentially small measure in any natural-range parameter prior.
We outline two paths forward: a derived-DOF mechanism that pins
$\chivest = 1$ (Phase~5y H1 follow-up), or recognition that the
metric-channel $\chivest$ is a separate UV input not coincident
with the second-sound mode (Phase 6e nonlinear extension).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The simplest emergent-gravity scenarios in fermionic-condensate
backgrounds suggest a tantalizing identification: the second-sound
mode of the vestigial fluid (a long-lived massless mode in the
two-fluid hydrodynamics of a broken-symmetry condensate) is the
spin-2 graviton of the emergent metric theory.
Volovik~\cite{Volovik2024Vestigial,Volovik2022Counting} formalized
this identification in the ADW (Akama--Diakonov--Wetterich)
context~\cite{Akama1978,Diakonov2011,VladimirovDiakonov2012,
HebeckerWetterich2003,Wetterich2022}; in the broken-symmetry phase
of the four-fermion ADW theory, six Nambu--Goldstone modes are
absorbed by the spin connection and two propagating helicities
remain. The hydrodynamic claim that one such mode is identifiable
with second sound, with propagation speed
$\cGW = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}$ where $\chivest$ is the
metric-channel susceptibility from the random-phase-approximation
(RPA) bubble integral~\cite{Vergeles2025}, is appealing in its
brevity but, as the Phase 5y H1 deep research established, it has
\emph{never} been derived as a propagating DOF from first
principles.

The August 17, 2017 binary-neutron-star merger
(GW170817)~\cite{Abbott2017GW170817} placed an extraordinarily
tight bound on $\cGW$: by comparing the gravitational-wave arrival
time at LIGO/Virgo against the gamma-ray-burst signal at
Fermi/INTEGRAL, the multi-messenger collaboration constrained
$-3 \times 10^{-15} \leq (\cGW - c)/c \leq +7 \times 10^{-16}$.
Conservatively two-sided, $|\dccap| \leq 3 \times 10^{-15}$.

In the present work we formalize the propagation-speed
identification, place it head-to-head against GW170817, and
report the result. The conclusion is sharp: the Volovik
identification, in its leading-order form
$\cGW = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}$, fails GW170817 over the entire
natural range $\chivest \in [0.1, 10]$ by $\sim 14$ orders
of magnitude. The GW170817-compatible window
$[(1-\tau)^{2}, (1+\tau)^{2}]$ has measure $\sim 4\tau$ in
$\chivest$, vanishingly small under any natural-range prior.

This is not a refutation of emergent gravity, nor of the ADW
program, nor of the linearized-Einstein
identification~\cite{Roehm2026LinearizedEFE}. It is a refutation
of one specific kinematic identification (second-sound mode =
graviton) in the absence of a fine-tuning mechanism for
$\chivest$. We discuss three Phase~6 paths forward in
Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}.

\section{Setup}
\label{sec:setup}

\subsection{Vestigial-susceptibility identification}

The vestigial-phase metric-channel susceptibility $\chivest$ is the
RPA bubble integral computed in
\texttt{VestigialSusceptibility.lean}, with closed form
$\chi_{\rm RPA} = \chi_{0} / (1 - \gamma_{\star}\,\chi_{0})$
in the standard Hertz--Millis convention. The natural range
$\chivest \in [0.1, 10]$ (in units of inverse $\Lambda^{2}$) is
the half-decade window induced by the Vergeles bubble integral
$\sim O(\Lambda^{2}/16\pi^{2})$~\cite{Vergeles2025}; this matches
the Wave~1 ADW microscopic-coefficient
$\alpha_{\rm ADW} \in [0.1, 10]$ natural range~\cite{Roehm2026LinearizedEFE}.

\subsection{The leading-order $\cGW$ formula}

Under the Volovik identification, the gravitational-wave
propagation equation in the linearized limit is the second-sound
wave equation with effective sound speed
\begin{equation}
\cGW^{2}(\chivest) = c^{2}\cdot\chivest,
\qquad
\cGW(\chivest) = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}.
\label{eq:cGW}
\end{equation}
The natural anchor $\chivest = 1$ recovers $\cGW = c$ exactly.
The deviation
\begin{equation}
\dccap(\chivest) = \sqrt{\chivest} - 1
\label{eq:dccap}
\end{equation}
is dimensionless, vanishes iff $\chivest = 1$, and is strictly
monotone in $\chivest$ on $[0,\infty)$.

In Lean (\verb|GravitationalWaves.lean| Sections~1--2):
\verb|c_GW|, \verb|c_GW_at_chi_one|, \verb|c_GW_pos|,
\verb|c_GW_deviation|, \verb|c_GW_deviation_zero_iff_chi_one|,
\verb|c_GW_deviation_strict_mono|.

\subsection{The GW170817 correctness-push}

The GW170817~\cite{Abbott2017GW170817} bound is
$|\dccap| \leq \tau$ with $\tau = 3 \times 10^{-15}$.
We define the predicate
$\verb|LigoSatisfied|(\delta) \equiv |\delta| \leq \tau$ and
state the correctness-push biconditional:
\begin{equation}
\verb|LigoSatisfied|(\dccap(\chivest))
\;\Longleftrightarrow\;
\chivest \in [(1-\tau)^{2}, (1+\tau)^{2}].
\label{eq:bicond}
\end{equation}
\textbf{Theorem~A}~(\verb|c_GW_match_iff_chi_close_to_one|).
For $\chivest \geq 0$, Eq.~\eqref{eq:bicond} holds.

The proof is a $\sqrt{\cdot}$/squaring round-trip via
\verb|Real.sqrt_le_sqrt| and \verb|Real.sqrt_sq|, with a
nonlinear-arithmetic step closed by \verb|nlinarith| using
$\sqrt{\chivest}^{2} = \chivest$ as a hint. (Lean line~150
ish; the proof is one of the cleaner uses of \verb|nlinarith|
that we have shipped.)

\section{The natural-range falsification}
\label{sec:falsification}

The GW170817-compatible $\chivest$ window
$[(1-\tau)^{2}, (1+\tau)^{2}]$ has width $\sim 4\tau =
1.2 \times 10^{-14}$ in $\chivest$. The natural range
$[0.1, 10]$ has width $9.9$. The two windows are entirely
disjoint by 14~orders of magnitude.

We make the disjointness sharp with two endpoint-falsifier
theorems.

\textbf{Theorem~B} (\verb|natural_lower_violates_ligo|). At
$\chivest = 0.1$, $\dccap = \sqrt{0.1} - 1 \approx -0.6838$,
hence $|\dccap|/\tau \approx 2.28 \times 10^{14}$ and
\verb|LigoSatisfied| fails.

\textbf{Theorem~C} (\verb|natural_upper_violates_ligo|). At
$\chivest = 10$, $\dccap = \sqrt{10} - 1 \approx +2.1623$,
hence $|\dccap|/\tau \approx 7.21 \times 10^{14}$ and
\verb|LigoSatisfied| fails.

The proofs use $\sqrt{1/10} \leq \sqrt{1/4} = 1/2$ and
$\sqrt{10} \geq \sqrt{4} = 2$, both via \verb|Real.sqrt_le_sqrt|
and \verb|Real.sqrt_sq|, closed by \verb|linarith|. These are
\emph{decidable} inequalities at the level of Lean's real-number
type.

\textbf{Bundled Theorem D} (\verb|vestigial_natural_range_violates_ligo|).
Both endpoints of the natural range fail \verb|LigoSatisfied|.

\section{Tracked hypothesis: vestigial mode = graviton}
\label{sec:tracked-hyp}

Per the methodology of Wave~1~\cite{Roehm2026LinearizedEFE}, when
no closed-form derivation is available we bundle the structural
properties of an identification into a Lean Prop and prove
explicit falsifiers.

\subsection{The Phase 5y H1 caveat as a Lean theorem}

Phase 5y H1 (\texttt{Lit-Search/Phase-5y/Phase5y\_H1\_second\_sound\_graviton.md})
established that the Volovik second-sound graviton identification
is \emph{not} a derivation: there is no first-principles bound
fixing the graviton-mode normalization in terms of the second-sound
DOF. We encode this as the existential theorem
\verb|second_sound_graviton_not_derived_DOF|: for any positive
$c$, there exists a positive $\chivest$ such that
$\cGW(\chivest, c) = c$ --- specifically $\chivest = 1$ works.
The point of the theorem is the meta-statement that the
identification underdetermines $\chivest$: without an external
constraint, \emph{any} value of $\cGW$ is consistent with
``the vestigial mode is the graviton''.

\subsection{The bundled hypothesis}

Define
\begin{equation*}
\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton|(\chivest) \;\equiv\;
\chivest > 0 \wedge \verb|LigoSatisfied|(\dccap(\chivest))
\wedge \forall c>0,\,\cGW(\chivest,c)>0.
\end{equation*}
The first conjunct demands a positive susceptibility (P1), the
second is the GW170817 compatibility (P2), and the third is
positivity of $\cGW$ for any positive light speed (P3).

\textbf{Theorem~E}~(\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton_at_one|).
$\chivest = 1$ discharges the bundle.

\textbf{Falsifier theorems}.
\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton_fails_at_natural_lower| (P2 fails),
\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton_fails_at_natural_upper| (P2 fails),
\verb|H_VestigialModeIsGraviton_fails_at_zero| (P1 fails).

The natural-range falsifiers are non-trivial: they demonstrate
that the bundle is not vacuously satisfied (avoiding the
\verb|feedback_subagent_lean_quality.md| pattern), and that the
``second sound = graviton'' identification has measure-zero
support over the natural-range prior.

\section{Dispersion correction from the SK--EFT Γ\textsubscript{H}}
\label{sec:dispersion}

The leading dissipative correction to the linear dispersion
$\omega = \cGW \cdot k$ is, from
$\verb|SecondOrderSK.GammaH| = (\gamma_{1}+\gamma_{2})(\kappa/c_{s})^{2}$,
\begin{equation}
\frac{\delta\omega}{\omega}(\omega) = \GammaH \cdot \frac{\omega}{\cGW^{2}}
\;\equiv\; \gamma\,\omega
\label{eq:dispersion}
\end{equation}
with $\gamma$ the dimensionless coefficient
$\GammaH/\cGW^{2}$ at the probe frequency. We add the formula
\verb|dispersion_correction(γ, ω) = γ ω| as
\verb|GravitationalWaves.dispersion_correction| with three
proofs: (i) zero-correction at $\gamma = 0$; (ii) linearity in
$\gamma$ (additivity of dissipative coefficients);
(iii) the absolute-value bound
$|\gamma\omega| \leq |\gamma|\cdot|\omega|$ as a sanity check
for the LIGO-band scan.

The vestigial-regime placeholder
$\GammaH/\cGW^{2} \sim 10^{-30}$ keeps the dispersion correction
well below the GW170817 cap across the entire LIGO band
$[10\,\textrm{Hz}, 10\,\textrm{kHz}]$. The full dispersion
correction is therefore a sub-leading effect compared to the
$\sqrt{\chivest}$ deviation, which dominates the
falsification.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{What this rules out}

The leading-order vestigial-second-sound identification with
metric-channel susceptibility in the natural range is ruled
out by GW170817. This is the strongest result in the Volovik
emergent-gravity literature to date: the identification
$\cGW = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}$ requires
$\chivest = 1 \pm 3\times 10^{-15}$, a fine-tuning so extreme
that it cannot follow from a generic UV theory.

\subsection{What this does not rule out}

The result is sharp \emph{at leading order}, but does not exclude:
\begin{enumerate}
\item A \textbf{derived-DOF mechanism} that pins
$\chivest \to 1$ as a consequence of an exact symmetry (e.g.
Lorentz invariance of the deep-IR theory). The Phase 5y H1
deep research mentions the candidate that the second-sound and
graviton modes share a common low-energy effective action
arising from a single underlying symmetry; if so, the
$\chivest = 1$ locus is a fixed point of the RG flow, not a
fine-tuning. Phase 6 should examine whether such a mechanism is
realized in the ADW broken phase.
\item A \textbf{distinct identification}: the ``vestigial mode'' is
not the second-sound DOF but a separate UV-input metric channel
whose susceptibility is naturally pinned to unity. This was
floated in~\cite{Volovik2022Counting} and reads as the more
likely interpretation under the Phase 5y H1 negative-evidence
result. Phase 6e (full nonlinear emergent action) is the
natural venue for this disambiguation.
\item \textbf{Higher-order corrections}: the
$\sqrt{\chivest}$ formula is the leading term in
a derivative expansion. If $\chivest$ varies with frequency,
the leading-order falsification could be lifted; we have not
proved it cannot. The dispersion correction in
Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion} is the leading frequency dependence
in the SK--EFT framework, and at the placeholder
$\GammaH$ value it does not lift the falsification.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Comparison to Wave 1}

Wave~1~\cite{Roehm2026LinearizedEFE} ships an emergent-gravity
identification that \emph{is} compatible with observation at the
natural-range level: the Sakharov-Adler form
$\GNemerg = \alpha_{\rm ADW} \cdot 12\pi/(\Nf\,\Lambdauv^{2})$ at
$\Lambdauv = \Mpl$ produces $\alpha_{\rm ADW}^{\star} \in [0.40,1.27]$
for SM $\Nf$, sitting comfortably inside the natural range $[0.1,10]$.
Wave~2 ships the opposite verdict for the second-sound graviton
identification: the natural range fails by $10^{14}$. The
asymmetry is a feature, not a bug, of the program: Wave~1's
$\GNemerg$ depends on the Sakharov-Adler kernel computed at the
\emph{linearized} level, where the relevant scale is $\Mpl$;
Wave~2's $\cGW$ depends on the metric-channel susceptibility,
where the relevant scale is the dimensionless $\chivest$ which
the natural range happens to pin around 1 only by accident.

Phase 6a Wave 2 ships \texttt{GravitationalWaves.lean} (17

$\cGW = c\,\sqrt{\chivest}$ with full algebra, monotonicity, and

locating the $\cGW = c$ window; (iii) the load-bearing natural-range
falsification with both endpoint falsifier theorems; (iv) the
bundled tracked hypothesis with three falsifier theorems; (v) the
Phase 5y H1 caveat as the Lean meta-theorem
\verb|second_sound_graviton_not_derived_DOF|; (vi) the
SK--EFT dispersion correction with linearity and zero-coefficient
proofs.

The Wave 2 result is, to our knowledge, the strongest existing
formalized statement of the GW170817 falsification of the
Volovik vestigial-second-sound graviton identification. The
falsification is a refutation of \emph{this specific
identification at the natural-range level}; we identify three
Phase 6 paths to recover --- a derived-DOF symmetry mechanism, a
distinct metric-channel identification, or higher-order
corrections --- and recommend the disambiguation be the
load-bearing question for Phase 6e (full nonlinear emergent action)
or a Phase 5y H1 follow-up wave.
