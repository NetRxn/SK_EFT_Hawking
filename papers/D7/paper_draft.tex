\author{John G.~Roehm}

\begin{abstract}
Recent classical tensor-network methods --- belief propagation on
2D/3D PEPS networks (Tindall, Mello, Fishman, Stoudenmire, Sels,
\textit{Science} \textbf{392}, 868 (2026)) and Chebyshev-TN density-
matrix expansions on quasicrystal Hamiltonians (Antão, Sun, Fumega,
Lado, \textit{PRL} \textbf{136}, 156601 (2026)) --- have collectively
narrowed the gap between classical and quantum many-body simulation.
We formalize a joint substrate in the Lean~4 proof assistant and
ship a kernel-verified \textit{simulability demarcation
biconditional}\footnote{To the best of our knowledge after a scoped
search of GitHub topics \texttt{lean4} / \texttt{coq} / \texttt{agda},
the Mathlib / mathlib4 indexes, and the Coq UniMath / Agda categories
ecosystems (2026-05-26), no prior proof-assistant formalization of a
classical-simulability vs.\ quantum-advantage demarcation on tensor-
network factor graphs exists. We make no broader first-claim and
welcome notification of prior work.}: a quantum many-body system on a
factor graph $G$ with factor weights $f_a$ and categorical-Chern data
$(c_0, c_1)$ is classically simulable in the combined BP / Chebyshev-
TN regime if and only if (i) a structural tree indicator on the
factor graph is below a fixed threshold (the underlying topology is
tree-like and factor weights are non-negative) AND (ii) the
topological coefficient $c_1$ vanishes. Outside this regime --- loopy
factor-graph topology OR non-trivial Chern coefficient --- quantum
processors retain a structural simulation advantage at the level of
the formalized substrate. The Lean substrate is fully generic over
the factor-graph type parameters; analog-Hawking applications via the
Kibble-Zurek-Unruh correspondence are sketched at the cross-module
substrate level in Section~\ref{sec:kzu-application}, with explicit
per-platform (BEC, polariton, graphene Dirac fluid) instantiation
bridges deferred to the D-bundle absorption pass. The 65 substantive
theorems shipped across seven contributing modules have kernel-only
axiom closure $[\mathtt{propext}, \mathtt{Classical.choice},
\mathtt{Quot.sound}]$ with zero project-local axioms; the structural
tree-indicator predicate uses \texttt{Classical.byCases} on an
undecidable graph property and so the "threshold check" is
information-theoretic, not algorithmic, at this substrate layer.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The boundary between classical and quantum many-body simulation has
moved substantially over 2025--2026. Tindall, Mello, Fishman,
Stoudenmire, and Sels demonstrated that classical tensor-network
contraction via belief propagation matches the D-Wave Advantage2
quantum annealer on 300+ qubit spin-glass dynamics across 2D square,
3D cubic, and 3D diamond lattices~\cite{TindallMelloFishmanStoudenmireSels2026Science392}.
Antão, Sun, Fumega, and Lado complemented this with a Chebyshev-TN
density-matrix algorithm that computes local Chern markers on
quasicrystal Hamiltonians with hundreds of millions of
sites~\cite{AntaoSunFumegaLado2026PRL136}. Together these two
results bracket a substantial swath of quantum-simulation territory
in which classical methods are competitive.

Where exactly does the boundary lie? When does classical simulation
suffice, and when do quantum processors retain a structural
advantage? This paper formalizes a substantive demarcation criterion
at the Lean~4 substrate level, producing a kernel-verified
biconditional that decomposes the simulability question into two
load-bearing axes:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textit{Tree-indicator condition}: a structural indicator
on the factor graph (encoding "this graph is a tree" at the
predicate level) must be at or below the fixed threshold $1/2$
(equivalently, the factor graph is tree-like and the factor weights
are non-negative). The Lean implementation
$\mathtt{loopCorrectionRate}\,G := \mathtt{if}\
\mathrm{IsTreeFactorGraph}\,G\ \mathtt{then}\ 0\ \mathtt{else}\ 1$
is a $\{0,1\}$-valued indicator, not a continuous Cramér-type large-
deviation rate function. The "LDP" framing in the Tindall-Sels
literature motivates the predicate but is not encoded inside the
present substrate; an upgrade to a substantive Cramér rate
function on loop-correction terms is a separate deliverable
(estimated ~200--400 LoC against the existing project LDP module
suite) flagged for a future revision.
\item \textit{Topological condition}: the
categorical Chern coefficient $c_1$ of the system must vanish.
\end{enumerate}

The biconditional `analog\_hawking\_quantum\_advantage\_demarcation'
(Lean module \texttt{AnalogHawkingDemarcation.lean}) ties these two
axes together at the type level. The substrate beneath it ---
seven Lean modules across the BP-on-TN, LDP-rate-function, Chebyshev-
TN, aperiodic-lattice, and Chern-bridge layers --- is fully generic
over the factor-graph type parameters and applies to any LDP-
controlled-BP / Chebyshev-TN classification setting. Analog-Hawking
radiation is one concrete application, formalized via the Kibble-
Zurek-Unruh correspondence (Section~\ref{sec:kzu-application}).

\subsection{Scope and contributions}

We make four main contributions:

\begin{itemize}
\item A Lean~4 formalization of belief propagation on bipartite
factor graphs in the Yedidia-Freeman-Weiss 2003 convention, with 24
substantive theorems covering message-update positivity, factorization,
fixed-point stability, and Bethe free-energy substrate
(Section~\ref{sec:bp-substrate}).

\item A kernel-verified biconditional characterization of BP-
favorable factor graphs in terms of a tree-indicator threshold:
$\mathrm{IsBPConvergenceFavorable}\ G\ f_a \iff
\mathrm{loopCorrectionRate}\ G \leq 1/2 \land
(\forall a\, y,\, 0 \leq f_a(y))$ where $\mathrm{loopCorrectionRate}$
is a $\{0,1\}$-valued structural tree indicator (not a continuous
LDP rate function in the present substrate; see
Section~\ref{sec:ldp-bp}). The threshold $1/2$ is fixed; the
biconditional collapses to the tree-predicate-plus-non-negativity
pair (Section~\ref{sec:ldp-bp}).

\item A categorical-Chern $\leftrightarrow$ real-space-Chern bridge
formalized via the Chebyshev tensor-network expansion, with
substantively distinct crystalline (band-edge $x = 1$) and
quasicrystalline (band-edge $x = -1$) limits whose difference
$2 c_1$ extracts the topological invariant
(Section~\ref{sec:chern-bridge}).

\item A unified demarcation theorem
(Section~\ref{sec:demarcation}) combining the LDP-BP and Chern
contributions: a system is classically simulable iff BP-LDP-
favorable AND $c_1 = 0$. Contrapositive yields the quantum-advantage
regime explicitly.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}

The Phase 6w D7 bundle's central contribution is a kernel-verified
biconditional demarcation between regimes where the formalized
classical tensor-network substrate suffices and where the
formalization identifies a structural simulation advantage for
quantum processors. The demarcation is falsifiable on two distinct
axes (tree-indicator threshold + Chern coefficient) and the substrate
is generic enough at the type level to apply beyond analog Hawking
to any factor-graph + Chebyshev-TN classification setting.

We are explicit about the substrate's current scope: (i) the
``loopCorrectionRate'' is a structural tree indicator, not a
continuous LDP rate function --- the LDP-framing terminology
motivates the predicate but is not encoded substantively in the
present Lean substrate; an upgrade to a Cramér-type rate function
on loop-correction terms is a separate ~200--400 LoC deliverable;
(ii) the per-platform analog-Hawking bridges (BEC, polariton, graphene
Dirac fluid) are at the cross-module substrate level via the
\texttt{KibbleZurekUnruh.lean} foundation theorem but are NOT yet
shipped as platform-specific instantiation lemmas attached to the
demarcation predicate; (iii) the threshold check is information-
theoretic rather than algorithmic (the tree-indicator predicate uses
\texttt{Classical.byCases} on an undecidable graph property).
Each scope-limitation point is a clean future-revision target.
