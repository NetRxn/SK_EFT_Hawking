\begin{abstract}
Recent classical tensor-network methods --- belief propagation on
2D/3D PEPS networks (Tindall, Mello, Fishman, Stoudenmire, Sels,
\textit{Science} \textbf{392}, 868 (2026)) and Chebyshev-TN density-
matrix expansions on quasicrystal Hamiltonians (Antão, Sun, Fumega,
Lado, \textit{PRL} \textbf{136}, 156601 (2026)) --- have collectively
narrowed the gap between classical and quantum many-body simulation.
We formalize a joint substrate in the Lean~4 proof assistant and
ship a kernel-verified \textit{simulability demarcation
biconditional}\footnote{To the best of our knowledge after a scoped
search of GitHub topics \texttt{lean4} / \texttt{coq} / \texttt{agda},
the Mathlib / mathlib4 indexes, and the Coq UniMath / Agda categories
ecosystems (2026-05-26), no prior proof-assistant formalization of a
classical-simulability vs.\ quantum-advantage demarcation on tensor-
network factor graphs exists. We make no broader first-claim and
welcome notification of prior work.}: a quantum many-body system on a
factor graph $G$ with factor weights $f_a$ and categorical-Chern data
$(c_0, c_1)$ is classically simulable in the combined BP / Chebyshev-
TN regime if and only if (i) the loop-correction rate function of the
factor graph --- the Cram\'er/Legendre transform at zero deviation of
the log-MGF of the Bernoulli loop-presence observable whose success
parameter is the graph's 4-cycle density --- vanishes and the factor
weights are non-negative, AND (ii) the topological coefficient $c_1$
vanishes. The zero-rate $\Leftrightarrow$ tree equivalence is proven
from the variational definition (not definitional). Outside this
regime --- strictly positive loop-correction rate OR non-trivial
Chern coefficient --- quantum processors retain a structural
simulation advantage at the level of the formalized substrate. The
Lean substrate is fully generic over the (finite) factor-graph type
parameters; analog-Hawking applications via the
Kibble-Zurek-Unruh correspondence are sketched at the cross-module
substrate level in Section~\ref{sec:kzu-application}, with explicit
per-platform (BEC, polariton, graphene Dirac fluid) instantiation
bridges deferred to the D-bundle absorption pass. The 81 substantive
theorems shipped across seven contributing modules have kernel-only
axiom closure $[\mathtt{propext}, \mathtt{Classical.choice},
\mathtt{Quot.sound}]$ with zero project-local axioms; the
loop-correction rate is built from decidable 4-cycle combinatorics
(a kernel-checked \texttt{Finset} filter), with noncomputability
entering only through the real-valued logarithm and supremum, as is
standard for rate functions.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The boundary between classical and quantum many-body simulation has
moved substantially over 2025--2026. Tindall, Mello, Fishman,
Stoudenmire, and Sels demonstrated that classical tensor-network
contraction via belief propagation matches the D-Wave Advantage2
quantum annealer on 300+ qubit spin-glass dynamics across 2D square,
3D cubic, and 3D diamond lattices~\cite{TindallMelloFishmanStoudenmireSels2026Science392}.
Antão, Sun, Fumega, and Lado complemented this with a Chebyshev-TN
density-matrix algorithm that computes local Chern markers on
quasicrystal Hamiltonians with hundreds of millions of
sites~\cite{AntaoSunFumegaLado2026PRL136}. Together these two
results bracket a substantial swath of quantum-simulation territory
in which classical methods are competitive.

Where exactly does the boundary lie? When does classical simulation
suffice, and when do quantum processors retain a structural
advantage? This paper formalizes a substantive demarcation criterion
at the Lean~4 substrate level, producing a kernel-verified
biconditional that decomposes the simulability question into two
load-bearing axes:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textit{Zero-rate condition}: the loop-correction rate
function of the factor graph must vanish (equivalently --- proven,
not definitionally --- the factor graph is tree-like), and the
factor weights must be non-negative. The Lean implementation is a
genuine Cram\'er/Legendre construction
(\texttt{loopCorrectionRate}\,$G := \sup_{\theta}\,
[-\log \mathrm{mgf}_G(\theta)]$, the Legendre transform at zero
deviation of the log-MGF $\mathrm{mgf}_G(\theta) = 1 - p +
p\,e^{\theta}$ of the Bernoulli \emph{loop-presence observable},
whose success parameter $p = \mathtt{loopDensity}\,G \in [0,1)$ is
the graph's 4-cycle density). The closed form $-\log(1-p)$ is a
theorem (\texttt{loopCorrectionRate\_eq\_neg\_log}, an explicit
least-upper-bound argument; the supremum is approached as $\theta
\to -\infty$ and not attained on loopy graphs), as are the
zero-rate $\Leftrightarrow$ tree equivalence
(\texttt{loopCorrectionRate\_eq\_zero\_iff\_tree}), strict
positivity on loopy graphs
(\texttt{loopCorrectionRate\_pos\_of\_not\_tree}), strict
monotonicity in loop density
(\texttt{loopCorrectionRate\_lt\_of\_loopDensity\_lt} --- the rate
separates loopy graphs by loop content, e.g.\ the complete
bipartite $2{\times}2$ graph has rate $\log(4/3)$,
kernel-checked), the exact finite-$n$ Cram\'er identity $(1-p)^n =
e^{-n \cdot I(0)}$ for the no-loop-correction event
(\texttt{no\_loop\_event\_prob\_eq\_exp\_neg\_rate}), and the
bridge to the project LDP-suite Cram\'er predicate via the
Bernoulli Kullback-Leibler rate function
(\texttt{loopCorrection\_isCramerIIDUpperBound}). This upgrades the
earlier $\{0,1\}$-valued tree indicator flagged in the
review-2026-06-05 finding D7-EV3. Honest residual scope: the rate
is attached to the \emph{combinatorial} loop-presence observable
(4-cycle density, matching the granularity of the tree predicate);
the attachment to the \emph{dynamical} loop-correction terms of the
Chertkov-Chernyak loop-series
expansion~\cite{ChertkovChernyak2006LoopSeries} over the BP message
space remains future work.
\item \textit{Topological condition}: the
categorical Chern coefficient $c_1$ of the system must vanish.
\end{enumerate}

The biconditional `analog\_hawking\_quantum\_advantage\_demarcation'
(Lean module \texttt{AnalogHawkingDemarcation.lean}) ties these two
axes together at the type level. The substrate beneath it ---
seven Lean modules across the BP-on-TN, LDP-rate-function, Chebyshev-
TN, aperiodic-lattice, and Chern-bridge layers --- is fully generic
over the factor-graph type parameters and applies to any LDP-
controlled-BP / Chebyshev-TN classification setting. Analog-Hawking
radiation is one concrete application, formalized via the Kibble-
Zurek-Unruh correspondence (Section~\ref{sec:kzu-application}).

\subsection{Scope and contributions}

We make four main contributions:

\begin{itemize}
\item A Lean~4 formalization of belief propagation on bipartite
factor graphs in the Yedidia-Freeman-Weiss 2003 convention, with 24
substantive theorems covering message-update positivity, factorization,
fixed-point stability, and Bethe free-energy substrate
(Section~\ref{sec:bp-substrate}).

\item A kernel-verified biconditional characterization of BP-
favorable factor graphs in terms of the vanishing of a continuous
loop-correction rate function:
$\mathrm{IsBPConvergenceFavorable}\ G\ f_a \iff
\mathrm{loopCorrectionRate}\ G = 0 \land
(\forall a\, y,\, 0 \leq f_a(y))$ where $\mathrm{loopCorrectionRate}$
is the Cram\'er/Legendre transform at zero deviation of the Bernoulli
loop-presence log-MGF (4-cycle density of the factor graph; see
Section~\ref{sec:ldp-bp}). The zero-rate $\Leftrightarrow$ tree
equivalence is itself a theorem proven through the Legendre
evaluation, and a general threshold form
($\mathrm{rate} \leq t \Leftrightarrow$ explicit loop-density bound
$1 - e^{-t}$) replaces the former fixed indicator threshold
(Section~\ref{sec:ldp-bp}).

\item A categorical-Chern $\leftrightarrow$ real-space-Chern bridge
formalized via the Chebyshev tensor-network expansion, with
substantively distinct crystalline (band-edge $x = 1$) and
quasicrystalline (band-edge $x = -1$) limits whose difference
$2 c_1$ extracts the topological invariant
(Section~\ref{sec:chern-bridge}).

\item A unified demarcation theorem
(Section~\ref{sec:demarcation}) combining the LDP-BP and Chern
contributions: a system is classically simulable iff BP-LDP-
favorable AND $c_1 = 0$. Contrapositive yields the quantum-advantage
regime explicitly.
\end{itemize}

\section{Discussion}

The Phase 6w D7 bundle's central contribution is a kernel-verified
biconditional demarcation between regimes where the formalized
classical tensor-network substrate suffices and where the
formalization identifies a structural simulation advantage for
quantum processors. The demarcation is falsifiable on two distinct
axes (loop-correction rate function + Chern coefficient) and the substrate
is generic enough at the type level to apply beyond analog Hawking
to any factor-graph + Chebyshev-TN classification setting.

We are explicit about the substrate's current scope: (i) the
loop-correction rate function is now a genuine Cram\'er/Legendre
construction (the review-2026-06-05 D7-EV3 upgrade): the Legendre
transform at zero deviation of the Bernoulli loop-presence log-MGF,
with the closed form $-\log(1 - p)$, the zero-rate
$\Leftrightarrow$ tree equivalence, strict positivity on loopy
graphs, strict monotonicity in loop density, the exact finite-$n$
Cram\'er identity, and the Kullback-Leibler bridge to the project
LDP suite all proven kernel-only --- the honest residual is that
the rate attaches to the \emph{combinatorial} loop-presence
observable (4-cycle density, the granularity of the tree
predicate), while the attachment to the \emph{dynamical}
loop-correction terms of the Chertkov-Chernyak loop-series
expansion over the BP message space remains future work; (ii) the
per-platform analog-Hawking bridges (BEC, polariton, graphene
Dirac fluid) are at the cross-module substrate level via the
\texttt{KibbleZurekUnruh.lean} foundation theorem but are NOT yet
shipped as platform-specific instantiation lemmas attached to the
demarcation predicate; (iii) the rate function is built from
decidable 4-cycle combinatorics (kernel-checked \texttt{Finset}
filter; the former \texttt{Classical.byCases} on the undecidable
tree predicate is gone from the definition), with noncomputability
entering only through the real-valued logarithm and supremum.
Each residual scope point is a clean future-revision target.
