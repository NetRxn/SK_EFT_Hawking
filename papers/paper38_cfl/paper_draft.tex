\begin{abstract}
The color-flavor-locked (\CFL) phase of dense quark
matter~\cite{AlfordRajagopalWilczek1999,Hatsuda2008} carries an
emergent one-form $\Zthree$ symmetry, identified by Hirono and
Tanizaki~\cite{HironoTanizaki2018} as the algebraic origin of the
quark--hadron continuity~\cite{SchaeferWilczek1999} between the
\CFL\ phase and confined hadronic matter. We formalize this emergent
symmetry in Lean~4 and prove that its cyclic generator
$\omega = \exp(2\pi i / 3)$ coincides with the bare-gauge \QCD\ center
$\Zthree$ generator established in a companion Lean module on
center-symmetry confinement. The two derivations --- one from the
ultraviolet side (bare-gauge center symmetry of \QCD), the other from
the infrared side (emergent diquark-sector one-form symmetry of the
\CFL\ phase) --- yield the \emph{same} generator. Algebraic consequences
($\omega^3 = 1$, $|\omega| = 1$, and the cube-root sum
$1 + \omega + \omega^2 = 0$ that distinguishes $\Zthree$ from
$\mathbb{Z}_2$) are derived through the cross-derivation
identification rather than by direct computation. The \CFL\ chiral
Lagrangian skeleton (kinetic plus mass terms) is encoded with the
chiral-limit Goldstone behavior ($m_q = 0$ vanishes the mass term) and
the in-\CFL-phase positivity. The Hirono--Tanizaki
topological-order-beyond-Landau--Ginzburg framing is encoded as a
structural assumption over $\Zthree$ charges $\{0, 1, 2\}$. The module
ships $13$ substantive theorems with zero \texttt{sorry} statements
and zero new axioms.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The color-flavor-locked phase of dense quark matter is a paradigm
example of a phase in which a nontrivial color-flavor symmetry pattern
is locked by a diquark condensate~\cite{AlfordRajagopalWilczek1999,
Hatsuda2008}. The phenomenology has been worked out in detail
~\cite{SonStephanov2001,SchaeferWilczek1999}, and a more recent
development by Hirono and Tanizaki~\cite{HironoTanizaki2018} reframes
the quark--hadron continuity in higher-form-symmetry
language~\cite{GaiottoKapustinSeibergWillett2015}: the \CFL\ phase
exhibits an emergent one-form $\Zthree$ symmetry, and that symmetry
matches the bare \QCD\ center symmetry of confined hadronic matter at
the level of the algebraic generator.

This paper presents a Lean~4 formalization of the structural content
of that match. The substantive output is a single load-bearing
theorem identifying the \CFL\ emergent $\Zthree$ generator with the
\QCD\ center $\Zthree$ generator at the level of the cyclic phase
$\omega = \exp(2\pi i / 3)$. The match is non-trivial because the two
generators arise from structurally distinct constructions: one from
the bare-gauge center symmetry of the ultraviolet \QCD\ Lagrangian
~\cite{Polyakov1978}, the other from the emergent diquark-sector
one-form symmetry of the infrared \CFL\ phase. Their coincidence is
an independent consistency check on the higher-form-symmetry
identification of the quark--hadron continuity.

\section{The cross-derivation identity}
\label{sec:cross-derivation}

The \CFL\ emergent $\Zthree$ generator is defined independently of the
\QCD\ center $\Zthree$ generator:
\begin{equation}
\omega_\CFL \;:=\; e^{2 \pi i / 3}.
\label{eq:cfl-omega}
\end{equation}
The bare-gauge \QCD\ center $\Zthree$ generator is the
$N = 3$ specialization of $\zeta_N$ from the companion module on
center-symmetry confinement,
\begin{equation}
\omega_\QCD \;:=\; \texttt{centerPhase}(\Zthree) \;=\; e^{2 \pi i / 3}.
\label{eq:qcd-omega}
\end{equation}
The structural anchor of this paper is the theorem
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;
\omega_\CFL \;=\; \omega_\QCD
\;}
\end{equation}
shipped as \texttt{CFL\_emergent\_Z3\_matches\_QCD\_center\_Z3}.
The proof reduces both definitions to their common closed form.

The structural significance is that the algebraic consequences derive
\emph{through} the identification:

\paragraph*{Theorem (\texttt{emergentZ3\_pow\_3}).}
$\omega_\CFL^3 = 1$, derived by rewriting via the identity above and
invoking the companion module's \texttt{centerPhase\_pow\_N} at
$N = 3$.

\paragraph*{Theorem (\texttt{emergentZ3\_norm\_one}).}
$|\omega_\CFL| = 1$, derived analogously through the companion module's
\texttt{centerPhase\_norm\_one}.

\paragraph*{Theorem (\texttt{emergentZ3\_sum\_cube\_roots}).}
$1 + \omega_\CFL + \omega_\CFL^2 = 0$, the algebraic fingerprint
distinguishing $\Zthree$ from $\mathbb{Z}_2$. The proof uses the
factorization $z^3 - 1 = (z - 1)(z^2 + z + 1)$ together with
$\sin(2\pi/3) > 0$ to derive $\omega_\CFL \ne 1$, then divides
$\omega_\CFL^3 - 1 = 0$ by $\omega_\CFL - 1 \ne 0$.

\section{CFL chiral Lagrangian skeleton}
\label{sec:lagrangian}

The \CFL\ chiral Lagrangian skeleton ships as a kinetic-plus-mass
encoding:

\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{cflKineticTerm\_nonneg}: the kinetic term is
non-negative for any \CFL\ diquark field configuration.
\item \texttt{cflMassTerm\_chiral\_limit}: in the chiral limit
$m_q = 0$, the mass term vanishes (recovering the Goldstone-mode
content).
\item \texttt{cflMassTerm\_pos\_in\_cfl\_phase}: in the \CFL\ phase
with $m_q > 0$, the mass term is strictly positive. The proof
\emph{requires both} hypotheses; dropping either falsifies the
conclusion.
\end{itemize}

\section{Hirono--Tanizaki topological-order framing}
\label{sec:topological-order}

The Hirono--Tanizaki framing identifies the \CFL\ phase as
exhibiting topological order beyond the Landau--Ginzburg
paradigm~\cite{HironoTanizaki2018}. We encode the framing as a
structural assumption parametric over $\Zthree$ charges:
\begin{equation}
H_{\mathrm{TopoLG}}(c) \;:=\;
(c \in \{0, 1, 2\}) \,\wedge\, (\text{topological order at } c).
\end{equation}
A witness ships at charge $c = 1$ (the topologically ordered sector).
Two falsifiers discharge the assumption: a trivial-sector falsifier
at $c = 0$ (no topological order; the trivial sector lies in
Landau--Ginzburg) and an out-of-range falsifier at $c = 3$
($c \notin \{0, 1, 2\}$).

\section{Cross-bridge: simultaneous chiral and CFL breaking}
\label{sec:cross-bridge}

Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation,
\texttt{cfl\_phase\_with\_gmor\_dual\_broken}, consumes both:
\begin{itemize}
\item the contrapositive of the
Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation (chiral-unbroken phase forbidden by
the relation for positive quark mass and non-trivial pion sector), and

The conclusion is that the system is simultaneously chiral-broken and
\CFL-broken --- an independent consistency check that the two breaking
patterns occur in concert in the \CFL\ phase. Both hypotheses are
load-bearing in the proof body.

\section{Outlook}
\label{sec:outlook}

The cross-derivation identification of the \CFL\ emergent $\Zthree$
generator with the \QCD\ center $\Zthree$ generator confirms the
higher-form-symmetry framing of the
quark--hadron continuity~\cite{HironoTanizaki2018,SchaeferWilczek1999}
at the algebraic-generator level. Natural extensions include the full
\CFL\ chiral Lagrangian (mesonic mass spectrum~\cite{SonStephanov2001},
gauge-mass mixing) and analogous higher-form-symmetry analyses for
other dense-quark-matter phases (2SC, color-spin-locked).
