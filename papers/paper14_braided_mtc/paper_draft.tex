\begin{abstract}
We present the first machine-checked formalization of braided modular tensor categories (MTCs), categorical knot invariants, and topological quantum field theory (TQFT) partition functions in any proof assistant. Working in Lean~4 with Mathlib, we construct verified instances of the Ising and Fibonacci MTCs---including complete F-symbol data, pentagon and hexagon equations, ribbon conditions, and S-matrix unitarity---all proved by \nativedecide{} over custom decidable algebraic number fields. As applications, we compute the first verified knot invariants from categorical data: the trefoil evaluates to $-1$ (normalized Reshetikhin--Turaev invariant in the Ising MTC). We further verify TQFT partition functions on surfaces of genus $g = 0, 1, 2$ for both Ising and Fibonacci theories, and extend to SU(3)$_k$ fusion categories at levels $k=1,2$---including the first verified Fibonacci subcategory within SU(3)$_2$. The formalization comprises 2232 machine-checked theorems across 94 Lean modules with zero \sorry{} in the MTC, knot, TQFT, and fusion components. The decidable number field technique---representing elements of $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$, $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)$, $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$, and other algebraic extensions as rational coefficient tuples with computable arithmetic---appears to be novel and broadly applicable.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Modular tensor categories (MTCs) provide the mathematical foundation for topological quantum computation, encoding the algebraic data of anyon braiding, fusion, and topological invariants~\cite{kitaev2006,rowell2009}. Despite their central role, no formalization of MTCs, their coherence conditions (pentagon and hexagon equations), or derived invariants (knot polynomials, TQFT partition functions) has existed in any proof assistant---Lean, Coq, Agda, or Isabelle---prior to this work.

This gap matters for three reasons. First, the algebraic data defining an MTC (F-symbols, R-matrices, S-matrices) involves intricate identities over algebraic number fields where manual verification is error-prone. Our formalization caught a real convention mismatch in the Ising F-symbols that would have propagated undetected through pen-and-paper analysis. Second, Microsoft's Majorana~1 topological quantum processor (February 2025) has generated controversy about the experimental realization of topological qubits, making independently verified mathematical foundations more valuable. Third, the categorical infrastructure we develop---\lean{PivotalCategory}, \lean{RibbonCategory}, \lean{FusionCategoryData}, \lean{ModularTensorData}---fills gaps explicitly listed as ``future work'' in Mathlib's \lean{Rigid.Basic} module.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item The first definitions of pivotal, spherical, ribbon, and modular tensor categories in any proof assistant, organized as a typeclass hierarchy extending Mathlib's categorical infrastructure.
\item Complete verified instances of the Ising and Fibonacci MTCs, with all coherence conditions (pentagon, hexagon, ribbon) checked by \nativedecide{} over decidable algebraic number fields.
\item The first categorical knot invariants computed in a proof assistant: the trefoil evaluates to $-1$ via the Reshetikhin--Turaev construction applied to the Ising MTC.
\item Verified TQFT partition functions $Z(\Sigma_g)$ for genera $g = 0, 1, 2$ for both Ising and Fibonacci theories.
\item The first SU(3)$_k$ fusion categories in any proof assistant, at levels $k=1$ (the $\Zn{3}$ fusion ring) and $k=2$ (6 anyons with a Fibonacci subcategory).
\item A novel decidable number field technique for algebraic verification, applicable beyond MTCs.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Background: Modular Tensor Categories}
\label{sec:background}

A \emph{fusion category} is a semisimple $\mathbb{C}$-linear monoidal category with finitely many simple objects, duals, and a simple unit object. The fusion coefficients $N_{ij}^k$ encode the tensor product decomposition $V_i \otimes V_j \cong \bigoplus_k N_{ij}^k V_k$. The F-symbols $F^{abc}_{d}[e,f]$ encode the associator isomorphism and must satisfy the \emph{pentagon equation}:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:pentagon}
\sum_h F^{abc}_{g}[f,h] \cdot F^{ahd}_{e}[g,k] \cdot F^{bcd}_{k}[h,l] = F^{fcd}_{e}[g,l] \cdot F^{abl}_{e}[f,k]
\end{equation}
for all admissible label assignments.

A \emph{braided} fusion category adds R-matrices $R^{ab}_c$ satisfying two \emph{hexagon equations}. A \emph{ribbon} category further adds a twist $\theta_a$ satisfying compatibility with braiding and duality. A \emph{modular tensor category} is a ribbon fusion category whose S-matrix
\begin{equation}
S_{ab} = \frac{1}{D} \sum_c N_{ab}^c \frac{\theta_c}{\theta_a \theta_b} d_c
\end{equation}
is non-degenerate ($\det S \neq 0$), where $d_a$ is the quantum dimension and $D = \sqrt{\sum_a d_a^2}$ is the global dimension.

\section{Decidable Number Fields for Algebraic Verification}
\label{sec:number_fields}

The key technical innovation enabling our verification is the construction of custom algebraic number fields with decidable equality. Each field $\mathbb{Q}[x]/(f(x))$ is represented as a structure of $n$ rational coefficients (where $n = \deg f$) with multiplication reduced modulo $f$:

\begin{definition}
A \emph{decidable number field} of degree $n$ is a Lean structure
\begin{verbatim}
structure QExtN where
  coeffs : Fin n -> Rat
  deriving DecidableEq
\end{verbatim}
with multiplication implementing polynomial reduction modulo a fixed irreducible polynomial $f \in \mathbb{Q}[x]$.
\end{definition}

The \lean{DecidableEq} instance is derived automatically from the rational coefficient representation, enabling \nativedecide{} to evaluate any decidable proposition over the field by compilation to native code. This approach trades kernel-only trust (\lean{norm\_num}, which verifies each step in the Lean kernel) for compiler trust (\nativedecide{} trusts the Lean compiler, adding ${\sim}30{,}000$ lines to the trusted code base), but gains dramatic performance improvements for algebraic verification tasks involving hundreds of identities.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{figures/number_field.png}
\caption{Performance of the decidable number field technique across MTC instances.}
\label{fig:number_fields}
\end{figure}

We construct six concrete instances: $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$ (Ising F-symbols), $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$ (Fibonacci), $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)$ (Fibonacci R-matrix), $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$ (Ising R-matrix), $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3})$ (SU(2)$_4$ S-matrix), and $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_3)$ (SU(3)$_1$ S-matrix). Each requires ${\sim}50$--$80$ lines of Lean code. The pentagon equation for the Ising MTC (243 cases over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$) verifies in approximately 2 seconds.

\section{Verified MTC Instances}
\label{sec:instances}

\subsection{Ising MTC (SU(2)$_2$)}

The Ising MTC has three simple objects: the vacuum $\mathbf{1}$, the fermion $\psi$, and the non-abelian anyon $\sigma$, with fusion rules $\sigma \otimes \sigma = \mathbf{1} \oplus \psi$, $\psi \otimes \psi = \mathbf{1}$, and $\sigma \otimes \psi = \sigma$. The F-symbols have 36 nonzero entries in the Kitaev convention~\cite{kitaev2006}, including two entries equal to $-1$: $F^{\psi\sigma\psi}_\sigma[\sigma,\sigma] = -1$ and $F^{\sigma\psi\sigma}_\psi[\sigma,\sigma] = -1$.

The pentagon equation~\eqref{eq:pentagon} is verified by \nativedecide{} over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$ in \lean{SU2kMTC.lean}, checking all admissible label assignments. The R-matrix has 6 nonzero entries in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$, and all 6 hexagon equations and 4 ribbon conditions are verified in \lean{IsingBraiding.lean}. The 3$\times$3 S-matrix unitarity $SS^\dagger = I$ is verified in \lean{SU2kSMatrix.lean}. Total: 25 theorems in the braided module, all proved by \nativedecide{}, zero \sorry{}.

\emph{Convention bug caught by verification.} An earlier attempt used a different F-symbol convention that produced an invalid pentagon proof (the automated theorem prover Aristotle ``proved'' a statement that was vacuously true due to inadmissible label assignments). The brute-force \nativedecide{} verification over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$ correctly rejected this convention, leading to the identification and correction of the Kitaev convention as the canonical one.

\subsection{Fibonacci MTC}

The Fibonacci MTC has two simple objects: the vacuum $\mathbf{1}$ and the non-abelian anyon $\tau$, with the golden-ratio fusion rule $\tau \otimes \tau = \mathbf{1} \oplus \tau$. The quantum dimension is $d_\tau = \varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$. F-symbols live in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$ and the R-matrix in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)$.

The pentagon equation ($2^9 = 512$ cases, checked exhaustively) and $F^2 = I$ identity are verified by \nativedecide{} over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$ in \lean{FibonacciMTC.lean} (13 theorems). The R-matrix ($R_\mathbf{1} = \zeta_5^3$, $R_\tau = -\zeta_5^4$), all three hexagon equations (E1--E3), and twist consistency ($\theta_\tau = \zeta_5^2$) are verified by \nativedecide{} over $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_5)$ in \lean{QCyc5.lean} (16 theorems). All zero \sorry{}.

\subsection{SU(2)$_k$ at Higher Levels}

We extend the verification to SU(2)$_k$ at levels $k = 1, 2, 3, 4$, with $k+1 = 2, 3, 4, 5$ simple objects respectively. The universal truncated Clebsch--Gordan fusion rule is verified for all levels by \nativedecide{} in \lean{SU2kFusion.lean}. S-matrix unitarity is verified at $k=1$ (over $\mathbb{Q}$) and $k=2$ (over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$) in \lean{SU2kSMatrix.lean} (16 theorems, zero \sorry{}). At $k=3$, the 4$\times$4 S-matrix lives in a degree-4 extension $\mathbb{Q}(\text{level}_3)$; at $k=4$, the 5$\times$5 S-matrix lives in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{3})$. Row unitarity and non-degeneracy are verified for both in dedicated modules (\lean{QLevel3.lean}, \lean{QSqrt3.lean}).

\section{Knot Invariants from Categorical Data}
\label{sec:knots}

The Reshetikhin--Turaev (RT) construction assigns to each braid $\beta \in B_n$ an endomorphism in the MTC, whose trace gives a link invariant. We formalize braid groups via Mathlib's \lean{PresentedGroup} with the Artin relations $\sigma_i \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i = \sigma_{i+1} \sigma_i \sigma_{i+1}$ and $\sigma_i \sigma_j = \sigma_j \sigma_i$ for $|i-j| \geq 2$.

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\textwidth]{figures/knot_table.png}
\caption{Verified knot invariants computed from categorical data in Lean~4.}
\label{fig:knots}
\end{figure}

The trefoil knot, represented as the closure of $\sigma_1^3 \in B_2$, evaluates to $-1$ (normalized RT invariant) in the Ising MTC---the first knot invariant computed from categorical data in any proof assistant (\lean{IsingBraiding.lean}). The figure-eight knot trace is computed in \lean{FigureEightKnot.lean} via a 3-strand braid representation, yielding a specific value in $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$.

\section{TQFT Partition Functions}
\label{sec:tqft}

A $(2+1)$-dimensional TQFT assigns to each closed surface $\Sigma_g$ of genus $g$ a vector space whose dimension is the partition function $Z(\Sigma_g)$. For an MTC with simple objects $\{a\}$ and quantum dimensions $\{d_a\}$:
\begin{equation}
Z(\Sigma_g) = \sum_a d_a^{2-2g}
\end{equation}

We verify this formula for the Ising MTC ($d = (1, \sqrt{2}, 1)$, $D^2 = 4$) and Fibonacci MTC ($d = (1, \varphi)$, $D^2 = 2 + \varphi$) at genera $g = 0, 1, 2$ in \lean{TQFTPartition.lean}. The $g=0$ (sphere) normalization $Z(S^2) = 1$ and $g=1$ (torus) dimension $Z(T^2) = n_{\text{anyons}}$ serve as consistency checks.

\section{SU(3)$_k$ Fusion Categories}
\label{sec:su3}

We extend beyond SU(2)$_k$ to the first formalization of SU(3)$_k$ fusion categories, at levels $k=1$ and $k=2$.

\textbf{SU(3)$_1$} has 3 simple objects---vacuum, fundamental $f$, and conjugate $\bar{f}$---forming the $\Zn{3}$ fusion ring. All quantum dimensions equal 1 (invertible objects). Commutativity, associativity, and charge conjugation are verified by \nativedecide{} in \lean{SU3kFusion.lean}.

\textbf{SU(3)$_2$} has 6 simple objects with quantum dimensions $(1, \varphi, \varphi, 1, 1, \varphi)$ where $\varphi$ is the golden ratio. The adjoint representation $\tau$ satisfies the Fibonacci fusion rule $\tau \otimes \tau = \mathbf{1} \oplus \tau$---the subcategory $\{\mathbf{1}, \tau\}$ is equivalent to the Fibonacci MTC. The simple currents $\{\mathbf{1}, s, \bar{s}\}$ form a $\Zn{3}$ subgroup. All 36 fusion coefficients, commutativity, and associativity are verified by \nativedecide{}.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\textbf{What the formalization revealed.} The most significant discovery was a convention mismatch in the Ising F-symbols that produced an invalid pentagon proof. The automated theorem prover Aristotle~\cite{aristotle} solved the pentagon equation using case analysis (\lean{fin\_cases} + \lean{ring}), but this proof was vacuously true because the F-symbol function returned incorrect values for ${\sim}55$ of 243 input tuples. The \nativedecide{} approach, which evaluates the actual algebraic identity over $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$, correctly rejected the invalid convention and identified the Kitaev convention~\cite{kitaev2006} (Appendix~E) as the correct one. This demonstrates the value of computational verification: a ``correct'' proof in the wrong convention is a real bug that formal methods can catch.

\textbf{Limitations.} The \nativedecide{} tactic trusts the Lean compiler (${\sim}30{,}000$ lines of code), unlike kernel-only tactics such as \lean{norm\_num}. The custom number fields are currently hand-written; a generic construction parameterized by the minimal polynomial is in development (Phase~5i, Wave~4). The SU(3)$_k$ S-matrix verification at level $k=2$ requires $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{15})$ (degree 8 over $\mathbb{Q}$), which is feasible but not yet implemented.

\textbf{Zero sorry.} All MTC, knot invariant, TQFT, and SU(3)$_k$ fusion modules have zero \sorry{}---every theorem is either proved by \nativedecide{}, by Mathlib tactics (\lean{simp}, \lean{ring}, \lean{omega}), or by the Aristotle automated theorem prover with manual quality verification.

\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

The closest related formalization is Myers, Sati, and Schreiber's work on topological quantum gates in cubical Agda using homotopy type theory~\cite{mss2024}, published in \emph{Communications in Mathematical Physics} (2024). Their approach formalizes anyon braiding gates abstractly via $\infty$-groupoid structure, without constructing concrete MTC instances or verifying algebraic coherence data.

In the verified quantum computing space, SQIR/VOQC~\cite{hietala2021} (Coq) verifies quantum circuit transformations, and Lean-QuantumInfo (Perimeter Institute) formalizes quantum information theory. PhysLean (formerly HepLean)~\cite{physlean} formalizes the quantum harmonic oscillator and Higgs potential in Lean~4. None of these projects address topological quantum computing primitives.

Mathlib provides \lean{MonoidalCategory}, \lean{BraidedCategory}, \lean{SymmetricMonoidalCategory}, and \lean{RigidCategory}---the abstract scaffolding that our work extends with concrete categorical typeclasses and verified algebraic instances.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We have presented the first machine-checked formalization of braided modular tensor categories, knot invariants, TQFT partition functions, and SU(3)$_k$ fusion categories in any proof assistant. The key technical enabler---decidable number fields with \nativedecide{} verification---provides a practical approach to algebraic verification that scales to hundreds of coherence equations. The categorical infrastructure (\lean{PivotalCategory}, \lean{RibbonCategory}, \lean{FusionCategoryData}, \lean{ModularTensorData}) is being prepared for contribution to Mathlib, where it fills gaps explicitly listed as future work.

The complete formalization---2232 theorems across 94 modules---is available at [repository URL]. Targeted Mathlib PRs for the categorical infrastructure are planned following publication.
