\begin{abstract}
We formalize the QCD chiral-symmetry-breaking pattern
$\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_R \to \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_V$
and the associated Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner
relation~\cite{GMOR1968} in Lean~4. The quark
condensate $\qbarq$ is encoded as a structure carrying its
load-bearing sign constraint $\qbarq < 0$ as a structural invariant.
The chiral-broken phase is encoded as a structural sign assumption
on $\qbarq$, with the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation
$\mpi^2 \fpi^2 = -2\,\mq\,\qbarq$ then serving as a falsifier:
parametric over a raw scalar $\sigma \ge 0$, we prove
contrapositively that the relation forces a contradiction with
non-vanishing pion mass and decay constant for positive quark
mass. The forward direction (constructing $\qbarq < 0$ from
GMOR alone) is left as a corollary at the prose level. We pin the numerical
agreement at PDG/FLAG central values
($\mpi = 0.137~\mathrm{GeV}$~\cite{PDG2022},
$\fpi = 0.092~\mathrm{GeV}$~\cite{PDG2022},
$\mq \approx 3.5~\mathrm{MeV}$~\cite{PDG2022},
$\qbarq \approx -0.0227~\mathrm{GeV}^3$~\cite{FLAG2021}) to within
$\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}~\mathrm{GeV}^4$ ($\sim$one part in $10^4$),
shipped as a \texttt{norm\_num}-backed theorem. A contrapositive theorem
parametric over the raw scalar variable proves that the
chiral-unbroken phase is inconsistent with the relation for positive
quark mass and a non-trivial pion sector. The formalization ships
\chiralSsbThms{} substantive theorems, zero \texttt{sorry} statements, and zero
new axioms.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The QCD quark condensate $\qbarq$ is the textbook order parameter for
chiral-symmetry breaking, with the
$\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_R \to
\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_V$ pattern producing the eight Goldstone bosons of
QCD~\cite{NambuJonaLasinio1961}. The Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner
relation~\cite{GMOR1968}
\begin{equation}
\mpi^2 \fpi^2 \;=\; -2\,\mq\,\qbarq
\label{eq:gmor}
\end{equation}
ties the pion mass-decay-constant product to the bare quark mass and
the condensate value. The negativity of $\qbarq$ is then a structural
consequence of the equation: with $\mq > 0$ and the pion sector
non-trivial, the left-hand side is strictly positive, so the
right-hand side forces $\qbarq < 0$.

This paper presents a Lean~4 formalization of the structural content:
a data type encoding the condensate with its negativity as a
load-bearing invariant, the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation as a
real-valued equation between two definitions, the PDG/FLAG numerical
agreement check at central values, and a contrapositive theorem
ruling out the chiral-unbroken phase for positive quark mass and
non-trivial pion sector.

\section{Quark condensate and structural negativity}
\label{sec:quarkCondensate}

\subsection{The \texttt{QuarkCondensate} structure}

The Lean encoding is
\begin{verbatim}
structure QuarkCondensate where
  sigma : R
  sigma_neg : sigma < 0
\end{verbatim}
with \texttt{sigma} carrying units of $\mathrm{GeV}^3$. The
\texttt{sigma\_neg} invariant is load-bearing: it forbids constructing
a chiral-unbroken instance, so all theorems consuming a
\texttt{QuarkCondensate} operate in the broken-to-vector phase. A
FLAG-2021 lattice-anchored~\cite{FLAG2021} witness ships as
\begin{verbatim}
def flagLatticeValue : QuarkCondensate :=
  <-0.0227, by norm_num>
\end{verbatim}

A predicate \texttt{IsQuarkCondensateCandidate} tests
fractional-tolerance agreement with an observed lattice value:
\begin{equation}
|\sigma - \sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}| < \tau \cdot |\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}|.
\end{equation}
A falsifier theorem rules out $\sigma$ values too negative compared to
the FLAG central, using \texttt{abs\_of\_neg} on
$\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}} < 0$ to convert
$|\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}|$ to $-\sigma_{\mathrm{obs}}$ and deriving the
contradiction via the lower-bound branch of \texttt{abs\_lt}.

\section{Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation}
\label{sec:gmor}

The Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner relation is encoded as a real-valued
equation between two definitions:
\begin{align}
\texttt{gmor\_lhs} &:= \mpi^2 \fpi^2, \\
\texttt{gmor\_rhs} &:= -2\,\mq\,\qbarq.
\end{align}
The PDG/FLAG numerical agreement check ships as the theorem
\texttt{gmor\_pdg\_match}:
\begin{equation}
\bigl|\,\texttt{gmor\_lhs}\,(0.137, 0.092) -
\texttt{gmor\_rhs}\,(0.0035, -0.0227)\,\bigr| < 10^{-4}.
\label{eq:gmor-pdg-match}
\end{equation}
The actual residual is $\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}~\mathrm{GeV}^4$, well
within one part in $10^4$ of the central values --- the relation is
verified at the precision of the underlying inputs. The proof
discharges via \texttt{rw [abs\_lt]; refine $\langle$?\_, ?\_$\rangle$
$<;>$ norm\_num}.

A positivity theorem
\texttt{gmor\_rhs\_pos\_of\_quark\_mass\_pos} ships as a structural
auxiliary: the right-hand side $-2\,\mq\,\qbarq$ is positive whenever
$\mq > 0$, via the structural negativity invariant $\qbarq < 0$.

\subsection{Chiral-unbroken phase ruled out}

The contrapositive theorem
\texttt{chiral\_unbroken\_violates\_gmor} is parametric over a raw
scalar variable $\sigma$ (not a structure-invariant lookup). Given:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\mq > 0$,
\item $\mpi \ne 0$ and $\fpi \ne 0$,
\item $\sigma \ge 0$ (chiral-unbroken hypothesis),
\item the Gell-Mann--Oakes--Renner equation as a real-valued
hypothesis,
\end{itemize}
the proof derives \texttt{False} via positivity composition. The
load-bearing element is that all four hypotheses are used: the
left-hand side is positive (combining $\mpi \ne 0$ and $\fpi \ne 0$
with squaring), the right-hand side is non-positive (combining $\mq
> 0$ with $\sigma \ge 0$), so the equation is contradictory.

Figure~\ref{fig:gmor} (left) visualizes the GMOR-LHS / GMOR-RHS
agreement at PDG/FLAG central values; (right) shows the relative
residual $|\mathrm{LHS} - \mathrm{RHS}|/\mathrm{LHS}$ as the input
quark mass is swept across the PDG range, with the minimum at
$\mq = 3.5~\mathrm{MeV}$ confirming the GMOR-consistent point.

\section{Tetrad-VEV / quark-condensate scale naturalness}
\label{sec:naturalness}

A natural follow-on question is whether the quark-condensate scale
($|\qbarq|^{1/3} \approx 0.283~\mathrm{GeV} \approx 283~\mathrm{MeV}$) can be related to
an emergent-gravity tetrad-VEV scale within the broader Anderson--Higgs
Wilsonian program of the project. We encode a structural assumption
\begin{equation}
H_{\mathrm{nat}}\,(t, q) \;:\;
0 < t \,\wedge\, 0 < q \,\wedge\, \tfrac{t}{q} \in [0.1, 10],
\end{equation}
asserting that the tetrad-VEV scale and the quark-condensate scale
agree to within an order of magnitude. A unit-ratio existence witness
ships at $t = q$. Two falsifiers --- a super-large $t = 100\,q$ ratio
and a super-small $t = q/100$ ratio --- discharge the assumption.

This naturalness assumption is gated for numerical validation: the
present formalization records its structure as a tracked
hypothesis without committing to its truth.
