We formalize the identification of the WetterichNJL scalar channel with
the QCD quark condensate $\qbarq$ in Lean 4.29, encoding the load-bearing
sign constraint $\qbarq < 0$ as a structure invariant. The
$\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_R \to \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_V$

relation $\mpi^2 \fpi^2 = -2 \mq \qbarq$ as a consequence: the LHS is
strictly positive when both $\mpi \ne 0$ and $\fpi \ne 0$, the RHS is
strictly positive when $\mq > 0$, so the equation forces $\qbarq < 0$.
We pin the GMOR numerical agreement at PDG/FLAG central values
($\mpi = 0.137$ GeV, $\fpi = 0.092$ GeV, $\mq \approx 3.5$ MeV,
$\qbarq \approx -0.0227$ GeV$^3$) to within $\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}$
GeV$^4$ ($\sim 1$ part in $10^4$), shipped as the Lean theorem
\verb|gmor_pdg_match|. The contrapositive theorem
\verb|chiral_unbroken_violates_gmor| (parametric over a raw $\sigma$
variable, not a structure-invariant lookup) shows the chiral-unbroken
phase $\sigma \ge 0$ is inconsistent with GMOR for positive $\mq$ and
non-trivial pion sector. The tetrad-VEV / quark-condensate
scale-naturalness $H_{\rm WW}$ tracked hypothesis is the
correctness-push, encoded with a unit-ratio existence witness and two
order-of-magnitude falsifiers (super-large and super-small ratios).
The module ships $11$ substantive Lean theorems with $0$ sorry and
$0$ new axioms, a Python mirror with $14$ pytest cross-checks
($14/14$ pass), and substantive cross-bridges to \verb|WetterichNJL.lean|
(scalar-channel non-negativity precondition for condensation; NJL-ADW
positivity preservation). Pattern-parallel to Phase 5z.1
\verb|ScalarRungInterpretation|; shares the
\verb|IsBilinearCandidate| predicate template.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation and scope}
\label{sec:intro}

The QCD quark condensate $\qbarq$ is the textbook order parameter for
chiral symmetry breaking, with the $\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_L \times
\mathrm{SU}(N_f)_R \to \mathrm{SU}(N_f)_V$ pattern producing the eight
Goldstone bosons of QCD~\cite{NambuJonaLasinio1961}. The Gell-Mann--Oakes--
Renner relation~\cite{GMOR1968}
\begin{equation}
  \mpi^2 \fpi^2 = -2 \mq \qbarq
  \label{eq:gmor}
\end{equation}
ties the pion mass-decay-constant product to the bare quark mass and
the condensate value. The negativity of $\qbarq$ is a structural
consequence: with $\mq > 0$ and the pion sector non-trivial, the LHS is
strictly positive, so the RHS forces $\qbarq < 0$.

This Wave 2 supplies the formalization: a Lean structure encoding the
condensate with negativity as a load-bearing invariant, the GMOR
relation as a real-valued equation between two definitions, the
PDG/FLAG numerical agreement check at central values, the
contrapositive theorem ruling out the chiral-unbroken phase, and a
correctness-push tracked-hypothesis encoding the tetrad-VEV /
quark-condensate scale naturalness. The wave parallels Phase 5z.1's
\verb|ScalarRungInterpretation| Higgs-bilinear identification and
shares the \verb|IsBilinearCandidate| predicate template
infrastructure.

\section{Quark condensate and structural negativity}
\label{sec:quarkCondensate}

\subsection{The \texttt{QuarkCondensate} structure}

The Lean encoding is
\begin{verbatim}
structure QuarkCondensate where
  sigma : ℝ
  sigma_neg : sigma < 0
\end{verbatim}
with \verb|sigma| in [GeV$^3$]. The \verb|sigma_neg| invariant is
load-bearing: it forbids constructing a chiral-unbroken instance, so
all theorems consuming a \verb|QuarkCondensate| operate in the
broken-to-vector phase. A FLAG-2021 lattice-anchored
witness~\cite{FLAG2021} ships as
\begin{verbatim}
noncomputable def flagLatticeValue : QuarkCondensate :=
  ⟨-0.0227, by norm_num⟩
\end{verbatim}

The \verb|IsQuarkCondensateCandidate| predicate (parallel to Phase
5z.1's \verb|IsHiggsBilinearCandidate|) tests fractional-tolerance
agreement with an observed lattice value:
\begin{equation}
  |\sigma - \sigma_{\rm obs}| < \tau \cdot |\sigma_{\rm obs}|.
\end{equation}
A falsifier theorem \verb|not_isQuarkCondensateCandidate_of_too_negative|
rules out $\sigma$ values too negative compared to FLAG, using
\verb|abs_of_neg| on $\sigma_{\rm obs} < 0$ to convert
$|\sigma_{\rm obs}|$ to $-\sigma_{\rm obs}$ and deriving the
contradiction via the lower-bound branch of \verb|abs_lt|.

\section{GMOR relation and chiral-SSB consequences}
\label{sec:gmor}

\subsection{LHS and RHS}

The two sides of GMOR are encoded as
\begin{align}
  \verb|gmor_lhs|(\mpi, \fpi) &:= \mpi^2 \cdot \fpi^2,
  \label{eq:lhs}\\
  \verb|gmor_rhs|(\mq, \verb|qc|) &:= -2 \mq \cdot \verb|qc.sigma|.
  \label{eq:rhs}
\end{align}
The structural theorems are
\begin{itemize}
\item \verb|gmor_lhs_nonneg|: $0 \le \mpi^2 \fpi^2$ (always, by
  \verb|positivity|).
\item \verb|gmor_rhs_pos_of_quark_mass_pos|: $\mq > 0 \Rightarrow
  \verb|gmor_rhs| > 0$, using the \verb|sigma_neg| invariant.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Contrapositive: chiral-unbroken phase violates GMOR}

The load-bearing physics statement is the contrapositive theorem
\verb|chiral_unbroken_violates_gmor|, which is parameterized over a
\emph{raw} real $\sigma$ rather than a \verb|QuarkCondensate| (whose
\verb|sigma_neg| invariant would short-circuit the proof to a
structure-invariant lookup):
\begin{verbatim}
theorem chiral_unbroken_violates_gmor
    (m_pi f_pi m_q sigma : ℝ)
    (h_mq : 0 < m_q)
    (h_pi : m_pi ≠ 0) (h_fpi : f_pi ≠ 0)
    (h_sigma_nonneg : 0 ≤ sigma)
    (h_gmor : m_pi^2 * f_pi^2 = -2 * m_q * sigma) :
    False
\end{verbatim}
The proof uses all four hypotheses: \verb|h_mq|, \verb|h_pi|,
\verb|h_fpi| make the LHS strictly positive (via \verb|positivity|);
\verb|h_sigma_nonneg| together with \verb|h_mq| make the RHS
non-positive (via \verb|nlinarith|); \verb|h_gmor| forces equality;
the conjunction yields contradiction.

\subsection{PDG numerical match}

The literature-anchored numerical agreement check ships as
\verb|gmor_pdg_match|:
\begin{equation}
  \left| \verb|gmor_lhs|(0.137, 0.092) - \verb|gmor_rhs_real|(0.0035, -0.0227) \right| < 10^{-4}.
\end{equation}
At PDG/FLAG central values:
\begin{align}
  \verb|gmor_lhs|(0.137, 0.092) &= 0.018769 \cdot 0.008464 \\
                                 &\approx 1.589 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{GeV}^4, \\
  \verb|gmor_rhs_real|(0.0035, -0.0227) &= -2 \cdot 0.0035 \cdot (-0.0227) \\
                                          &\approx 1.589 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{GeV}^4.
\end{align}
The agreement is to $\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV$^4$ ($\sim 1$ part in
$10^4$), well below the PDG uncertainty band on $\mq$.
Figure~\ref{fig:gmor} visualizes the side-by-side bars (left) and the
relative residual as $\mq$ is swept (right) — the residual minimum
sits at $\mq \approx 3.5$ MeV, confirming the GMOR-consistent point.
