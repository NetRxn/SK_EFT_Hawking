emergent-gravity black holes:\\
BCH four laws across Schwarzschild and BEC-acoustic substrates}

\author{John G.\ Roehm}

\begin{abstract}
The Bardeen--Carter--Hawking (BCH) four laws of black-hole mechanics
admit two structurally distinct realizations on an emergent-gravity
ADW substrate, separated by a critical mass
$\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf) = \Nf \Ladw / (12\pi \aADW)$. Above $\Mc$
the BH follows the textbook Hawking 1975 profile
$\Thawk = \hbar/(8\pi M)$~\cite{Hawking1975CMP}: $dM/dt < 0$,
$d\Thawk/dt > 0$ (heats as it evaporates), finite evaporation time
$t_{\rm evap} \sim M^3$. Below $\Mc$ the BH follows the BEC-acoustic
backreaction profile of Balbinot, Fagnocchi, Fabbri, Procopio
(PRD~\textbf{71}, 064019)~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}: linear-in-$t$
cooling with extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$, so $\Thawk \to 0$ in
\emph{infinite} time --- the analog of near-extremal
Reissner--Nordstr\"om. The genuine regime partition is the
\emph{sign-flip of $d\Thawk/dt$ during Hawking-radiation
evaporation}: positive in the Schwarzschild branch, negative in the
BEC-acoustic branch. We formalize both regimes in
\texttt{BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean} (\bhThermoTotal{} theorems
and lemmas; zero \texttt{sorry}; zero new axioms) and ship the
classification, the regime-partition criterion, and the four-laws
bundle in tracked-hypothesis mode (Outcome-3 per the project's
discipline for non-derived structural identifications). The full
second law uses Glorioso--Liu SK-EFT entropy-current
monotonicity~\cite{GloriosoLiu2018,CrossleyGloriosoLiu2017,GloriosoCrossleyLiu2017}
without invoking pointwise NEC. The substrate-response coefficient
ansatz $\dADW = (\aADW - 1)\Ladw$ vanishes at $\aADW = 1$ (the bare
Sakharov--Adler limit) and provides four falsifier theorems for any
candidate ADW substrate. The critical-mass functional form
$\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf)$ is project-original and explicitly disclosed
as such: no published derivation exists.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

The Bardeen--Carter--Hawking four laws codify a thermodynamic
structure on stationary BH solutions of classical general relativity.
On a Schwarzschild background, quantum field theory endows the
surface gravity $\kappa$ with a thermal interpretation as the Hawking
temperature $\Thawk = \hbar\kappa/(2\pi)$~\cite{Hawking1975CMP},
and the resulting heat-capacity sign $C(M) = (dM/d\Thawk)$ is
\emph{negative}: the BH \emph{heats} as it loses mass to Hawking
evaporation, with finite evaporation time $t_{\rm evap}
\propto M_0^3$.

Analog and emergent-gravity systems offer a structurally different
evaporation pattern. In the BEC-acoustic backreaction analysis of
Balbinot, Fagnocchi, Fabbri, Procopio~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD},
backreaction of thermally radiated phonons on the fluid background
\emph{shrinks} the sonic horizon and \emph{decreases} $\Thawk$ in
time, with linear-in-$t$ leading order
\begin{equation}
  \Thawk(t) = \frac{\hbar c}{2\pi}\,\kappa \left[
    1 - \frac{563}{720\pi}\,\varepsilon\,\kappa^{3}\,c\,A_{0}\,t
  \right]
  \label{eq:Tsonic}
\end{equation}
and asymptotic extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$ giving $\Thawk \to 0$ at
\emph{infinite} time --- the analog of near-extremal
Reissner--Nordstr\"om. Reference~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD} itself
contrasts this regime with the moving-domain-wall analog in
$^{3}$He-A studied by Jacobson--Koike~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002,
JacobsonVolovik1998}, which has \emph{non}-vanishing
end-temperature of the evaporation process and therefore behaves
more like Schwarzschild.

This Letter formalizes both regimes within a single Lean module
\texttt{BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean} and ships the
sign-of-$d\Thawk/dt$ regime partition as the load-bearing
correctness-push theorem. We do \emph{not} attempt a single
closed-form $\Thawk(M)$ that interpolates between regimes; we treat
the existence of a critical mass $\Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf)$ separating
them as a tracked-hypothesis bundle $H_{\rm RegimePartition}$ with
each branch's sign claim grounded in its primary literature. The
choice of $\Mc$ is the dimensional ansatz $\Nf \Ladw / (12\pi \aADW)$
implied by Sakharov--Visser $1/G_N \propto \Nf \Lambda^2$ at the
Wave-1 emergent-gravity scale; this is project-original and
explicitly flagged.

\section{Setup and main result}
\label{sec:result}

\paragraph{Data carriers and regime classifier.}
\texttt{BHData} bundles bulk-thermodynamic observables
$(M, Q, J, A, r_h, \Thawk, \kappa)$ with positivity hypotheses for
the dimensional quantities; \texttt{ADWParams} bundles substrate
parameters $(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf, \chi_{\rm vest})$ with strict
positivity for each. The decidable \texttt{Regime} inductive type
has three constructors (\texttt{Schwarzschild}, \texttt{Boundary},
\texttt{ADWExtremality}) and a piecewise classifier
\begin{equation}
  \texttt{classify}(b, p) = \begin{cases}
    \texttt{Schwarzschild} & b.M > \Mc(p) \\
    \texttt{ADWExtremality} & b.M < \Mc(p) \\
    \texttt{Boundary} & b.M = \Mc(p),
  \end{cases}
  \label{eq:classify}
\end{equation}
with the critical mass
\begin{equation}
  \Mc(\aADW, \Ladw, \Nf) = \frac{\Nf\,\Ladw}{12\pi\,\aADW}.
  \label{eq:Mc}
\end{equation}
The classifier admits three iff-theorems
(\texttt{classify\_*\_iff}); positivity of $\Mc$ follows from
positivity of all input fields.

\paragraph{Two-regime profiles.}
For $b.M > \Mc(p)$, the textbook Hawking 1975
result~\cite{Hawking1975CMP} (in natural units) is
$\Thawk^{\rm Schw}(M) = 1/(8\pi M)$, with
\texttt{T\_H\_schwarzschild\_pos} and
\texttt{T\_H\_schwarzschild\_strict\_decreasing} formalized.
Combined with the standard Hawking-emission $dM/dt < 0$, this
establishes $d\Thawk/dt > 0$ in the Schwarzschild branch.

For $b.M < \Mc(p)$, the BEC-acoustic backreaction of
Balbinot~\textit{et~al.}~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD} applies. Their
leading-order result is the linear-in-$t$ form Eq.~\eqref{eq:Tsonic}
with the rational extrapolation $t \sim 1/T^3$ giving
$\Thawk \to 0$ in infinite time. The Lean module's analytic anchor
for the strict-decreasing claim is the exponential alias
\begin{equation}
  \Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t) = \Thawk^{(0)} \cdot \exp(-t / \tau_{\rm cool}),
  \label{eq:Tacoustic}
\end{equation}
which matches the project's Python anchor
\texttt{src/wkb/backreaction.py} (line 449) — note that
\texttt{backreaction.py} evolves the full coupled-ODE system rather
than assuming the exponential form, with Eq.~\eqref{eq:Tacoustic}
serving as the strictly-decreasing analytic alias for proof purposes.
We prove \texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_pos},
\texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_at\_zero}, and the load-bearing
\texttt{T\_H\_acoustic\_evolution\_strict\_decreasing}: for
$\Thawk^{(0)} > 0$, $\tau_{\rm cool} > 0$, and $t_1 < t_2$,
$\Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_2) < \Thawk^{\rm acoustic}(t_1)$. This is
the BEC-acoustic regime's primary-source-grounded sign claim, with
the exponential alias preserving the strictly-decreasing structure
of the Balbinot 2005 linear-in-$t$ leading order plus the rational
$t \sim 1/T^3$ asymptote.

\paragraph{Tracked-hypothesis bundle.} Per the project's discipline
for non-derived structural identifications, we bundle the regime
partition into a Lean structure with five mutually-independent
fields (anti-$\exists$-absorption-pattern audit clean):
\begin{align*}
  H_{\rm RegimePartition}(b, p, \Thawk^{(0)}, &\dot T_{\rm evap}, \delta) :=\\
  &\quad M_{\rm c\_form\_consistent} \;:\; 0 < \Mc(p)\\
  &\;\wedge\; T_{H0\_pos} \;:\; 0 < \Thawk^{(0)}\\
  &\;\wedge\; \mathit{evap\_dT\_dt\_above} \;:\; b.M > \Mc(p) \to 0 < \dot T_{\rm evap}\\
  &\;\wedge\; \mathit{evap\_dT\_dt\_below} \;:\; b.M < \Mc(p) \to \dot T_{\rm evap} < 0\\
  &\;\wedge\; \delta\_\mathit{consistent\_with\_ansatz} \;:\;
    \delta = (\aADW - 1) \cdot \Ladw.
\end{align*}
The bundle is genuinely non-trivial: four falsifier theorems witness
the four independent ways the bundle's substantive content can be
violated.
\verb|falsifier_acoustic_decay_form| (the BEC-acoustic
strictly-decreasing claim cannot be reproduced by a non-decreasing
form);
\verb|falsifier_schwarzschild_heating| (a positive heating rate at
the Schwarzschild branch — sign claim for $b.M > \Mc$);
\verb|falsifier_third_law_form| (the regime-partition bundle's third-
law slot is not vacuous-true; $t \to \infty$ approach is required);
\verb|falsifier_alpha_ADW_dependence| (the substrate-response
coefficient $\delta$ must depend on $\aADW$ via the Wave-9 ansatz,
not be a free parameter). The correctness-push theorem
\verb|regime_partition_criterion| combines these with the classifier
to produce the explicit sign-of-$d\Thawk/dt$-flips-at-$\Mc$
statement.

\paragraph{Four laws by regime.} The four-laws bundle
$H_{\rm BCH}(p, b)$ partitions across regimes. The zeroth law
($\kappa$ constant on the horizon) and first law
($dM = (\kappa/8\pi) dA + \Omega\,dJ + \Phi\,dQ$) are
substrate-agnostic. The second law (entropy non-decrease) is
formalized via Glorioso--Liu SK-EFT entropy-current
monotonicity~\cite{GloriosoLiu2018,CrossleyGloriosoLiu2017,%
GloriosoCrossleyLiu2017} on the open Schwinger--Keldysh action,
without invoking pointwise NEC; this is the load-bearing
substrate-class generalization compared to the standard
Bekenstein--Hawking-on-area-law treatment. The third law
(Israel's strong form: $\kappa \to 0$ attainable only at infinite
affine time; equivalently, no extremal-horizon formation in finite
proper time) holds in both branches under the regime-specific
asymptotics: the BEC-acoustic branch reaches the
$\Thawk \to 0$ extremality only at $t \to \infty$ (Balbinot 2005
$t \sim 1/T^3$ extrapolation), and the Schwarzschild branch
evaporates completely at finite proper time without ever crossing
the $\kappa \to 0$ locus along the evaporation trajectory. Recent third-law revisions for
extremal BPS BHs~\cite{KehleUnger2022ThirdLaw,Reall2024ThirdLawBPS}
do not bear on the Schwarzschild branch; their applicability to the
ADW-extremality branch is a Phase-6 cross-bridge open question.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\paragraph{What is novel.} Three claims are project-original and
explicitly flagged: (1) the critical-mass functional form
$\Mc = \Nf \Ladw / (12\pi \aADW)$, no published ADW derivation
exists; (2) the BEC-acoustic exponential approximation
Eq.~\eqref{eq:Tacoustic} as the load-bearing analytic anchor for the
strict-decreasing claim, an analytic compression of the coupled-ODE
system in~\cite{Balbinot2005PRD}; (3) the substrate-response
coefficient $\dADW = (\aADW - 1)\Ladw$ as a unified four-falsifier
witness across the bundle's degrees of freedom.

\paragraph{What is not asserted.} We do \emph{not} derive a single
closed-form $\Thawk(M)$ interpolating the regimes; the boundary
$M = \Mc$ is a discrete-classification boundary in the inductive
type, not a smooth crossover. We do \emph{not} prove that the
Glorioso--Liu second law operates strictly identically in the
two regimes; entropy-current monotonicity holds in each regime under
its respective hypothesis bundle, but the cross-regime entropy
matching at the boundary is a Phase-6 cross-bridge.

\paragraph{Comparison: Jacobson--Koike contrast.} The
moving-domain-wall analog in $^{3}$He-A studied by
Jacobson--Koike~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002} gives a heating profile
($\Thawk(v) = \Thawk^{(0)}(1 - v^2/c_\perp^2)$ monotonically
decreasing in $v$, but the back-reaction \emph{slows} the soliton
$dv/dt < 0$, so $\Thawk$ \emph{increases} in time). This regime is
structurally similar to Schwarzschild, not to near-extremal
Reissner--Nordstr\"om. We cite~\cite{JacobsonKoike2002,
JacobsonVolovik1998} only as the contrast case, not as the cooling
anchor.

\paragraph{Substrate-class context.} The $^{3}$He-A cell and the
BEC-acoustic substrate are members of complementary
Sakharov-criterion classes. The Volovik--Jannes formalisation in the
program's
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/JacobsonThermoGRDarkEnergy.lean} (Phase 6m
Track C) shows that $^{3}$He-A satisfies all four Sakharov
conditions (\texttt{JTGR7}: emergent IR Lorentz, universal coupling,
induced Newton constant, and the $\Lambda_{\mathrm{eff}} =
\Lambda_{\mathrm{HK}}$ equilibrium identity), while the
Finazzi--Liberati--Sindoni acoustic-BEC violates condition (ii)
(universal coupling: depletion-sector atoms do not propagate on the
BEC effective metric; \texttt{JTGR8}). The Phase 6n unification
(\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/CrooksAnalogHawking/SakharovHorizonCrooks.lean})
shows that the four Sakharov conditions are biconditional with the
horizon-Crooks detailed-balance identity at the local Rindler
horizon (\texttt{horizonCrooks\_substrate\_partition}; the
companion deep paper~\cite{Roehm2026D3} carries the
full subsection). The regime partition formalised here
(sign-flip of $d\Thawk/dt$) is \emph{not} directly equivalent to
Sakharov-criterion satisfaction: the BEC-acoustic ($M < M_c$)
cooling regime persists \emph{despite} Sakharov-criterion failure
on the FLS substrate, because the regime partition is a
thermodynamic-evolution claim about $\Thawk(t)$ rather than a
substrate-induced-gravity claim. The two classifications
(Sakharov-criterion + regime partition) are independent program
axes; horizon-Crooks unifies them only at the substrate-induced-
gravity layer (the Jacobson 1995 equilibrium-Clausius
reading~\cite{JacobsonEFE1995} of entropic gravity), not at the
thermodynamic-evolution layer of the regime classifier. The
fluctuation-theorem reading of the unification is consistent only
with the equilibrium-Clausius derivation of the Einstein equation;
it does not entail Verlinde-class entropic-force gravity, which the
program separately disfavours at $\geq 5\sigma$ across eight
independent observational checks (Phase 6m Track B).

\paragraph{Reproducibility.} The result is reproducible in two
layers. The Lean layer is \texttt{BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean}
(\bhThermoTotal{} theorems and lemmas, zero \texttt{sorry}, zero new
axioms, zero \texttt{maxHeartbeats} overrides) at the project's
pinned Mathlib4 commit. The Python layer is
\texttt{src/wkb/backreaction.py} (the BEC-acoustic ODE evolution at
line 449) and the
\texttt{src/core/visualizations.py:fig\_T\_H\_evolution\_regime\_partition}
visualization. All numerics quoted in the abstract and figure
caption are committed-pinned.

\paragraph{Outlook.} The Glorioso--Liu second law's all-orders
generalization~\cite{Kirklin2024GSLAllOrders} and the entropy
current's pull-back to the SK-double~\cite{FaulknerSperanza2024} are
the natural Phase-6 cross-bridges for promoting the
\bhThermoTotal{}-theorem regime-partition bundle to a fully open-
system second-law derivation in both branches.
