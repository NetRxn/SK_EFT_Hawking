- **Gate:** NumericalFreshness (count-literal drift, carried from

- **Location:** `papers/I2/paper_draft.tex:500-515` (per-module

- **Observed:** §5 declares the per-module counts as `QSqrt2 (11)`,

  `QCyc5Ext (36)`, `QCyc15 (18)`, `QCyc15SqrtPhi (10)`,
  `QCyc16 (12)`. Sum = 161. Paper §5 line 515 reads "The
  cumulative count is approximately 161 declarations

  \texttt{noncomputable def} + \texttt{structure} + \texttt{class}
  + \texttt{inductive}) across the nine algebraic-number-field
  modules under the same counting convention used in §\ref{sec:hopf}
  above; convention-sensitive recounts (e.g.\ counting
  \texttt{example} blocks or \texttt{instance} declarations) yield

- **Evidence:** Direct grep on each of the nine number-field
  modules with the canonical convention the paper itself states
  (theorem + lemma + def + noncomputable def + structure + class
  + inductive — note: the paper's stated convention does not
  include `abbrev`, but `abbrev` does not contribute to any of the

  ```
  $ for f in QSqrt2 QSqrt3 QSqrt5 QCyc3 QCyc5 QCyc5Ext QCyc15 \
            QCyc15SqrtPhi QCyc16; do

        lean/SKEFTHawking/${f}.lean
  ```

### 5.6 — 🔵 RECOMMENDED — §8 round-2 broadening conflates surface-code QEC with non-Abelian-anyon platforms

- **Gate:** NarrativeGrounding (attribution-claim,
  technical-categorization)
- **Location:** `papers/I2/paper_draft.tex:786-792` ("Ising-anyon
  braid matrices in \texttt{IsingGates.lean} match the structure of
  gates targeted by non-Abelian-anyon topological-quantum-computing
  roadmaps in the broader research community pursuing
  Majorana-fermion-based non-Abelian-anyon platforms (including
  industrial proposals such as Microsoft Quantum's, Google Quantum
  AI's surface-code work, and academic/national-lab Majorana-detection
  experiments)")
- **Observed:** The parenthetical opens by describing
  "Majorana-fermion-based non-Abelian-anyon platforms" and then
  enumerates "Microsoft Quantum's [proposal], Google Quantum AI's
  surface-code work, and academic/national-lab Majorana-detection
  experiments". Surface-code quantum error correction (Google
  Quantum AI's Sycamore + Willow surface-code experiments) is not a
  Majorana-fermion-based platform and is not a non-Abelian-anyon
  scheme; it uses Pauli stabilizer measurements on
  superconducting-qubit physical-qubit lattices and exploits the
  toric-code-class topological order, which is *abelian*. Microsoft
  Quantum's Majorana roadmap and academic Majorana-detection
  experiments are correctly grouped with Majorana-fermion non-Abelian
  topological QC; Google Quantum AI's surface-code work is in a
  different categorical bucket (stabilizer QEC on superconducting
  qubits, not topologically-protected non-Abelian-anyon braiding).
- **Evidence:** Surface code is a Pauli stabilizer code with abelian
  toric-code-class topological order; non-Abelian-anyon TQC is a
  separate and more ambitious paradigm. This is textbook (e.g.,
  Nayak et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 80, 1083 (2008), §I; Kitaev's
  fault-tolerant-by-anyon-braiding proposal vs. surface-code
  fault-tolerance-by-stabilizer-measurement). The two paradigms are
  often discussed in the same review papers but as distinct
  approaches, not as variants of the same approach.
- **Expected:** Either (a) drop the "Google Quantum AI's
  surface-code work" clause from the Majorana-fermion parenthetical,
  OR (b) restructure the sentence so that the parenthetical
  separates Majorana-non-Abelian-anyon platforms (Microsoft Quantum,
  academic/national-lab Majorana-detection) from
  surface-code-stabilizer QEC (Google Quantum AI), perhaps by saying
  "...non-Abelian-anyon platforms (including Microsoft Quantum's
  Majorana program and academic/national-lab Majorana-detection
  experiments), which is a distinct paradigm from but
  complementary to surface-code-stabilizer fault-tolerance research
  (e.g., Google Quantum AI's recent surface-code experiments)..."
- **Fix:** Remove "Google Quantum AI's surface-code work" from the
  parenthetical. Severity is **RECOMMENDED** rather than REQUIRED
  because the prose is imprecise rather than wrong (the
  surface-code work is real, the Google Quantum AI attribution is
  generic), but a careful reader in the topological-QC field will
  flag this conflation and infer the author hasn't sharply
  distinguished the two paradigms — a small but real credibility
  cost on a software paper that otherwise stays within scope.

## QI Candidate

**qi-round-broadening-introduces-new-issues.** Round-1 finding 5.4
(Microsoft Quantum uncited) was remediated by broadening to a
community-level claim, but the broadening introduced a new
categorical conflation (surface-code QEC ≠ Majorana non-Abelian-anyon
platforms — finding 5.6 above). This is a class of failure mode for
attribution-claim remediation: when a specific attribution is
removed by broadening, the broader prose may sweep in adjacent but
categorically distinct topics. **Suggested mitigation:** when a
Stage-10 / Stage-13 finding flags an uncited attribution, the
remediation diff should be re-reviewed *for what it pulls in*, not
just for whether it removes the original specific claim. A simple
rule of thumb: a remediation that turns "X's Y" into "the broader
community pursuing Y (including X, Z, W)" should run a fresh narrative
check on the new Z/W list to confirm they belong in the same
parenthetical category. This is bundle-level and cross-stage; the
per-stage prompts may not catch it.

This QI extends the round-1 QI candidate
`qi-attribution-prior-art-cross-check` — the round-1 QI suggested a
deterministic check on the *original* attribution; this round-2
extension suggests an additional deterministic check on the
*remediation diff*'s broadening targets.
