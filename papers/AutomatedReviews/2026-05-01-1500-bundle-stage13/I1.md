## QI Candidate

**Pattern: round-1 BLOCKER fixes that name-correct entries without
table-correct attribution survive deterministic supersession ledger
review but fail deterministic registry inspection.**

The round-1 3.1 finding's remediation surfaced and fixed the wrong
*name* (`chirality_wall_three_pillars` → real entry names) but
preserved the wrong *table claim* (`AXIOM_METADATA` for entries
actually in `PLACEHOLDER_THEOREMS` / `HYPOTHESIS_REGISTRY`). The
supersession ledger evidence text's "Round-2 deterministically
confirmed" was confirmed against the *paper's prose* (cite keys
match real names; line numbers match the cited file), not against
*the registry the prose names*. This is a class-of-failure pattern:
a fix can satisfy a string-level check (the round-2 claims-reviewer
verified that real entry names appear, that line numbers resolve)
without satisfying a table-level check (the entries are in the
registry the prose names them as belonging to).

**Pipeline gap:** The Stage-13 round-2 verification protocol does
not currently include a "registry attribution sanity check" — for
each `AXIOM_METADATA` / `PLACEHOLDER_THEOREMS` / `HYPOTHESIS_REGISTRY`
/ `BORDISM_HYPOTHESES` mention in paper prose, walk the prose's
citation, parse the registry it claims to live in, and assert that
the named entry is in that exact registry (not a sibling registry).

**Suggested QI register entry:** Add `validate.py CHECK 24
prose_registry_attribution` that, for each paper draft, scans for
references of the form `[REGISTRY_NAME] entry [name]` (or `entry
[name] in [REGISTRY_NAME]`) and verifies the entry is in the named
registry. The check would have caught the round-1 3.1 round-2
remediation incompleteness at re-invocation time. Owner: Phase 7a
robustness sub-wave (extend 7a.1 BUNDLE_LIFT_PROCEDURE step 13's
deterministic-verification list). Target: before bundle I2 lift.

This is a Stage 14 candidate because it generalizes beyond I1 —
every methodology paper that describes the project's structural
registries is at risk of cross-registry mis-attribution, and the
remediation is structurally identical (registry parser + cross-walk).
