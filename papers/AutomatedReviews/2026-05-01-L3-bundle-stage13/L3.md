### 5.1 — 🔴 BLOCKER — Prose enumeration of "four falsifier theorems" misrepresents the four Lean falsifier theorems

- **Gate:** LeanProofSubstance (also touches Gate 7 NarrativeGrounding)
- **Location:** `papers/L3/paper_draft.tex:199-203`
- **Observed:** Prose claim:
  > The bundle is genuinely non-trivial: four falsifier theorems witness the four ways the bundle can fail (negative $M_c$ via parameter inversion; classifier-vs-functional disagreement; $\delta_{\rm ADW} \neq 0$ at $\alpha_{\rm ADW} = 1$; classifier non-decidability). Each falsifier is load-bearing: removing any conjunct trivializes the bundle.
- **Evidence:** The actual four falsifier theorems in `lean/SKEFTHawking/BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean` are:
  - `falsifier_acoustic_decay_form` (line 628) — strict monotone-decay falsifier (a candidate that matches Balbinot at t₁ but fails to decrease cannot match at t₂)
  - `falsifier_schwarzschild_heating` (line 660) — Schwarzschild-regime sign-of-dT/dt falsifier (observed nonpositive contradicts bundle)
  - `falsifier_third_law_form` (line 688) — Israel-strong vs Kehle-Unger-bounded contradiction
  - `falsifier_alpha_ADW_dependence` (line 723) — sign of `(α_ADW − 1)·Λ_UV` for `α_ADW > 1`
- **None of the four prose-described falsifiers correspond to any of the four Lean falsifiers.** The prose lists falsifier *targets* (parameter inversion, classifier-vs-functional disagreement, `δ_ADW ≠ 0` at `α_ADW = 1`, classifier non-decidability) that **do not exist** as theorems in the module. In particular:
  - "$\delta_{\rm ADW} \neq 0$ at $\alpha_{\rm ADW} = 1$" — actually `falsifier_alpha_ADW_dependence` proves the **opposite**: it shows `0 < (α_ADW − 1)·Λ_UV` when `α_ADW > 1`, which would falsify a sign-flipped measurement; it does *not* test a `δ ≠ 0 at α = 1` measurement (which is trivially excluded by the ansatz `δ = (α−1)Λ`).
  - "negative $M_c$ via parameter inversion" — never falsified anywhere; `M_c_pos` proves it positive, not a falsifier theorem.
  - "classifier-vs-functional disagreement" — no corresponding theorem exists.
  - "classifier non-decidability" — `Regime` has `deriving DecidableEq` (line 165 inductive declaration); falsifying decidability is structurally impossible, not a substantive falsifier.
- **Expected:** Prose enumeration that reflects the actual Lean theorems. A substantive draft would read approximately:
  > Four falsifier theorems anchor the bundle: (i) `falsifier_acoustic_decay_form` — any candidate $T_H^{\rm alt}(t)$ matching the BEC-acoustic profile at $t_1$ but failing to strictly decrease must disagree at $t_2$; (ii) `falsifier_schwarzschild_heating` — observation of $dT_H/dt \le 0$ in the Schwarzschild regime contradicts the bundle's positive-sign claim; (iii) `falsifier_third_law_form` — Israel-strong (uniformly unbounded approach time on a tail of small $\varepsilon$) and Kehle-Unger-bounded (approach time bounded above by $\tau_{\max}$ on a tail) cannot both hold; (iv) `falsifier_alpha_ADW_dependence` — sign reversal of $(\alpha_{\rm ADW} - 1)\Lambda_{\rm UV}$ for $\alpha_{\rm ADW} > 1$ falsifies the substrate-response ansatz.
- **Fix:** Rewrite L3 lines 199–203 to match the actual Lean falsifiers' content. Each prose falsifier must name the corresponding Lean theorem and accurately describe its falsifying observation.
- **Why this is a BLOCKER:** Misrepresenting which propositions a Lean module proves is exactly the failure class Gate 5 exists to catch. The current prose claims four specific falsifiers, none of which exist; a reviewer or reader who consults the Lean module will see the mismatch immediately. This is also a credibility-cost finding: the project leans on Lean-grounded falsifiability claims as a primary credibility lever; misrepresenting the Lean falsifiers undermines that claim.

### 6.1 — 🔴 BLOCKER — Israel third-law parenthetical mislabels the formulation it claims to encode

- **Observed:** Prose:

The L3 parenthetical "($\kappa \to 0$ at finite proper time)" describes the **violation** form (what Kehle-Unger 2022 demonstrate as a counterexample for charged-scalar matter), not the third law itself. Compare paper27's careful handling at the same position (paper27/paper_draft.tex § discussion paragraph) — paper27 consistently uses the Israel-strong-form definition.
- **Expected:** The parenthetical must define the third law, not its violation. Replace with `($\kappa \to 0$ requires *infinite* proper time)` or `(extremality cannot be reached in finite proper time)`.
- **Fix:** Edit line 216 from `(\kappa \to 0$ at finite proper time)` to `($\kappa \to 0$ requires *infinite* proper time, i.e., extremality is unreachable in finite proper time)`. Verify the rest of the paragraph (lines 217–224) is internally consistent with this corrected definition; the existing prose ("Schwarzschild evaporates completely at finite proper time, satisfying the third law trivially via never reaching the $\kappa \to 0$ locus along the evaporation trajectory") is awkward but not contradictory once the parenthetical is corrected — though it could be clearer that "satisfying the third law trivially" means **the trajectory never approaches the extremal locus**, not that finite-time-evaporation defies the third law.

### 6.2 — 🟡 REQUIRED — `H_RegimePartition` mathematical Prop description does not match the Lean structure's actual fields

- **Gate:** AssumptionDisclosure
- **Location:** `papers/L3/paper_draft.tex:193-198`
- **Observed:** Prose Prop:

- **Evidence:** The actual Lean structure (`lean/SKEFTHawking/BHThermodynamicsFourLaws.lean:443-449`) is:

    M_c_form_consistent          : 0 < M_c p

    evap_dT_dt_above             : b.M > M_c p → 0 < dT_dt_evap

  > $H_{\rm RegimePartition}(b, p, T_H^{(0)}, \dot T, \delta) \;\equiv\; M_c(p) > 0 \;\wedge\; T_H^{(0)} > 0 \;\wedge\; (b.M > M_c \Rightarrow \dot T > 0) \;\wedge\; (b.M < M_c \Rightarrow \dot T < 0) \;\wedge\; \delta = (\alpha_{\rm ADW} - 1)\Lambda_{\rm UV}$
- **Why REQUIRED rather than BLOCKER:** the existential vs. structure-form discrepancy is recoverable in prose without changing the Lean source, and the paper does cite `regime_partition_criterion` correctly elsewhere (which actually exposes the sign-flip content). But submission requires the prose-level Prop to match the formal artifact — readers will check.

### 6.3 — 🟡 REQUIRED — Eq. (Tacoustic) misrepresents Balbinot-2005 leading order as exponential when the published primary form is linear

- **Location:** `papers/L3/paper_draft.tex:154-159`
- **Observed:** Prose:

- **Evidence:**
  - The paper's own Eq.~(eq:Tsonic) at lines 87–91 correctly cites Balbinot 2005's primary form: $T_H(t) = (\hbar c / 2\pi) \kappa [1 - (563/720\pi) \varepsilon \kappa^3 c A_0 t]$, which is **linear-in-t** to leading order.
  - The Lean module formalizes the **exponential** form `T_H_acoustic_evolution = T_H0 · exp(-t/τ_cool)` — a different functional form that happens to share the strict-decreasing property.
  - `src/wkb/backreaction.py:730-755` makes this explicit: lines 731–736 say *"The exact solution of d(kappa)/dt = -kappa²/C gives kappa(t) = kappa_0/(1 + t/tau_cool). This is hyperbolic decay (not exponential). The exponential approximation kappa ~ kappa_0 exp(-t/tau_cool) is valid only for t << tau_cool."* The exponential is a **backwards-compatibility alias** (line 755), not the canonical analytic form.
- The prose calls the exponential "the leading-order approximation" — but Balbinot's leading order is linear (Eq. (Tsonic) at line 87–91), not exponential. The exponential is an early-time-only approximation that, while strictly-decreasing, is a separate model from Balbinot's primary form.
- **Fix:** Either

  - (b) Re-formalize the Lean theorem `T_H_acoustic_evolution_strict_decreasing` against the rational form `T_H(t) = T_H^{(0)} / (1 + t/τ_cool)` (which is `backreaction.py`'s canonical solution) and rewrite Eq.~(eq:Tacoustic) accordingly. This is a more invasive fix and would re-trigger paper27 too; (a) is the cheaper resolution.
- **Cache:** the registry's `notes` field for Balbinot2005PRD says "Mirrors `src/wkb/backreaction.py` exponential-decay leading-order form" — this registry comment is itself slightly stale relative to the post-rewrite `backreaction.py` which prefers rational. Suggest updating the registry comment to cross-reference both.

## QI Candidate (systemic — bibitem-text drift across lifts)

The three CitationIntegrity BLOCKERs (Findings 1.1–1.3) share a common pattern: **paper27's bibitems are correct, the L3 bibitems were re-typeset from scratch during the lift, and the lift introduced fresh defects in the bibliographic strings even though the registry truth is correct and the cache hashes are current.**

This is a process gap in `BUNDLE_LIFT_PROCEDURE.md`'s lift step: the procedure assumes registry-truth verification suffices, but registry-truth is not what readers see — the **bibitem text in the .tex** is what readers see, and that string can drift independently.

**Proposed QI:** add a step to `BUNDLE_LIFT_PROCEDURE.md`: when a single source paper's bibitems are inherited by a new bundle, **the bibitem .tex strings should be copy-pasted verbatim from the source paper, not re-typeset**. A `validate.py --check bundle_bibitem_inheritance` script could compare the L3-bundle bibitem text against paper27's bibitem text for shared bibkeys and flag any divergence; this would catch all three of 1.1–1.3 mechanically. (Long-term, a single canonical bibitem-renderer that consumes registry truth and emits identical .tex across paper27 + L3 would be the cleanest fix.)

This QI is added to the supersession ledger / Phase 7a robustness register as a candidate for the next infrastructure pass; not blocking on this review's remediation, but worth picking up after the immediate fixes ship.
