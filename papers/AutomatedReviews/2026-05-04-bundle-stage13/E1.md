#### 5.1 — 🔵 ADVISORY — Universal-κ-independence claim is conditional on Γ(ω) = 1
- **Gate:** NarrativeGrounding
- **Location:** `papers/E1/paper_draft.tex:38-40, 159-161, 174-176, 287-288`
- **Observed:** Paper states "$\omega < \ln(3)/(2\pi)\,\kappa \approx 0.175\,\kappa$ (universal, $\kappa$-independent)" and "model-independent spectral signature".
- **Evidence:** The `0.175κ` crossing follows from solving `1/(e^{2π ω/κ} − 1) = 0.5` — but this assumes `Γ(ω) = 1` (the unit-greybody / smooth-horizon limit explicitly used in body §4 numerics). Eq. \eqref{eq:gain} shows G = Γ(ω)/(e^{2π ω/κ_eff}−1); for Γ ≠ 1 the threshold shifts to `ω/κ_eff = ln(2Γ+1)/(2π)`. Additionally κ_eff ≈ κ(1 − c_1 D²) means the `0.175κ_eff` in `κ_eff` units differs from `0.175κ` in `κ` units by the dispersive correction (≈ 19% at smooth horizon, ≈ 46% at steep horizon).
- **Expected:** The universal `0.175` value is universal *only* in the limits (i) Γ → 1 (smooth-horizon greybody saturation), and (ii) D → 0 (no dispersive correction to κ_eff). Both hold approximately at the smooth-horizon baseline (D = 0.60 ⇒ κ_eff ≈ 0.81 κ ⇒ shifted threshold = 0.175 × 0.81 ≈ 0.142 κ in `κ` units). The paper's Stage-9 carry-forward item #5 flagged this; a single qualifier sentence in §4 ("In the Γ(ω) = 1 / smooth-horizon limit, the threshold ...") would convert the present claim into a defensible model-independent statement *within stated assumptions*.
- **Fix:** ADVISORY only — non-blocking for Tier-4 letter. Add one parenthetical "($\Gamma(\omega) \to 1$ limit; full greybody profile shifts the crossing by an O($D^2$) amount)" in §4 line ~159 to forestall referee challenge. Tier-4 letter gets latitude on this.
- **Cache:** derived.

## QI Candidate

### QI-E1-1 — Tier-4 device-parameter verification can be partially automated via provenance-table cross-product

The bulk of the Stage 13 work for a Tier-4 device-parameter letter consists of recomputing dependent quantities (`T_H`, `D`, `δ_disp`, `G(ω)`) from primary-source values in `PARAMETER_PROVENANCE` and comparing to in-paper rounded values. This is mechanical and produces high-value findings (parameter drift detection within 0.5% tolerance) at low cognitive cost.

**Proposal:** Add a `validate.py --check tier4_device_audit` validator that, given a paper's tier-4 status and a `device_parameter_audit_table` declaration in `bundle_metadata.json`, cross-products provenance values × derivation formulas (T_H, D, δ_disp, G(ω) at table frequencies) and emits FAIL on >0.5% mismatch, WARN on rounding-only drift. This converts a Tier-4 reviewer pass from ~10 hand-recomputations to a single CI gate. Generalizes to any bundle whose Tier 4 readiness hinges on device-spec recovery (E1 today; future E3+ if more experimental letters are drafted).

This is not E1-specific damage, but a systemic productivity opportunity surfaced by the review.
