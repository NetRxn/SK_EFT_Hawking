# Adversarial Review — Bundle D1 (Formally Verified Analog Hawking Radiation Across Three Platforms)

## Summary

Bundle D1 review found **20 BLOCKER**, **5 REQUIRED**, **4 RECOMMENDED** findings.
Citation Integrity (Gate 1) is the dominant failure class: of 26 verifiable external
bibitems, at least 9 carry the wrong title, wrong author list, wrong arXiv ID, or
wrong venue — including a wrong-target citation (Jana2020 pointing at a paper that
does not match the bibitem title or the CITATION_REGISTRY entry, which itself has
the wrong arXiv ID). Three priority/superlative phrases ("strongest quantitative
test", "strongest-yet universal prediction", "To our knowledge, no prior published
work") are flagged for removal under user policy 2026-05-11. The "Stage-13 anchor
δ_diss = (1+ξ²)⁻¹" claim in §2.3 is an orphan formula — neither implemented in
`src/core/formulas.py`/`transonic_background.py` nor proved in any Lean module. The
Heidelberg platform is labelled `^{87}$Rb BEC` in Table I but is in fact `^{39}$K`
per `constants.py` / `transonic_background.py:heidelberg_K39`. The 109/119 = 92%
reuse claim has no reproducible artifact ("Phase 5w cross-platform reuse counter"
referenced in `docs/agents/claims-reviewer-bundle-prompts.md` does not exist as a
script). Stale theorem-count literal (`5812` vs canonical `5855`/`5830`). Two
cross-bundle bridges (D1↔E1, D1↔E2) verified consistent on shared device specs.
The paper is **not submission-ready** as-is.
