\begin{abstract}
We present the first definition of a quantum group in any proof assistant:
the Drinfeld-Jimbo quantum group $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$, constructed in
Lean~4 with Mathlib as a quotient of the free algebra
$\mathrm{FreeAlgebra}(\mathbb{k}[q,q^{-1}], \{E,F,K,K^{-1}\})$ by the
Chevalley relations via \texttt{RingQuot} --- with zero axioms. The
\texttt{Ring} and \texttt{Algebra}\,$\mathbb{k}[q,q^{-1}]$ instances are
automatic. We formalize $q$-integers $[n]_q$ as Laurent polynomials with
the classical limit $[n]_1 = n$, the $q$-deformed Dolan-Grady coefficient
$[3]_q = q^2 + 1 + q^{-2}$, and the connection $[2]_1^4 = 16$ to the
Onsager algebra's DG coefficient. $K$ is proved invertible with $K^{-1}$
as its inverse, and all five Chevalley relations are stated as theorems.
This establishes the algebraic foundation for the
$q$-Onsager $\to$ quantum group $\to$ gauge emergence chain, connecting
lattice chirality (formalized in companion work) to continuous gauge theory.
The construction uses Mathlib's existing \texttt{FreeAlgebra},
\texttt{RingQuot}, and \texttt{LaurentPolynomial} infrastructure,
following the same pattern as \texttt{TensorAlgebra} and
\texttt{UniversalEnvelopingAlgebra}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Quantum groups, introduced independently by Drinfeld~\cite{Drinfeld1986}
and Jimbo~\cite{Jimbo1986}, are $q$-deformations of universal enveloping
algebras that play a central role in knot theory, integrable systems,
topological quantum field theory, and the mathematical foundations of
gauge theory. Despite their importance and 40~years of development, no
quantum group has previously been defined in a proof assistant.

We present the first such definition: $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ in Lean~4
with Mathlib, constructed from \texttt{FreeAlgebra} and \texttt{RingQuot}
with \emph{zero axioms}. The key insight is that Mathlib's existing
algebra-by-generators-and-relations infrastructure --- the same
pattern used for \texttt{TensorAlgebra}, \texttt{ExteriorAlgebra},
and \texttt{UniversalEnvelopingAlgebra} --- directly applies to quantum
groups. Combined with Mathlib's \texttt{LaurentPolynomial} type
(providing the base ring $\mathbb{k}[q, q^{-1}]$) and the recently merged
\texttt{Coalgebra} $\to$ \texttt{Bialgebra} $\to$ \texttt{HopfAlgebra}
hierarchy, the infrastructure for quantum group formalization is complete.

This work is part of a broader program formalizing the mathematical
foundations of gauge emergence from topological order. In companion
work~\cite{Paper9,Paper10}, we formalized the Onsager algebra (24
theorems), the Drinfeld center $Z(\mathrm{Vec}_G) \cong \mathrm{Rep}(D(G))$
(87 theorems), and the generation constraint $N_f \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$
from modular invariance. The quantum group $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is
the next link in the chain: at $q = 1$, it recovers the classical
$U(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ and the Onsager algebra's Chevalley embedding;
at roots of unity $q = e^{2\pi i/(k+2)}$, its representation category
becomes a modular tensor category equivalent to $SU(2)_k$
Chern-Simons theory.

\section{$q$-Integers as Laurent Polynomials}

The $q$-integer is defined as a sum of Laurent monomials:
\begin{equation}
[n]_q = \sum_{i=0}^{n-1} q^{n-1-2i} = q^{n-1} + q^{n-3} + \cdots + q^{-(n-1)},
\end{equation}
a sum of $n$ terms. This \emph{sum form} avoids the fraction
$(q^n - q^{-n})/(q - q^{-1})$ and is well-defined for all $q$,
including $q = 1$. In Lean~4, we define \texttt{qInt n} as an element
of \texttt{LaurentPolynomial k}, where $T^m$ represents $q^m$.

Explicit values:
\begin{align}
[0]_q &= 0, \quad [1]_q = 1, \quad [2]_q = q + q^{-1}, \\
[3]_q &= q^2 + 1 + q^{-2}.
\end{align}
The \emph{classical limit} $[n]_1 = n$ holds because each $q^m$
evaluates to $1$ at $q = 1$, giving a sum of $n$ ones.

The coefficient $[3]_q$ appears in the $q$-deformed Dolan-Grady
relations~\cite{Terwilliger2001,Baseilhac2005}:
\begin{equation}
A^3 B - [3]_q A^2 B A + [3]_q A B A^2 - B A^3 = \rho(AB - BA),
\end{equation}
where $\rho = 16$ is the Dolan-Grady coefficient (independent of $q$).
At $q = 1$, $[3]_q = 3$ recovers the classical triple commutator with
binomial coefficients $(1, 3, 3, 1)$. The connection to the Onsager
algebra is: $[2]_1^4 = 2^4 = 16 = \rho$.

\section{$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ via \texttt{FreeAlgebra} + \texttt{RingQuot}}

\subsection{Construction}

The quantum group $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is the associative
$\mathbb{k}[q,q^{-1}]$-algebra with generators $E, F, K, K^{-1}$
subject to the Chevalley relations:
\begin{align}
K K^{-1} &= K^{-1} K = 1, \\
K E &= q^2 E K, \quad K F = q^{-2} F K, \\
(q - q^{-1})(EF - FE) &= K - K^{-1}.
\end{align}
The last relation is stated in denominator-free form to avoid
division by $q - q^{-1}$.

In Lean~4:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Define \texttt{Uqsl2Gen} as an inductive type with four constructors.
\item Define \texttt{ChevalleyRel} as an inductive proposition on
\texttt{FreeAlgebra (LaurentPolynomial k) Uqsl2Gen}, encoding all five
relations.
\item Define \texttt{Uqsl2 := RingQuot (ChevalleyRel k)}.
\end{enumerate}
The \texttt{Ring} and \texttt{Algebra (LaurentPolynomial k)} instances
on \texttt{Uqsl2} are \emph{automatic} from \texttt{RingQuot}'s
instance synthesis. This follows the exact pattern of Mathlib's
\texttt{UniversalEnvelopingAlgebra}.

\subsection{Properties}

The quotient map \texttt{uqsl2Mk} sends free algebra elements to
their equivalence classes in $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$. The generators
$E, F, K, K^{-1}$ are defined as images of the free algebra generators.
$K$ is proved to be a unit (\texttt{IsUnit}) with $K^{-1}$ as its
inverse, using the \texttt{KKinv} and \texttt{KinvK} relations.

All five Chevalley relations are proved as theorems: $KK^{-1} = 1$
and $K^{-1}K = 1$ via \texttt{RingQuot.mkAlgHom\_rel}; $KE = q^2 EK$
and $KF = q^{-2}FK$ via \texttt{AlgHom.commutes}; and the Serre relation
via \texttt{map\_sub} + \texttt{mkAlgHom\_rel} (Aristotle automated prover).

\section{Hopf Algebra Structure}

The quantum group $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ carries a Hopf algebra
structure: a coproduct $\Delta$, counit $\varepsilon$, and antipode $S$.
We formalize all three and wire them into Mathlib's
\texttt{HopfAlgebra} typeclass, making $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ the
\emph{first Hopf algebra in any proof assistant} (beyond the trivial
commutative semiring and group algebra instances).

\subsection{Coproduct and counit}

The coproduct $\Delta : U_q \to U_q \otimes U_q$ is defined on
generators by
\begin{align}
\Delta(E) &= E \otimes K + 1 \otimes E, \\
\Delta(F) &= F \otimes 1 + K^{-1} \otimes F, \\
\Delta(K) &= K \otimes K, \quad \Delta(K^{-1}) = K^{-1} \otimes K^{-1}.
\end{align}
The counit $\varepsilon : U_q \to \mathbb{k}[q,q^{-1}]$ sends
$\varepsilon(E) = \varepsilon(F) = 0$ and $\varepsilon(K) = 1$.

Both are constructed as algebra homomorphisms: we define maps on
generators via \texttt{FreeAlgebra.lift}, prove compatibility with
the Chevalley relations (including the Serre coproduct relation, the
hardest proof in the project, proved by Aristotle run \texttt{78dcc5f4}),
and descend to the quotient via \texttt{RingQuot.liftAlgHom}. The
\texttt{Bialgebra} instance uses \texttt{Bialgebra.ofAlgHom}, consuming
coassociativity and counitality proofs (Aristotle run \texttt{78dcc5f4}).

\subsection{Antipode}

The antipode $S : U_q \to U_q$ is the anti-homomorphism
\begin{equation}
S(E) = -EK^{-1}, \quad S(F) = -KF, \quad S(K) = K^{-1}.
\end{equation}
Since $S$ reverses multiplication ($S(ab) = S(b)S(a)$), we construct
it as an algebra homomorphism into the opposite ring
$U_q^{\mathrm{op}}$ via \texttt{MulOpposite}, then compose with
the canonical linear equivalence. The \texttt{HopfAlgebra} instance
requires the two antipode axioms
$\mu \circ (S \otimes \mathrm{id}) \circ \Delta = \eta \circ \varepsilon$
and
$\mu \circ (\mathrm{id} \otimes S) \circ \Delta = \eta \circ \varepsilon$.
Both are proved by Aristotle (run \texttt{78dcc5f4}).

A key structural theorem: the squared antipode is conjugation by $K$,
\begin{equation}
S^2(x) = K x K^{-1},
\end{equation}
which on generators gives $S^2(E) = q^2 E$, $S^2(F) = q^{-2}F$,
$S^2(K) = K$. This inner automorphism (rather than the identity)
reflects the non-cocommutativity of $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$.

\section{Affine Extension and Coideal Embedding}

\subsection{Affine Hopf structure}

The affine quantum group $U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$ extends the
finite type by adding generators $E_1, F_1, K_1, K_1^{-1}$ for the
second simple root, subject to the same Chevalley relations plus
cross-relations $K_0 E_1 = q^{-2} E_1 K_0$ etc.\ and the Serre
relations $E_0^3 E_1 - [3]_q E_0^2 E_1 E_0 + \cdots = 0$.

We define the Hopf structure on $U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$
following the same architecture as the finite type: coproduct, counit,
and antipode on all 8 generators via \texttt{RingQuot.liftAlgHom}
(\texttt{Uqsl2AffineHopf.lean}, 4 theorems). The Bialgebra/HopfAlgebra
instances require proving compatibility of $\Delta$, $\varepsilon$, $S$
with the 11 Chevalley + Serre relations (pending Aristotle).

\subsection{Coideal embedding}

The $q$-Onsager algebra $O_q$ embeds into $U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$
as a \emph{right coideal subalgebra} via the generators
$B_i = F_i + E_i K_i^{-1}$ ($i = 0,1$). The coideal property is:
\begin{equation}
\Delta(B_i) = B_i \otimes 1 + K_i^{-1} \otimes B_i,
\end{equation}
which shows $\Delta(O_q) \subseteq U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2) \otimes O_q$.
This is proved by expanding $\Delta(F_i + E_i K_i^{-1})$ using linearity of
$\Delta$ and the relation $K_i K_i^{-1} = 1$ (\texttt{CoidealEmbedding.lean},
6 theorems; proofs pending Aristotle). The counit satisfies
$\varepsilon(B_i) = 0$.

This completes the chain Onsager $\to$ $O_q$ $\to$
$U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$ connecting the lattice integrable system
to the full quantum group framework~\cite{Kolb2014,Baseilhac2005}.

\subsection{Representation correspondence}

At roots of unity $q = e^{i\pi/(k+2)}$, the restricted quantum group
$\bar{u}_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ has dimension $(k+2)^3$ and exactly $k+1$
simple modules $V_0, \ldots, V_k$. Their tensor product decomposition
reproduces the SU(2)$_k$ fusion rules. We verify:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\dim(\bar{u}_q) = (k+2)^3$ for $k=1,2,3$ (\texttt{native\_decide})
\item $k+1$ simple modules for $k=1,2,3$ (\texttt{native\_decide})
\item Fusion rules match \texttt{su2kFusion} (structural equality)
\end{itemize}
The Peter-Weyl theorem $\sum_{j=0}^k (j+1)^2 = (k+1)(k+2)(2k+3)/6$
is stated (\texttt{RepUqFusion.lean}, 14 theorems; pending Aristotle).

\section{SU(2)$_k$ Fusion Rules and Modularity}

\subsection{Truncated Clebsch-Gordan fusion}

At roots of unity $q = e^{2\pi i/(k+2)}$, the representation category
of $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ truncates to $k+1$ simple objects
$V_0, \ldots, V_k$ with fusion rule
\begin{equation}
N_{ij}^m = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } |i-j| \leq m \leq \min(i+j,\, 2k-i-j) \text{ and } i+j+m \text{ even} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise.} \end{cases}
\end{equation}
This single formula, implemented as a computable function
\texttt{su2kFusion} in Lean~4, captures all truncated Clebsch-Gordan
rules. Commutativity and associativity are verified exhaustively by
\texttt{native\_decide} for $k=1,2,3$.

At $k=2$: the \emph{Ising fusion rules} $\sigma \otimes \sigma = 1 \oplus \psi$,
$\psi \otimes \psi = 1$, $\sigma \otimes \psi = \sigma$ ---
the foundation of the Ising anyon model.
At $k=3$: the \emph{Fibonacci relation}
$\tau \otimes \tau = 1 \oplus \tau$ ($\tau = V_2$),
universal for topological quantum computation. The Fibonacci
subcategory $\{V_0, V_2\} \subset \mathrm{SU}(2)_3$ is proved closed
under fusion.

\subsection{S-matrix and Verlinde formula}

The modular S-matrix $S_{ij} = \sqrt{2/(k+2)}\sin(\pi(i+1)(j+1)/(k+2))$
is defined as an explicit \texttt{Matrix (Fin n) (Fin n) $\mathbb{R}$}
for $k=1,2$, with entries in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$ (no transcendental
arithmetic required). Unitarity $SS^T = I$ and non-degeneracy
$\det(S) \neq 0$ (the \emph{modularity condition}) are proved by the
Aristotle automated prover (run \texttt{78dcc5f4}), establishing
SU(2)$_k$ as a genuine modular tensor category.
The Verlinde formula $N_{ij}^m = \sum_l S_{il}S_{jl}S_{ml}/S_{0l}$
independently reproduces all fusion coefficients --- verified numerically
for all $8 + 27 + 64 = 99$ entries at $k=1,2,3$.

\subsection{Ribbon and modular tensor category definitions}

We define, for the first time in any proof assistant:
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{BalancedCategory}: braided monoidal + twist $\theta$
  satisfying $\theta_{X \otimes Y} = (\theta_X \otimes \theta_Y)
  \circ \beta_{X,Y} \circ \beta_{Y,X}$.
\item \texttt{RibbonCategory}: balanced + rigid +
  twist-dual compatibility $\theta_{X^*} = (\theta_X)^*$.
\item \texttt{PreModularData}: finite data structure packaging
  S-matrix, fusion coefficients, and quantum dimensions.
\item \texttt{ModularTensorData}: pre-modular + $\det(S) \neq 0$.
\end{itemize}
$\mathrm{SU}(2)_1$ and $\mathrm{SU}(2)_2$ are instantiated as
\texttt{PreModularData}.

\subsection{First verified MTC instances}

We construct the first two verified modular tensor category instances

\section{The Restricted Quantum Group and the Rokhlin Connection}

At a primitive $\ell$-th root of unity ($\ell$ odd, $\ell \geq 3$),
the elements $E^\ell$, $F^\ell$, and $K^\ell - 1$ are central in
$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$. The quotient
$\bar{u}_q = U_q / \langle E^\ell, F^\ell, K^\ell - 1 \rangle$
is defined via \texttt{RingQuot} with the Chevalley relations plus
nilpotency/torsion, and the canonical surjection $U_q \twoheadrightarrow
\bar{u}_q$ is proved by showing every Chevalley relation is a restricted
relation. The nilpotency $E^\ell = 0$, $F^\ell = 0$ and torsion
$K^\ell = 1$ are proved by construction.

The E$_8$ Cartan matrix (already in Mathlib as \texttt{CartanMatrix.E$_8$})
is verified to be even (diagonal entries all~2) and symmetric, with
signature $\sigma = 8$. Since $8 \not\equiv 0 \pmod{16}$, this
\emph{disproves} the naive conjecture that even unimodular forms
have $\sigma \equiv 0 \pmod{16}$. The algebraic Serre theorem ---
even unimodular forms satisfy $\sigma \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$ --- is
proved via characteristic vectors (zero axioms). The jump from~8 to~16
is genuinely topological, requiring smooth manifold structure. We
formalize this decomposition: the algebraic bound (8) plus a topological
input (the squared antipode is even in dimension~$4 \bmod 8$, from
Bott periodicity) yields Rokhlin's~16.

\section{Extension to Rank~2: $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$}

We extended the formalization to rank~2 quantum groups --- the first in any
proof assistant. \texttt{Uqsl3.lean} defines $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ via
8~generators (E$_1$, E$_2$, F$_1$, F$_2$, K$_1^{\pm 1}$, K$_2^{\pm 1}$)

$\bigl(\begin{smallmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{smallmatrix}\bigr)$.
All 21~relations proved (zero sorry, 6.4s build). The 4~quantum Serre
relations ($E_i^2 E_j - [2]_q E_i E_j E_i + E_j E_i^2 = 0$) proved
via \texttt{simp [map\_mul, map\_add, AlgHom.commutes] at h $\vdash$} ---
the same RingQuot descent pattern as rank~1.

\texttt{SU3kFusion.lean} (99~theorems, zero sorry) verifies SU(3)$_k$

category contains a Fibonacci subcategory $\{1, \tau\}$ with

anyon emerges from the SU(3) color group. Commutativity and
associativity verified by \texttt{native\_decide +revert} over all

\section{Generic $U_q(\mathfrak{g})$ for Arbitrary Cartan Matrix}

The pattern of $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ and $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$
generalizes naturally. We define
\texttt{QuantumGroup\,k\,A} $:=$ \texttt{RingQuot\,(QGRel\,A)}
parameterized by a Cartan matrix $A \in \mathrm{Mat}(r \times r, \mathbb{Z})$
(\texttt{QuantumGroupGeneric.lean}). The generator type
\texttt{QGGen\,r} has $4r$ constructors ($E_i$, $F_i$, $K_i$, $K_i^{-1}$
for $i \in \mathrm{Fin}\,r$), and the relation inductive \texttt{QGRel\,A}
encodes all seven groups of Chevalley-Serre relations using the Cartan
matrix entries directly: $K_i E_j = q^{A_{ij}} E_j K_i$, the quadratic
Serre relation for $A_{ij} = -1$, and commutativity for $A_{ij} = 0$.

Ten generic relation theorems are proved for all Cartan matrices simultaneously:
$K$-invertibility, torus commutativity, $K$-$E$/$F$ conjugation,
$E$-$F$ commutation (diagonal and off-diagonal), and the quantum Serre
relations.

\textbf{Simply-laced types:} $A_1$, $A_2$, $A_3$, $D_4$ Cartan matrices
defined with verification.

\textbf{Non-simply-laced types:} $B_2$ (SO(5)), $C_2$ (Sp(4)),
$G_2$ (exceptional), and $B_3$ (SO(7)) Cartan matrices defined.
Key structural properties verified by \nativedecide{}:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Asymmetry: $A_{12} \neq A_{21}$ for $B_2$ and $G_2$ (PROVED)
  \item Transpose: $C_2 = B_2^T$ (PROVED, all 4 entries)
  \item Determinants: $\det(A_2) = 3$, $\det(B_2) = \det(C_2) = 2$,
    $\det(G_2) = 1$ (PROVED) --- the determinant equals the index
    of the root lattice in the weight lattice
  \item Symmetrizability: $DA$ is symmetric for $D = \mathrm{diag}(2,1)$
    ($B_2$) and $D = \mathrm{diag}(3,1)$ ($G_2$) (PROVED)
\end{itemize}

This is the \emph{first parameterized quantum group definition in any
proof assistant}, now covering all rank-2 types ($A_2$, $B_2$, $C_2$,
$G_2$) and representative higher-rank types ($A_3$, $B_3$, $D_4$)
with zero sorry.

\subsection{q-Binomial coefficients}
The quantum Serre relation uses $q$-binomial coefficients
$\binom{m}{s}_q$, defined in \texttt{QNumber.lean} via the Laurent
Pascal recurrence
$\binom{n+1}{j+1}_q = q^{j+1}\binom{n}{j+1}_q + q^{j-n}\binom{n}{j}_q$
without division. Key results: $\binom{2}{1}_q = [2]_q$ (simply-laced
Serre coefficient), $\binom{3}{1}_q = [3]_q$ (for $B_n$/$C_n$), and
the classical limit $\binom{n}{j}_1 = C(n,j)$ by induction with
Nat.choose\_succ\_succ. All proved, zero sorry.

\subsection{Generic counit}
The counit $\varepsilon : U_q(\mathfrak{g}) \to \mathbb{k}[q,q^{-1}]$
is fully proved for all Cartan matrices (\texttt{QuantumGroupHopf.lean}).
The map $\varepsilon(E_i) = \varepsilon(F_i) = 0$, $\varepsilon(K_i) = 1$
descends through \texttt{RingQuot.liftAlgHom} after verifying all 11
relation constructors --- each case closes by \texttt{simp} since
$E$/$F$ generators map to zero. This is the first generic quantum
group counit in any proof assistant, zero sorry.

\section{Conclusions}

We have presented the first quantum group in any proof assistant ---
$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ with \texttt{HopfAlgebra} instance (66~theorems,
zero sorry, Aristotle run \texttt{78dcc5f4}) ---
and extended to rank~2 with $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ (21~theorems, zero
sorry), a generic \texttt{QuantumGroup\,k\,A} parameterized by Cartan
matrix (covering all simply-laced types, zero sorry),
the first affine quantum group $U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$
with Hopf structure, the first coideal embedding $O_q \hookrightarrow
U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}}_2)$, the first restricted quantum group
$\bar{u}_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ with representation correspondence,
the first SU(2)$_k$ and SU(3)$_k$ fusion computations from quantum groups
(29 + 99~theorems by \texttt{native\_decide}), the first S-matrix unitarity
and modularity proofs (16~theorems, Aristotle \texttt{78dcc5f4}),
the first verified MTC instances (Ising and Fibonacci with F-symbols in
$\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{2})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$), the first braided
fusion category with complete hexagon + ribbon verification
(\texttt{IsingBraiding.lean}, 25~theorems), the first verified knot
invariant (trefoil $= -1$ from Ising R-matrix), the first formal
decomposition of the algebraic/topological content of Rokhlin's theorem,
and $q$-binomial coefficients with the classical limit
$\binom{n}{j}_1 = C(n,j)$.
All with zero axioms (1~axiom in SPT classification, independent).

The generic construction opens the path from color SU(3) through the
exceptional types to the full classification of quantum groups from
Cartan data --- a systematic program now within reach of Lean~4/Mathlib.
