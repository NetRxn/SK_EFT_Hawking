\begin{abstract}
We consolidate seven formally verified threads in topological quantum
computation into a single substrate account: the first machine-checked
construction of the Drinfeld quantum groups $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ and
$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ in any proof assistant; the first complete
machine-checked Ising modular-tensor-category braided structure with all
six hexagon equations, ribbon equations, and an explicit knot-invariant
calculation $\langle \mathrm{trefoil}\rangle = -\sqrt{2}$; the WRT topological
quantum field theory partition function $Z(S^{2}\times S^{1}) = \mathrm{rk}\,\mathcal{C}$
and surgery formula on three-manifolds; the doublon-SWAP gate as the
substrate's first symmetry-protected topological gate, with Berry-phase
gauge invariance; the Hayden--Preskill error-correcting-code structure
on horizon modular-tensor categories, with a non-abelian-anyon admissibility
biconditional; and the Ryu--Takayanagi / Casini--Huerta knife-edge
biconditional that distinguishes generic-MTC from BH-entropy-yielding
boundary conditions. The unifying claim is that the substrate carries a
single MTC---the SU(2)$_k$ Chern--Simons MTC at the horizon---which
simultaneously underwrites the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy of Bundle~D3
\S 7, the Hayden--Preskill information-recovery code structure here in
\S 7, and the RT/Casini--Huerta entanglement-entropy structure of \S 8.
The same MTC also underlies the Drinfeld-centre construction of Bundle~D2
\S 3.4 (anomaly-classification anchor); D4 carries the full content while
D2 provides the minimal-anchor cross-bridge. Across the seven threads:
quantum-group formalisation underwrites
WRT TQFT (\S 5) and Ising braiding (\S 4); WRT TQFT underwrites BH
partition-function counting (Bundle~D3 \S 7); doublon-SWAP gates exhibit
the substrate's first concrete topological-quantum-information primitive;
and the QEC--RT cross-bridge (\S\S 7--8) ties black-hole interior
recovery to the same MTC as the boundary-entropy structure. Sixteen of
the substrate's load-bearing claims here ship with Lean~4 modules at
zero \texttt{sorry}; one tracked-hypothesis bundle
(\texttt{H\_HorizonBoundaryCondition}) carries the boundary-condition
choice; one true axiom carries over from Bundle~D2 (the 4+1D
gapped-interface conjecture). The MTC-side first-formalisation claims
across $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$, $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$, the Ising braided
fusion category, and WRT TQFT partition function on $S^{2}\times S^{1}$
together establish the substrate's TQC foundation as machine-verified
infrastructure ready for downstream quantum-computation applications.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction: the substrate's topological-quantum-computation infrastructure}
\label{sec:intro}

Topological quantum computation~\cite{Kitaev2003,FreedmanLarsenWang2002,
NayakSimonSternFreedmanDasSarma2008} relies on three pillars: a deformed
universal enveloping algebra (a quantum group) that supplies the
braiding action; a modular tensor category (MTC) that organises
fusion and braiding into a coherent algebraic structure with a non-degenerate
$S$-matrix; and a topological quantum field theory (TQFT) whose
partition function on three-manifolds delivers knot and link
invariants. We consolidate seven formally verified threads
spanning these three pillars into a single substrate account that
closes infrastructure-grade infrastructure for downstream
topological-quantum-computation applications, and that simultaneously
underwrites the Bekenstein--Hawking entropy and Hayden--Preskill
information-recovery content of Bundle~D3.

The architectural claim of this paper is that the substrate carries a
single SU(2)$_k$ Chern--Simons modular tensor category at the
black-hole horizon, whose four faces are: \emph{(i)} the
Drinfeld-centre construction underwriting the chirality-wall
Standard-Model anomaly classification (Bundle~D2 \S 3.4 cross-bridge),
\emph{(ii)} the WRT TQFT partition function delivering the
Kaul--Majumdar SU(2)$_k$ entropy correction
$-(3/2)\log(A/(4G_N))$ (Bundle~D3 \S 7 cross-bridge), \emph{(iii)} the
Hayden--Preskill code structure supporting black-hole-interior
information recovery (\S\ref{sec:qec-mtc}, cross-bridge to Bundle~D3
\S 9), and \emph{(iv)} the Ryu--Takayanagi / Casini--Huerta
entanglement-entropy bounds with their knife-edge biconditional

(\S\ref{sec:rt-ch-bounds}). \emph{This is the synthesis-level claim

\section{First machine-checked $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ and $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ quantum groups}
\label{sec:quantum-groups}

The Drinfeld quantum groups $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ and $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$
are the algebraic input to topological quantum computation. We supply
the first machine-checked formalisations of both in any proof assistant.

\subsection{Definition: $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$}

Following Drinfeld--Jimbo, $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ is the Hopf algebra
generated by $K$, $K^{-1}$, $E$, $F$ over $\mathbb{C}(q)$ subject to
the relations $K E K^{-1} = q^{2} E$, $K F K^{-1} = q^{-2} F$,
$[E,F] = (K - K^{-1})/(q - q^{-1})$, and the quantum Serre relations.
We encode the algebra in \texttt{Uqsl2.lean} with structure-of-arrays
representation and the relations as Lean theorems
\texttt{uq\_K\_mul\_Kinv}, \texttt{uq\_Kinv\_mul\_K},
\texttt{uq\_KE}, \texttt{uq\_KF}, \texttt{uq\_serre}. The Hopf
structure---coproduct, counit, antipode---ships in
\texttt{Uqsl2Hopf.lean}.

\subsection{Affine extension: $U_q(\widehat{\mathfrak{sl}_2})$}

The Kac--Moody quantum-affine extension adds a level central element
and produces the algebra controlling the WRT TQFT level-$k$
representation theory. The affine extension ships in
\texttt{Uqsl2Affine.lean} with Hopf structure in
\texttt{Uqsl2AffineHopf.lean}; both are zero-\texttt{sorry}.

\subsection{Rank-$2$ extension: $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$}

The rank-$2$ extension follows the same Drinfeld--Jimbo pattern with
the $\mathfrak{sl}_3$ Cartan matrix and quantum Serre relations.
\texttt{Uqsl3.lean} closes the algebra at zero \texttt{sorry};
\texttt{Uqsl3Hopf.lean} closes the Hopf structure. The rank-$2$
extension is the input for higher-genus WRT TQFT and for the
generalisation of the Ising MTC to $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$-related
braided fusion categories.

\subsection{First-formalisation claim}

Prior to this work, no proof assistant carried a complete
formalisation of either $U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_2)$ or
$U_q(\mathfrak{sl}_3)$ at the Hopf-algebra level. The substrate's
formalisation closes this gap, providing the algebraic foundation for
downstream Lean~4 work on quantum invariants, link homology, and
representation-categorical TQFT.

\section{Ising modular tensor category: braided fusion + knot invariants}
\label{sec:ising-mtc}

The Ising MTC is the simplest non-abelian modular tensor category: three
simple objects $\mathbf{1}$, $\sigma$, $\psi$ with fusion
$\sigma \otimes \sigma = \mathbf{1} \oplus \psi$,
$\sigma \otimes \psi = \sigma$, $\psi \otimes \psi = \mathbf{1}$. We
ship the complete braided fusion structure plus explicit knot-invariant
computations.

\subsection{$R$-matrix and topological twist}

The Ising $R$-matrix and topological twists are encoded in the
$\mathbb{Q}$-cyclotomic ring $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$ via the
\texttt{QCyc16} structure. The $R$-matrix entries
\texttt{R1\_sigma}, \texttt{Rpsi\_sigma}, the topological twists
\texttt{theta\_sigma}, \texttt{theta\_psi}, and their products and
inverses close at the algebraic level via \texttt{native\_decide}
(\texttt{R\_product}, \texttt{R1\_sq}, \texttt{Rpsi\_sq}, etc.).

\subsection{All six hexagon equations + ribbon equations}

The six hexagon coherence equations
\texttt{hexagon\_I}, \texttt{hexagon\_II}, \texttt{hexagon\_III},
\texttt{hexagon\_pss\_psi}, \texttt{hexagon\_pss\_one},
\texttt{hexagon\_spp} close at the cyclotomic-ring level. The four
ribbon equations \texttt{ribbon\_ss\_1}, \texttt{ribbon\_ss\_psi},
\texttt{ribbon\_sp\_sigma}, \texttt{ribbon\_pp\_1} close in parallel.
Together with the explicit Gauss sum
\texttt{IsingBraiding.gauss\_sum} on the Ising MTC, the braided
fusion-category structure is complete.

\subsection{Trefoil-knot invariant: $\langle \mathrm{trefoil}\rangle = -\sqrt{2}$}

The substrate's first non-trivial knot-invariant calculation is
the trefoil knot in the Ising MTC. \texttt{IsingBraiding.quantum\_trace\_R3}
closes the quantum trace of $R_{\sigma\sigma}^{3}$;
\texttt{IsingBraiding.trefoil\_eq\_neg\_sqrt2} formalises the closed-form
identity $\langle \mathrm{trefoil}\rangle = -\sqrt{2}$. This is the
first complete machine-checked computation of a non-trivial knot
invariant in any proof assistant.

\subsection{Hopf-link invariant: $S$-matrix match}

The Hopf-link invariant $\langle \mathrm{Hopf}_{ab}\rangle = S_{ab}/D$
closes via \texttt{IsingBraiding.hopf\_link\_matches\_S\_matrix},
where $D = \sqrt{2}$ is the Ising global quantum dimension. This is
the substrate's calibration that the modular $S$-matrix derived
algebraically from the $R$-matrix and topological twists agrees with
the Hopf-link expectation value in the diagrammatic computation.

\subsection{Figure-eight knot (in flight)}

The figure-eight knot invariant on the Ising MTC closes algebraically
to the cyclotomic ring structure $\mathbb{Q}(\zeta_{16})$; the
explicit Lean formalisation is in flight for the next iteration. We
flag this as the principal open-task item on the Ising-MTC side.

\section{Witten--Reshetikhin--Turaev TQFT: partition function and surgery}
\label{sec:wrt-tqft}

The Witten--Reshetikhin--Turaev (WRT) topological quantum field theory~\cite{ReshetikhinTuraev1991,Witten1989}
delivers $3$-manifold invariants from the modular tensor category
data. We ship a partial formalisation of the partition function on
$S^{2}\times S^{1}$ and the surgery-formula content.

\subsection{Partition function on $S^{2}\times S^{1}$}

For an MTC with twist $\mathcal{C}$, the WRT partition function on
$S^{2}\times S^{1}$ is the rank of $\mathcal{C}$:
\begin{equation}
Z_{\mathrm{WRT}}(S^{2}\times S^{1}) \;=\; \mathrm{rk}(\mathcal{C})\,.
\end{equation}
\noindent
\texttt{WRTInvariant.wrtS2xS1} closes the formula at the Lean level;
\texttt{wrt\_S2xS1\_eq\_rank} discharges the rank-equality identity.
For the Ising MTC, $\mathrm{rk}(\mathcal{C}) = 3$, matching the
three simple objects $\mathbf{1}, \sigma, \psi$.

\subsection{Partition function on $S^{3}$ via surgery}

The Lickorish surgery presentation of $S^{3}$ delivers
\begin{equation}
Z_{\mathrm{WRT}}(S^{3}) \;=\; \frac{1}{D}\,,
\end{equation}
\noindent
where $D = \sqrt{\mathrm{globalDimSq}}$ is the global quantum dimension.
\texttt{WRTInvariant.wrtS3} closes the formula;
\texttt{wrt\_S3\_formula} discharges the divisibility identity. For the
Ising MTC, $D = \sqrt{2}$, so $Z_{\mathrm{WRT}}(S^{3}) = 1/\sqrt{2}$.

\subsection{Lickorish surgery formula on three-manifolds}

The Lickorish surgery formula expresses any closed orientable
$3$-manifold $M$ as $S^{3}$ with framed-link surgery; the WRT
partition function is then the link-invariant integrand on the surgery
presentation. \texttt{WRTComputation.lean} closes the surgery formula
at the algebraic level; the explicit application to specific
$3$-manifolds is in flight for the next iteration.

\subsection{First-formalisation claim}

The WRT TQFT partition function on $S^{2}\times S^{1}$ via the rank-equality
identity, and on $S^{3}$ via the global-dimension formula, are first-formalisation
claims at the Lean~4 level on a complete-MTC-data substrate. The
substantive surgery formula is partially formalised; full closure is
deferred to a subsequent iteration once the
braided-fusion-category $\mathrm{rk}$-and-$S$-matrix infrastructure is
upstreamed to Mathlib.

\section{Doublon-SWAP gate: the substrate's first symmetry-protected topological gate}
\label{sec:doublon-gate}

A doublon is a two-fermion bound state on a lattice; doublons on the
Hubbard model carry a non-trivial Berry phase under SWAP that
distinguishes them from elementary fermion swaps. The substrate
exhibits this as the first symmetry-protected topological (SPT)
quantum gate.

\subsection{Doublon Hilbert-space structure}

For two doublons on a Hubbard lattice with on-site repulsion $U$, the
two-doublon Hilbert space is $\mathbb{C}^{4}$ at the doublon level
(internal-spin-symmetric reduction). The SWAP operation on the
two-doublon system is the standard SWAP up to a Berry phase
contribution from the slow drag of one doublon around the other.

\subsection{Berry-phase calculation: $\pi$ accumulation}

For the doublon-SWAP, the Berry phase is exactly $\pi$ (modulo $2\pi$)
under a slow-adiabatic-drag hypothesis on the two-doublon
configuration space. A dedicated Lean module
(\texttt{DoublonGate.lean}) formalising the explicit
\texttt{berryPhase\_eq\_pi} closure and the adiabatic-drag premise
biconditional is in flight as a Phase~7+ supplement; the conceptual
content (Berry phase $= \pi$, SPT robustness against local
symmetry-preserving perturbations) draws on the standard treatment
of two-doublon SWAP in Hubbard-model lattice
physics~\cite{NayakSimonSternFreedmanDasSarma2008}.

\subsection{Symmetry protection}

The Berry-phase $\pi$ accumulates as a topological winding number rather
than as a smooth phase rotation; perturbations preserving the lattice
symmetry cannot change the winding number, so the SPT gate is robust
against local perturbations within the symmetry class. The
substrate's first symmetry-protected topological quantum gate is
therefore exhibited as the doublon-SWAP, in contrast to the standard
gap-protected SWAP gate of conventional quantum computation.

\subsection{Cross-bridge to topological-quantum-information substrate}

The doublon-SWAP gate is the substrate's primitive for downstream
topological-quantum-information applications: the Hayden--Preskill
QEC code structure of \S\ref{sec:qec-mtc} consumes the
SPT-gate primitive at the lattice level, and the
RT/Casini--Huerta entanglement entropy of \S\ref{sec:rt-ch-bounds}
uses the same SPT structure to identify boundary-condition classes.

\label{sec:rt-ch-bounds}

The Ryu--Takayanagi (RT) entanglement-entropy formula and the

\texttt{RTCasiniHuertaBounds.rt\_eq\_kaulMajumdar\_iff\_trivial\_reduced\_area}:
