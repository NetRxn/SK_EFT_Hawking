\title{Three Generations of Standard-Model Fermions from Modular Invariance:\\
A Machine-Checked Derivation}

\author{J.~Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We present the first formally verified derivation that the number of
Standard-Model fermion generations satisfies $N_f \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$,
following the global modular constraint of Wang~\cite{Wang2024}. The
chain combines two independent ingredients machine-checked in Lean~4:
the chiral central charge $c_- = 8 N_f$ derived from $16$ left-handed
Weyl components per generation in the with-$\nu_R$ convention, and
the framing-anomaly constraint $24 \mid c_-$ following from
$\eta(\tau+1) = e^{2\pi i/24}\,\eta(\tau)$ via Mathlib's
\texttt{Complex.exp\_add}. The minimal nontrivial solution is
$N_f = 3$. Without right-handed neutrinos, $c_- = 15/2$ is fractional,
giving a formal independent argument for $\nu_R$ from modular
invariance alone. The algebraic core of the Rokhlin bound is verified
through homological degree~5 by an explicit minimal free resolution
$\dim \mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2,\mathbb{F}_2) =
1,2,2,2,3,4$ together with a change-of-rings adjunction
$\mathrm{Hom}_A(A \otimes_{A(1)} M, N) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{A(1)}(M, N)$,
the first such computation in any proof assistant. Three textbook
topology results (ko cohomology, ASS convergence, ABP splitting) enter
as tracked hypotheses. The verification is fully automated by
\texttt{lake build} on a $5812$-theorem $322$-module library with one
axiom and zero \texttt{sorry}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The Standard Model has three generations of fermions, and the
perturbative gauge anomalies cancel within each generation
independently~\cite{GrossJackiw1972}. The question of why nature
chose three rather than one, two, or four is therefore unresolved at
the perturbative level. Wang~\cite{Wang2024} proposed that a
\emph{global} modular constraint --- $24 \mid c_-$ on the chiral
central charge of the partition function under the modular
$T$-transformation $\tau \to \tau+1$ --- combined with the SM's
fermion content $c_- = 8 N_f$, forces $N_f \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$. The
proposal recasts the empirical three-generation count as the minimal
output of a topological consistency requirement and is therefore
falsifiable by any internally consistent SM extension violating the
constraint.

This Letter records the first machine-checked formalization of this
chain in Lean~4 with Mathlib. Every coefficient on the path from
modular forms to particle counting is either derived from Mathlib
infrastructure or registered as a tracked assumption with explicit
hypothesis status. The two independent pieces converge through the
arithmetic identity $24 = 8 \times 3$, where $8$ is fixed by the SM
fermion content and $24$ is fixed by the Dedekind eta function.

\section{The chiral central charge from SM fermion content}
\label{sec:central_charge}

In the with-$\nu_R$ convention each SM generation contains $16$
left-handed Weyl components:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\hline
Multiplet & $SU(3) \times SU(2)$ & Components & $c$ \\
\hline
$Q_L$ & $(\mathbf{3}, \mathbf{2})$ & 6 & 3 \\
$\bar{u}_R$ & $(\bar{\mathbf{3}}, \mathbf{1})$ & 3 & 3/2 \\
$\bar{d}_R$ & $(\bar{\mathbf{3}}, \mathbf{1})$ & 3 & 3/2 \\
$L$ & $(\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{2})$ & 2 & 1 \\
$\bar{e}_R$ & $(\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{1})$ & 1 & 1/2 \\
$\bar{\nu}_R$ & $(\mathbf{1}, \mathbf{1})$ & 1 & 1/2 \\
\hline
Total & & 16 & 8 \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

Each Weyl fermion contributes $c = 1/2$ to the chiral central charge
upon $4 \to 2$ dimensional reduction~\cite{AlvarezGaumeWitten1984}.
The coefficient $8$ in $c_- = 8 N_f$ is therefore not free: it is
\emph{derived} from the count
$\texttt{total\_components\_with\_nu\_R} = 16$ by the Lean theorem
\texttt{WangBridge.fermion\_count\_gives\_central\_charge}.

\paragraph{Without $\nu_R$.} Removing right-handed neutrinos drops
the Weyl count to $15$ and gives $c_- = 15/2$ per generation. A
fractional central charge is a gravitational anomaly: the 2D theory
is mathematically inconsistent~\cite{AlvarezGaumeWitten1984}. The
Lean theorem
\texttt{WangBridge.central\_charge\_fractional\_without\_nu\_R}
encodes this by constructing an explicit contradiction from the
assumption $15/2 \in \mathbb{N}$, complementing the
$\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly argument of
Garc\'ia-Etxebarria and Montero~\cite{GarciaEtxebarria2019}.

\section{The framing anomaly from the Dedekind eta function}
\label{sec:framing}

The Dedekind eta function
\begin{equation}
\eta(\tau) = q^{1/24} \prod_{n=1}^{\infty}(1 - q^n),
\quad q = e^{2\pi i \tau},
\end{equation}
satisfies $\eta(\tau+1) = e^{2\pi i/24}\,\eta(\tau)$. Under the
$T$-transformation $\tau \to \tau+1$, the infinite product is
invariant since $q(\tau+1) = q(\tau)$, while the prefactor $q^{1/24}$
shifts by $e^{2\pi i / 24}$. The shift is verified in
\texttt{ModularInvarianceConstraint.qParam\_shift} by reduction to
Mathlib's \texttt{Complex.exp\_add}; no project-originated lemma is
required. The ``24'' in the modular phase has two complementary
origins: analytically, the exponent in the $q$-expansion of
$\eta(\tau)$~\cite{Rademacher1973}; physically, the Casimir energy
$E_0 = -c/24$ on a 2D conformal field theory torus, traceable to
zeta regularization $\zeta(-1) = -1/12$ and the framing anomaly on a
spin 3-manifold~\cite{diFrancesco1997}.

The 2D partition function transforms as
$Z(\tau+1) = e^{2\pi i c_-/24}\,Z(\tau)$. Modular invariance demands
$e^{2\pi i c_-/24} = 1$, equivalently $24 \mid c_-$, formalized as
\texttt{ModularInvarianceConstraint.framing\_anomaly\_constraint}.
Combining with $c_- = 8 N_f$:
\begin{equation}
24 \mid 8 N_f \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad 3 \mid N_f.
\label{eq:gen_constraint}
\end{equation}
The constraint is sharp: $24 \nmid 8$ and $24 \nmid 16$, but
$24 \mid 24$. The factorization $24 = 8 \times 3$ separates the
mathematical input (zeta regularization gives $24$) from the physical
input (fermion counting gives $8$); their ratio is the generation
constraint.

\section{Machine-checked Ext computation over $A(1)$}
\label{sec:ext}

The algebraic core of the Rokhlin bound --- that
$\sigma(M) \equiv 0 \pmod{16}$ on a closed spin 4-manifold rather than
the weaker $\sigma \equiv 0 \pmod{8}$ --- is the input that
distinguishes the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly classification from a
purely $E_8$-lattice-driven $\mathbb{Z}_8$. To formalize this we
compute the graded $\mathbb{F}_2$-dimensions of
$\mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2, \mathbb{F}_2)$ through
homological degree five, where $A(1) = \langle \mathrm{Sq}^1,
\mathrm{Sq}^2 \rangle$ is the eight-dimensional sub-Hopf algebra of
the Steenrod algebra. The computation is shipped in three modules.
\texttt{A1Ring.lean} formalizes the left regular representation, the
Adem relation $\mathrm{Sq}^1 \mathrm{Sq}^1 = 0$ and the Cartan
relation $\mathrm{Sq}^1 \mathrm{Sq}^2 = \mathrm{Sq}^2 \mathrm{Sq}^1
+ \mathrm{Sq}^3$ via \texttt{native\_decide}.
\texttt{A1Resolution.lean} constructs differentials $d^n$ with
$d^2 = 0$ at every level and verifies exactness by enumeration of
kernels and reduced row-echelon witnesses. \texttt{A1Ext.lean}
certifies minimality and reads off
\begin{equation}
\dim_{\mathbb{F}_2} \mathrm{Ext}^n_{A(1)}(\mathbb{F}_2, \mathbb{F}_2)
= 1, 2, 2, 2, 3, 4 \quad (n = 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5).
\end{equation}
The Hom-tensor adjunction
$\mathrm{Hom}_A(A \otimes_{A(1)} M, N) \cong \mathrm{Hom}_{A(1)}(M, N)$
is established in \texttt{ChangeOfRings.lean} as pure homological
algebra, reducing the full Adams spectral sequence input from
$\mathrm{Ext}_A$ to the finite $\mathrm{Ext}_{A(1)}$. We are not aware
of any prior machine-checked Ext computation over a Steenrod
sub-algebra in any proof assistant.

Three textbook topology results enter as tracked hypotheses, not as
project-originated axioms: ko cohomology of a point through degree
five~\cite{Adams1974}, Adams spectral sequence
convergence~\cite{BeaudryCampbell}, and the ABP splitting
$\mathrm{MSpin} \cong \mathrm{ko} \vee \mathrm{ko}\langle 2 \rangle
\vee \cdots$~\cite{ABP1967}. Each is cited at standard textbook depth
and is independently verifiable by a topologist; closure to
project-originated proofs depends on upstream Mathlib
algebraic-topology development.

\section{The 16-convergence and the role of $\nu_R$}

The number 16 appears in four formally distinct settings:
\textit{(i)} the Weyl-component count per SM generation in the
with-$\nu_R$ convention; \textit{(ii)} the bordism group
$\Omega_5^{\mathrm{Spin}^{\mathbb{Z}_4}} \cong \mathbb{Z}_{16}$
classifying global anomalies of theories with a $\mathbb{Z}_4$
symmetry~\cite{GarciaEtxebarria2019}; \textit{(iii)} the Rokhlin
divisor in the signature of a closed spin 4-manifold,
$\sigma(M) \equiv 0 \pmod{16}$~\cite{Rokhlin1952}; and \textit{(iv)}
the period of the Kitaev periodic table of free-fermion topological
superconductors in symmetry class~D~\cite{Kitaev2009}, reduced from
$\mathbb{Z}$ to $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ under
interactions~\cite{FidkowskiKitaev2010}.

Wang~\cite{Wang2024} establishes the algebraic-topology relationship
between (i)--(iii) via the string-bordism $\mathbb{Z}_{24}$ class and
the associated Smith homomorphism. We record the four occurrences as
\texttt{RokhlinBridge.sixteen\_convergence\_full} without claiming a
formal equivalence; the equivalence remains an external Wang input.
The Kitaev relationship to (iv) is suggestive but not formalized.

The constraints from the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly and modular
invariance behave differently with respect to $\nu_R$:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lccc}
\hline
& $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ ($\nu_R$ in) & $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ ($\nu_R$ out) & Modular inv. \\
\hline
Constraint & none & $16 \mid N_f$ & $3 \mid N_f$ \\
Minimal $N_f$ & --- & $16$ & $3$ \\
Combined min & $\mathbf{3}$ & $\mathrm{lcm}(16,3) = \mathbf{48}$ & --- \\
\hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

With $\nu_R$, the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly cancels for every $N_f$
(\texttt{RokhlinBridge.z16\_anomaly\_always\_cancels\_with\_nu\_R}),
so the only remaining constraint is modular invariance and the
minimal solution is $N_f = 3$. Without $\nu_R$, both constraints must
hold simultaneously, forcing
$N_f = \mathrm{lcm}(16,3) = 48$
(\texttt{RokhlinBridge.constraints\_without\_nu\_R}). Since $N_f = 3$
is observed, the joint constraint is direct evidence for $\nu_R$.
The Rokhlin bound is sharp: the K3 surface saturates with
$\sigma = -16$, while the $E_8$ manifold has $\sigma = 8$ but is not
spin --- formalized as
\texttt{RokhlinBridge.rokhlin\_strictly\_stronger}: $16 \nmid 8$.

\section{Conclusion}

Combining $c_- = 8 N_f$ from SM fermion counting with $24 \mid c_-$
from Dedekind-eta modular invariance gives $N_f \equiv 0 \pmod{3}$,
with $N_f = 3$ as the minimal nontrivial solution. The chain is
machine-checked end to end: the coefficient $8$ via Mathlib finite
sums in \texttt{SMFermionData.lean}; the modular factor $24$ via
\texttt{Complex.exp\_add} in \texttt{ModularInvarianceConstraint.lean};
the divisibility step via
\texttt{Nat.Coprime.dvd\_of\_dvd\_mul\_left} in
\texttt{GenerationConstraint.lean}; the algebraic Rokhlin core via
the explicit minimal free resolution in \texttt{A1Ring.lean},
\texttt{A1Resolution.lean}, and \texttt{A1Ext.lean}; and the
change-of-rings step in \texttt{ChangeOfRings.lean}. The
$24 \mid c_-$ constraint admits a deeper structural reading: the
five-step chain spin-bordism $\Omega_5^{\mathrm{Spin}}(BG_{\mathrm{SM}})$
$\to$ anomaly polynomial $\to$ modular invariance $\to$
Niemeier-lattice classification~\cite{Niemeier1973} $\to$ Schellekens'
classification of meromorphic $c=24$ CFTs~\cite{Schellekens1993},
geometrically completed for the strongly-rational, weight-1-non-trivial
subset by M\"oller--Scheithauer~\cite{MollerScheithauer2024}, places
$24 \mid c_-$ as a corollary of holomorphic-VOA classification rather
than a stand-alone modular-consistency requirement; the deep companion
paper~\cite{Roehm2026D2} carries the full derivation. The
without-$\nu_R$ argument upgrades from suggestive to formal: a
fractional central charge $c_- = 15/2$ is gravitationally anomalous;
no fractional natural number exists; therefore $\nu_R$ exists if the
modular framing-anomaly constraint applies to the SM. The full
verification builds in $\sim 30~\mathrm{s}$ on commodity hardware
under Lean~4 v4.29.0 against Mathlib commit \texttt{8850ed93}
(library state: 5812 theorems across 322 modules, 1 axiom
\texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom} unused in this chain, zero
\texttt{sorry}). The Mathlib infrastructure that formalizes modular
forms for analytic number theory directly constrains the
Standard-Model generation count.
