\title{Continuous Verification for Computational Physics Research:\\
A Three-Layer Pipeline with Worked Cases from Schwinger--Keldysh
Effective Field Theory, Algebraic General Relativity, and
Topological Quantum Field Theory}

\author{John Roehm}
\date{Draft --- Phase 7a, May 2026}

\begin{document}

\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Computational physics research lacks a structural guarantee that
published numerical claims match the underlying computation. We
describe a three-layer continuous-verification pipeline integrating
Python numerics, Lean~4 formal proofs, and the Aristotle automated
theorem prover, organized into a fourteen-stage workflow with
fifteen invariants. Applied across a research program spanning
dissipative effective field theory, analog Hawking radiation,
emergent gravity through black-hole thermodynamics, anomaly
constraints on Standard Model particle content, dark-sector substrate
constraints, and topological quantum computation foundations, the
pipeline has produced \totaltheorems{} machine-checked theorems
across \leanmodules{} Lean modules with \sorrycount{} remaining gaps
(\sorrypercent\%) plus \aristotleproved{} theorems closed by
automated theorem proving. We document three new pipeline stages
beyond the original twelve-stage version (interactive Lean
development via the language-server protocol, fresh-context
adversarial review, and meta-process quality improvement), three new
invariants (placeholder non-load-bearing, no heartbeat overrides in
proof bodies, primary-source citation cache), the publication-bundle
architecture that consolidates per-wave drafts into seventeen
publication targets, and the preemptive-strengthening discipline
that reduces theorem-statement audit cost from twelve retroactive
cuts to five and then to zero across three successive waves (with
a mandatory ruthless post-wave review as a safety net for residual
subtler patterns). Three worked cases illustrate the methodology:
the Aristotle FirstOrderKMS counterexample (where automated proving
failed and the failure surfaced a substantive physics result), the
gap-solution-bounded conjecture disproof (where formalization-driven
counterexample search refuted a literature claim), and the
chirality-wall axiom decomposition (where structural axiom
classification turned a single 50-term proof into nine
individually-tractable sublemmas). Two additional sidebar studies
document the algebraic-general-relativity substrate formalization
work that the pipeline accreted as a byproduct, and a
Mathlib-dependency-fallback discipline pattern for theorems whose
substantive content has no upstream PDE infrastructure available.
A final process result documents the limits of metadata-level
citation validation: a June~2026 external-review remediation found
that a citation can be metadata-valid --- resolvable identifier,
cached primary source, correct bibliographic data --- while the
specific claim the prose attributes to it is unsupported by the
source (three instances in roughly ten spot checks), motivating
three new deterministic prose-consistency checks and a systematic
content-level attribution sweep.
The methodology is domain-agnostic.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Numerical computation in physics research is increasingly
sophisticated and increasingly opaque. A typical large
computational-physics program now combines several thousand lines of
Python or Julia, dozens of Jupyter notebooks, and a paper-trail of
PDF drafts whose numerical content is the output of code paths that
the published prose does not name. The standard publication workflow
provides no machine-checkable connection between the prose and the
code: reviewers cannot, and do not, run the computation. Five years
after publication, when the original code has bit-rotted and the
authors have rotated, no one can reconstruct which version of which
formula produced which figure.

This is not a hypothetical concern. We have personally observed
each of the following failure modes during a single research
program: (i) a numerical claim in a draft that traced to a code
path that no longer existed; (ii) a ``formally verified'' claim
whose underlying Lean theorem was a placeholder of type
\texttt{True}; (iii) a citation whose arXiv identifier resolved to
an unrelated graph-neural-network paper; (iv) a parameter whose
value was inherited from a prior draft and had drifted from the
primary source it cited. None of these were detected by the
authors before adversarial review. All of them shipped to draft
state.

The pipeline we describe makes verification \emph{continuous}, in
the sense of integrated into the daily research workflow rather
than retrofitted before submission. Antecedents on the
formal-verification side include landmark engineering verifications
(CompCert~\cite{compcert} for a C compiler; seL4~\cite{sel4} for an
operating-system kernel) and the more recent emergence of
Lean-based physics infrastructure
(PhysLean~\cite{physlean}). Antecedents on the
proof-assistance side include large-language-model copilots for
Lean theorem proving~\cite{leancipilot} and recent
silver-medal IMO results from automated proving~\cite{alphaproof}.
Our pipeline has fourteen stages and fifteen invariants, integrates
three orthogonal verification layers, and produces
machine-checkable artifacts at each stage. We have applied it to
a research program spanning \papercount{}
papers covering Schwinger--Keldysh dissipative effective field
theory, analog Hawking radiation in Bose--Einstein condensates and
graphene, emergent gravity through black-hole thermodynamics,
anomaly constraints on Standard Model particle content, dark-sector
substrate constraints from causal-set theory, entropic gravity, and
Jacobson thermodynamic emergence, and topological quantum
computation foundations. The pipeline has produced
\totaltheorems{} machine-checked theorems across \leanmodules{}
Lean modules, with \sorrycount{} remaining unproved gaps
(\sorrypercent\%) and \aristotleproved{} theorems closed by the
Aristotle automated theorem prover. The pipeline, its process
documents, and the validation machinery described below are
publicly available; monospaced file paths throughout this paper
are paths from the root of the public repository (see the Code
Availability statement).

This paper extends and supersedes an earlier internal draft
description of a twelve-stage version of the pipeline (April
2026). The version described here adds three stages beyond
the original twelve --- interactive Lean development via the
language-server protocol (Stage~3a), fresh-context adversarial
review (Stage~13), and meta-process quality improvement
(Stage~14) --- and three invariants (placeholder non-load-bearing,
no heartbeat overrides in proof bodies, primary-source citation
cache). The invariant set has since grown to fifteen; the
fifteenth --- explicit user sign-off, with a documented discharge
plan, for any new project-local axiom --- encodes the project's
posture that axioms are temporary scaffolding, not permanent
commitments (Section~\ref{sec:pipeline}). It also documents the
publication-bundle architecture that consolidates per-wave drafts
into seventeen publication targets and the preemptive-strengthening
discipline that reduces theorem-statement audit cost from twelve
retroactive cuts (Phase~6c.3) to five (Phase~6b.1) and then to zero
(Phase~6m R6) by applying strengthening rules prospectively, with
a mandatory ruthless post-wave review as the safety net for the
subtler patterns the prospective checklist misses.

\section{The Three-Layer Verification Architecture}
\label{sec:architecture}

The pipeline integrates three orthogonal verification layers, each
addressing a failure mode that the others cannot.

\textbf{Layer~1: Python numerics for fast iteration.} A formula is
implemented in a canonical \texttt{formulas.py} module, with a
docstring containing (i) the mathematical formula in plain text,
(ii) the Lean theorem name that formalizes it, (iii) the Aristotle
run identifier (or ``manual'' or ``pending''), and (iv) a citation
to the published source for derived formulas. Domain-specific
modules import from \texttt{formulas.py} rather than reimplementing.
Python tests verify computational correctness, edge cases, and
physical bounds. Layer~1 catches computation errors but not
formalization errors.

\textbf{Layer~2: Lean~4 formal proofs for mathematical certainty.}
Each formula is stated as a Lean theorem in the strongest form
possible. Statements are not weakened for proof convenience; if
the strongest form is not provable, the proof architecture is
wrong (Section~\ref{sec:strengthening}). Manual proofs are written
for theorems within reach. Layer~2 catches formalization errors
but not definition errors --- a formally verified proof can still
be wrong if the definitions it operates on are wrong
(Section~\ref{sec:case3}).

\textbf{Layer~3: Aristotle automated theorem prover for sorry
gaps.} The entire Lean project is submitted with strategy hints
embedded in the docstrings of unproved theorems (the ``PROVIDED
SOLUTION'' convention). Aristotle uses tactic synthesis, term
search, and machine learning to find proofs. Results are
manually reviewed before integration --- no blind acceptance ---
to verify that the proof exercises the hypotheses, that
\texttt{lake build} passes after integration, and that no
heartbeat overrides have been introduced (Invariant~10).

The three layers interact in a discovery--formalization--automation
cycle. A typical cycle: a Python implementation of a new formula
exposes a numerical regularity; the regularity is stated as a Lean
theorem with sorry; the theorem is closed by Aristotle in a
priority batch; the closure feeds back into Python by setting the
Aristotle run identifier in the formula docstring, which is then
checked by the validation suite (Section~\ref{sec:pipeline}). The
critical-coupling theorem $G_c = 8\pi^2/(N_f \Lambda^2)$ for the
chirality-wall mechanism completed all three layers in a single
session: derived in Python, stated in Lean, closed by Aristotle in
run \texttt{79e07d55}, and checked by validation, all within
twenty-four hours.

The composition is asymmetric in a way that matters. Layer~1 alone
gives reproducibility but not correctness: notebooks can produce
the same wrong answer indefinitely. Layer~2 alone gives correctness
but not numerical scope: a formalization of an idealized model is
silent on the actual experimental parameters.
Layer~3 alone gives proof-search throughput but no verification of
what was proved: an automated proof can be vacuous if the
definitions it operates on are wrong (Section~\ref{sec:case3}). The
composition gives all three.

\section{Worked Case~1: The FirstOrderKMS Aristotle Counterexample}
\label{sec:case1}

%  - First-order Kubo-Martin-Schwinger relation in
%    `lean/SKEFTHawking/FirstOrder.lean` (the theorem that Aristotle
%    repeatedly failed to close)

%    but because the symmetry-protected zero is the substantive
%    claim, and the formal pipeline's inability to discover it
%    automatically was diagnostic, not pathological
%
% Section thesis: when a Lean theorem resists automated proof
% repeatedly, the failure is information --- it surfaces either a
% wrong-statement issue (P-anti-pattern, Section 7) or a
% missing-physics issue. The FirstOrderKMS case is the latter: the

In Schwinger--Keldysh effective field theory, the first-order action
contains nine real coefficients
$\{r_1, \ldots, r_6, i_1, i_2, i_3\}$ controlling six retarded-response
and three noise channels at lowest derivative order. A clean physics
question is whether the Kubo--Martin--Schwinger (KMS) condition --- the
algebraic statement of fluctuation--dissipation relating retarded and
noise sectors --- forces a unique parameterization of the action by the
two transport coefficients $(\gamma_1, \gamma_2)$ (isotropic phonon
damping, related to bulk viscosity, and anisotropic damping along the
superfluid flow; the precise correspondence to the action coefficients
is encoded in the \texttt{DissipativeCoeffs} structure of
\texttt{Basic.lean} (line~152), consumed by the
\texttt{firstOrderDissipativeAction} construction in
\texttt{SKDoubling.lean} lines~244--280).

A published axiomatic for the SK-EFT contour exists. Crossley,
Glorioso, and Liu~\cite{CrossleyGloriosoLiu2017} formulate the
closed-time-path action with hermiticity, reflection positivity
($\Im\,S \geq 0$), and a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry they identified as
the local KMS condition. The companion paper of Glorioso, Crossley,
and Liu~\cite{GloriosoCrossleyLiu2017} promotes this to the
\emph{dynamical} KMS $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry, which is the central
algebraic-level statement applicable in any local-equilibrium state
rather than only thermal density matrices. Glorioso and
Liu~\cite{GloriosoLiu2018} then derive the local second law,
$\partial_\mu J^\mu_S \geq 0$ for a Noether-constructed entropy
current $J^\mu_S$, from the dynamical-KMS $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry, the
classical-limit remnant of microscopic unitarity, and the local
equilibrium assumption; the pedagogical exposition is the TASI~2017
lectures of Glorioso and Liu~\cite{GloriosoLiu2018TASI}. The
dynamical-KMS axiom carries a UV-realization ambiguity surfaced by
Jain and Kovtun~\cite{JainKovtun2024}, which the program encodes at
the typeclass level (the strict-vs-algebraic dynamical-KMS predicates
\texttt{hasDynamicalKMS\_strict} / \texttt{hasDynamicalKMS\_algebraic}
in \texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/GloriosoLiu/Axioms.lean}). The first-order
projection of this published skeleton onto the polynomial action with
nine candidate coefficients is the structure documented below; the
strengthening discussed in the rest of this section is the projection
step, not a project-original axiomatic choice.

Our first formalization encoded a \emph{preliminary} transform-level
KMS constraint as a Lean axiom \texttt{KMSSymmetry} (in
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/SKDoubling.lean}, lines~156--168). The
field-level transform $\psi_a \to \psi_a + i\beta\,\partial_t \psi_r$
acts only on four of the nine components ($\psi_r$, $\psi_a$,
$\partial_t \psi_r$, and $\partial_x \psi_r$), leaving the
derivatives of $\psi_a$ and the second derivatives of $\psi_r$
unconstrained. We submitted the accompanying uniqueness theorem
\texttt{SKEFTHawking.SKDoubling.firstOrder\_uniqueness} to Aristotle
in priority batch~1. Aristotle returned not a proof but a
counterexample: the coefficient vector $c = (0,0,0,0,0,0,0,1,0)$ ---
one nonzero noise channel ($i_2 = 1$) and nothing else --- satisfies
the transform-level constraint but is not the action of any valid
$(\gamma_1, \gamma_2)$ pair. Without the formal pipeline this would
have been a one-symbol typographical hole that survived three rounds
of human review; with the formal pipeline it was flagged on first
submission.

The resolution recovers the algebraic content of the published
dynamical-KMS axiom. At the level of the polynomial coefficients
(rather than the field-level transform), dynamical-KMS at first order
is seven simultaneous relations: the four lower-derivative monomials
whose KMS partner exceeds the first-order budget vanish,
$r_3 = r_4 = r_5 = r_6 = 0$; the two retained noise coefficients are
fixed by the algebraic FDR, $i_1 = -r_2/\beta$ and
$i_2 = (r_1 + r_2)/\beta$; the spatial-noise coefficient is forced to
zero, $i_3 = 0$. The strengthened axiom \texttt{FirstOrderKMS} (in
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/SKDoubling.lean}, lines~367--379) encodes
all seven; the uniqueness theorem \texttt{firstOrder\_uniqueness}
(lines~412--439, Aristotle run~\texttt{270e77a0}) closes cleanly
under it (under positivity, dynamical-KMS, and $\beta > 0$ at
positive temperature). The two-parameter $(\gamma_1, \gamma_2)$ family is
recovered as a corollary. The four field components Aristotle's first-pass
weak constraint \emph{did} hold are exactly the four whose KMS partners
fit within the first-order derivative budget; the five it \emph{did not}
hold --- the derivatives of $\psi_a$ and second derivatives of $\psi_r$
--- are precisely the five whose KMS partners exceed first order and
instead surface at second order in the SK-EFT loop expansion (where
they are formalized in the program's Glorioso--Liu axiomatic Lean
modules \texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/GloriosoLiu/\{Axioms,FirstOrderProjection,SecondOrderProjection\}.lean}). The four-of-nine partition
recovered by Aristotle's counterexample is, in this reading, a
\emph{theorem} of first-order projection rather than an empirical
observation.

The published analog-Hawking spectrum and noise-floor formula in the
project's Tier~1 deep papers depend numerically on $i_3 = 0$; the
original weakened constraint did not force $i_3 = 0$, and a paper
using it would have shipped the wrong noise floor. The pedagogical
content of this case study survives intact: an automated theorem
prover's \emph{failure to prove} a stated theorem can be diagnostic
information about the theorem's correctness, not a deficiency of the
prover. The structural content is upgraded by the framing above: the
diagnosis is that the original axiom encoded a \emph{projection} of
dynamical-KMS that omitted the second-order content the published
axiom carries, not a project-original ad-hoc choice that happened to
be wrong.

content of this case; the upgraded framing identifies precisely
\emph{why} the original statement was wrong --- it omitted the
second-order content the dynamical-KMS axiom carries explicitly in
the published Glorioso--Liu skeleton.

\section{Worked Case~2: The Gap-Solution-Bounded Conjecture Disproof}
\label{sec:case2}

% TODO: substantive draft. Substrate to draw from:
%  - The literature claim that a gap solution must be bounded
%  - The formalization-driven counterexample search that refuted it
%  - The Lean module containing the explicit unbounded counterexample
%  - The PR (or commit-pinned counterexample reference) that
%    documents the formal disproof
%
% Section thesis: formal verification is not just for confirming
% known results; the systematic structure of a Lean formalization
% can drive counterexample search that informal pen-and-paper work
% does not. In this case, attempting to prove the conjecture
% surfaced the precise sufficient hypothesis that was missing,
% which then exposed an explicit counterexample.

The emergent-gravity sector of the project formalizes the
Asymptotic-Darboux-Witt (ADW) gap equation, a Nambu--Jona-Lasinio-type
self-consistency condition relating a fermion-bilinear gap $\Delta$,
a four-fermion coupling $G$, a UV cutoff $\Lambda$, and a fermion
flavor count $N_f$. The published literature on this and analogous
NJL gap equations frequently asserts as ``obvious'' that the gap
solution is bounded by the cutoff: $\Delta < \Lambda$ for all
supercritical $G > G_c$. The bound is morally consistent with the
EFT interpretation (the gap is a low-energy parameter; an
above-cutoff gap is past the regime of validity) and is widely
treated as folklore.

Our first formalization of the gap equation included a
\texttt{gap\_solution\_bounded} theorem asserting exactly this. The
proof was not closing under interactive iteration, so the theorem was
submitted as part of Aristotle priority batch~1
(\texttt{SKEFTHawking.TetradGapEquation.gap\_solution\_monotone}
in \texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/TetradGapEquation.lean},
lines~329--345; the deprecated false claim survives as a
commented-out stub at lines~307--321). Aristotle returned an explicit
counterexample, again rather than a proof: at
$G = 1/(c_4/2 \cdot (1 - \log 2))$, $N_f = 1$, $\Lambda = 1$, the
gap solution is exactly $\Delta = 1 = \Lambda$, a fixed point of the
self-consistency equation that saturates the would-be bound.
Sufficiently strong coupling drives $\Delta$ to the cutoff and (we
later verified by direct numerical solve) above it for further
increase in $G$. The folklore claim is false on its face for the
parameter range one would actually want.

The theorem was rewritten as \texttt{gap\_solution\_monotone}
(asserting only that $\Delta(G)$ is monotone in $G$, which is true)
plus a tracked-hypothesis bound that names the additional structural
assumption (an upper bound on $G$) under which a $\Delta < \Lambda$
conclusion follows. The corrected statement and its proof are in
Aristotle run~\texttt{79e07d55}, alongside eight other gap-equation
theorems that closed in the same batch. The original
\texttt{gap\_solution\_bounded} survives in the codebase as a
commented-out stub at lines~307--321 of \texttt{TetradGapEquation.lean},
preserved as a citable counterexample anchor for downstream papers
that would otherwise inherit the folklore.

The case is a clean
illustration of formalization-driven counterexample search: the
counterexample exists in the literature only because the formal
pipeline forced its production.

% case decomposed nine-deep and Aristotle closed all nine
% sub-lemmas in a single submission.

The chirality-wall mechanism is a Standard-Model obstruction to
electroweak baryogenesis under the constraint that the Higgs
sector is a substrate-emergent bilinear of an underlying
condensed-matter Hilbert space. The compound viability statement
\emph{electroweak baryogenesis is forbidden in the Standard Model
without right-handed neutrinos}
(\texttt{sm\_no\_nu\_R\_ewbg\_doubly\_forbidden} in
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/EWBaryogenesisChiralityWall.lean})
combines two independent obstructions: (i) the chirality wall
arising from the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly classification, and
(ii) the Standard-Model crossover-not-first-order phase
transition. Each obstruction has multiple internal moving parts.
Stated monolithically, the theorem has roughly fifty term-level
constraints distributed across the two obstructions and their
case distinctions.

A monolithic proof attempt failed against both interactive
tactics and against Aristotle. The interactive-MCP loop's
\texttt{lean\_goal} returned a goal state that was too large to
parse on a single screen; tactic candidates that closed any single
component cascaded the rest into states that the tactic search
could not resolve. Aristotle priority-batch submission returned
\texttt{OUT\_OF\_BUDGET} after six hours of search.

The fix was a nine-fold structural decomposition driven by the
project's structural-axiom registries in
\texttt{src/core/constants.py}: \texttt{AXIOM\_METADATA} (the
true-axiom table; tier $\in \{\texttt{closed}, \texttt{hard},
\texttt{removed}\}$), \texttt{PLACEHOLDER\_THEOREMS} (placeholder
markers per Invariant~9), \texttt{HYPOTHESIS\_REGISTRY} (tracked
\texttt{Prop} bundles; tier $\in \{\texttt{algebraic},
\texttt{hard}, \texttt{very\_hard}, \texttt{open}\}$), and
\texttt{BORDISM\_HYPOTHESES} (Lean Mathlib bordism-deferred
hypotheses; tier $\in \{\texttt{topological}\}$). Together these
form a four-table eliminability landscape covering six tier
values; each entry carries a \texttt{reason} or
\texttt{evidence\_*} field documenting the structural rationale.

The chirality-wall theorem's load-bearing structural assumptions
decompose along three pillars, each anchored on one entry of one
of these tables (registry line numbers as of June~2026):
(i) the $\mathbb{Z}_{16}$ anomaly chain, backed
by the chiral-central-charge entry
\texttt{c\_minus\_equals\_8Nf} in
\texttt{HYPOTHESIS\_REGISTRY} (line 2282, tier
\texttt{algebraic}, derived from particle content);
(ii) the four-plus-one-dimensional gapped-interface hypothesis,
encoded at decomposition time as the
\texttt{gapped\_interface\_axiom} entry in
\texttt{AXIOM\_METADATA} (line 1391; tier \texttt{hard} at the
time of the decomposition, since retired: on 2026-05-19 the
project-local axiom was converted into the tracked-hypothesis
\texttt{Prop} \texttt{TPFConjecture}, with every consumer theorem
now taking the conjecture as an explicit hypothesis parameter and
the entry's eliminability moved to \texttt{closed} --- the tracked
dimensional ladder of evidence is unchanged: proven at $1{+}1$D
and $2{+}1$D, conjectural at $3{+}1$D and $4{+}1$D);
(iii) the topological-pivoting-frame no-go evasion, anchored on

\texttt{PLACEHOLDER\_THEOREMS} (line 2127; per Invariant~9, this

\section{The Fourteen-Stage Wave Execution Pipeline}
\label{sec:pipeline}

The pipeline is organized as fourteen sequential stages, each with
a gate that must pass before the next stage begins. Skipping
stages or reordering them produces rework and allows errors to
propagate downstream. Wave-level work --- adding new physics,
extending formalization, or producing experimental predictions
--- proceeds through all fourteen.

replaces the prior write-tactic / \texttt{lake build} / parse /

The current pipeline extends the original twelve-stage version
(paper15 draft, April 2026) with three additional stages.
Stage~3a, interactive Lean development via the language-server
protocol, replaces the previous ``write tactic, run \texttt{lake
build}, parse error, iterate'' development loop (approximately
fifteen seconds per cycle) with a millisecond-latency interactive
loop using the \texttt{lean-lsp-mcp} tool integration. The
interactive loop reads the proof state at any line and column,
tests candidate tactic sequences without editing the file, and
returns ``no goals'' when the proof is complete. Per tactic-test cycle, the interactive
loop replaces a $\sim\!15$\,s lake-build round-trip with
millisecond-scale LSP state inspection. End-to-end
proof-construction time on a given theorem improves by a factor
determined by the number of tactic-test cycles the proof requires;
we have not measured this factor on a controlled benchmark set, and
quantitative speedup claims would require such a benchmark to be
load-bearing.

Stage~13, fresh-context adversarial review, runs a Lean-aware
adversarial reviewer agent in a fresh context window against each
paper or bundle near submission readiness. The agent works through
eight finding classes (citation integrity, parameter provenance,
formal-verification-claim substantiation, cross-paper
consistency, narrative grounding, assumption disclosure, count
freshness, and production-run health --- the same eight classes
enumerated in detail in Section~\ref{sec:adversarial}) and emits
a structured markdown report.
Findings are auto-picked up as graph nodes; ``BLOCKER'' findings
flip the affected readiness gate to blocked. Stage~13 is meaningful
only on a codebase that already passes its own internal validation
(Stages~1--12); it backstops the failure classes that internal
validation cannot detect by construction.

Stage~14, meta-process quality improvement, scans the cumulative
adversarial-review findings for systemic patterns: failure classes
that recur across multiple papers, or findings that indicate a gap
in the pipeline itself. Such patterns are recorded in a quality-
improvement register with owners and target dates. Stage~14 is
advisory; it never blocks publication. Its purpose is feeding
pipeline improvements back into subsequent waves. The April~2026
external-adversarial-review round, which surfaced a thirteen-
dimensional problem space across the project, was the seed event
for the register.

Stages~1--12 are the substantive computational and formalization
work. Stage~1 establishes the canonical sources of truth: physical
constants in \texttt{constants.py}, formulas in
\texttt{formulas.py}, figure functions in \texttt{visualizations.py}.
Stage~2 implements formulas with provenance metadata. Stages~3--4
formalize and prove (interactive plus automated). Stage~5 verifies
the formal layer compiles cleanly. Stage~6 runs Python tests.
Stage~7 runs the automated cross-layer validation suite ---
thirty-three registered checks at the time of writing
(June~2026), enumerated live by \texttt{validate.py --list} ---
spanning formula--theorem correspondence, parameter provenance,
count freshness, citation integrity, and bundle consistency
(Sections~\ref{sec:provenance} and~\ref{sec:adversarial}).
Stage~8 generates publication-
quality figures. Stage~9 runs a figure-reviewer agent. Stage~10
drafts the paper with full provenance for every claim. Stage~11
produces reproducible computational notebooks. Stage~12 syncs
documentation and runs counts regeneration. Each stage's gate must
pass before proceeding.

The pipeline's invariants must hold at all times, not just at stage
completion:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Canonical formulas.} \texttt{formulas.py} is the only
place physics formulas live.

\item \textbf{Canonical constants.} \texttt{constants.py} is the only
place experimental parameters and the theorem registry live.

\item \textbf{Canonical visualizations.} \texttt{visualizations.py}
is the only place figure functions live.

\item \textbf{Every formula has a Lean theorem.} Every Lean theorem
has a proof (zero sorry target).

\item \textbf{Every computed quantity has bounds.} Physical bounds
are tested and enforced by validation.

\item \textbf{Every paper claim traces to computation.} Numerical
claims match formula output within 0.5\%.

\item \textbf{Narrative derives from data.} Feasibility claims need
computed support.

\item \textbf{Every parameter has verified provenance.} Each value
traces to a published source via the
\texttt{PARAMETER\_PROVENANCE} table.

\item \textbf{Placeholder theorems are non-load-bearing.} Theorems
proved as \texttt{True := trivial} are documentation markers
only. Tracked in a \texttt{PLACEHOLDER\_THEOREMS} registry; not
counted as substantive.

\item \textbf{No heartbeat overrides in proof bodies.} A proof body
that hits the heartbeat limit is evidence that the proof
architecture is wrong; the fix is sub-lemma decomposition, not
raising the budget.

\item \textbf{Every external bibitem has a primary-source cache
file.} Each non-in-prep entry in the citation registry carries a
resolvable path to a cached copy of the cited primary source,
held in a per-phase literature cache maintained alongside the
repository, structurally preventing the hallucinated-citation
failure mode.

\item \textbf{Provenance DOIs resolve to the registry.} Every DOI
mentioned in any parameter-provenance source must resolve to a
registry bibkey; every cited bibkey must exist in the registry.

\item \textbf{Stage~14 quality-improvement register is auto-
regenerated and manually curated, never wiped.} Closed items
are preserved verbatim across regenerations.

\item \textbf{Every paper-shaped output lifts into a publication
bundle.} New drafts, section additions, and notebook companions
identify their target bundle at Stage~1 and record it in the
draft-mapping document at Stage~12. No new stand-alone paper
drafts are created without explicit user authorization to add a
new bundle target beyond the current seventeen.

\item \textbf{Every new project-local axiom requires explicit
user sign-off.} Locked in May~2026. No axiom ships on
recommendation-only authority; every new axiom carries either a
discharge plan (a scheduled wave producing a substantive
constructive proof) or a documented argument that no constructive
proof is feasible against the current upstream mathematics
library. The posture is that axioms are temporary scaffolding,
not permanent commitments: the project's last pre-existing
project-local axiom was retired into a tracked-hypothesis
\texttt{Prop} on 2026-05-19 (Section~\ref{sec:case3}), taking the
live project-local axiom count to zero.

\end{enumerate}

Invariant~9 addresses a subtle inflation problem:
\placeholdertheorems{} of our \totaltheorems{} theorems are
\texttt{True := trivial} placeholders that serve as documentation
markers. These are tracked in a registry
(\texttt{PLACEHOLDER\_THEOREMS}) and excluded from substantive
theorem counts; the substantive count is \substantivetheorems{}.

Invariant~14 is the publication-bundle architecture, described in
Section~\ref{sec:provenance}.

\section{The Preemptive-Strengthening Discipline}
\label{sec:strengthening}

The strengthening pass is an audit that runs at the end of each
wave, examining each new theorem statement for six recurring
anti-patterns. Originally retroactive --- the audit ran after
theorems were stated and proved, with retroactive cuts applied
where the audit identified a vacuous statement --- the pass has
been moved to the prospective phase: each theorem statement is
checked against the same six anti-patterns \emph{before} the
statement is written. The reduction in audit cost is
substantial: the wave that introduced the discipline (Phase~6c.3)
required twelve retroactive cuts; the next wave (Phase~6b.1)
required five; the wave after that (Phase~6m R6) required zero.

The six anti-patterns are:

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{P2 --- Bundle redundancy.} A theorem stating a
conjunction is redundant if dropping any conjunct produces a
statement of equal information content. The pattern occurs when
multiple conjuncts are algebraically equivalent: for instance,
$\mathrm{satisfiesAt}\ m\ \mathrm{WEP}\ \wedge\ \mathrm{satisfiesAt}\
m\ \mathrm{EEP}\ \wedge\ \mathrm{satisfiesAt}\ m\ \mathrm{SEP}$ when
$\mathrm{violationLevel}\ m = \mathrm{none}$ is one substantive
theorem dressed as three. The fix is to write the substantive form
($\mathrm{violationLevel}\ m = \mathrm{none}$) plus a separate
extraction lemma if downstream callers need the bundle shape.

\item \textbf{P3 --- Multiplication-only triviality.} A theorem
whose substantive content is exhausted by an obvious arithmetic
identity ($0.34 = 2 \times 0.17$, for instance) is structural, not
physical. The fix is to replace with a load-bearing comparison
that separates a conformant case from a violator
($0.34 < 1.0$).

\item \textbf{P4 --- Self-discharging axiom.} An axiom whose
statement reduces to truth via algebra alone is vacuous. The fix
is to either remove the axiom or write the substantive content
elsewhere.

\item \textbf{P5 --- Falsifier as restated hypothesis.} A
``falsifier'' theorem whose body restates its hypotheses without
exercising any computation is structural-tautology repackaging.
The fix is to ensure the falsifier exercises the hypothesis.

\item \textbf{P6 --- Cross-module bridge integrity.} If a
theorem's docstring references another module's theorem, the body
must \texttt{import} that module and \emph{call} the theorem ---
not merely list it as a documentation pointer. The audit pattern
is: every cross-reference in a docstring must be backed by a Lean
call in the body. A bridge whose body never invokes the named
target is documentation-only and rots silently as the codebase
evolves.

\item \textbf{P7 --- Defining the conclusion.} If a function is
defined as \texttt{:= <obvious target>}, a theorem that asserts
$0 \leq C/A$ for that function is trivially true. The fix is to
either push the substantive content elsewhere (a tracked-hypothesis
proposition, or a project-local infrastructure module) or commit to
a non-trivial concrete model.

\end{itemize}

The discipline is applied as a five-question prospective checklist
on each theorem statement before it is written. The reduction in
strengthening-pass cost is the primary metric, but a ruthless
post-wave review remains mandatory --- empirically, the
prospective discipline catches the more obvious failure modes
($\exists$-absorption, biconditional-tautology in conditional
violators) but misses subtler patterns: identity-function wrappers
($\theta\ h_a = h_b$), within-own-$\pm 2\sigma$-band tautologies,
pairwise-distinctness on inductive constructors, and definitional-
unfolding-as-physics. Both passes are mandatory.

The discipline is not aesthetic. Each anti-pattern corresponds to
a specific failure of the theorem-statement-as-falsifiable-claim
property: a theorem that can be discharged by \texttt{rfl} or
\texttt{decide} or \texttt{not\_lt.mpr h\_disagree} or
$0 \leq C$ for any positive $C$ does not constitute a falsifiable
physical claim. The strengthening discipline ensures that what we
prove is what we claim.

Sentence-level provenance is the mechanism that scales the per-claim
audit discipline from a single paper to a multi-paper publication
program. Each prose sentence in every paper draft acquires a record
in a structured registry (\texttt{papers/<paper>/prose\_state.json},
schema-defined in \texttt{scripts/sentence\_state.py}) that captures
the claim type (numeric, theorem-reference, citation, parameter,
formal-claim, qualitative, transition, methodology, or metaclaim), a
chain of backing links (formula in \texttt{formulas.py}, theorem in
the Lean source, axiom in \texttt{AXIOM\_METADATA}, parameter in
\texttt{PARAMETER\_PROVENANCE}, citation in
\texttt{CITATION\_REGISTRY}, hypothesis in
\texttt{HYPOTHESIS\_REGISTRY}), an agent verdict (PASS, FAIL, WARN,
INFO, UNGROUNDED, TRANSITION) from the most recent claims-reviewer
run, and a content-hash sentence identifier
(\texttt{sentence:<paper>:<section\_slug>:<sha8>}) that survives
prose edits within a paper but flips when the substantive claim
changes.

The sentence ID is the stable handle. Adversarial-review findings
are pinned to sentence IDs rather than line numbers, so an author's
prose edit in one paragraph does not invalidate findings on
neighboring paragraphs. Re-review is incremental: only sentences
whose hash has changed since the last claims-reviewer run need fresh
chain resolution; the rest are loaded from the prior
\texttt{claims\_review.json}.

Cross-paper consistency clusters are the next layer. A claim made in
two or more papers (a numerical bound, a theorem statement, a
parameter value) groups its sentence IDs into a cluster registered
in \texttt{papers/claim\_clusters.json}, with a
\texttt{cross\_bundle: true} flag for clusters spanning two or more
publication bundles. The validation check
\texttt{validate.py --check bundle\_consistency} walks
the registry and flags any cluster whose member sentences disagree
on the cluster's load-bearing fact. The load-bearing example is
GW170817 vs vestigial-graviton: the L1 PRL splash and the D3 deep
paper both report the LIGO $\Delta c/c$ bound and the
$7\times 10^{14}$ falsification factor; the
\texttt{bundle\_consistency} check enforces that the
two reports agree character-for-character on the numerical content.

The publication-bundle architecture (codified in the process
document \texttt{docs/PAPER\_STRATEGY.md} in the public repository)
consolidates the project's seventy-plus mapped sources (per-wave
drafts plus Lean-only synthesis handles) into seventeen
publication targets organized into five tiers: a flagship review
(\texttt{F}), eight themed deep papers
(\texttt{D1}--\texttt{D8}), three PRL-style headline letters
(\texttt{L1}--\texttt{L3}), three infrastructure papers (\texttt{I1},
\texttt{I2}, \texttt{I3}), and two experimental letters (\texttt{E1},
\texttt{E2}). Each per-wave draft maps to one bundle target via an
append-only assignment table
(repository document \texttt{docs/PAPER\_DRAFT\_MAPPING.md}); each
bundle accumulates
contributions from its sources via a documented lift workflow
(repository document \texttt{docs/BUNDLE\_LIFT\_PROCEDURE.md},
fourteen canonical steps);
late-arriving Phase~6X waves absorb into already-drafted bundles via
a hot-swap protocol
(repository document \texttt{docs/LATE\_PHASE6\_ABSORPTION\_PROTOCOL.md},
stages~A--G with explicit branches D.1 / D.2 / D.3 / D.4 by bundle
state). Per-bundle
state is captured in \texttt{papers/<bundle>/bundle\_metadata.json},
whose fields include the three review-stage
statuses (Stage~9 / 10 / 13), a freshness flag, the source-manifest
last-regeneration timestamp, the stage-13 review document path, and
the recorded-review timestamps consumed by the readiness semantics
of Section~\ref{sec:adversarial}. Pipeline
Invariant~14 codifies the contract: every paper-shaped output lifts
into a bundle target identified at Stage~1 and recorded at
Stage~12. The architecture is what makes seventy-plus-source to
seventeen-publication consolidation tractable without sacrificing
per-claim provenance: the sentence-level audit trail moves with the
prose; the cluster registry preserves cross-bundle consistency; the
bundle metadata captures every reviewer agent's verdict.

Two deposit-time process standards extend the bundle architecture
to publication mechanics. First, the manuscript-level disclosure
of machine assistance is \emph{register-derived rather than
prose-discretionary}: a single canonical disclosure text
(repository document \texttt{docs/DISCLOSURE\_TEXT.md}) exists in
exactly two variants, differing only in one conditional sentence
crediting the Aristotle automated theorem prover, and the variant
each bundle carries is computed deterministically
(\texttt{scripts/aristotle\_usage\_by\_bundle.py}) from the
canonical registers --- the Aristotle run registry, the per-paper
declared Lean modules, the draft-to-bundle mapping, and the Lean
dependency export --- rather than chosen by the author. Second,
deposit sequencing (repository document
\texttt{docs/ARXIV\_DEPOSIT\_PLAN.md}) is gated on completion of
the content-level attribution sweep described in
Section~\ref{sec:adversarial}, and the deposit-time conversion of
in-preparation self-citations into deposited identifiers is a
single documented scripted pass
(\texttt{scripts/convert\_inprep\_citations.py}) that updates the
citation registry, rewrites the affected bibliography items, and
writes the cache stubs the primary-source invariant requires ---
keeping Invariant~11 unbroken across the deposit transition.

\label{sec:three_layer_extended}

The role of automated theorem proving in the pipeline has shifted
substantially over the year of operation. The original twelve-stage version of the pipeline (an earlier
internal draft, April 2026) treated Aristotle as the primary
proof-construction tool: a Lean
theorem statement was registered with a strategy-hint comment in
its docstring, the entire project was submitted in a priority
batch, and the returned proofs were integrated after a manual
diff review. The cycle time was approximately one batch per day,
with batch sizes of ten to forty theorems.

The introduction of Stage~3a --- interactive Lean development via
the \texttt{lean-lsp-mcp} integration --- changed the balance.
Interactive iteration on a single theorem now takes seconds, not
hours; the tool reads the goal state at the cursor, tests four to
six candidate tactic sequences without editing the file, and
returns the resulting goal state for each. A typical proof that
would have been submitted to Aristotle in the original workflow
now closes interactively in three to five tactic-test cycles
(see Section~\ref{sec:pipeline} for the per-cycle latency
discussion). Aristotle has correspondingly retreated from
default proof-construction tool to fallback-for-residual-sorries.

The residual role is genuine, not vestigial. Three classes of
proof retain a strong indication for Aristotle. The first is
proofs whose tactic search space is too large for interactive
candidate-testing: lengthy algebraic manipulations on
non-commutative ring expressions, multi-step bidegree
decompositions with case splits at every step, and Sobolev-type
inequalities with auxiliary functions whose definitions are not
suggested by the local goal state. The second is proofs that
submit cleanly only after a structural decomposition into
sub-lemmas (the chirality-wall case of Section~\ref{sec:case3}),
where the sub-lemmas are individually small but numerous enough
that interactive proof of all of them is more expensive than a
single batch submission. The third is the diagnostic role
illustrated in Section~\ref{sec:case1} and
Section~\ref{sec:case2}: an Aristotle failure on a mathematically
plausible statement is informative when the failure is reported
as an explicit counterexample, surfacing a wrong axiom or a
folklore-but-false claim that interactive iteration would not

\section{The Fresh-Context Adversarial-Reviewer Pattern}
\label{sec:adversarial}

% TODO: substantive draft. Substrate to draw from:
%  - .claude/plugins/physics-qa/agents/adversarial-reviewer.md
%  - WAVE_EXECUTION_PIPELINE.md Stage 13 section
%  - The April 2026 external-adversarial-review round that
%    surfaced the 13-dimension problem space (seed event)
%  - The 8 finding classes (citation integrity, parameter
%    provenance, narrative grounding, cross-paper consistency,
%    count freshness, formal-verification-claim substantiation,
%    assumption disclosure, review-state hygiene)
%  - The supersession ledger semantics (docs/review_finding_supersessions.json)
%
% Section thesis: the adversarial reviewer runs in a fresh context
% (no memory of the author's prior justifications), works through
% 8 finding classes systematically, and produces a structured
% markdown report that flips readiness gates to blocked when
% BLOCKER findings are present. The fresh-context property is
% load-bearing --- the reviewer must not be persuadable by the
% author's prior context.

The fresh-context adversarial reviewer is the pipeline's
load-bearing safeguard against the failure classes that internal
validation cannot detect by construction. The April~2026
external-adversarial-review round on the project's per-paper drafts
exposed a thirteen-dimensional problem space --- thirteen distinct
failure-mode categories that none of the project's internal
\texttt{validate.py} checks were designed to surface --- including
wrong-target arXiv citations (one paper cited a graph-neural-network
arXiv ID where the intended citation was a torsion-cosmology
paper), parameter values that had drifted from their primary
sources after late-stage rounding, narrative overclaims that were
factually wrong (a ``first machine-checked'' claim that proved to
have an unrecognized prior art in Coq), and ``formally verified''
prose claims whose Lean theorems were placeholder
\texttt{True := trivial} markers. The seed event motivated
Stage~13 as a permanent pipeline component.

The agent's design rests on the \emph{fresh-context} property: the
reviewer runs in a context window that has not seen the author's
prior justifications, internal communications, or in-development
state. By construction, a reviewer in the same context as the author
has internalized the author's framing; the reviewer's findings are
limited to what the author's framing permits. A fresh-context
reviewer reads the paper as a reader would, with no privileged
backchannel into the author's intent, and emits findings against
the artifact rather than against the author. The architectural
choice is that the agent runs in a separate Claude Code session
(separate context window, separate skill loading); the prompt is
the \texttt{physics-qa} plugin's adversarial-reviewer agent
definition plus the paper or bundle target.

The agent works through eight finding classes in strict order, one
class at a time, against eleven readiness gates:
(1)~CitationIntegrity --- every \texttt{\textbackslash bibitem} is
fetched against arXiv or DOI and compared character-for-character;
(2)~ParameterProvenance --- every numerical parameter is checked
against \texttt{PARAMETER\_PROVENANCE}; (3)~LeanProofSubstance ---
every cited Lean theorem is read at its declaration site and its
proof body inspected for placeholder patterns; (4)~CrossPaperConsistency
--- the paper is diff'd against every other paper for shared
constructs; (5)~NarrativeGrounding --- first-claims, unification
claims, attribution claims, feasibility claims, and
simulation-evidence claims are scanned against the artifact;
(6)~AssumptionDisclosure --- every cited theorem's load-bearing
hypotheses must appear in prose; (7)~CountFreshness --- count
literals must match the canonical \texttt{counts.tex} macros;
(8)~ProductionRunHealth --- numerical-evidence claims must trace
to a successful \texttt{ProductionRun} graph node.

The output is a structured markdown report at
\texttt{papers/AutomatedReviews/<DATE>-internal-adversarial/<paper>.md}
with finding severity in \{BLOCKER, REQUIRED, RECOMMENDED\}.
\texttt{ReviewFinding} graph nodes are auto-extracted by
\texttt{scripts/build\_graph.py:extract\_review\_finding\_nodes}
with \texttt{FLAGS} edges to the affected paper or bundle, and
BLOCKER findings flip the affected
\texttt{ReadinessGate} status to \texttt{blocked}. Author-side fix
appends to a supersession ledger
(\texttt{docs/review\_finding\_supersessions.json}); on next graph
build the supersession overrides the finding's status to
\texttt{fixed} or \texttt{accepted}; \texttt{validate.py --check
readiness\_submission\_gate} re-evaluates the bundle.

The bundle-level extension of the pattern was added in Phase~6i
Wave~7. The orchestrator
\texttt{scripts/review\_runner.py --bundle <X> --prep-brief}
emits a per-bundle review brief; the agent walks every source
paper assigned to the bundle (per
\texttt{docs/PAPER\_DRAFT\_MAPPING.md}) plus the bundle's own
draft; output goes to
\texttt{papers/AutomatedReviews/<DATE>-bundle-stage13/<X>.md}.
The per-bundle anchor list at
\texttt{docs/agents/claims-reviewer-bundle-prompts.md} carries the
load-bearing claims and citations the bundle's review must verify.
Cross-bundle cluster mismatches (\texttt{cross\_bundle: true}
clusters in \texttt{cluster\_bundle\_index.json}) emit
\texttt{BundleConsistencyMismatch} findings.

The review-state semantics were hardened in June~2026 with a
\emph{recorded-review} requirement. A bundle's readiness verdict
can reach green only when three conditions hold simultaneously:
zero open blocker-class findings, at most five open advisories,
\emph{and} a recorded fresh-context Stage-13 review --- the review
event itself, with its date and report path, captured in the
bundle's \texttt{bundle\_metadata.json}. The third condition is
the substantive one. Before the change, a bundle with no recorded
findings was indistinguishable from a bundle that had been
reviewed and passed; ``no findings recorded'' is not evidence of
review, it is evidence of nothing. The hardened semantics
introduce an explicit \emph{yellow (unreviewed)} verdict class for
bundles with zero open blockers but no recorded fresh-context
review, so the readiness heatmap cannot silently launder the
absence of review into a pass.

The June~2026 external-review remediation also surfaced the most
instructive citation-integrity lesson of the program to date. The
pipeline's citation machinery to this point --- the primary-source
cache (Invariant~11), registry identifier resolution, and the
bibliographic-title drift detector --- validates citation
\emph{metadata}. The remediation identified a failure class that
metadata validation cannot catch by construction: a citation can
be metadata-valid --- the identifier resolves, the primary source
is cached, the bibliographic data are correct --- while the
\emph{claim the prose attributes to it} is fabricated. Three
instances were found in roughly ten spot checks of load-bearing
attributions. In one, a draft attributed a quantitative
susceptibility-range estimate to a cached source that is a
unitarity proof in a lattice-gravity framework and contains no
such analysis. In another, the prose attributed an observational
exclusion to the wrong observables, at the wrong significance,
with the wrong year, relative to the cached paper. In the third,
the citation registry's own change history carried a fabricated
title and wrong journal coordinates for a real paper whose actual
framing is conditional rather than a no-go. All three passed every
metadata-level check, by construction: the sources are real,
cached, and correctly identified --- the attributed \emph{content}
was wrong.

The deterministic response shipped in the same remediation as
three new validation checks. The first
(\texttt{validate.py --check axiom\_count\_prose\_consistency})
scans every paper draft for axiom-count claims and fails any
present-tense claim that disagrees with the live machine-generated
count --- motivated by drafts still asserting the single-axiom
posture after the last project-local axiom was retired into a
tracked-hypothesis \texttt{Prop} on 2026-05-19; the calibration
run alone surfaced sixteen stale prose sites across eleven drafts.
The second
(\texttt{validate.py --check prose\_theorem\_reference\_coverage})
requires every Lean-identifier-shaped reference in monospaced
bundle prose to resolve to a live declaration in the Lean
dependency export, with a documented per-instance waiver mechanism
(this paper's deliberately historical reference to the disproved
folklore bound of Section~\ref{sec:case2} is the canonical
waiver). The third
(\texttt{validate.py --check theorem\_name\_embedded\_citations})
covers declaration names that embed author--year tokens: when a
paper's prose cites such a declaration, the citing paper's
bibliography must contain a matching entry, so a reader can
actually follow the citation the name encodes. The same
remediation also replaced a manually maintained deep-paper
headline count of cross-platform Lean-theorem reuse with a
deterministic scripted count
(\texttt{scripts/count\_theorem\_reuse.py}); the manual figure of
$109/119$ corrected to $106/114 \approx 93\%$, with both the
numerator and the denominator of the manual count having drifted.

Deterministic checks, however, guard \emph{presence and
consistency}; only reading the source against the claim verifies
\emph{support}. The systematic response is a content-level
attribution sweep (repository document
\texttt{docs/roadmaps/AttributionContentSweep\_Roadmap.md}). For
every load-bearing citation--claim pair on a bundle's anchor list,
a fresh-context agent reads the cached primary source and issues a
dated verdict: supported; supported-with-caveat (the paper must
then carry the caveat); not-supported (prose and registry
corrected, with a ledger-style record); or source-missing (fetch,
then verify). Each bundle visit pairs the content pass with a
fresh-context Stage-13 re-invocation, works falsification-critical
bundles first and the flagship last (so the flagship inherits its
siblings' corrections), and installs the register-derived
disclosure block of Section~\ref{sec:provenance}. The sweep's exit
criterion is every bundle green under the recorded-review
semantics above. The base rate that motivated the sweep ---
roughly three fabricated attributions per ten spot-checked --- is
exactly the kind of number a verification program must be willing
to publish about itself: the metadata layer the program had been
treating as citation integrity is necessary but not sufficient.

The algebraic substrate of Section~\ref{sec:phase6f_substrate}
operates pointwise on real-valued tensor components. A neighboring
question --- equally classical, equally ``in the textbooks'' ---
is whether the connection-level structure on a smooth manifold
admits a parallel formalization. The Phase~6f Wave~7 catch-up
shipped the connection-level layer; Wave~8 lifted it from
component-coordinate scope to Bonn's bundle-level
\texttt{IsCovariantDerivativeOn} API. We sketch the result here,
keeping detail proportionate to a methodology-paper sidebar; the

The starting point is the indefinite-signature metric typeclass
\texttt{IsLorentzianMetric}. Mathlib's existing infrastructure on
Riemannian-bundle metrics
(\texttt{Mathlib.Geometry.Manifold.IsRiemannianBundle}, due to
S.~Gou\"ezel) is positive-definite by design; Wave~7's contribution
is the indefinite-signature analogue.

$(-, +, +, +)$), with Minkowski as a primitive witness
(\texttt{$\eta$\_isLorentzianMetric}). Wave~7 (modules
\texttt{LorentzianMetric.lean} + \texttt{RiemannianConnection.lean},

from the Wave~1 algebraic version which constructs Riemann from
the abstract type rather than from the metric), the
Koszul-formula closed form for the Levi-Civita Christoffel
symbols, and a uniqueness predicate at the
Levi-Civita-Christoffel level. A predicate-level falsifier
asserts that a Riemannian-signature metric cannot satisfy
\texttt{IsLorentzianMetric}.

Wave~8 Sessions~1 through~5 (modules
\texttt{RiemannCoordinate.lean},
\texttt{RiemannDifferentialBianchi.lean},
\texttt{BundleRiemannAux.lean}, \texttt{BundleRiemann.lean},
\texttt{LeviCivita.lean}; \phaseSixfWeightTotal{}~theorems total ---

plus quadratic decomposition, the algebraic first Bianchi

\section{Structural-Prop Scoping Under Mathlib-Dependency Fallback}
\label{sec:structural_prop_scoping}

% TODO: substantive draft. Substrate to draw from:
%  - lean/SKEFTHawking/{HawkingPenroseSingularityCurveTheoretic,
%    AreaTheoremCurveTheoretic, RiccatiComparison, ...}.lean
%  - Phase 6g Wave 5 memory file
%  - The 1-D wave equation Fourès-Bruhat distillation case
%  - The pattern: when Mathlib lacks the PDE infrastructure
%    (Cauchy-Kowalevski, energy estimates), structure the
%    substantive content as a tracked Prop bundle that the
%    project-local proof discharges modulo the missing infra,
%    and document the missing infra as a "Mathlib upstream
%    target" rather than as a sorry
%
% Section thesis: when a substantive Lean theorem cannot be
% closed because the upstream PDE / measure-theoretic /
% functional-analytic machinery is unavailable in Mathlib, the
% structurally-correct response is not to invent the missing
% theory in-project. It is to scope the substantive content as a
% tracked Prop bundle, prove every other hypothesis in the
% bundle, and let the missing-infra Prop be the gating hypothesis
% that downstream proofs consume. This avoids in-project
% reinvention of Mathlib while keeping the substantive content
% Lean-checkable.

A recurring failure mode in the project's first two years was
in-project reinvention of upstream mathematical machinery whenever a
target theorem depended on infrastructure absent from Mathlib. The
Cauchy problem for the Einstein vacuum equations, the
Cauchy--Kowalevski theorem, energy estimates for hyperbolic systems,
and distributional Sobolev embedding all entered our Lean wishlist at
various points. Each attempt to formalize from scratch consumed
two-to-eight weeks of effort and produced incomplete substrate that
the next wave was reluctant to reuse. The cumulative cost of these
attempts ran into the months.

The Phase~6g Wave~5 finding, refined at Waves~9 and~10, was that
\emph{the structurally-correct response is not in-project
reinvention}. The pattern is straightforward: when a theorem
depends on Mathlib-absent infrastructure, scope the missing
infrastructure as a tracked-\texttt{Prop} bundle, prove every other
hypothesis in the bundle within project scope, and let the
missing-infrastructure \texttt{Prop} be the gating hypothesis that
downstream proofs consume as an assumption. The result is a
substantive theorem of the form ``modulo Mathlib provides
\texttt{IsLocallyWellPosed}, the Cauchy problem is well-posed and
satisfies $X$.''

Wave~5 itself
(\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/CauchyProblem.lean}) shipped the
Einstein-vacuum Cauchy problem in this form. Wave~9 produced four
substantive curve-theoretic modules
(\texttt{RiccatiComparison.lean}, \texttt{MazurSigmaModelRigidity.lean},
\texttt{HawkingPenroseSingularityCurveTheoretic.lean},
\texttt{AreaTheoremCurveTheoretic.lean}) yielding \phaseSixgWnineTotal{}~theorems
(\riccatiComparisonThms{} + \mazurSigmaModelRigidityThms{} +
\hawkingPenroseCurveThms{} + \areaTheoremCurveThms{} by module) on
Raychaudhuri-Riccati comparison, the
$\sigma$-model 1D rigidity (Mazur's lemma:
$f' \geq 0 \wedge f(0) = f(L) \implies f \equiv f(0)$), the
curve-theoretic Hawking--Penrose singularity theorem with a
de~Sitter strong-energy-condition counterexample, and area-evolution
ODE monotone rigidity. Wave~10 closed the Phase~6g cycle with the
distillation case study: the one-dimensional wave equation
$\partial^2 u / \partial t^2 = c^2\, \partial^2 u / \partial x^2$
reduced from the Einstein vacuum equations in harmonic gauge.
\texttt{WaveEquation1D.lean} ships an
\texttt{IsClassicalWaveSolution} predicate, two explicit witnesses
(constant solution; linear traveling-wave solution),
the linearity corollary
(\texttt{add\_isClassicalWaveSolution}: sum of solutions is a
solution), and the cross-bridge to the structural-\texttt{Prop}
\texttt{IsLocallyWellPosed} that downstream Cauchy-problem theorems
consume.

The discipline is what makes substantive Lean content tractable in
a research domain whose mathematical foundation is not yet in
Mathlib. The cost of admitting a structural assumption with a clear
provenance link to an upstream target is small; the cost of
reinventing the upstream target is large; the cost of \emph{mixing}
in-project partial reinvention with downstream theorems that
consume it is unbounded, because partial reinvention rots
asymmetrically when Mathlib eventually catches up. The pattern's
emergence in Phase~6g, midway through the project, is itself a
methodology lesson: structural-\texttt{Prop} scoping is the right
default from the first wave; we encoded the discipline only after
two months of recoverable cost.

\section{Lessons and Future Work}
\label{sec:lessons}

We close with four lessons drawn from a year of running the
pipeline at the scale described.

\textbf{Continuous verification is cheaper than retrofitted
verification, by a margin large enough to be a workflow choice,
not a methodology preference.} Each of the four failure modes we
opened with --- a numerical claim that traces to a non-existent
code path, a ``formally verified'' claim whose theorem is a
placeholder, a citation that resolves to the wrong paper, a
parameter that has drifted from its primary source --- would
have shipped to draft state under the pre-pipeline workflow. Each
was caught, in this project, by the pipeline's continuous
checks before it could be cited downstream. The cost of running
the checks is on the order of seconds per stage; the cost of
finding and fixing a propagated error after publication is on the
order of weeks per finding, in the best case.

\textbf{Automated theorem proving is a force multiplier on
formalization work, but only when the formalization itself is
well-decomposed.} A monolithic fifty-term proof goal is
intractable to both manual proof and to Aristotle. A decomposed
nine-deep structure with each sublemma at twelve terms or fewer is
tractable to both, and Aristotle closes the sublemmas in a single
priority batch. The decomposition discipline is the pipeline's
load-bearing investment: it pays back in throughput at every
subsequent wave.

\textbf{Adversarial review is a separate role from authorship,
and the separation is load-bearing.} A reviewer in the same
context as the author has, by construction, internalized the
author's prior justifications. The reviewer's findings will, by
construction, be limited to what the author's context permits.
The fresh-context adversarial-reviewer pattern --- the reviewer
agent runs in a context window that has not seen the author's
work --- is the structural fix. The author fixes; the author
re-invokes; iteration converges to clean.

\textbf{Metadata-valid is not content-true.} The citation
machinery of Invariant~11 --- cached primary sources, resolving
identifiers, title-drift detection --- structurally prevents
hallucinated \emph{references}. It does not prevent fabricated
\emph{attributions}: prose ascribing to a real, cached, correctly
cited source a claim the source does not make. At a measured base
rate of roughly three per ten spot-checked load-bearing
attributions (Section~\ref{sec:adversarial}), this is the failure
mode that survived every deterministic layer of the pipeline. The
response --- deterministic prose-consistency checks where the
property is mechanizable, fresh-context content reading where it
is not --- is the pipeline's newest layer, and there is no reason
to believe it is the last.

Three pipeline extensions matured during the year under review
and are now load-bearing. First, a meta-process quality-improvement register
(Stage~14) that surfaces systemic patterns across waves; the seed
event is the April~2026 external-adversarial-review round that
exposed a thirteen-dimensional problem space that internal
validation could not detect by construction. Second, the
publication-bundle architecture that consolidates per-wave drafts
into seventeen publication targets while preserving sentence-level
provenance; this paper is drafted under that architecture. Third, an automated cross-bundle consistency check
that walks a cross-bundle cluster registry and flags member-
sentence drift across bundle boundaries, preventing the same fact
from being stated differently in two papers.

The pipeline is open-source, domain-agnostic, and immediately
applicable in any research domain where the connection between
computation and published claims must be trustworthy. We
specifically encourage adoption in any setting where the cost of a
correctness failure post-publication exceeds the cost of running
continuous verification --- a condition that holds in
pharmaceutical software (FDA SaMD algorithmic vigilance),
verified-risk financial modeling, climate modeling, and any other
research domain where the audit trail from code to claim is itself
the deliverable.
