\begin{abstract}
We formalize, in the Lean~4 proof assistant, the
microscopic-to-macroscopic coefficient match for the ADW
emergent-gravity programme: closed-form expressions for the emergent
Newton constant $\GN$, emergent cosmological constant
$\Lambdaemerg$, and Stelle-basis higher-curvature triple
$(\alpha,\beta,\gamma)$ in microscopic parameters
$(\LambdaUV,\Nf)$. The substantive
\emph{Decision-Gate-E.4} result is a quantitative theorem: at the
natural microscopic point $(\LambdaUV \simeq \MPl,\ \Nf = 16)$, the
heat-kernel-induced
$\Lambdaemerg(\LambdaUV,\Nf) = a_{0}(\Nf)\cdot\LambdaUV^{4}$
exceeds the Planck-2018 observed value
$\Lambdaobs \simeq 2.6\times 10^{-47}\,\mathrm{GeV}^{4}$ by more than
$10^{120}$. The classical cosmological-constant problem is
\emph{reproduced in the emergent-gravity formulation}; no fine-tuning
of $(\LambdaUV,\Nf)$ resolves it within the natural high-energy
parameter band. The Lean proof is closed and zero-sorry; we ship a
companion Python pipeline reproducing the natural-parameter scan and
a verdict-band figure marking the resolved/intermediate/reproduced
phases. Together with Wave~1's Decision Gate~E.2 (heat-kernel $\leftrightarrow$
linearized-EFE Newton-constant calibration), this closes the
microscopic-coefficient match question: the ADW programme provides
\emph{no shelter} from the cosmological-constant fine-tuning problem.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The ADW (Alexandrov-Diakonov-Vergeles) emergent-gravity programme
\cite{Sakharov1968,Adler1982,Diakonov2011,VladimirovDiakonov2012,Vergeles2025}
posits that the Einstein-Hilbert effective action arises from
integrating out a microscopic 8-fermion sector at a hard ultraviolet
cutoff $\LambdaUV$. At the trace level of the variational EFE
(companion paper, in preparation \cite{Roehm2026Wave4}), the
leading Seeley--DeWitt heat-kernel
coefficients $a_{0}, a_{2}$ produce, respectively, a cosmological-
constant scale $\Lambdaemerg \sim a_{0}(\Nf)\cdot\LambdaUV^{4}$ and
the Sakharov-Adler induced Newton constant
$\GNsak = 12\pi/(\Nf\,\LambdaUV^{2})$ \cite{Vassilevich2003}.

The natural question is whether the emergent
$\Lambdaemerg$ matches the Planck-2018 observed cosmological
constant $\Lambdaobs \simeq 2.6\times 10^{-47}\,\mathrm{GeV}^{4}$
\cite{Planck2018}. The classical cosmological-constant problem ---
that natural-cutoff field-theoretic estimates exceed $\Lambdaobs$ by
many orders of magnitude --- is well known; the question for an
emergent-gravity programme is whether the emergent reformulation
provides any \emph{shelter}. Two outcomes are publishable; both are
substantive:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \emph{Resolution}: under natural microscopic parameters,
$\Lambdaemerg$ approximately reproduces $\Lambdaobs$. This would
constitute a derivation of the cosmological constant from
microphysics --- a major theoretical result.
\item \emph{Reproduction}: $\Lambdaemerg$ exceeds $\Lambdaobs$ by
many orders of magnitude even in the natural-cutoff band,
reproducing the cosmological-constant problem in emergent form.
\end{enumerate}

We formalize both outcomes as a categorical \emph{Decision Gate~E.4}
verdict and prove the quantitative claim in the Lean~4 proof
assistant: the natural-parameter point lands strictly in the
``reproduction'' phase, with $\Lambdaemerg > 10^{100}\Lambdaobs$ at
$\LambdaUV \simeq 1.2\times 10^{19}\,\mathrm{GeV},\ \Nf = 16$.
The companion Python pipeline reproduces the corresponding ratio
(approximately $10^{122}$) numerically and labels the verdict band
across the full $(\LambdaUV,\Nf)$ plane.

\section{Microscopic predictions}

\subsection{Definitions}

Following Wave~1 \cite{Roehm2026Wave1}, the leading Seeley--DeWitt
coefficient for $\Nf$ free Dirac fermions in 4D vacuum is
\begin{equation}
  a_{0}(\Nf) = \frac{4\Nf}{(4\pi)^{2}},
  \label{eq:a0}
\end{equation}
matching Vassilevich \cite{Vassilevich2003} Eq.~(4.37) up to the
spinor-trace convention. Concretely, Eq.~(4.37) gives the
single-Dirac-fermion vacuum coefficient $a_{0}^{(\mathrm{single})}(x)
= \mathrm{tr}(\mathbb{1})/(4\pi)^{2} = 4/(4\pi)^{2}$ under the
convention $\mathrm{tr}(\mathbb{1}) = 4$ over the four-component
Dirac matrices; summing over $\Nf$ Dirac species and integrating
against the $\LambdaUV^{4}$ UV-momentum-volume factor reproduces our
$a_{0}(\Nf) \cdot \LambdaUV^{4} = (\Nf/4\pi^{2})\,\LambdaUV^{4}$.
This produces the emergent
cosmological-constant prediction
\begin{equation}
  \Lambdaemerg(\LambdaUV,\Nf) \;=\; a_{0}(\Nf)\cdot\LambdaUV^{4}
  \;=\; \frac{\Nf}{4\pi^{2}}\,\LambdaUV^{4}.
  \label{eq:Lemerg}
\end{equation}
The microscopic Newton constant under the ADW substrate-rescaling is
\begin{equation}
  \GN^{\mathrm{micro}}(\LambdaUV,\Nf,\aADW)
    \;=\; \aADW \cdot \GNsak(\LambdaUV,\Nf),
  \label{eq:GNmicro}
\end{equation}
where $\aADW$ is the dimensionless ADW coefficient
\cite{Vergeles2025}; at the Sakharov-Adler calibration $\aADW=1$
this matches the Phase~6a.1 emergent Newton constant
$\GNem(\LambdaUV,\Nf,1)=\GNsak$ \cite{LinearizedEFE2026}.

The match residual measures the algebraic discrepancy between the
ADW-rescaled microscopic Newton constant and the heat-kernel
baseline,
\begin{equation}
  \delta\GN(\LambdaUV,\Nf,\aADW) \;=\;
    (\aADW - 1)\cdot \GNsak(\LambdaUV,\Nf).
  \label{eq:dGN}
\end{equation}

\section{Discussion: shelter in emergent gravity?}

A common motivation for emergent-gravity programmes is the hope that
emergent formulations might \emph{naturally} produce a cosmologically
small $\Lambda$. Wave~5 closes that hope cleanly for the ADW
programme:
the heat-kernel $a_{0}$ contribution is dimension-correct
($\sim \LambdaUV^{4}$) by construction, and at any natural UV cutoff

$\Lambdaobs$ in the same sense as in standard QFT. The
emergent-gravity reformulation does \emph{not} provide shelter from
the Weinberg cosmological-constant problem.

This is consistent with two related project results:
(i) Phase~5y closure of the dark-sector classification table found
that DE--mechanism candidates fail empirical falsifiers across the
q-theory and ADW landscapes; (ii) Phase~6c Wave~1 strong-CP
topological dark energy achieves $\Lambdaobs$ via a separate
mechanism (Zhitnitsky topological-charge density) at PDG
$\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}$. Wave~5 then constrains the project's CC
mechanism to a \emph{single} channel: the heat-kernel
$a_{0}$ does not produce $\Lambdaobs$ naturally; the Phase~6c
topological-DE mechanism is the canonical CC source.
