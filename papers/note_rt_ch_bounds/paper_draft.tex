\title{Ryu--Takayanagi area law versus a microscopic horizon entropy
with $-(3/2)\,\log(A/4 G_N)$ correction: a Lean~4 inconsistency note}

\author{John G. Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher; \texttt{NetRxn137@gmail.com}}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We formalize, in the Lean~4 proof assistant, the structural
inconsistency between the classical Ryu--Takayanagi area
law~\cite{RyuTakayanagi2006} $S = A / (4 \GN)$ (with no log
correction) and the Kaul--Majumdar microscopic horizon
entropy~\cite{KaulMajumdar2000} $S = A/(4\GN) - (3/2)\log\!\redArea$
(with a universal $-3/2$ log coefficient). The two formulas agree
only on the knife-edge case $A = 4\GN$ (reduced area unity); otherwise
they necessarily differ by exactly the log-correction term. We encode
the Ryu--Takayanagi formula as an external-hypothesis structural
predicate \texttt{H\_RT\_Formula\_Valid} (because the bulk holographic
dual is not constructed in this project) and the universal 2D-CFT
entanglement-entropy log bound $S(L) \le (c/3) \log(L/\varepsilon)$
of Holzhey--Larsen--Wilczek~\cite{HolzheyLarsenWilczek1994} and
Calabrese--Cardy~\cite{CalabreseCardy2004}---with
Casini--Huerta~\cite{CasiniHuerta2009} as the free-QFT
specialization---as a similar predicate
\texttt{H\_CasiniHuerta\_Bound\_Valid} (the predicate name retains
the historical \texttt{H\_CasiniHuerta\_*} prefix used in the Lean
module; see footnote~\ref{fn:ch-name}). The
substantive result is a knife-edge biconditional locating
classical-RT/Kaul--Majumdar agreement at the unique reduced-area
value $A/(4\GN) = 1$, plus a numerical anchor pinning the gap at
canonical reduced area $A/(4\GN) = 2$ to exactly
$(3/2)\log 2 \approx 1.040$. A substantive cross-bridge consumes
both the external Ryu--Takayanagi hypothesis and the Kaul--Majumdar
entropy function, deriving the inconsistency at any non-trivial
reduced area; a falsifier proves that the Kaul--Majumdar function
fails the Ryu--Takayanagi hypothesis. The structural conclusion is
that classical Ryu--Takayanagi must be augmented with a quantum
log correction at the area-log level for consistency with the
Kaul--Majumdar microscopic count. The bulk minimal-surface
construction~\cite{LewkowyczMaldacena2013} and the
Casini--Huerta proof from modular-Hamiltonian methods are deferred.
The module ships \rtChThms{} substantive theorems with zero \texttt{sorry}
statements and zero new axioms.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Two distinct frameworks for black-hole and entanglement entropy
agree on leading-area behavior but disagree at logarithmic order.
Ryu and Takayanagi's holographic area law~\cite{RyuTakayanagi2006},
$S_{\mathrm{RT}} = A / (4 \GN)$, identifies entropy with a bulk
minimal-surface area; this is the leading classical statement, with
no log correction. Kaul and Majumdar's microscopic horizon
entropy~\cite{KaulMajumdar2000},
\begin{equation}
S_{\mathrm{KM}}(A, \GN) \;=\; \redArea \,-\, \tfrac{3}{2}\,\log\!\redArea,
\label{eq:km}
\end{equation}
includes a universal $-3/2$ log coefficient extracted from the
microscopic counting of horizon Chern--Simons states. A separate
non-universality witness in the literature is Sen's 4D Schwarzschild
log coefficient $77/45 \approx 1.711$~\cite{Sen2013Schwarzschild},
which differs from $-3/2$,
indicating that the microscopic log coefficient is not universal
across Lagrangian regularizations.

This paper presents a Lean~4 formalization of the structural content
of this disagreement. We do \emph{not} derive the Ryu--Takayanagi
formula from first principles; the bulk holographic dual is not
constructed in this project. Instead, we encode it as an
external-hypothesis structural predicate and ask: what structural
consequences follow when it is combined with a microscopic horizon
entropy carrying a log correction?

The output is a small but precise theorem set: a knife-edge
biconditional locating the unique reduced area where the two
formulas agree, a numerical anchor pinning the gap at canonical
reduced area $A/(4\GN) = 2$ to $(3/2)\log 2 \approx 1.040$, a
cross-bridge proving inconsistency at every non-trivial reduced area,
and a falsifier proving that the Kaul--Majumdar function fails the
Ryu--Takayanagi hypothesis directly.

\section{External-hypothesis predicates}
\label{sec:tracked}

\paragraph{Ryu--Takayanagi.}
\begin{verbatim}
structure H_RT_Formula_Valid
    (S_BH : R -> R -> R) : Prop where
  rt_proportional :
    forall A G_N, 0 < A -> 0 < G_N ->
    S_BH A G_N = A / (4 * G_N)
\end{verbatim}
A candidate entropy function $S_{\mathrm{BH}} : \mathbb{R} \times
\mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}$ satisfies \texttt{H\_RT\_Formula\_Valid} if
and only if it equals $\redArea$ on the positive cone of
$(A, \GN)$.

\paragraph{Casini--Huerta.}
\begin{verbatim}
structure H_CasiniHuerta_Bound_Valid
    (S_ent : R -> R)
    (c_central UV_cutoff : R) : Prop where
  c_pos : 0 < c_central
  uv_pos : 0 < UV_cutoff
  ch_bound :
    forall L, UV_cutoff < L ->
      S_ent L <=
      (c_central / 3) * Real.log (L / UV_cutoff)
\end{verbatim}
For a 2D conformal-field-theory entanglement entropy with central
charge $c$ and UV cutoff $\varepsilon$, the universal log
bound~\cite{HolzheyLarsenWilczek1994,CalabreseCardy2004} asserts
$S_{\mathrm{ent}}(L) \le (c/3) \log(L / \varepsilon)$ on the
substantive case $L > \varepsilon$%
\footnote{\label{fn:ch-name}The Lean predicate retains the historical
\texttt{H\_CasiniHuerta\_Bound\_Valid} name from earlier project
documents; the universal 2D-CFT result is from Holzhey--Larsen--Wilczek
1994 and Calabrese--Cardy 2004. Casini--Huerta~\cite{CasiniHuerta2009}
is the rigorous free-QFT specialization.}.

\section{Structural consequences of Ryu--Takayanagi}
\label{sec:rt-consequences}

\paragraph*{Theorem (\texttt{rt\_entropy\_pos}).}
Under \texttt{H\_RT\_Formula\_Valid}, the entropy is strictly positive
on the positive cone of $(A, \GN)$. The proof rewrites via the
hypothesis and applies positivity of division.

\paragraph*{Theorem
(\texttt{rt\_kaulMajumdar\_gap\_at\_reduced\_area\_two}).}
At canonical reduced area $A / (4 \GN) = 2$ (equivalently
$A = 8\GN$), the gap between classical Ryu--Takayanagi and the
Kaul--Majumdar microscopic entropy is exactly $(3/2)\log 2
\approx 1.040$. The proof unfolds the Kaul--Majumdar definition,
normalizes the reduced-area arithmetic, and applies \texttt{ring}.

\section{Knife-edge biconditional}
\label{sec:correctness_push}

The structural anchor of this paper is the knife-edge biconditional
\begin{verbatim}
theorem
  rt_eq_kaulMajumdar_iff_trivial_
   reduced_area
    (A G_N : R) (hA : 0 < A) (hG : 0 < G_N) :
  A / (4 * G_N) = kaulMajumdarS A G_N 0
   <-> A / (4 * G_N) = 1
\end{verbatim}
locating classical-Ryu--Takayanagi / Kaul--Majumdar agreement at the
unique reduced area $A / (4\GN) = 1$. By contrapositive, the two
formulas necessarily disagree at every non-trivial reduced area.

The forward direction proceeds by extracting
$\log\!\redArea = 0$ from the assumed equality, applying
$\exp \circ \log = \mathrm{id}$ on positives to derive
$\redArea = 1$, and discharging. The reverse direction collapses
via $\log 1 = 0$.

\paragraph*{Theorem (\texttt{rt\_falsified\_by\_kaul\_majumdar}).}
Under \texttt{H\_RT\_Formula\_Valid} for a candidate $S_{\mathrm{BH}}$,
the Kaul--Majumdar entropy function is structurally incompatible
with $S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ at non-trivial reduced area. The proof body
invokes both the hypothesis (rewriting $S_{\mathrm{BH}}$ to $\redArea$)
and the contrapositive of the biconditional (deriving the
inconsistency).

\section{Casini--Huerta bound positivity and a witness}
\label{sec:ch}

The Casini--Huerta log bound's positivity ships as
\texttt{ch\_log\_bound\_pos\_at\_log\_pos}: under
\texttt{H\_CasiniHuerta\_Bound\_Valid}, the bound
$(c/3) \log(L / \varepsilon)$ is strictly positive on the substantive
case $L > \varepsilon > 0$. The proof combines the structural
hypothesis fields ($c > 0$, $\varepsilon > 0$) with logarithm
positivity and multiplication of positives.

A concrete witness ships as
\texttt{H\_CasiniHuerta\_Bound\_Valid\_witness\_saturated}:
the saturated entropy function
\begin{equation}
S_{\mathrm{sat}}(L) := (c/3) \log(L / \varepsilon)
\end{equation}
satisfies the Casini--Huerta hypothesis with the bound met at
equality. This pins the hypothesis as non-vacuous.

\section{Falsifier: Kaul--Majumdar fails Ryu--Takayanagi}
\label{sec:falsifier}

The Kaul--Majumdar entropy function fails
\texttt{H\_RT\_Formula\_Valid} (theorem
\texttt{kaulMajumdar\_not\_H\_RT}): at $A = 8$ and $\GN = 1$, the
microscopic entropy is $2 - (3/2)\log 2 \approx 0.96$, not the
$2 = A / (4\GN)$ that Ryu--Takayanagi requires. The proof extracts
the discrepancy via $\log 2 > 0$ and a linear-arithmetic step. This
is the sharp distinction between microscopic-quantum-corrected
entropy and classical-area-law entropy.

\section{Formalization summary}
\label{sec:formalization}

The Lean module
\texttt{lean/SKEFTHawking/RTCasiniHuertaBounds.lean} ships \rtChThms{}
substantive theorems, zero \texttt{sorry} statements, and zero new

\section{What is and is not done here}
\label{sec:scope}

The formalization deliberately stays on the structural side. It does
not:

\begin{itemize}
\item Construct the bulk minimal surface; the
Lewkowycz--Maldacena~\cite{LewkowyczMaldacena2013} replica-trick
derivation of Ryu--Takayanagi is not formalized.
\item Derive the Casini--Huerta bound from modular Hamiltonian
methods; the bound enters as a structural hypothesis.
\item Identify which conformal field theory on the boundary
corresponds to a given microscopic substrate; the AdS/CFT spectrum
dictionary is not constructed.
\end{itemize}

These are natural extensions but require substantial additional
infrastructure (replica-trick formalization, modular-Hamiltonian
machinery, holographic-dictionary entries) that we leave for future
work.
