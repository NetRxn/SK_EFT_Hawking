\title{A Falsifiable Hawking Spectrum for Polariton Microcavities at the Paris-LKB Device Parameters}

\author{J.~Roehm}
\affiliation{Independent Researcher}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
We give the SK-EFT-predicted analog Hawking spectrum, surface gravity
$\kappa$, dispersive parameter $D$, and stimulated-Hawking gain at the
Paris-LKB exciton-polariton platform's published device
parameters~\cite{Falque2025}. The reservoir-corrected condensate sound
speed $c_s \approx \SI{0.40}{\micro\meter/\pico\second}$ and upstream
healing length $\xi \approx \SI{3.4}{\micro\meter}$ give
$T_H \approx \SI{85}{\milli\kelvin}$ at the smooth-horizon configuration
($\kappa = \SI{7e10}{\per\second}$, $D \approx 0.60$) and
$T_H \approx \SI{134}{\milli\kelvin}$ at the steep-horizon reach
($\kappa = \SI{1.1e11}{\per\second}$, $D \approx 0.93$). The leading
dispersive correction $\delta_{\rm disp} = -\pi D^2/6$ spans
$\sim$0.19--0.46 across the measured range; perturbative SK-EFT
predictions are reliable in the smooth regime and leading-order at
steep horizon. The stimulated-Hawking gain
$G(\omega) = \Gamma(\omega) / (e^{2\pi \omega/\kappa_{\rm eff}} - 1)$
crosses the single-shot $5\sigma$ threshold $G > 0.5$ at
$\omega < \ln(3)/(2\pi)\,\kappa \approx 0.175\,\kappa$ (universal,
$\kappa$-independent) — yielding
$\mathrm{SNR} \approx 11$ for $N_{\rm probe} \sim 100$ probe photons
per mode --- a $10^3$--$10^6$ improvement over spontaneous-correlation
detection. We emit one falsifiable spectral window:
$\omega/\kappa \in [0.05, 0.30]$ at the smooth-horizon configuration,
where the SK-EFT noise-floor formula is the cleanest. The Lean~4
verification of the underlying chain (5812 theorems / 322 modules / 1
axiom / 0 sorry) is independent of platform parameters, so the
prediction can be inverted: a measured $G(\omega)$ profile constrains
$(c_s, \xi, \kappa)$ jointly and falsifies the SK-EFT framework if
inconsistent. We close with a request to the LKB team for current
device-parameter values to allow band-tightening before measurement.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Analog Hawking radiation has been confirmed in atomic Bose-Einstein
condensates at $T_H \approx \SI{0.35}{\nano\kelvin}$
\cite{Steinhauer2016,deNova2019} but has not yet been observed in any
condensed-matter platform with a temperature advantage large enough to
clear the systematics floor. Exciton-polariton condensates offer such
an advantage: with stronger interactions and sharper spatial gradients
than atomic BECs, predicted $T_H$ in microcavity polaritons sits in
the $\SI{0.1}{\kelvin}$--$\SI{1}{\kelvin}$ range
\cite{Gerace2012,Nguyen2015}. The 2025 Paris-LKB demonstration of
all-optically tunable acoustic horizons with tailored curvature and
the first observation of negative-energy Bogoliubov modes in the
post-horizon region~\cite{Falque2025,Giacobino2025} establishes every
prerequisite for Hawking detection in this platform.

This Letter provides the quantitative SK-EFT prediction needed to
guide the measurement. Three corrections distinguish our prediction
from prior estimates: (i) the reservoir correction to $c_s$, where
the excitonic reservoir absorbs 50--75\% of the polariton blueshift
without contributing to the dynamical sound speed, reducing $c_s$ by
a factor of $\sim$2--3 relative to reservoir-free Bogoliubov estimates
\cite{Estrecho2021,Stepanov2019,Claude2023}; (ii) a stimulated-Hawking
detection pathway via CW pump-probe spectroscopy~\cite{Grisins2016}
that bypasses the quantum-noise floor by operating at the
classical-mean-field level; (iii) formally verified spectral formulas
derived from a Lean~4 chain (5812 theorems / 322 modules / 1 axiom /
0 sorry; \texttt{lake build} clean).

\section{Reservoir-corrected device parameters}

The polariton sound speed enters the acoustic metric as
$c_s = \sqrt{g\,n_{\rm cond} / m^*}$, where only the condensate
density $n_{\rm cond}$ (not the total density including dark
excitons) contributes to the dynamical response. We adopt
Falque et al.~\cite{Falque2025} \S IV.1's measured values directly:
$c_s \approx \SI{0.40}{\micro\meter/\pico\second}$, upstream healing
length $\xi \approx \SI{3.4}{\micro\meter}$. The downstream healing
length is $\xi \approx \SI{4.0}{\micro\meter}$. Two configurations

\section{Hawking temperature and dispersive correction}

The Hawking temperature is universal,
\begin{equation}
T_H = \frac{\hbar \kappa}{2\pi k_B},
\label{eq:T_H}
\end{equation}
formalized in
\texttt{AcousticMetric.hawking\_temp\_from\_surface\_gravity}.
At the smooth-horizon baseline this gives
$T_H \approx \SI{85}{\milli\kelvin}$; at the steep-horizon reach
$T_H \approx \SI{134}{\milli\kelvin}$. The leading dispersive
correction follows from the adiabaticity parameter $D$:
\begin{equation}
\delta_{\rm disp} = -\frac{\pi}{6} D^2,
\label{eq:delta_disp}
\end{equation}
giving $\delta_{\rm disp} \approx -0.19$ (smooth) and
$\delta_{\rm disp} \approx -0.46$ (steep). Perturbative SK-EFT is
reliable in the smooth regime; at the steep-horizon reach,
$D \approx 0.93$ pushes Eq.~\eqref{eq:delta_disp} into the leading-
order regime where higher-derivative corrections become non-negligible.
The steep-horizon prediction should be treated as leading-order,
not all-orders.

\section{Stimulated-Hawking gain spectrum}

A coherent probe at frequency $\omega$ incident on the analog horizon
undergoes anomalous Bogoliubov scattering with amplification
\begin{equation}
G(\omega) = \frac{\Gamma(\omega)}{e^{2\pi\omega/\kappa_{\rm eff}} - 1},
\label{eq:gain}
\end{equation}
where $\Gamma(\omega)$ is the greybody transmission factor and
$\kappa_{\rm eff} \approx \kappa(1 - c_1 D^2)$ incorporates the
leading dispersive correction~\cite{Finazzi2012}. The gain peaks at
low frequencies: $G(0.1\,\kappa) \approx 1.14$,
$G(0.3\,\kappa) \approx 0.18$, $G(\kappa) \approx 0.002$. The
single-shot $5\sigma$ threshold $G > 0.5$ (i.e.,
$N_{\rm probe} \, G^2 \geq 25$ for $N_{\rm probe} = 100$) is crossed
at $\omega < \ln(3)/(2\pi)\,\kappa \approx 0.175\,\kappa$, which is
universal and independent of $\kappa$ — a model-independent spectral
signature of the SK-EFT prediction. Spatial attenuation is bounded below by 1 in
\texttt{PolaritonTier1.attenuation\_ge\_one} and is monotone in
$\Gamma$ via \texttt{PolaritonTier1.attenuation\_mono\_Gamma}; both
are manual Lean proofs with zero \texttt{sorry}.

For shot-noise-limited detection,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{SNR}(\omega) \approx
\sqrt{N_{\rm shots} \cdot N_{\rm probe}} \cdot G(\omega).
\end{equation}
At $\omega = 0.1\,\kappa$ with $G \approx 1.14$, a single CW
measurement with $N_{\rm probe} = 100$ probe photons per mode gives
$\mathrm{SNR} \approx 11$ — a $10^3$--$10^6$ improvement over
spontaneous-Hawking correlation analysis, which requires
$\sim 10^9$ repetitions to resolve correlations at the
$5 \times 10^{-5}$ level~\cite{Grisins2016,Jacquet2022}.
Quasinormal-mode resonances unique to the driven-dissipative
setting~\cite{Jacquet2023QNM,Burkhard2025} concentrate the
stimulated signal at specific frequencies, providing additional
detection windows.

\section{Falsifiable window and noise floor}

The polariton condensate carries an effective driven-dissipative
noise temperature
\begin{equation}
T_{\rm noise} = \frac{m^* c_s^2}{k_B} \approx \SI{0.81}{\kelvin},
\end{equation}
which exceeds $T_H$ by an order of magnitude. This noise is spectrally
featureless (frequency-independent in the hydrodynamic regime) and
does not affect the stimulated measurement, which operates at the
classical mean-field level~\cite{Amelio2020}. The Hawking signal in
contrast has a distinct Planckian frequency dependence in $G(\omega)$.
Correlation analysis exploits this shape difference.

The falsifiable window is therefore
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\omega/\kappa \in [0.05, 0.30]\;}
\end{equation}
at the smooth-horizon configuration. Inside this band, the SK-EFT
prediction $G(\omega) = \Gamma(\omega)/(e^{2\pi\omega/\kappa_{\rm eff}} - 1)$
is the cleanest:
$\delta_{\rm disp}$ is well-controlled ($D = 0.60$ deep in the
perturbative regime); the noise floor is white; and the gain
threshold is crossed (the stimulated signal exceeds the probe by
$\geq 10\%$). A measured $G(\omega)$ profile inside this band
constrains $(c_s, \xi, \kappa)$ jointly, with three sub-percent
uncertainties anywhere on the band falsifying the framework.

\section{Cross-platform context}

The polariton platform sits between BEC (cold, slow, $T_H \sim
\SI{0.35}{\nano\kelvin}$) and graphene Dirac fluids (hot, fast,
$T_H \approx \SI{2.4}{\kelvin}$). The Hawking temperature
$T_H = \hbar\kappa/(2\pi k_B)$ is universal across all three: only
$\kappa$ differs, set by the local velocity-gradient scale. The
SK-EFT corrections all factor through the same dispersive parameter
$D$ and dissipative ratio $\Gamma_{\rm pol}/\kappa$, so the formal
chain that proves $\mathrm{Re}\,G \geq 0$ for the BEC carries
cleanly to the polariton case via
\texttt{PolaritonTier1.attenuation\_ge\_one}. This cross-platform
universality is the structural payoff of the SK-EFT framework: the
prediction here is not a phenomenological re-tuning of a BEC formula,
but the same Lean-verified chain instantiated at LKB parameters.

\section{Conclusion}

The SK-EFT-predicted Hawking spectrum at the Paris-LKB
exciton-polariton platform's measured device parameters is a
falsifiable, formally verified quantitative target. We give explicit
$T_H$ values at the smooth and steep horizons, the universal
$\omega < 0.175\,\kappa$ stimulated-gain detection threshold, the
$\mathrm{SNR} \approx 11$ achievable at $\omega = 0.1\,\kappa$ with
$\sim 100$ probe photons per mode, and the falsifiable spectral
window $\omega/\kappa \in [0.05, 0.30]$ at the smooth horizon. The
LKB platform~\cite{Falque2025,Giacobino2025} now has every
prerequisite for the measurement; the SK-EFT framework supplies the
prediction; the formal verification chain (`lake build` clean,
v4.29.0, Mathlib commit \texttt{8850ed93}) supplies the structural
guarantee that the prediction is internally consistent.
