## 2. Wave 2 — Complete the EFT Chain (Days 4-8)

### 2D. Full WKB Connection Formula [LLM + SymPy + Lean/Aristotle]

**Goal:** Derive the exact WKB connection formula that maps the SK-EFT damping rate to modified Bogoliubov coefficients. This completes the theoretical chain: EFT parameters → damping rate → complex turning point → Bogoliubov coefficients → observable Hawking spectrum.

**Prerequisites:** Deep research results from 1R (WKB prompt).

**The calculation:**

*Step 1 — Dissipative mode equation:*
Start from the linearized phonon equation on the transonic background with the SK-EFT dissipative terms included. The mode equation in the WKB regime:

d²φ/dx² + [k²(x) + iΓ(k,ω,x)/c_s(x)] φ = 0

where k²(x) has a zero at the horizon (the turning point) and Γ is the damping rate from Phase 2.

*Step 2 — Complex turning point:*
With dissipation, the real turning point x_h shifts to x_h + iδx_imag where δx_imag = Γ_H/(κ·c_s). Analyze the Stokes line structure around the complex turning point.

*Step 3 — Connection formula:*
Match WKB solutions across the complex turning point. The standard Hawking result n_k = 1/(e^{2πω/κ} - 1) is modified by the dissipative shift. Derive the exact modification.

*Step 4 — Modified Bogoliubov coefficients:*
Express |α_k|² and |β_k|² in terms of the EFT parameters. The standard unitarity relation |α|² - |β|² = 1 may be modified by dissipation — determine how.

## 3. Wave 3 — Cross the Gravity Wall (Days 6-12)

### 3F. ADW Mean-Field Gap Equation [LLM + SymPy + Numerical + Lean/Aristotle]

**Goal:** Test whether the Akama-Diakonov-Wetterich mechanism can produce emergent spin-2 gravity from emergent (not fundamental) Dirac fermions. This is the "fermion bootstrap" calculation at Level 2 of the gravity hierarchy.

**Prerequisites:** Deep research results from 1R (ADW prompt).

**The calculation:**

*Step 1 — Set up Wen's model:*
Write down Wen's 3+1D lattice QED Hamiltonian that produces emergent Dirac fermions. Identify the low-energy effective Hamiltonian with 4-component Dirac spinors and emergent U(1) gauge field.

*Step 2 — Add ADW interaction:*
Introduce the ADW multi-fermion interaction. The specific form depends on the research results, but schematically:

H_ADW = -G ∫ d⁴x (ψ̄ γ^a ∂_μ ψ)(ψ̄ γ_a ∂^μ ψ) + permutations

where G is the coupling constant and the tensor structure is chosen to produce a tetrad condensate.

*Step 3 — Hubbard-Stratonovich decomposition:*
Introduce the auxiliary tetrad field e^a_μ to decouple the multi-fermion interaction:

exp(-G ∫ (4-fermion)²) → ∫ De exp(-∫ e²/G + e · (4-fermion))

This converts the interacting fermion problem to free fermions coupled to the tetrad field.

*Step 4 — Integrate out fermions:*
Compute the fermion determinant det(∂̸ + e^a_μ γ_a ∂^μ) in the background of the tetrad field. This gives the effective action S_eff[e].

*Step 5 — Saddle-point (gap) equation:*
Minimize S_eff[e] with respect to e^a_μ. The gap equation is:

δS_eff/δe^a_μ = 0

Solve for ⟨e^a_μ⟩. A nontrivial solution with Lorentzian signature (η_ab ⟨e^a_μ⟩⟨e^b_ν⟩ has signature -+++) means the fermion bootstrap works at mean-field level.

*Step 6 — Fluctuation analysis:*
Expand around the saddle point. Count Nambu-Goldstone modes: GL(4,ℝ)/SO(3,1) breaking gives 16-6 = 10 broken generators → 6 massive spin-connection modes + 2 massless spin-2 graviton modes + 2 massive modes (eaten by spin connection). Verify the Vergeles mode counting.

**Success criteria:**
- Nontrivial solution ⟨e^a_μ⟩ ≠ 0 exists → fermion bootstrap works at mean-field
- Solution has Lorentzian signature → emergent gravity is physical
- NG mode counting matches Vergeles → spin-2 graviton emerges
- If no nontrivial solution → fermion bootstrap fails at mean-field (publishable negative result)
