## 1. Wave 1 — SM Fermion Data and ℤ₁₆ Anomaly Computation [Pipeline: Stages 1-12]

**Goal:** Formalize the Standard Model's fermion content as algebraic data, compute
the ℤ₄ charges, and verify the ℤ₁₆ anomaly class. This is the core deliverable.

### The computation (from García-Etxebarria & Montero):

The discrete ℤ₄ symmetry generated by X = 5(B-L) - 4Y has a global anomaly
classified by Ω₅^{Spin^{ℤ₄}} ≅ ℤ₁₆. Each left-handed Weyl fermion with
odd ℤ₄ charge contributes +1 to the anomaly index. The SM fermion content:

| Fermion | ℤ₄ charge X | Components | Contribution |
|---------|-------------|------------|-------------|
| Q_L (quark doublet) | 1 | 6 (3 color × 2 weak) | +6 |
| ū_R (up antiquark) | 1 | 3 | +3 |
| d̄_R (down antiquark) | -3 ≡ 1 mod 4 | 3 | +3 |
| L (lepton doublet) | -3 ≡ 1 mod 4 | 2 | +2 |
| ē_R (positron) | 1 | 1 | +1 |
| ν̄_R (right-handed ν) | 5 ≡ 1 mod 4 | 1 | +1 |

- With ν_R: 6+3+3+2+1+1 = **16 ≡ 0 mod 16** (anomaly-free)
- Without ν_R: 6+3+3+2+1 = **15 ≡ -1 mod 16** (anomalous)
- 3 generations without ν_R: 3×(-1) = **-3 mod 16 ≠ 0** (forces hidden sectors)
